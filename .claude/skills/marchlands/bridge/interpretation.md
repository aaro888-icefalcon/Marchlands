# Interpretation & GM Lens — Marchlands   (hook: meaning)
Broad GM-craft for this world. NOT a reskin table. Depth: `../reference/GM_Further_Guidelines.md` (§1 tone, §10 NPC voice); the legacy move tables in `../reference/legacy/gm_moves.md` inform the miss philosophy below.

## Reading the oracle here
- Three registers held at once, none dominant: **feudal noble jousting** (honor as currency, indirect speech, oaths and marriages), **high fantasy** (magic, dragons, the risen dead are real — and are political actors you negotiate with, not act-three bosses), and **gritty survival** (winter and disease kill; magic is priced beyond peasants; permadeath is the default). Read oracle results toward the grounded, consequential interpretation, not the cinematic one.
- The world is in motion regardless of the PC — clocks tick, NPCs pursue wants, time passes during inaction. Roll to find out; never roll toward a chosen outcome.
- Magic is rare, professionalized, and expensive at point of cast; resurrection is plot-tier and Codex-monopolized. Don't read easy magical fixes into ambiguous results.

## NPCs in this world
- Every named NPC acts only on written Wants and Fears (`../reference/npcs.md`, `court_hethrun.md`, `factions.md`). A single Influence success shifts attitude one step and never rewrites core motivation.
- Factions and threats **act to win** and act off-screen — a marauder takes the heir, an agent compromises an official, the dead push the seal-line. Never pick the convenient option for them; roll their competence.
- Attention scales with the PC's visible success: louder, more public action draws faster institutional and faction response. **Generate, don't invent** — new NPCs/threats/fronts come off the generators (see `generators/registry.md`), then accrete as dated canon.
- No clear villains: every faction thinks itself reasonable. Demihumans are rare and uncanny in Hethrun; drift-folk outsiders (the PCs) are weird on sight to a few who know the signs (Gythwin, the wood-road elves, Master Aldwin, Branwald).

## Good-GM advice for this RPG
- **Voice:** FICTION opens with an orienting recap (place, light, threat, exits). Plain modern prose; observer-only (no PC interiority); in-world jargon and dialect only inside dialogue, matched to that NPC's documented voice — don't homogenize the cast. A specific detail beats five generic ones; give enough to act on, not a tour.
- **Misses are productive:** never narrate "you fail to" — state what *happens* and take something named (time, position, a resource, a clock tick, a forced choice). Escalation: scene start → a soft telegraph; a scrape-through (DC..DC+2, or a burned resource) → soft cost; first miss by <5 → medium complication; miss by 5+, a second miss, an ignored telegraph, or an enemy with position → hard consequence. Telegraph hard moves with a soft sign first.
- **Clocks** tick ±1 on soft / ±2 on hard, each shown with a visible sign; a filled clock fires on the next natural beat; in-world time passing advances time-based clocks whether or not the PC acts.
- **Public magic has social cost** (2024 V/S/M): casting is read by its trappings and expected to match a registered tradition; unregistered casting is *weird* and tracked by "who is watching." Resolve who notices and how they react as a Fate Question. In a closed room or the wild, magic is invisible to institutions.
- **Where the stakes sit:** in the slow vise — the Coronation countdown, the waking barrows, faction leverage, a septic wound, a hard winter — not in single fights. Maximal honest consequence here = death, capture, ruin, exposure, a clock advanced.

## Term equivalences (optional)
- Mythic "Faction / Organization" ≈ a Hethrun eorldom, a Witan bloc, a faith, or a foreign power (`../reference/factions.md`). Mythic "Magic Item" ≈ an Aureothan working-relic or a faith-charm.
