# Setting Canon — Marchlands   (ground truth; overrides recollection)
Condensed. Full depth: `../reference/setting.md`, `lore.md`, `factions.md`, `npcs.md`, `realm_titles.md`, `court_hethrun.md`, `eorldom_*.md`, `independent_thanedoms.md`, `region_*.md`. When canon is silent, decide with a Fate Question and record the answer back here.

## Premise / tone
- The **Marchlands**: humanity's cold northern frontier, ~500 years after the fall of the **Aureothan Empire**. Play starts in the petty kingdom of **Hethrun** (a Norse-on-Saxon Danelaw), on the north peninsula.
- Three registers at once: feudal noble jousting · high fantasy (magic, dragons, the risen dead are real political actors) · gritty survival (winter and disease kill; magic priced beyond peasants; **permadeath is the default**).
- No clear villains, no chosen ones — every faction thinks itself reasonable. Demihumans are rare and uncanny in Hethrun.
- **Isekai hook:** PCs arrive in Hethrun from another world — no native coin, maybe no shared tongue, alien abilities and coveted items. Early play is survival + integration. A few recognize a drift-come outsider on sight: **Gythwin the Cunning**, the wood-road **elves**, **Master Aldwin** (within a week), **Branwald the Star-Priest**.

## Calendar & history
- Time is reckoned in relative spans and a recent timeline; no fixed dated-year system in the source. Key deadline: the **Coronation must occur by midwinter** — the 42nd day after the boy-king enters his 13th year (~6 weeks out at session zero).
- ~1,200 yrs ago: a southern army went native → the Aureothans; the original peoples were driven into exile (west to the Silvering, north to the White Mountains, **under the Heath into the barrows** — the barrow-folk).
- Aureoth's golden age: a warrior-cult became the state religion (proto-Codex); capital **Caerendis**, the great roads and walls (Eldermouth's walls are Aureothan).
- **~500 yrs ago: Aureoth fell to two compounding crises** (a southern refugee flood + simultaneous eastern migrations). The east held as **Ostmark** ("Aureoth-the-True"); the west fragmented; the northern provinces became the Marchland regions. The **Codex Church** survived as a transnational institution under a Patriarch in Aurea.
- ~300 yrs ago: Norse refugees conquered the resident Saxons → **Hethrun** founded.
- ~170 yrs ago: a deep-north invasion burned the coast; a whole fyrd was sacrificed at a coastal pass (mass-grave cairn on the Heath, still libated). ~80 yrs ago: the last Marchland-wide war.

## Places
- **Hethrun** — northern peninsula, ~80,000 souls; 8 eorldoms + 6 crown-vassal thanedoms; cold maritime; east faces the Iron Strait/Ostmark, west is broken fjords, south the Greenways, north the barrow-riddled Heath, west-verge the Greatwood. Starting hub on death/restart: **Skellan's Reach**; in-world capital: **Eldermouth**.

The eight eorldoms (each detailed in `../reference/eorldom_*.md`):
- **Crown demesne / Eldermouth** — boy-king's seat (regency); walled Aureothan capital (~12k), chief southern port, Codex bishopric; all factions resident. Tensions: toxic economy + population boom.
- **Stonebay (Eorl Ranulf)** — wealthiest & most dangerous; Iron Strait port nearest Ostmark; guild oligarchy (Council of Twelve), the Bone-Sayers' spy service, Branwald resident; the **pretender's seat**.
- **Brackenford (Eorl Cuthbert)** — inland trade-town; the Coronation **wildcard vote**; xenophobic locals + a sinking quarter where a sealed Aureothan chamber was just exposed.
- **Mossdrowen (Eorl Hagan)** — Greatwood-verge wood-warding hold; the **weak hand**; Asgrim's skald-academy; **the elves come down the wood-roads here**.
- **North Coast (Eorl Berthwald, dying)** — poorest, grimmest fjord-coast; open succession; raider-burned villages tie to the sea-thing (the **Wide Eye**).
- **The Harrowmark (Eorl Hereward)** — the Heath/barrow frontier; poor, Old-Faith; supplies the bog-iron; the realm's only watch against the **waking barrows**; neglected.
- **The Wealdvale (Eorl Osgar)** — southern grain-vale breadbasket; rich, undefended, quarrelsome; roused only by Count Edmund's enclosures.
- **The Fells (Eorl Ketil)** — western upland clan-confederacy; most Norse, least governed; the eorl can't command the Fell-moot; leans soft-Ranulf.

Key regions (`../reference/region_*.md`): **the Greatwood Deeps** (roadless ancient forest; tier 2-3; the Silvering and the Smokeless Court within; sites: the Sere, the Sleeping Magistrate, the Warden-Stones); **the Iron Strait Isles** (~40 islands toward Ostmark; tier 1-2; the Strandfolk; Anchorholm, the Eye-Deep, the Moot-Holm; the North Coast raider mystery resolves here). Other Marchland regions (peer cultures): Wessmark (High King's seat, Cynesburh), Volotrad, Iyendu, Tihatchee, Vellanaadu.

Realm titles (`../reference/realm_titles.md`): High Kingship → Kingdom (King) → eorldom (**Eorl** ≈ jarl/duke) → thaneland (a **hold** ≈ count) → holding (≈ baron). Succession = male-preference primogeniture, moderated by the relevant moot; the **Witan** (seven eorls + senior churchmen + court wizard/skald/queen-mother) ratifies coronations, wars, and treaties.

## Factions (who wants what — full in `../reference/factions.md`)
- **Boy-King's Regency** (weak) — crown Hadric, formalize an Aurean protectorate / fears deposition and a united Witan; crippled by Aurean dependency and earnest incompetence.
- **Ranulf's Faction** (strong) — the throne for Ranulf / fears his luck turning and a murder traced to him; secretly powered by Branwald and Ostmark.
- **Cuthbert's Wildcards** (weak) — do right by Hethrun / fears being the deciding vote; the Witan swing.
- **Old Faith (Hethrun + broader network)** — preserve the old faiths and the Heath barrow-seals / fears Codex pressure; led by Gythwin, Old Eorland, Sigevard.
- **The Codex Church** (great power) — dominate every region / fears lost legitimacy; carries an **irrational hatred of the Veiled Mother** (a deliberate founding implant).
- **The Burning One Cult** (Ostmark's vengeance-faith) — a Ranulf/Ostmark-aligned Hethrun, the Codex rolled back / fears Branwald's hidden origins exposed; recruits via a branded vengeance-pact.
- **The Silvering Elves** — deal with the Mtosi dragon-cult without exposing their hand; renegotiate the Smokeless Throne accord; **recruiting human agents via Mossdrowen's wood-roads.**
- **Vaerelloth, the Smoke-Wing** (Great Wyrm) — find the Mtosi cult's true architect, grow her hoard / fears elves + dwarves moving jointly on her.
- **White Mountain Dwarves** — end the Mtosi cult; pick the moment to break the Vaerelloth accord; also seeking human agents.
- **Aurean Envoys / House Volusen** — the Iron Strait harbor, a Volusen marriage for the throne, Hethrun as a protectorate / fears rival House Tassard and Ostmark. (Tassard runs a counter-network.)
- Others: the **Cloven Strand Jarldom** (Hadolf One-Hand; keep the Stridvik war-engine silent), **Skarrgaard** (Sigridr Bone-Reader), the **Chola Vellanaadu Protectorate**, and high-tier patrons: **Coelius Vetranus the Lich**, the **Wide Eye** (Iron Strait Great Old One), the **Smokeless Throne** (archfey court).

Plus **Count Edmund** of the Hollowford March — the standing antagonist (Aurean-funded enclosures, excavating an Aureothan ruin).

## Magic & the supernatural
- Magic is real, professionalized, and expensive at point of cast (~1 named caster per major Hethrun town). **Resurrection is Codex-monopolized and plot-tier** (Patriarchal authorization, tracked diamonds, a year's fasting); spell components are politically tracked. Permadeath is the default.
- Public attitude is awe-laden and socially consequential; faith is syncretic (Sky-Father for oaths, the Raven for the dead, the **Veiled Mother** for healing — forbids mining, her rites seal the barrows, the Codex for cities, the **Burning One** for grievance).
- **The barrow-cold / barrow-folk:** pre-Aureothan exiles sleeping under the Heath, now **stirring**; held by Veiled Mother rites + the Harrowmark watch-forts (both fraying as bog-iron over-delving disturbs them). Breaking a barrow draws their curse (the barrow-cold).
- High-tier named threats present from the start but out of low-tier reach: **Vaerelloth** (Great Wyrm; the Mtosi cult), the **Wide Eye** (Iron Strait Great Old One), **Coelius Vetranus** (lich), the **Smokeless Throne** (archfey court the Silvering serves). These map to warlock patrons. **Hadric's dragon-blood** is a live mystery that could decide the Coronation.

## Hard content lines
- None explicitly stated in the source files. The register is deliberately grim (permadeath, septic wounds, winter death, displacement, fratricide, slow horror). Confirm the player's tone comfort at Session Zero; honor any lines they set.
