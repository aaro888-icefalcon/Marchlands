## Premise / tone
- The **Marchlands**: humanity's cold northern frontier, ~500 years after the fall of the **Aureothan Empire**. Play is in **Hethrun** (a Norse-on-Saxon Danelaw) on the north peninsula — until lately a **major Marchland power** (~2 million souls), whose king rose to **Marshal of the High King**. He **died suddenly**, and the realm is now tipping into **civil war** (see History & Factions).
- Three registers at once: feudal noble jousting · high fantasy (magic, dragons, the risen dead are real political actors) · gritty survival (winter and disease kill; **threats run high-powered and deadly**; potent/guaranteed magic stays costly and rare; **permadeath is the default**). Low magic is *common* (see Magic) — the grit is in the **danger**, not in magic-scarcity.
- No clear villains, no chosen ones — every faction thinks itself reasonable. Demihumans are rare and uncanny in Hethrun.
- **Isekai hook (drift-folk):** people arrive from other worlds — a **known, ongoing phenomenon**; the **Crossing** (≈8 million New Yorkers torn in at once) is its unprecedented mass-event. Drift-folk arrive with nothing and usually no locally-useful skills → an exploited underclass; *rarely* one keeps class powers (**drift-marked** — see Drift-folk). Early play is survival + integration.

## Calendar & history
- Time is reckoned in relative spans; no fixed dated-year system.
- **THE LIVE SPINE — the succession crisis:** Hethrun's late king (boy-king **Hadric**'s father) rose to **Marshal of the High King** and was rallying a **Marchlands-wide invasion of the Aureothan Empire** (i.e., **Ostmark**, "Aureoth-the-True," in the east). He **died suddenly** — and the mobilized armies and ambitions turned *inward*. The **Coronation** (Hadric must be crowned by midwinter, ~6 weeks out) is the flashpoint of a realm sliding into **civil war**: **Eorl Ranulf of Stonebay is already in open war**; **Count Edmund** (Aurea-backed, west) and **Count Halfdan** (northern, deep-north-backed) are poised to follow.
- ~1,200 yrs ago: a southern army went native → the Aureothans; the original peoples were exiled (west to the Silvering, north to the White Mountains, **under the Heath into the barrows** — the barrow-folk).
- Aureoth's golden age: a warrior-cult became the state religion (proto-Codex); capital **Caerendis**, the great roads and walls (Eldermouth's walls are Aureothan).
- **~500 yrs ago: Aureoth fell** to two compounding crises; the east held as **Ostmark**; the west fragmented; the north became the Marchland regions. The **Codex Church** survived transnationally under a Patriarch in Aurea.
- ~300 yrs ago: Norse refugees conquered the resident Saxons → **Hethrun** founded. ~170 yrs ago: a deep-north invasion burned the coast (mass-grave cairn on the Heath, still libated). ~80 yrs ago: the last Marchland-wide war.

## Places
- **Hethrun** — northern peninsula, **~2,000,000 souls** (a major Marchland power); 8 eorldoms + crown-vassal thanedoms; cold maritime; east faces the Iron Strait/Ostmark, west broken fjords, south the Greenways, north the barrow-riddled Heath, west-verge the Greatwood. Starting hub on death/restart: **Skellan's Reach**; capital: **Eldermouth**.

The eight eorldoms (each detailed in `../reference/eorldom_*.md`):
- **Crown demesne / Eldermouth** — boy-king's seat (regency); walled Aureothan capital (**~45k**), chief southern port, Codex bishopric; all factions resident. Tensions: toxic economy + population boom + a leaderless war-mobilization.
- **Stonebay (Eorl Ranulf)** — wealthiest & most dangerous; Iron Strait port nearest Ostmark; guild oligarchy (Council of Twelve), the Bone-Sayers' spy service, Branwald resident; **the pretender — and the one already AT WAR.**
- **Brackenford (Eorl Cuthbert)** — inland trade-town; the Coronation **wildcard vote**; xenophobic locals + a sinking quarter where a sealed Aureothan chamber was just exposed.
- **Mossdrowen (Eorl Hagan)** — Greatwood-verge wood-warding hold; the **weak hand**; Asgrim's skald-academy; **the elves come down the wood-roads here**.
- **North Coast (Eorl Berthwald, dying)** — poorest, grimmest fjord-coast; **open succession** (a vacuum **Count Halfdan** is moving into); raider-burned villages tie to the sea-thing (the **Wide Eye**).
- **The Harrowmark (Eorl Hereward)** — the Heath/barrow frontier; poor, Old-Faith; supplies the bog-iron; the realm's only watch against the **waking barrows**; neglected and fraying.
- **The Wealdvale (Eorl Osgar)** — southern grain-vale breadbasket; rich, undefended, quarrelsome; roused by Count Edmund's enclosures.
- **The Fells (Eorl Ketil)** — western upland clan-confederacy; most Norse, least governed; leans soft-Ranulf.

Key regions (`../reference/region_*.md`): **the Greatwood Deeps** (roadless ancient forest; the Silvering & the Smokeless Court; the Sere, the Sleeping Magistrate, the Warden-Stones); **the Iron Strait Isles** (~40 islands toward Ostmark; the Strandfolk; Anchorholm, the Eye-Deep, the Moot-Holm). Peer Marchland regions: Wessmark (High King's seat, Cynesburh), Volotrad, Iyendu, Tihatchee, Vellanaadu (Chola-stock). Deep-north powers: the **Cloven Strand Jarldom** (Hadolf One-Hand) & **Skarrgaard** (Sigridr Bone-Reader) — now patrons of **Count Halfdan**.

Realm titles (`../reference/realm_titles.md`): High Kingship → Kingdom (King) → eorldom (**Eorl** ≈ jarl/duke) → thaneland (a **hold** ≈ count) → holding (≈ baron). Succession = male-preference primogeniture, moderated by the relevant moot; the **Witan** (seven eorls + senior churchmen + court wizard/skald/queen-mother) ratifies coronations, wars, and treaties.

## Factions (who wants what — full in `../reference/factions.md`)
**The civil war forming (the power-players):**
- **Boy-King's Regency** (the legitimate weak center) — crown **Hadric** by midwinter, hold the realm together / fears deposition; crippled by Aurean dependency, earnest incompetence, and the dead Marshal-king's unfinished war.
- **Eorl Ranulf of Stonebay** (strong) — **already in open war**; the throne for Ranulf / fears his luck turning and a murder traced to him. Secretly powered by **Branwald** and **Ostmark** (the very empire the late king meant to invade).
- **Count Edmund** of the Hollowford March (Aurea-backed, west) — ambitious hardliner & encloser; excavating an Aureothan ruin; **poised to rise.** *(Holds the keep this campaign's opening is set in.)*
- **Count Halfdan** (deep-north-backed, north) — a hard northern marcher lord moving into the **North Coast succession vacuum**, tied to **Skarrgaard / the Cloven Strand**; ambitious, **poised**; his troops are skirmishing Ranulf's incursions (and took the opening's captives).
- **Cuthbert's Wildcards** (weak) — do right by Hethrun / fears being the deciding Witan vote.

**Standing powers (full in `factions.md`):** **Old Faith** (preserve the old faiths & the Heath barrow-seals; Gythwin, Old Eorland, Sigevard) — **indifferent to drift-folk.** · **The Codex Church** (great power; dominate every region; irrational hatred of the **Veiled Mother**) — **divided on drift-folk** (a revered prophet was one; so was a reviled heretic). · **The Burning One Cult** (Ostmark's vengeance-faith, Ranulf-aligned). · **The Silvering Elves** & **White Mountain Dwarves** (vs. the Mtosi dragon-cult; recruiting human agents via Mossdrowen's wood-roads). · **Vaerelloth the Smoke-Wing** (Great Wyrm). · **Aurean Envoys / House Volusen** (protectorate-seekers & Edmund's backers; rival House Tassard). · High-tier patrons: **Coelius the Lich**, the **Wide Eye**, the **Smokeless Throne**.

## Magic & the supernatural
- **Common at the low end, rare at the high:** cantrips are widespread (most villages have a dabbler — any class: artificer/wizard/sorcerer/bard/warlock/cleric/druid); **low spells (1st–2nd) medium-common** (a village hedge-caster; towns several; cities many); **mid (3rd–5th) medium-uncommon** (towns a practitioner or two, cities several — for real coin); **high (6th+), guaranteed cures, & resurrection are rare, costly, Codex-gated & plot-tier.** Casting basic magic in public is **unremarkable.**
- **Grit lives in the threats:** monsters, Named Threats, and the deep wilds run **high-powered and lethal** — that, plus winter, disease, and the cost of *reliable* high magic, keeps death real. Permadeath is the default.
- Faith is syncretic (Sky-Father for oaths, the Raven for the dead, the **Veiled Mother** for healing — forbids mining, her rites seal the barrows; the Codex for cities; the **Burning One** for grievance).
- **The barrow-cold / barrow-folk:** pre-Aureothan exiles sleeping under the Heath, now **stirring** (Veiled Mother rites + Harrowmark watch-forts hold them, both fraying as bog-iron over-delving and the Crossing's Working disturb the seals). Breaking a barrow draws the barrow-cold.
- High-tier named threats present but out of low-tier reach: **Vaerelloth** (Great Wyrm), the **Wide Eye** (Iron Strait Great Old One), **Coelius Vetranus** (lich), the **Smokeless Throne** (archfey). **Hadric's dragon-blood** is a live mystery that could decide the Coronation.

## Drift-folk (the isekai underclass)
- **Known, ongoing phenomenon;** the Crossing is the mass-event. Most folk **don't much care**; if a person's known drift-folk it's a **minor stigma or minor boon** by context. Arriving with nothing & no local skills, drift-folk are in practice **dispossessed, exploited, indentured** — an underclass. The hardship is **economic, not demonic.**
- **Codex: divided** (a revered **prophet** was drift-folk; so was a recent reviled **"anti-Christ"** — holy or damned, case-by-case, politically charged). **Old Faith: indifferent.**
- **Drift-marked (kept class powers):** rare — needs luck + nearness to a portal at transfer. Retention by original level — **L1 10% · L3–4 5% · L5–6 3% · L7–8 1%.** A drift-marked one = a **skilled local professional.**
- **Terms:** powered → **drift-blessed / drift-marked** (neutral-good), **drift-tainted** (hostile). Unpowered → **drift-free** (neutral), **drift-cursed** (pitying).
