# System Profile — Marchlands (D&D 2024 / 5.5e)   (hook: resolve)
Full rules: `../reference/5_5e_Reference.md` · GM craft: `../reference/GM_Further_Guidelines.md` · monsters: `../reference/bestiary.md`

- **Dice convention:** d20 + ability mod (+ proficiency) vs DC. Express a roll as `dice.py roll 1d20+5`; advantage/disadvantage = `dice.py roll 1d20+5 adv|dis`.
- **Core resolution:** meet-or-beat the DC. Ladder: 5 trivial / 10 easy / 15 medium / 20 hard / 25 very hard / 30 near-impossible. Set the DC from fiction and difficulty **before** the roll; a good plan grants advantage or better position, never a lower DC.
- **Degrees of success?:** no — skill checks are binary vs DC (no margin tiers). Specials: nat 20 = Heroic Inspiration (auto-hit/crit on attacks); nat 1 = auto-miss on attacks. In a rule-mode Fate Question, collapse Exceptional results. Death saves and Lingering Injuries do use margins (below).
- **Stats / skills:** STR DEX CON INT WIS CHA; standard 2024 skill list (Athletics; Acrobatics, Sleight of Hand, Stealth; Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, Religion; Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Perception, Survival; Deception, Intimidation, Performance, Persuasion). Backgrounds grant ability boosts + an Origin Feat; species grant traits only.
- **Defenses / health:** AC, HP, death saves. At 0 HP and not killed outright: d20 vs DC 10 each turn (3 successes = stable; 3 failures = dead; nat 20 = regain 1 HP and rise; nat 1 = two failures; a hit at 0 HP = one failure, two on a crit). Massive damage (one hit ≥ HP max while at 0) = dead outright. Exhaustion: −2 to all d20 tests per level, −5 ft speed per level; 6 levels = death.
- **Combat:** initiative d20+Dex (surprised = disadvantage on initiative only, no skipped turn). Attack roll vs AC; damage = weapon/spell dice + mod; **PC** crits roll the dice twice; **monsters do not crit** (only PCs and GM-named villains). **Weapon Mastery** on martial weapons (Cleave, Graze, Nick, Push, Sap, Slow, Topple, Vex). Use the `5_5e_Reference.md` §19 output blocks for enemy turns and PC prompts.
- **Defeat / death is real:** death saves, then dead — no rescue. **Lingering Injuries (§20):** roll 1d20 on the injury table when a PC takes a crit, drops to 0 HP, or fails a death save by 5+. Stabilizing spends a healer's-kit charge.
- **NPC stat units:** AC · HP · attack bonus · damage dice · CR. Stat enemies from `../reference/bestiary.md` (pull the whole group at first contact; it has a reskin generator). On the fly: decide the expected value → Fate Question → `oracle.py answer npc_statistics` (Yes = as expected; ExcYes +25%; No −25%; ExcNo −50%).
- **Routing default:** the RPG resolves anything with a stat — combat, attacks, ability/skill checks, saves, spells, death saves, the Influence action. Defer to a **Fate Question** for world uncertainty the rules don't model (does an off-screen NPC know X; is the road watched; did a faction already move; is the contact present; what's here / what happens next).
- **Subsystems as Fate Questions:** (1) **public/visible-magic social fallout** — who notices unregistered casting (2024 V/S/M trappings) and how an institution reacts; (2) **faction influence / off-screen faction moves** — which faction acted, whom they reached, whether leverage landed. Influence DCs are GM-dynamic; one success shifts attitude one step max.
