# The Greatwood Deeps — Region Profile

## Essence

The Greatwood Deeps are the old-forest interior beyond Hethrun's wood-road verge — the territory the verge-crofters point at and do not enter. Where `eorldom_mossdrowen.md` ends at the Greatwood verge, this region begins: a roadless expanse of ancient timber running west and south toward the Silvering's true borders. It is not Hethrun's. It is not, quite, anyone's. The Silvering elves hold its margins, the Smokeless Throne courts its hollows, and older things hold its wounded heart. For a Hethrun party this is the first genuinely wild place — a tier-2-to-3 region of fae law, deep quiet, and a forest that remembers being injured. Drift-folk hooks, House Hollantun's bargain, and the wood-road's longer mysteries all lead here.

## Geography and terrain

The Deeps climb from the verge in three rough bands. The **outer wood** — a day or two in — is old but ordinary: huge trees, little undergrowth, green gloom, game-trails that a careful traveler can follow. The **middle wood** is the elf-marches, where the trees stand in patterns too regular to be chance and the light comes wrong; here the Silvering's wardens watch, and a traveler is permitted or is not. The **heart** is the warped ground (see *Arratu Land*, below) — a region of the forest where an old working festers and the wood has grown sick around it.

There are no roads and no true settlements; the wood-road dies at the verge. Travel is measured in days of trackless walking, and direction itself is unreliable in the middle wood and unusable in the heart. Climate is cool, wet, and still — the canopy holds the weather out and the silence in. The Deeps are large enough that no one, elf or human, has mapped them, and old enough that the Aureothan empire's three centuries here left only a single tomb the trees have since swallowed.

## Wilderness tag: Arratu Land

The heart of the Deeps is **the Sere** — a stretch of forest, perhaps ten miles across, where the wood is wrong. Centuries ago a working was made here and broke; the elves will not say whose, or what it was meant to do, only that it was never meant to last and now cannot be stopped. The Sere is not dead — it is *over-alive*. Trees grow in a single season and rot in the next; water runs uphill in places; a traveler's wounds knit too fast and then keep knitting; the blights of the verge (`bestiary.md`, Group 2A) are spawned here and spread outward. The Silvering wardens patrol the Sere's edge to keep it from widening and do not enter. The Smokeless Throne, by contrast, finds the Sere useful, and courts its hollows.

**Manifests as:**
- A genuinely hazardous zone — the warped magic has physical, unpredictable effects on those who linger; the deeper one goes, the less reliable the world becomes (a major dungeon-equivalent in open air; tier 3).
- The source of the verge's blight — stopping the blight at Mossdrowen means, eventually, addressing the Sere, which no one knows how to do.
- The thing the Silvering most wants contained and the Smokeless Throne most wants used — a fault-line between two powers, with the players able to tip it.

## Wilderness tag: Overgrown Tomb

In the outer wood, a day past the verge, stands **the Sleeping Magistrate** — an Aureothan-era tomb the forest has all but eaten. It was a provincial magistrate's grave-house in the empire's last century, raised when the Greatwood was held timber and the Deeps were merely "the back of the wood." Three hundred years of root and bough have cracked its dome, pried its door, and pulled half of it underground; from outside it reads as a moss-furred hillock with worked stone showing through. The verge-crofters know it and avoid it. The elves regard it as a human matter and ignore it. Something inside does not ignore visitors.

**Manifests as:**
- A small-to-medium dungeon (tier 1-2) — the magistrate's grave-house, partly collapsed, partly root-flooded, with genuine Aureothan grave-goods and an honest threat keeping them (a Barrow-Dead-style haunt; see `bestiary.md`, Group 1A — the Magistrate makes a fine Mummy-statblock capstone).
- An accessible, low-stakes entry into the Deeps — a clear objective a day's walk in, before the region's deeper strangeness is committed to.
- A historical key — the Magistrate's grave-records, if recovered, date and place the Aureothan presence in the wood and may bear on the Sere's origin.

## Notable sites

- **The Sere.** The warped heart of the Deeps. *Tier-3 hazard region; the source of the verge blight.*
- **The Sleeping Magistrate.** Aureothan tomb, forest-swallowed, a day past the verge. *Tier 1-2 dungeon.*
- **The Warden-Stones.** A ring of standing stones in the middle wood where the Silvering's marches formally begin; a traveler who waits at the stones is, in time, met — and judged. *Parley site; the proper way to enter elf-held wood without giving offense.*
- **Hollantun's Glade.** The clearing where the verge-thane Leofric of Hollantun (`eorldom_mossdrowen.md`) made his bargain; what he bargained with keeps a court here. *Tier-2 site; the destination of the Hollantun hook.*
- **The Smokeless Court.** A hollow in the middle wood the Smokeless Throne's emissaries use; its location moves, and finding it twice is never the same walk. *Tier 2-3; faction site, see `factions.md`.*

## Active encounter themes

- Silvering wardens (elf-marches patrols — they challenge before they harm; whether a party is permitted onward is a roleplay outcome, not a fight)
- Wood-Things and Deepwood Beasts (`bestiary.md`, Groups 2A and 2B — the verge groups deepen and intensify here)
- Blight spreading outward from the Sere (the further in, the thicker)
- Smokeless Throne emissaries (offering bargains — the wood's signature temptation)
- Drift-folk who wandered in and did not wander out (the Deeps catch the lost; some are dead, some are changed, a few are alive and want the way home)
- Direction-failure and lost time (the middle wood and the Sere bend travel itself; treat as a recurring non-combat hazard)

## Place in the campaign

The Greatwood Deeps are a destination, not a thoroughfare — a region the campaign reaches when it is ready for genuine wilderness and fae law rather than feudal intrigue. It rewards a tier-2 party and punishes a careless one. Three threads run into it from the established corpus: the **verge blight** (its cure is in the Sere), **House Hollantun's bargain** (its counterparty courts Hollantun's Glade), and the **Smokeless Throne** (whose dealings are easiest here, on its own ground). Hold the Deeps in reserve for the campaign's second act; let the verge of `eorldom_mossdrowen.md` be the players' first taste, and let what they glimpse there pull them inward.
