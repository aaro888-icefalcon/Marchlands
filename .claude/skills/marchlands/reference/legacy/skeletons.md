## Prologue Summary

Thirty minutes ago, New York City disappeared around you.

The first thing was the mark. It opened over the skyline, black and clean-edged, turning slowly above towers, bridges, hospitals, subway grates, apartment blocks, rivers, and streets. It was not smoke, weather, aircraft, fire, or projection. People stopped walking. Cars drifted through red lights. Phones came up. Then people started vanishing.

You saw strangers go first. Then someone you knew. [Insert named loved one, friend, colleague, or familiar person from the PC’s Earth history.] Their last visible moment was [specific grounded image]. Then the same pressure took you.

You came up on wet grass under cold rain, on a long slope of black turf and old stones. Other New Yorkers were around you, soaked and shouting and reaching for phones that had no signal. Behind you, one of the barrow-stones carried the same mark that had turned above New York.

Your hand came against the stone as you caught yourself on the slope. Something entered through the bones of your arm. Not speech. Not memory exactly. Competence. Stance. Grip. Footwork. The shape of danger. The weight of tools and weapons you had not known a minute earlier. For [PC name], this became [insert class-specific awakening: weapon mastery, spell-shapes, familiar bond, divine pressure, rogue timing, ranger tracking sense, etc.]. The stone did not explain itself.

The others changed unevenly. Most stayed ordinary. Tara Singh had pale light moving under the skin of her fingers and kept saying she had not done anything. Owen Price moved like a man whose training had suddenly become sharper than it had any right to be. Rachel Kim started triaging injuries. Marcus Vale gathered names and objects like evidence. Ben Alvarez, bleeding from the scalp, still helped move supplies. Leah Rosen asked where her mother was.

Owen found the caravan down the lower slope, where the mist pooled. One broken cart. One mule torn open in the mud. Two local guards dead nearby. Coin gone. Useful things left behind: rope, knives, a hatchet, damp cloaks, a cracked lantern, oats, a tally-stick, a broken wheel-pin, and a torn road token. The corpses showed signs that did not fit a simple robbery: frost in a beard despite above-freezing rain, old dirt under nails, drag marks into the mist, and one cold click from the marked stone when blood touched near the carving.

That was enough time for the survivors to become a crowd with factions. Rachel wanted shelter and wound care. Owen wanted a perimeter and weapons. Marcus wanted procedure. Tara wanted people to stop looking at her hands. Leah stayed small and watchful near the adults who looked least likely to abandon her.

Then the hound barked from below the cart.

Hroth Soot-Hand came up through the rain with his hands open and his crew already spreading through the mist. Aescwynn looked past the survivors to the stone. Osred held the right flank with a weighted net. Lilla watched the slope and the road-line. Sibba stood near the crew like a translator who had learned not to speak first. Others were half-seen behind rain and fog.

Hroth said, “Easy. All of you. Hands where I can see them. You’re drift-come. You’re on bad ground. We can get you fed, warm, and legal if you don’t do anything stupid.”

Behind you, the marked stone clicked once.
