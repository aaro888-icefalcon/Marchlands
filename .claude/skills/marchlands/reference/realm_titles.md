# The Kingdom of Hethrun — Realm Structure

The realm-level frame for Hethrun: the feudal title hierarchy, the realm's geography and travel distances, every eorldom and independent thanedom in summary, the seven-eorl Witan, and the cross-eorldom marriage and feud web.

## Using this file

This file is the map that ties the realm together. Each eorldom has its own `eorldom_<name>.md` carrying full detail on its seat, court, thane houses, lands, minor communities, and hooks; the six independent thanedoms are detailed together in `independent_thanedoms.md`. This file holds only what spans eorldoms: the title system, the realm geography, the Coronation-crisis vote at eorl level, and the marriage and feud web. For the royal court and the succession procedure see `court_hethrun.md`. For the crisis clocks see `active_threats.md`.

## The feudal title hierarchy

The Kingdom of Hethrun's titles map onto five tiers. The table gives the CK3-equivalent, the Hethrun term, the holder, and the unit governed.

| Tier | Hethrun title | Holder | Governs |
|------|---------------|--------|---------|
| Empire | the Marchland High Kingship | the High King (Cyneberht, at Wessmark) | the six Marchland kingdoms |
| Kingdom | the Kingdom of Hethrun | the King (Hadric, age 12; regency under Lady Isolde) | the realm |
| Duchy | an eorldom | an Eorl | several thanelands |
| County | a thaneland | a hold-rank Thane (a *hold*) | several holdings |
| Barony | a holding — a hall, town, or abbey | a lesser Thane, the count, or the Church | one Castle, City, or Temple |

A *hold* (Norse *hauldr*) is a thane of the first rank, wealthy enough to hold a thaneland and a seat in the eorl's hall-moot — the count-equivalent. Lesser thanes hold single halls beneath a hold, as barons sit beneath a count. Succession at every tier is male-preference primogeniture, moderated by the relevant moot: an eorl's hall-moot may set aside an unfit heir for a capable sibling or cousin, and the Witan does the same at the kingdom tier. Eorls are vassals of the Crown of Hethrun; hold-rank thanes of their eorl; lesser thanes of their count. The crown demesne and the six independent thanedoms are held of the crown directly.

## Offices of the High Kingship

Above the kingdom tier sit the offices of the Marchland High Kingship — appointments the High King at Wessmark grants across the six kingdoms, distinct from the inherited title-ladder above. The chief of these is military:

- **Marshal of the High King.** The paramount *military* office of the High Kingship — the High King's appointed war-leader, empowered to muster and command a Marchlands-wide host drawn from the six kingdoms when a realm-spanning war is declared. It is granted to a proven king or great lord, not inherited, and it confers standing far beyond the holder's own crown. **Hethrun's late king (Hadric's father) held the office of Marshal of the High King** at his death, and was using it to rally a Marchlands-wide invasion of the Aureothan Empire (Ostmark); when he died suddenly the muster fell apart and the mobilization turned inward. The office is now vacant, and its absence is part of what leaves the realm's gathered armies leaderless and the realm sliding toward civil war.

## The realm: geography and scale

The Kingdom of Hethrun occupies the northern peninsula of the Marchlands — a realm of some twelve to thirteen days' ride corner to corner. Its eastern coast faces the Iron Strait and Ostmark beyond; its western coast is the broken-fjord North Coast, open to the sea. The southern boundary is the Greenways, arable river valleys running toward the Greatwood and the Hollowford March. The northern interior is the Heath, a moor-and-bog plateau riddled with barrows. The western interior climbs through hill country to the Greatwood verge, beyond which lies elven territory.

The realm's eight eorldoms occupy this ground as follows: the crown demesne holds Eldermouth and the central Greenways; Stonebay holds the Iron Strait coast; the North Coast eorldom holds the western fjord-coast; the Harrowmark is the Heath; the Wealdvale is the southern Greenways; Brackenford holds the inland centre; Mossdrowen holds the Greatwood verge; the Fells hold the western upland above that verge. Climate is cold-temperate maritime — long wet winters, short bright summers, autumn that bites early on the Heath and lingers in the Greenways.

## Travel and distances

Distances between the realm's chief seats, mounted. Walking is roughly half again to twice as long; winter and the Heath add more.

| Route | Time (mounted) | Notes |
|-------|----------------|-------|
| Eldermouth – Stonebay | 3 days | coast road, reasonably safe, two villages en route |
| Eldermouth – Brackenford | 2 days | southern road, Aureothan stone for 60 miles |
| Brackenford – Mossdrowen | 2 days | wood-road, less safe, crosses the Greatwood verge |
| Eldermouth – Harrowfast (Harrowmark) | 1–2 days | the moor begins where the lowland ends; dangerous without a guide |
| Eldermouth – Wealdham (Wealdvale) | 2 days | south through the Greenways; Hollowford checkpoints |
| Eldermouth – Sandhaven (North Coast) | 2 days | west to the fjord-coast |
| Mossdrowen – Carngarth (the Fells) | 2–3 days | climbing into the upland; rough track |

The realm is compact enough that the Witan can be summoned to the Kingsmound within a week — which is what makes the Coronation deadline real.

## The six independent thanedoms

An **independent thanedom** is a thaneland held of the Crown of Hethrun directly, under no eorl. Their thanes are county-rank but answer only to the King, which makes them free agents: in the Coronation crisis they are courted directly, by both sides, with no eorl mediating. They do not sit the Witan, but their fyrd and their public backing sway it. The six are detailed in `independent_thanedoms.md`; in summary:

| Thanedom | Thane (count) | Location | Character | Coronation lean |
|----------|---------------|----------|-----------|-----------------|

## The seven-eorl Witan and the Coronation vote

The Witan that must confirm Hadric's coronation is the seven eorls of Hethrun plus the realm's senior churchmen. The succession procedure is in `court_hethrun.md`; the Coronation Clock is in `active_threats.md`. The seven eorls' session-zero leans:

- **Ranulf of Stonebay** — himself the pretender.
- **Cuthbert of Brackenford** — the wildcard, genuinely undecided.
- **Hagan of Mossdrowen** — weak; votes as his ablest advisor steers him, which currently means regency.
- **Berthwald of the North Coast** — dying; his vote is hostage to his own unresolved succession.
- **Hereward of the Harrowmark** — soft regency, for stability while the barrows wake; neglected by the regency and therefore winnable.
- **Osgar of the Wealdvale** — soft regency; his vote belongs to whoever credibly promises to check the Hollowford March.
- **Ketil of the Fells** — soft Ranulf, by Fell distrust of the foreign-born regent; courtable by a regency that respects Fell autonomy.

Only Ranulf is committed. Two regency-leaning eorls (Hereward, Osgar) lean on conditions the regency is currently failing to meet; one Ranulf-leaning eorl (Ketil) is courtable. The Coronation is a seven-way canvass, and three of the seven are exactly the winnable, conditional vote a party of agents is sent to secure.

## The marriage, fostering, and feud web

The threads that bind the realm's noble houses across eorldom borders. Each is a live connection a GM can pull; the houses themselves are detailed in their `eorldom_<name>.md` files. Ties internal to a single eorldom live in that eorldom's file. New ties from the Harrowmark, Wealdvale, Fells, and the thanedoms are added as those files are built.

- **The Holmgarth–Thornwic blood feud.** House Holmgarth of Stonebay and House Thornwic of Brackenford carry a forty-year unsettled feud — a Holmgarth border raid killed Thornwic kin, no weregild paid. The Coronation gives each a lawful way to strike at the other across the eorldom line.
- **The Merrowdene–Ranulf estrangement.** House Merrowdene of Stonebay and Eorl Ranulf's line were once sworn-brothers; a broken marriage-promise turned them. Gunnhild of Merrowdene now leads the prosperity-party against Ranulf's war inside his own eorldom.
- **Castermere's debt and marriage web.** House Castermere of Stonebay has married into a dozen Stonebay guild families and holds the Mossdrowen eorldom's debt — Stonebay holding economic leverage over Mossdrowen.
- **The Greycliff debt.** House Greycliff of Stonebay is mortgaged to the Bone-Sayers; whoever holds the paper holds Greycliff's hall-moot vote and fyrd.
- **Fostering: Mildgyth of Brackenford at Wivelham.** Eorl Cuthbert's younger daughter is fostered with House Wivelham, the Old Faith covert house — which is how Mildryd's network reaches the eorl's own family.
- **The Maerstow poisoning.** House Maerstow of Brackenford is slowly poisoning Cuthbert's ill heir Wulfnoth to clear the Brackenford succession for a Maerstow marriage. A live clock (see `active_threats.md`).
- **Sedgemoor, bought.** House Sedgemoor of Brackenford is paid by Cassian Drey and House Volusen to steer the undecided Cuthbert toward the regency — the regency's hidden hand in the wildcard eorldom.
- **Aldermere, turned.** A crown-thane house secretly feeds Ranulf intelligence from the Royal Hall and cultivates House Hartwell as a fellow defector.
- **The Hartwell lever.** A crown-thane house whose prestige rests on the bought lie of the Hart-Ford; whoever recovers the proof owns its vote.
- **The Fenwick split and the Berthwald succession.** House Fenwick's two estranged branches back the two candidates for the North Coast eorldom — the elder for Wulfric the Younger, the younger for a cadet claimant. Over both looms **Count Halfdan**, the deep-north-backed (Skarrgaard / the Cloven Strand) marcher lord moving into the vacuum from outside, his troops already skirmishing Ranulf's raiders along the fjord-coast.
- **Beaconfell married into Merrowdene.** A Beaconfell child of the North Coast is wed into Merrowdene of Stonebay, bridging two regency-leaning blocs across eorldoms.
- **Caldfen, for sale by marriage.** House Caldfen of the North Coast, desperate to refresh a Blighted line, will trade its vote and fyrd for a credible marriage.
- **The Greldon letters.** House Greldon of Mossdrowen has agreed in writing, via the Bone-Sayers, to deliver itself to Ranulf — a traitor's confession waiting in a courier's satchel.

## How this file relates to the eorldom files

This file is the frame; the `eorldom_<name>.md` files and `independent_thanedoms.md` are the detail. A query about a specific place, court, or thane house belongs in its eorldom file; a query about the realm's shape, the title system, the Witan, or a cross-eorldom tie belongs here. Once every eorldom file is built, the master house-by-house Coronation-alignment table is assembled here, drawing each eorldom's local alignment into one realm board; until then, each eorldom file carries its own.
