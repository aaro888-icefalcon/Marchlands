# Eorldom: the Fells

The eorldom of the Fells — the western upland, held by Eorl Ketil, the realm's high clan-country. This file covers the Fells as a place: the hill-fort seat of Carngarth, Ketil's clan-court, the three clan-houses, the upland lands, and the local hooks. The realm frame and the marriage/feud web are in `realm_titles.md`.

## The Fells eorldom — essence

The Fells are the western upland — a high, hard country of crag and fell and hill-pasture above the Greatwood verge, the most Norse and least governed corner of Hethrun. Where the lowland eorldoms are feudal, the Fells are a clan-confederacy: the "thanes" of the Fells are clan-chiefs, the eorl is first among chiefs rather than a lord over vassals, and the whole upland keeps the old Norse ways and the old Norse independence. Three clan-houses hold its thanelands. The Fells distrust Eldermouth, distrust the foreign-born regent, and distrust the lowland's quarrels — and the realm should be wary in turn, because the Fells breed hard fighters, and a clan-country that decides to march is a weight in any war.

## Carngarth — the seat

Carngarth is a hill-fort burh of roughly 2,000 on a high crag at the centre of the Fells — Eorl Ketil's seat, the clan-gathering place, a hard town of dry-stone walls and turf-roofed halls where the upland's three roads meet. It is the only burh in the Fells; everything else is steading and clan-holt. Two pressures define it.

**Feuding Clans.** Carngarth is where the Fell clans meet, and where the Fell clans meet, the Fell feuds meet too. The burh is neutral ground by ancient custom — weapons peace-bound at the gate, blood-debt suspended within the walls — but the feuds press against that custom constantly, and Carngarth's life is a permanent negotiation between clans who owe each other dead kin. The eorl's chief labour is holding that negotiation together.

**Tradition-Bound.** The Fells keep the old Norse ways harder than anywhere in Hethrun. The lowland's Saxon-Norse blend, its Codex church, its softening customs have barely climbed the passes. Carngarth keeps the law-speaker and the moot, the old gods alongside the Veiled Mother, the hard inheritance-customs, the feud-law and the weregild-reckoning. The Fellfolk regard the lowland as half-foreign and themselves as the truer Hethrun — older, harder, and free.

## Carngarth — quarters and notable places

Ketil's Hall is the largest hall in the Fells, a long dry-stone-and-turf hall on the crag's height, hung with the feud-trophies and oath-rings of generations of clan-reckonings. The Moot-Yard below it is where the Fell-moot gathers — the open assembly of clan-chiefs and free Fellfolk where the upland's law is spoken and its feuds are judged. The Weapon-Gate is the burh's single gate, where every comer's blade is peace-bound by the gate-warden before entry. The Drovers' Stair is the steep road down toward the lowland, where the Fell herds go down to the Wealdvale's market each year. The Veiled Mother's Howe outside the walls is the Fells' chief Old Faith site — older than Carngarth, a turf shrine over a cairn the clans will not name.

## Carngarth — significant locals

Full NPC detail is in `npcs.md`. Resident in the eorldom: Eorl Ketil, the high chief — old, hard, and shrewd, a man who has held a feud-riven upland together for thirty years by being the cleverest reckoner of blood-debt in the Fells, and who trusts the foreign-born regent in Eldermouth not at all. Seeded local figures: Lawspeaker Hrut, the keeper of the Fell law, an old man who can recite three centuries of weregild-reckonings from memory and whose word in the moot Ketil himself does not lightly cross; Asta the Pass-Warden, who keeps the Carn Pass and knows every soul that goes up or down it; and Old Sigrún, the Veiled Mother's priestess of the Howe, who tends the cairn the clans will not name and knows why they will not.

## Eorl Ketil's court

Ketil's Hall runs on two faults.

**Divided Rule.** Ketil is eorl, but the Fells are a confederacy, and his writ runs only as far as the three clan-chiefs allow it. He cannot command the Fells; he can only persuade, reckon, and broker. Major decisions — and certainly the decision of whether the Fells march in the Coronation crisis — belong to the Fell-moot, not to the eorl alone. Ketil rules by being indispensable to the peace between the clans, and that is a different and more fragile thing than a lowland eorl's authority.

**Foreign Ways.** From the lowland's side, the Fell court keeps foreign ways — the old hard Norse customs the rest of Hethrun has half-abandoned, the feud-law and the moot and the old gods, a court that looks to Eldermouth like something a century out of date. From the Fells' own side, it is the lowland that has gone soft and strange, and the foreign-born regency is the proof. This mutual estrangement is why the Fells lean, warily, toward Ranulf — not from love of the pretender, but because a Norse-Hethrun strongman is comprehensible where a foreign regent is not. The household: Ketil; his eorlswife, a chief's daughter of one of the clans, which is itself a strand of the upland's balance; and his heir, a son raised hard in the old way and impatient with his father's careful brokering.

## House Skarrfell — Fells clan-house

**Seat & banner-mark:** Skarrfell, a clan-holt on the rocky northern fells; a grey wolf.
**Head:** Chief Hrothgar of Skarrfell — a young, fierce, ambitious clan-chief of thirty, the loudest and hungriest voice in the Fell-moot.
**Character — Hungry for Glory.** Skarrfell is the rising clan, and Hrothgar means it to rise further. He sees the Coronation crisis as the Fells' chance — a war in the south is plunder, renown, and weregild-wealth, and a Fell host that marches for the winning claimant earns a debt the new king will have to pay. Hrothgar wants the Fells in the war, on Ranulf's side, and wants Skarrfell at the head of the host.
**Wants:** the Fells to march south for Ranulf; plunder and renown; Skarrfell raised above the other clans by the marching.
**Secret:** Hrothgar's hunger has made him reckless — he has already taken Ranulf's coin and Ranulf's promises through a Stonebay intermediary, ahead of any Fell-moot decision, which is close to a breach of the confederacy's own law.
**Coronation lean:** hard for Ranulf — and pushing the whole eorldom that way.
**Ties:** locked in the bitterest live feud in the Fells, against Chief Thorgest of House Garrholt — a feud Skarrfell is winning.
**Hook:** Hrothgar will recruit any sword for a raid south and push the Fell-moot toward war; a party can be hired into his ambition, or can be the ones to expose that he took Ranulf's coin before the moot ever spoke.

## House Vestfell — Fells clan-house

**Seat & banner-mark:** Vestfell, an old clan-holt on the western fells above the sea-cliffs; a grey eagle.
**Head:** Chief Ingrith of Vestfell — an old, shrewd, deliberate woman of sixty-five, the most respected chief in the Fells after Ketil himself.
**Character — Wants No Part.** Vestfell is the eldest and most cautious clan, and Ingrith's politics are isolationist to the bone. She argues in the Fell-moot that the Coronation is a lowland quarrel that can only cost the Fells dead sons and burned steadings, that the upland's business is to guard its own passes and herd its own beasts, and that a clan-country that marches into a king's war marches home smaller. Her voice is the great counterweight to Hrothgar's.
**Wants:** the Fells kept out of the lowland's war entirely; Fell autonomy preserved; the passes watched and the moot's peace kept.
**Secret:** little is hidden — Ingrith's caution is open and consistent. Her vulnerability is age: when she dies, the Fells lose the one voice that reliably checks Skarrfell, and her own heir is less sure-footed.
**Coronation lean:** isolationist — really uncommitted; what Ingrith wants is not a side but to be let alone, and a faction that credibly guarantees Fell autonomy wins her toward keeping the Fells home.
**Ties:** the senior voice of the moot's caution; an old ally of Ketil's brokering.
**Hook:** winning Ingrith means winning the Fells toward neutrality — which quietly serves the regency, by denying Ranulf the Fell spears Hrothgar would bring him. Her age makes that a clock.

## House Garrholt — Fells clan-house

**Seat & banner-mark:** Garrholt, a clan-holt on the southern fells toward the Greatwood verge; a red stag.
**Head:** Chief Thorgest of Garrholt — a proud, hard-pressed, dangerous man of fifty, a chief watching his clan bleed and growing desperate enough to do anything about it.
**Character — Cornered Clan.** Garrholt is losing. A generations-old blood-feud with House Skarrfell has turned against it — Skarrfell's rising strength has cost Garrholt steadings, herds, and fighting men year on year, and the weregild-reckoning runs ever further against Thorgest. The clan is being slowly destroyed by the feud, and Thorgest knows it, and the knowledge has made him a desperate man.
**Wants:** Garrholt saved; the feud against Skarrfell turned back, by any means; the clan's land and honour preserved.
**Secret:** Thorgest's desperation. A cornered clan that will take any bargain to survive can be bought by anyone willing to arm it — and Thorgest has begun to listen to offers he would once have refused, from the regency's brokers and from worse.
**Coronation lean:** for sale — Garrholt will follow whoever arms it against Skarrfell, which means whichever faction Skarrfell does *not* back; since Skarrfell leans Ranulf, Garrholt drifts, bitterly, toward the regency.
**Ties:** the Skarrfell–Garrholt feud, the live wire of the Fells (see `realm_titles.md`); Ketil's hardest brokering labour.
**Hook:** a faction that backs Garrholt in its feud buys the clan; a party can be hired into the feud on either side, mediate it before it destroys Garrholt and unbalances the Fells, or exploit Thorgest's desperation.

## Minor settlements of the eorldom

Seeded for texture; each is a name, community tags, and a hook.

**Stenholt** — clan-steading, *Feuding Clans · Tradition-Bound.* A Skarrfell steading on the feud-line with Garrholt land — a hard, watchful place of armed herders where the blood-feud is a daily fact and a stranger is read very carefully before being fed.

**Highfold** — village, *Honest Folk · Outpost.* The largest steading-cluster of the upland after Carngarth, a herders' and weavers' village under Vestfell's protection; remote, decent, self-reliant, and quietly proud of having no part in anyone's quarrels.

**Garrcleft** — clan-steading, *Oppressed · Doomed.* A Garrholt steading that has changed hands twice in the feud — burned once, half-abandoned, its remaining families clinging on under a clan that may not be able to protect them much longer.

## Minor courts and lesser halls

**The Fell-moot** — court tag *Assembly Rule.* The open assembly of clan-chiefs and free Fellfolk is the true governing court of the Fells — it judges feuds, speaks the law, and will decide whether the upland marches; canvassing the Fells means swaying the moot, not only the eorl.

**Lawspeaker Hrut's hall** — court tag *Old Authority.* The keeper of the Fell law holds a power no chief commands — Hrut's recitation of precedent settles feuds and binds reckonings, and a ruling from the Lawspeaker carries weight in the moot that even Ketil must reckon with.

## The eorldom's lands — the western upland

The Fells' lands are the high western upland of Hethrun — crag, fell, and hill-pasture climbing above the Greatwood verge, falling west toward the sea-cliffs and the North Coast and south toward the wood. It is hard country: herders' steadings scattered across the high pasture, dry-stone walls, and the three passes that are the upland's gates. Notable sites:

**The Carn Pass** — the chief pass linking the Fells to the lowland, the road by which the Fell herds go down and any lowland force would have to come up. Kept by the pass-warden; held, the Fells are very nearly unassailable. *Strategic site; the upland's gateway.*

**The Fellmoot Stones** — a ring of standing stones on a high moor near Carngarth, older than the Norse settlement and older, the Lawspeaker holds, than Aureoth. The great Fell-moots of feud and war are held within the ring; the clans reckon an oath sworn at the Stones unbreakable. The markings on the stones are no Norse work, and no one in the Fells can read them. *Ancient site; oath-ground and clue-site.*

**The High Tarns** — a chain of cold, deep upland lakes in the northern fells. The herders water their beasts at most of them and avoid one — the black tarn the Veiled Mother's people call the Drowned Eye — where the old stories say the first Fell-settlers gave something to the water to be allowed to stay. *Investigation / horror site.*

**The verge-edge** — the Fells' southern slopes fall into the upper Greatwood verge, where the upland and the elf-touched wood meet; Fell herders lose beasts there and do not follow them in. (Bestiary: the Verge-Beasts, `bestiary.md` Group 6A, range onto the southern fells.)

## Adventure hooks

- **Will the Fells march** (tier 1–2, the eorldom's spine) — the Fell-moot will decide whether the upland enters the Coronation war and on whose side; Skarrfell pushes for Ranulf, Vestfell for staying home, and the moot's verdict is genuinely in play.
- **The Skarrfell–Garrholt feud** (tier 1–2) — the upland's live wire; a party can be hired into it, mediate it before it destroys Garrholt and unbalances the Fells, or exploit it.
- **Hrothgar's coin** (tier 1) — Chief Hrothgar took Ranulf's coin and promises before the Fell-moot ever spoke; exposing that breach of the confederacy's law could collapse the pro-Ranulf push entirely.
- **Garrholt's bargain** (tier 1–2) — cornered Chief Thorgest is listening to dangerous offers to save his clan; a party can reach him before he takes the worst of them.
- **The Drowned Eye** (tier 1–2) — the black tarn the herders shun, and the old story of what the first Fell-settlers gave the water; an investigation a party may open by chance or by Old Sigrún's quiet asking.

## At a glance

The Fells are the autonomous eorldom — a Norse clan-confederacy that the Hethrun crown governs in name and barely in fact, where the eorl brokers rather than commands and the Fell-moot, not the Witan, decides whether the upland marches. Eorl Ketil leans soft for Ranulf, but only out of distrust of the foreign-born regent: the Fells are courtable by any faction that credibly respects Fell autonomy and lets the upland alone. Its three clan-houses are the contest in miniature — Skarrfell hungry for Ranulf's war, Vestfell wanting no part of it, Garrholt cornered and for sale — and the Skarrfell–Garrholt feud is the live wire under all of it. Crisis stance: soft Ranulf, genuinely winnable, and decided by the moot. Bestiary groups operating here: the Verge-Beasts (6A) on the southern slopes and the Arrival-Hunters (A1).
