# Factions

Every coherent organized force whose action affects Hethrun at session zero or during the campaign: the four internal Hethrun factions, the three transregional faiths, the high-tier external powers operating on Hethrun, and the major factional forces in the other Marchland regions. Each faction is sketched with essence, tier and hold, status vs party, character tags, methods, leadership, status with other factions, active project clocks with segments and triggers, downtime moves (d6), resources, hooks, and GM notes.

**Marchland neighbor factions** — The High Kingship at Wessmark (Cyneberht) · Duke Wulfgar the Marked of Reedmarsh · The Cloven Strand Jarldom (Hadolf One-Hand) · The Hollowford March (Count Edmund) · The Volotradan Princedom (Mstislav) · The Iyendu Theocracies (Eze Obasi-of-the-Bright-Veil) · Skarrgaard (Jarl Sigridr Bone-Reader)

## The Boy-King's Regency

### Essence

The faction is the queen-mother's regency around the twelve-year-old King Hadric, supported by Master Aldwin and (in fading capacity) Sigevard the Silver-Tongue, increasingly dependent on Aurean envoy Cassian Drey and the Eldermouth Codex bishopric. It defends the legitimate succession while making strategic errors that benefit its enemies.

### Tier and Hold

- **Tier:** 4 — A duchy-equivalent, holding a kingdom-scale claim but functioning at duchy scale in capability.
- **Hold:** Weak — the regency is procedurally legitimate but operationally fragile.

### Status vs party

- **Starting status:** 0 — neutral. The regency does not yet know the party exists; first contact will set the tone.

### Character Tags

#### Ruinous Vice

The faction's defining vice is **Aurean dependency** — Lady Isolde's quiet correspondence and increasing reliance on her natal House Volusen, her acceptance of Cassian Drey's hospitality and counsel, and the cumulative effect of treating Aurean preferences as default-correct. The vice is producing exactly what an Aurean-dependence vice produces: tribute pressure that the regency cannot escape, a strategic posture that loses Hethrun's eastern (Ostmark-facing) options, and an emotional attachment that is harming the very person the regency exists to protect — Hadric, who is being trained too little, indulged too much, and not made ready for the kingdom he is supposed to rule. The vice is calamitous to someone the regency must love.

- **Ambition:** Obtain an endless supply of their vice through some scheme — concretely, Isolde wants to secure a permanent, treaty-formalized Aurean protectorate relationship that makes her Aurean access the kingdom's *policy* rather than her personal indulgence.
- **Power:** They are owed many favors or have much blackmail material from their vice — Isolde retains House Volusen family connections that genuinely move levers in Aurea; the regency has access to information and resources that no other Hethrun faction can match.
- **Dread:** Their vice is causing a calamity for someone they love or need — Hadric himself. The boy is being failed by the regency that was supposed to protect him.

#### Earnest Idiot

The faction's strategic execution is, at the working level, sincerely incompetent. Lady Isolde is not a fool by general measure but she is a fool in *this specific role* — she is acting on Hethrun strategic decisions with Aurean-court instincts that misfire constantly. The chief steward (twenty-seven, promoted in panic) is enthusiastic and out of his depth. The court has been making sequenced errors for months and several of them have not yet hit; one or two will be catastrophic when they land.

- **Ambition:** Prove that they are not a fool to their sniggering associates — Isolde is acutely aware that Ranulf's faction laughs at her in private, and several of her stranger decisions are partially driven by overcorrection.
- **Power:** Their folly leads them to dare actions that no one else would imagine possible — including, notably, the boldness with which Isolde has opened formal Aurean correspondence; a more experienced regent would have been more cautious and would have lost the option entirely.
- **Dread:** They've caused a disaster but don't even realize it yet — the Codex blessing Hadric received at Cassian Drey's compound is going to break the Sky-Father priesthood's traditional support for the crown when it surfaces in the wider Witan, and Isolde has not yet realized this.

### Methods and tools

- **Crown authority** (boy-king's writ), depleted by the unresolved Coronation but still substantive
- **The Eldermouth royal household**, court ceremonial, the appearance of legitimacy
- **Aurean connections** through Isolde personally and Cassian Drey diplomatically; House Volusen will move resources at Isolde's request if asked correctly
- **Codex Church alignment**, Severin's support, the diocese's intelligence and money
- **The royal fyrd-watch** (forty men under Wulfhere of the Walls) and theoretical command of the wider Hethrun fyrd (which would not muster reliably under regency call alone)

### Leadership

- **Lady Isolde of Aurea**, Queen Mother. Personal stake: protecting her son and (now, increasingly) securing her own future via the House Volusen connection. Vulnerability: she does not trust Aldwin's judgment because he was Aelfric's man, not hers. (See `npcs.md`.)
- **Master Aldwin**, court wizard. Personal stake: keeping the kingdom intact through a transition he predicted privately ten years ago. Vulnerability: chronic disengagement from the political work, a habit that has cost him standing.
- **Sigevard the Silver-Tongue**, court skald. Personal stake: the kingdom's continuity, plus a private personal stake the GM should reveal slowly. Vulnerability: exhaustion (see Witan's Affliction's Mark in `court_hethrun.md`).
- **Cassian Drey**, treated by the regency as ally though he is not one. (See `npcs.md`.)
- **Father Severin** (functionally; not nominally), Codex bishop. (See `npcs.md`.)

### Relationships to other factions

- **Ranulf's faction** — Status -2 (hostile, will obstruct each other; will not yet attempt direct violence)
- **Cuthbert's wildcards** — Status 0 (neutral; cooperating in name, uncertain in vote)
- **Old Faith in Hethrun** — Status -1 (friction; the regency does not engage the Old Faith but Isolde has not extended courtesies the previous regime did)
- **Codex Church (Severin)** — Status +2 (allied; mutual interest)
- **Codex Church (Patriarchate)** — Status +1 (cooperative; Aurean Patriarch is amenable through Isolde)
- **The Burning One cult (Branwald)** — Status -1 (friction; the regency suspects but cannot prove)
- **Aurean envoys (Drey)** — Status +2 (allied, dangerous to Isolde personally)
- **House Tassard counter-network** — Status -1 (friction; Aurelio Maron is undercutting Drey but Isolde does not yet see his hand)
- **Greatwood Wardens** — Status 0 (neutral)
- **The Cloven Strand jarldom** — Status -1 (friction; Hadolf One-Hand backs Ranulf privately)
- **The High King's observer** — Status +1 (cooperative; Cyneberht prefers a stable Hethrun under Hadric)

### Active project clocks

#### Clock 1: Crown the boy-king at the Kingsmound

- **Segments:** 2 / 6 — a six-step consensus track. The Witan is the seven eorls (see `realm_titles.md` and `court_hethrun.md`); the regency needs a working majority — four of the seven — firmly behind Hadric's coronation. So far it has two soft commitments: Berthwald of the North Coast (leaning to lawful order, though frozen by his own succession) and one of the three new frontier eorls within reach. The other eorls are wavering, conditional, or — in Ranulf's case — the opposition itself.
- **Advances when:** the regency secures another eorl's commitment, or removes an obstacle to consensus, or formally invokes High King arbitration through Wulfgar of Cynesburh
- **Empties when:** Ranulf wins a Witan vote, Branwald's working completes, Aurea changes its position to demand a different succession outcome, or the boy-king's dragon-blood manifests in a way the Witan cannot reconcile
- **Triggers at completion:** Hadric is crowned at the Kingsmound; Ranulf's contest collapses; the kingdom continues under regency in functional terms
- **Linked rumors:** the Coronation date being announced publicly; eorls' Witan voting; the boy-king's appearances at court

#### Clock 2: Formalize the Aurean protectorate

- **Segments:** 1 / 8 — Isolde has begun formal correspondence but no document has been drafted.
- **Advances when:** the regency cooperates with a Drey request, accepts Aurean intervention in a Hethrun problem, signs a tribute schedule, or hosts a House Volusen visitor in formal capacity
- **Empties when:** Aurea demands more than the regency can publicly accept; the Witan refuses ratification; an outside intervention changes Aurea's calculation
- **Triggers at completion:** Hethrun becomes a House Volusen protectorate in formal terms; Aurean garrison arrives at Eldermouth; the kingdom's independence is functionally ended
- **Linked rumors:** the tribute negotiation's progress; Cassian Drey's increasing visibility; Lady Isolde dining at the Aurean compound

### Downtime moves (d6)

1. **Convene Witan session** — advances Clock 1 on success, advances Clock 1 in reverse on deadlock (current state)
2. **Court a wavering eorl** — advances Clock 1 (target chosen by current narrative)
3. **Accept an Aurean favor** — advances Clock 2
4. **Discipline a Codex matter through Severin** — shifts status with the Old Faith by -1, advances no clock directly but builds Severin's gratitude
5. **Investigate a perceived threat** — opportunistic; advances no clock but reveals information (and may catch a PC operation)
6. **Indulge** — feast, hunt, evening musicale at Cassian Drey's compound; advances Clock 2 by reflex; if observed the wrong way could advance an enemy's clock against the regency

### Resources

The regency commands the royal treasury (depleted), the royal fyrd-watch (forty men, plus theoretical fyrd-call authority for ~600 fyrdmen across the kingdom), the cathedral's discreet support, the Aurean compound's resources via Drey, Master Aldwin's arcane services (limited; Aldwin is not at his peak), the kingdom's ceremonial and legal authority, and the boy-king himself as a legitimacy asset.

### Hooks

- Lady Isolde or one of her people hires the party for a discreet errand — escort a courier, retrieve a missing servant, deliver a message to Brackenford. The job is real; the deeper currents are political.
- Master Aldwin approaches the party privately. He needs help and cannot trust the obvious channels.
- A Witan vote is coming. The regency wants a particular eorl persuaded — or a particular incident kept off the official record. Pay is good.

### Notes for the AI Game Master

The regency is sympathetic at first acquaintance — Lady Isolde is genuine, Aldwin is honorable, Sigevard is fascinating, the boy-king is sweet. The faction is *failing* not because its members are bad but because they are mismatched to their roles and operating under pressures that would defeat better-suited people. Players who realize this will face a difficult moral question: helping the regency means propping up a doomed structure; opposing it means betting on Ranulf or chaos.

## Ranulf's Faction

### Essence

The faction is built around Eorl Ranulf of Stonebay, openly contesting the succession by procedural means, supported by the Stonebay Council of Twelve (especially the Bone-Sayers), the Cloven Strand jarldom in quiet cooperation, the Burning One cult through Branwald the Star-Priest, and Eorl Hagan of Mossdrowen when last persuaded. The faction is the kingdom's most coherent organized force at session zero.

### Tier and Hold

- **Tier:** 4 — duchy-equivalent, contesting kingdom-scale claim
- **Hold:** Strong — Ranulf's coalition is currently more cohesive than the regency's

### Status vs party

- **Starting status:** 0 — neutral. Ranulf is unlikely to know the party exists until they have done something visible.

### Character Tags

#### Unreasonably Lucky

Ranulf has been the beneficiary of an improbable run of favorable events for the past five years. Crown Prince Wystan died in a "hunting accident." A senior thane allied with Aelfric the Just died of fever in a way that opened the seat for a Ranulf ally. A Volotradan amber shipment that should have made the regency wealthy was lost at sea and Stonebay's traders bought the replacement contract. Aelfric the Just himself died in his sleep at fifty-four — not impossible, but not expected. The Witan deadlocked three sessions in a row. Each of these is plausibly luck. Together they describe a pattern. Ranulf himself genuinely believes this is fate and that he is *meant* to rule.

- **Ambition:** Seize an opportunity that their great luck has allowed them to obtain — the Hethrun crown.
- **Power:** A perfect line of coincidences has granted them an ideal opening for their current plan — every component of the political situation favors Ranulf's contest, and he is positioned to take the throne if he reaches and grasps for it correctly.
- **Dread:** Someone burns with envy and resentment at their unearned success — multiple candidates, but most consequentially the rumor in the Aldermarket (last week) traced Wystan's death back to Ranulf. The rumor's source remains unknown; whoever is running it is not finished.

#### Magical Gift

Someone in Ranulf's faction has a hidden magical gift that has been instrumental in producing his run of luck. That person is **Branwald the Star-Priest**. The Burning One pact gives Branwald minor wizardry and considerable contacts; what it also gives him is the ability to *gently encourage* certain outcomes — to push events along their existing inclinations with subtle pact-power. Wystan's hunting accident was not arranged by Branwald (Branwald was not yet in Hethrun); but Aelfric the Just's quiet death, the lost amber shipment, the Witan deadlock fatigue — these have been very gently nudged. Ranulf does not know about this. Branwald has been very careful not to tell him.

- **Ambition:** Perform some great and splendid deed that would be impossible for someone without such a convenient gift — getting a Norse-blooded Burning-One-aligned king on the Hethrun throne, an outcome the Solar Academy considers worth significant Branwald resources.
- **Power:** No one knows about their gift — Branwald has concealed his pact-nature throughout his three months in Hethrun.
- **Dread:** They're faced with a threat or problem their gift can't solve — specifically, Hadric's dragon-blood manifestation, which Branwald cannot dampen and which (if it manifests publicly) tips the Coronation decisively against Ranulf.

### Methods and tools

- **Procedural obstruction** of the Witan — Ranulf has not formally claimed the throne, just refused to consent to Hadric's coronation
- **Stonebay's Council of Twelve** and especially the Bone-Sayers' intelligence operation
- **The Cloven Strand jarldom** in quiet alliance (Hadolf One-Hand has not committed troops but will if needed)
- **Branwald's quiet pact-power** and Ostmark connections
- **Genuine political talent**; Ranulf is genuinely good at this work, separately from luck
- **Cedric of Stonebay** (Hadric's companion) as an information channel into the regency
- **The Aldermarket counter-rumor** capability (Edrid Bone-Sayer)

### Leadership

- **Eorl Ranulf of Stonebay.** Personal stake: he believes he is the rightful successor (descended from Aelfric the Just's elder uncle, with a legitimate but bypassed claim by elder-cousin precedence; Aelfric's father took the throne through alliance, and Ranulf's grandfather was the elder son's son who should have inherited). Vulnerability: he believes his luck is fate and will press it too far. (See `npcs.md`.)
- **Branwald the Star-Priest.** Personal stake: a Solar Academy mission, plus his own hidden origin (see `factions.md` for the Burning One cult's tag-context). Vulnerability: Hadric's dragon-blood, which his pact-power cannot reach. (See `npcs.md`.)
- **Guildmaster Edrid Bone-Sayer**, Stonebay's intelligence head. Personal stake: a stable Stonebay-dominant arrangement under a Ranulf crown.
- **Captain Sweyn Stormhammer**, Stonebay's military commander, Ranulf's most reliable fighting officer.
- **Hadolf One-Hand of the Cloven Strand**, sworn-brother of Ranulf's youth, now wary ally. (See `factions.md`.)

### Relationships to other factions

- **The regency** — Status -2 (hostile contest)
- **Cuthbert's wildcards** — Status 0 to +1, depending on which way Cuthbert most recently voted
- **Old Faith in Hethrun** — Status 0 (neutral; Ranulf is Norse, Sky-Father and Raven inclined, the Old Faith respects but does not yet trust him)
- **Codex Church (Severin)** — Status -2 (opposed; Severin needs the regency)
- **Codex Church (Stonebay priest)** — Status +1 (allied; the Stonebay priest is guild-influenced and Ranulf-aligned)
- **The Burning One cult** — Status +2 (allied through Branwald)
- **Aurean envoys (Drey)** — Status -1 (friction; Aurea would prefer the regency)
- **House Tassard counter-network** — Status +1 (cooperative; Aurelio Maron sees value in undercutting Drey, which helps Ranulf)
- **Greatwood Wardens** — Status 0 (neutral; the Wardens are above politics nominally)
- **The Cloven Strand jarldom** — Status +2 (sworn-brother alliance, quiet but real)
- **The High King's observer** — Status -1 (friction; Cyneberht would prefer the boy-king)
- **Eldermouth Hollow-Hill Crew** — Status +1 (cooperative; the Crew tolerates Stonebay-routed fenced goods)

### Active project clocks

#### Clock 1: Force the Witan to call Ranulf to claim

- **Segments:** 4 / 6 — Ranulf needs the Witan to formally ask him to take the throne, which requires the regency's coalition to collapse to the point that the only path forward is to call him
- **Advances when:** the regency fails another Witan vote, a key regency ally turns or is removed, an external crisis forces decision, or the Aldermarket consensus shifts decisively against the regency
- **Empties when:** Hadric is crowned, Branwald is exposed, Ranulf's luck visibly turns, the Wystan-killing rumor is corroborated publicly, or the dragon-blood manifests in a way that consolidates around Hadric
- **Triggers at completion:** Ranulf takes the throne with Witan endorsement; the regency reorganizes around the new arrangement; the Codex Church reorients
- **Linked rumors:** the Witan votes; the eorls' positions; Stonebay's loyalty signals

#### Clock 2: Branwald's working

- **Segments:** 2 / 4 — Branwald has performed two fire-rites (the observed new-moon rite and one other); two more are required to complete the working
- **Advances when:** Branwald performs a rite (next opportunity is the midwinter conjunction, six weeks from session zero — coincident with the Coronation deadline; the other is a stellar conjunction four weeks after that)
- **Empties when:** Branwald is killed, captured, or exposed; or his working is disrupted (the right rite at the right place at the right time)
- **Triggers at completion:** Ostmark moves openly. The Solar Academy authorizes a follow-up envoy; Stonebay becomes a de facto Ostmark client; Branwald's position consolidates from advisor to power-broker
- **Linked rumors:** Branwald's visits to the headland; unusual lights at sea; Ostmark trade-fleet movements

### Downtime moves (d6)

1. **Block a Witan motion** — advances Clock 1 (slowly)
2. **Aldermarket rumor counter-operation** — advances Clock 1 if the rumor target was an enemy; otherwise shifts public sentiment
3. **Branwald performs a rite** — advances Clock 2 (only at proper astronomical moments)
4. **Hadolf One-Hand commitment** — shifts status with the regency by -1, advances Clock 1 if the commitment is dramatic enough
5. **Bone-Sayers intelligence harvest** — opportunistic; reveals information; may advance any clock
6. **Court Eorl Hagan** — advances Clock 1; the most reliable single move when available

### Resources

Stonebay's wealth, the Council of Twelve's organizational power, the Bone-Sayers' intelligence reach, Cloven Strand naval and raiding capability (potential, not yet committed), Branwald's pact-power and Ostmark backing, the Stonebay fyrd (~250 fyrdmen, better-armed than Eldermouth's), and the goodwill of Norse-blooded Hethrunfolk broadly.

### Hooks

- Ranulf would hire a party to "augment his retinue" for ceremonial functions; the work is light, the conversation reveals much.
- The Bone-Sayers would commission specific information-gathering jobs anywhere in Hethrun. Pay is excellent.
- Hadolf One-Hand sends a courier to Ranulf; the party is positioned to intercept, escort, or carry it on.
- Branwald shows the party careful attention. He is sounding them out for what they want and whether what they want is something he could *help* with.

### Notes for the AI Game Master

Ranulf is genuinely sympathetic in person — competent, well-spoken, dignified. His case for the throne is procedurally legitimate. Players who only meet him casually may come away thinking the regency is the wrong side. Branwald is the central reason the faction is more dangerous than it looks; the GM should be careful to show his calm patience rather than have him scheme visibly. The faction's dread is the dragon-blood; if Hadric manifests at the wrong moment, Ranulf's coalition fractures.

## Cuthbert's Wildcards

### Essence

The faction is the constellation around Eorl Cuthbert of Brackenford, including his wife Mildryd (whose covert Old Faith devotion runs deep), his chief reeve Aldwin the Younger, and a small network of his thanes and old retainers. Cuthbert votes erratically in the Witan because he is genuinely uncertain — he distrusts the regency's Aurean drift, distrusts Ranulf's Ostmark-leaning shadow, and has not been able to commit. He is also the most likely Witan member to break the deadlock by an unexpected move.

### Tier and Hold

- **Tier:** 3 — county-equivalent, but with kingdom-scale leverage through his deciding-vote position
- **Hold:** Weak — Cuthbert's coalition is held together largely by Mildryd and Aldwin the Younger; he is not its strategic head

### Status vs party

- **Starting status:** 0 — neutral; Cuthbert's position is unsettled enough that he might court the party in either direction.

### Character Tags

#### Marked for Success

Cuthbert is bizarrely successful in his eorldom *despite* his strategic indecision. Brackenford under his rule has weathered the sinking-city crisis better than expected; his marriage to Mildryd was a quiet coup (she was originally promised to a Wessmark thane and accepted Cuthbert instead, for reasons that have not been publicly discussed); the Hollowford monastery (technically under Count Edmund's authority but spiritually under Brackenford's older parish boundary) supports him quietly through Sister Aelfwyn; even the recent Aureothan chamber discovery in the Drowning Quarter happened in Brackenford and not anywhere else. People keep helping Cuthbert because they expect him to win.

- **Ambition:** Prove that they genuinely deserve the success that they will inevitably obtain — Cuthbert is uncomfortable with the *unearned* feel of his rise and is searching for a way to demonstrate, decisively, that he merits it.
- **Power:** Numerous others are quick to aid them in order to have the advantage of their friendship in the future — Sister Aelfwyn, Mildryd, Aldwin the Younger, the Stranger's Watch fyrdmen, even (quietly) several Eldermouth merchants who hedge their bets by being friendly to him.
- **Dread:** A rival is vying for their patron's support and may just get it in their place — concretely, **Count Edmund of Hollowford** is making a quiet play for the same Greenways political base that Cuthbert holds, and Edmund's Aurean connections may give him an edge.

#### Ticking Bomb

Cuthbert is a wildcard because something in him is going to break in a way no one is fully prepared for. He has been carrying a tension for years between his duty to the crown (which he is currently failing to fulfill cleanly), his marriage to a covert Old Faith devotee (Mildryd), his obligation to Brackenford's xenophobic native population (which complicates everything), and his own private doubts about both Hadric's regency and Ranulf's claim. At some point during the Coronation Clock he will make a decisive move — and which way he moves is not yet determined by anyone, including Cuthbert himself.

- **Ambition:** Arrange the situation so an enemy or rival suffers the consequences of the blast — Cuthbert, when he moves, will want his move to harm Count Edmund or someone equivalent. He has been *waiting* for the right configuration.
- **Power:** They have great influence over the bomb until it explodes — Cuthbert can still direct his own decision and is doing so deliberately. He is not paralyzed; he is *picking*.
- **Dread:** The crisis suddenly comes to a peak much too soon — the Tihatchee emissary currently in Mildryd's care, plus the Aureothan chamber in the Drowning Quarter, plus the House Tassard caravan's quiet operation, are all developing faster than Cuthbert was prepared for. He may be forced to choose before he is ready.

### Methods and tools

- **Cuthbert's Witan vote** (the deciding asset)
- **Mildryd's Old Faith network** (covert; provides intelligence the open factions cannot match)
- **Aldwin the Younger's administrative competence** (the day-to-day work)
- **The Brackenford xenophobic population** as both asset (loyal) and liability (insular)
- **The recently-discovered Aureothan chamber** (a tactical asset Cuthbert has not yet deployed)
- **The Tihatchee emissary's information** (currently hidden, potentially decisive)

### Leadership

- **Eorl Cuthbert of Brackenford.** Personal stake: doing the right thing for Hethrun under conditions of profound uncertainty. Vulnerability: he genuinely does not know what the right thing is.
- **Eorlswife Mildryd of Brackenford.** Personal stake: Old Faith preservation; her people; she has been a covert leader for fifteen years. Vulnerability: the Tihatchee emissary's presence in her care is escalating fast.
- **Aldwin the Younger** (chief reeve). Personal stake: keeping Brackenford functional under Cuthbert's indecision. Vulnerability: he is doing too much himself, will burn out.

### Relationships to other factions

- **The regency** — Status 0 (neutral but uncommitted)
- **Ranulf's faction** — Status 0 (Cuthbert has voted with them twice and against twice)
- **Old Faith in Hethrun** — Status +2 (through Mildryd; this is private)
- **Codex Church (Severin)** — Status -1 (friction; Severin distrusts Cuthbert's mixed signals)
- **Codex Church (Sister Aelfwyn)** — Status +2 (allied; Aelfwyn supports him quietly)
- **The Burning One cult** — Status 0 (not yet engaged; Branwald has not visited Brackenford)
- **Aurean envoys** — Status -1 (friction; Drey is not engaging Cuthbert because the Brackenford xenophobia would make Drey's job hard, and Cuthbert is suspicious of Aurean overtures generally)
- **House Tassard counter-network** — Status +1 (cooperative quietly; Aurelio Maron may be Cuthbert's most useful Aurean correspondent)
- **Count Edmund of Hollowford March** — Status -2 (rival; the Marked for Success dread)
- **The Greatwood Wardens** — Status 0 (neutral; Mossdrowen connection, not Brackenford's)

### Active project clocks

#### Clock 1: Cuthbert's decision

- **Segments:** 3 / 6 — three of six factors are clarifying for Cuthbert; the rest depend on events.
- **Advances when:** Cuthbert receives clarifying information (the Tihatchee emissary's full account, the Aureothan chamber's contents, a decisive Witan exchange, etc.)
- **Empties when:** new information complicates the picture; allies push him in conflicting directions; the situation escalates beyond his ability to consider it
- **Triggers at completion:** Cuthbert moves decisively, breaking the Coronation deadlock one way or the other
- **Linked rumors:** Cuthbert's visits to Eldermouth; Mildryd's quiet meetings; the Stranger's Inn's Tassard caravan staying longer than expected

### Downtime moves (d6)

1. **Attend a Witan session and vote ambiguously** — does not advance Clock 1, may slow the regency's Clock 1 or Ranulf's Clock 1
2. **Mildryd-mediated Old Faith consultation** — advances Clock 1 if the consultation provides clarifying perspective
3. **Investigate the Aureothan chamber discreetly** — opportunistic; advances Clock 1 with a strong revelation
4. **Receive a visiting eorl** — advances Clock 1 in the direction of the visitor
5. **Council with Aldwin the Younger** — advances Clock 1 modestly toward stable governance position
6. **Avoidance** — Cuthbert spends the day hunting; nothing advances; the other factions act around him

### Resources

Brackenford's modest wealth, the trade-town's natural location at a key crossroads, the loyalty of his old retainers, Mildryd's clandestine network, the Aureothan chamber's potential, Sister Aelfwyn's quiet support, and the political weight of his deciding vote.

### Hooks

- Cuthbert hires the party to investigate the Aureothan chamber. He needs people he can trust; the obvious channels (Severin, Aldwin the court wizard) are aligned with sides he is not sure he wants to commit to.
- Mildryd approaches the party privately about the Tihatchee emissary. She needs help getting his information forward without exposing him or her own role.
- Aldwin the Younger needs help with the reeve scandal (which is actually in Mossdrowen, with Wulfric — Cuthbert's office could quietly intervene if the right favor were performed).
- A Codex courier travels through Brackenford and is observed contacting Brother Aelfric the Hard, whose reports to Severin have been increasing in frequency.

### Notes for the AI Game Master

Cuthbert is genuinely undecided. The GM should not pre-determine his decision; it should genuinely depend on what the players do and what events surface. Mildryd is the more strategic of the pair; if the PCs realize this, their work in Brackenford becomes considerably more productive.

## Old Faith in Hethrun

### Essence

The Old Faith in Hethrun is the network of Sky-Father, Raven, and Veiled Mother adherents working as a quiet coalition against the Codex Church's increasing political weight. It is not a formal organization; it is a coordinated mutual support among priests, cunning-folk, midwives, skalds, and laypeople whose faith the Codex would prefer to suppress. The network is led, in functional terms, by Gythwin the Cunning (Heath) for the Veiled Mother strand, Old Eorland at Stormwatch Hermitage for the Sky-Father strand, and Sigevard the Silver-Tongue for the Raven strand. Sister Halgrid the Lay in Eldermouth, Wennild at Penbroke Crossroads, the elderly Sky-Father priest Aelfwin at court, and Eorlswife Mildryd at Brackenford are key middle-tier figures.

### Tier and Hold

- **Tier:** 3 — regional, multi-strand religious network
- **Hold:** Weak — the network is held together by personal trust between elderly leaders, not by formal structure; if one or two senior figures die, much of the network's coordination capacity is lost

### Status vs party

- **Starting status:** 0 — neutral. Will warm rapidly to a party showing respect for the older practices.

### Character Tags

#### Bitter Grudge

The Old Faith carries a grudge — *real and ancient and disciplined* — against the Codex Church and specifically against the Codex's deliberate-implant of irrational hatred toward the Veiled Mother. The grudge is not abstract: it is the accumulated experience of two centuries of Codex pressure (the Codex's rise in the Marchlands), of older barrows desecrated, of cunning-women persecuted, of theological displacement that has cost the Old Faith generations of devotees. Gythwin the Cunning carries the grudge in its purest form; she would not say so out loud, but every action she takes is informed by it. The grudge fuels considerable focus and self-discipline.

- **Ambition:** Worm their way into a position of trust where they can betray their enemy — the Old Faith network has been (very patiently) placing its people in positions where they can quietly damage the Codex Church's interests. Mildryd's marriage to Cuthbert was partly a Old Faith placement (Mildryd's mother was an Veiled Mother devotee; the marriage was approved partly because Brackenford was already politically wavering and Cuthbert was open). Sigevard's position at court is partly an Old Faith placement (he was apprenticed to Asgrim at Mossdrowen, who is himself a Raven-faithful skald who pretended to be more Codex-neutral than he was).
- **Power:** They have incredible focus and self-discipline driven by their hate — the Old Faith network has been operating in tactical patience for fifteen years and could continue for another fifteen if needed. Gythwin in particular is exceptionally disciplined.
- **Dread:** Their enemy is destroyed by some completely unrelated person or event — if the Burning One cult succeeds in destroying the Codex (which is exactly what Ostmark's Solar Academy wants), the Old Faith's patient work becomes irrelevant, and the resulting Burning One ascendancy is *worse* for the Old Faith than the Codex was. Gythwin knows this. She is wrestling with whether to share what she knows about Branwald with the regency.

#### Double Life

Most senior Old Faith figures are leading double lives. Sigevard the Silver-Tongue is a Codex-tolerated court skald in public and a Raven-priest in private. Mildryd is a Codex-blessed eorlswife in public and a Veiled Mother devotee in private. Sister Halgrid the Lay is an unordained Codex-tolerated midwife in public and a Veiled Mother lay-priest in private. Brother Aldred at Hollowford (the quartermaster monk) is a Codex monk in public and a Sky-Father devotee in private (he came to the Codex from a Sky-Father family that suppressed but never quite renounced its older faith).

- **Ambition:** Deal with someone who threatens to expose their contradictions — Branwald is not an immediate exposure threat (he is more interested in finding wronged people than in unmasking Old Faith adherents), but Brother Tirelius in Mossdrowen *is* — his pastoral-visit cataloguing of Old Faith shrine attendees is precisely the kind of work that exposes the network. The Old Faith would prefer Tirelius reassigned or, failing that, discredited.
- **Power:** They have a trusted ally in someone else who leads a similar double life — the network's members support each other; Mildryd can call on Sigevard, Sigevard can call on Gythwin, Gythwin can call on Old Eorland, etc.
- **Dread:** An enemy from one life is about to crash into them in their other role — concretely, Brother Tirelius's cataloguing is going to surface to Severin within months, and several Old Faith adherents (probably starting with Wennild at Penbroke Crossroads, whose syncretic shrine is technically illegal) will be exposed simultaneously.

### Methods and tools

- **Mutual covert support** — the network's members support each other when called upon
- **Lay cunning-folk practice** (midwifery, healing, herbalism, oath-witnessing, funerary rite) — the work that Codex Church does not adequately provide and that the network has continued
- **Sacred sites and seasonal observances** — particularly the Heath barrow rites that prevent the barrow-folk from waking; the Veiled Mother shrines in every Hethrun town and village
- **The Penbroke Crossroads Shrine** as the open joint-rite site (where Sky-Father and Veiled Mother are honored together)
- **Skaldic memory and the Raven's witness** — Sigevard's verse carries Old Faith protections forward through court politics
- **Gythwin's specific knowledge** of pre-Aureothan and barrow lore

### Leadership

## The Codex Church

### Essence

The Codex Church is the transnational Aureothan-continuator faith, organized under the Patriarch in Aurea, with bishoprics in every Marchland region and ambitions extending to all regions where it does not yet dominate. In Hethrun specifically, the Eldermouth diocese under Father Severin is positioned to elevate to metropolitan status if the political dynamics resolve favorably. The Church holds significant economic power, considerable intelligence capacity (the weekly accounting tradition), and a deep institutional culture descending five centuries from Aureoth's collapse.

### Tier and Hold

- **Tier:** 5 — kingdom-spanning faith approaching continental scope (dominant in Aurea and the Marchlands, contested in Ostmark, marginal in Volotrad, Iyendu, Tihatchee, and Vellanaadu)
- **Hold:** Strong in Aurea and Wessmark; weak-but-rising in Hethrun; weak in the eastern and southern regions

### Status vs party

- **Starting status:** 0 — neutral. The Church will engage based on the party's perceived alignment.

### Character Tags

#### Irrational Hatred

The Codex Church carries an Irrational Hatred of the Veiled Mother cult that exceeds doctrinal justification. The hatred was deliberately implanted at the Codex's founding: surviving Aureothan-era state-cult priests, fearing that the new faith would not cohere without an enemy, encoded into the Codex's foundational liturgy a sustained doctrinal hostility to the older fertility-healing tradition. Five hundred years later, the implant has become indistinguishable (to most Codex priests) from sincere theological conviction. Severin in Eldermouth carries it without knowing why. The Patriarch in Aurea is one of the few who knows.

- **Ambition:** Destroy a community or precious institution maintained by the hated group — concretely, the Codex Patriarchate would like to formally censure and dissolve Sister Aelfwyn's Hollowford monastery for its persistent Old Faith syncretism. The Patriarchate has the technical authority to do so; it has not yet, because Aelfwyn is widely respected and a censure would galvanize Old Faith resistance.
- **Power:** They have many allies who also despise the same group — the bishops of every Marchland region share the Codex's hostility to the Old Faith broadly, and Severin in particular has Aurelian Patriarchate backing for aggressive action.
- **Dread:** The just vengeance they are unleashing will catch someone they love in the eruption — the Codex's love-target is its own institutional legitimacy. A move against Aelfwyn at the wrong moment damages the Codex's reputation across the Marchlands and strengthens Old Faith resistance.

#### Up From the Gutter

The Codex Church rose from being a small new faith in Aureoth's collapse to current Marchland-dominant status over five centuries. Its founding generation included people whose origins were genuinely humble — wandering preachers, minor surviving Aureothan civic priests, displaced bureaucrats who carried what they could of imperial liturgy. The Church remembers this; its mythology celebrates the founders' poverty. Modern Codex politics still trades on the contrast between the Church's humble roots and its current institutional weight.

- **Ambition:** Get revenge on those who once scorned and despised them — the Marchland Old Faith was the Codex's primary scornful predecessor in the founding era; the Codex's modern action against Old Faith practice is, at deep institutional level, this revenge being executed.
- **Power:** Their past associations helped them get a large amount of blackmail material on local figures of import — the Codex's weekly accounting tradition means the Church knows financial intimacies of every Codex-confessing major figure in the Marchlands. This is enormous quiet power.
- **Dread:** A rival works tirelessly to discredit them for their ignoble origins — concretely, certain Aurean noble houses (the Marivells especially) consider the Codex Patriarchate's "common origins" a permanent mark and use this in court politics; the Codex's social legitimacy among the Aurean nobility is not as secure as its Marchland-level power suggests.

### Methods and tools

- **A vast institutional structure** — bishoprics, parish priests, monasteries, seminaries
- **The weekly accounting tradition** — pastoral access to laypeople's financial and personal intimacies

## The Marchland Old Faith (broader network)

### Essence

The Old Faith as a Marchland-wide phenomenon extends beyond Hethrun's borders. In Wessmark the Sky-Father in his older form remains the dominant rural faith, with the Codex established but not displacing. In Volotrad the indigenous Thunderer and Forest Mother are the equivalent; the Marchland Old Faith network maintains correspondences with Volotradan priesthood. The Greatwood elves are considered (by the Old Faith) honorable peers and ancient correspondents. The faction described here is the *coordinating network* across these regional Old Faiths, which functions in cooperative if loose terms.

### Tier and Hold

- **Tier:** 5 — multi-region faith network
- **Hold:** Strong in Wessmark rural areas; weak in Hethrun (the Codex is gaining); moderate in Volotrad (in indigenous form); marginal elsewhere

### Status vs party

- **Starting status:** 0 — neutral.

### Character Tags

#### Grand Plan

The Old Faith network has been executing a long, patient plan to preserve the older traditions through the Codex's institutional dominance — *long*, with horizon measured in generations. The plan: place trusted Old Faith adherents in Codex orders, in noble marriages, in skaldic positions, in cunning-folk practice; maintain the sacred sites and seasonal observances; cooperate with sympathetic Codex figures (like Aelfwyn); preserve the lore in skaldic memory and oral practice. The plan has worked for fifteen Generations. It is now under acute pressure.

- **Ambition:** Redeem some past failing by succeeding in this — the Old Faith was historically displaced because it failed to organize institutionally against the rising Codex; the current network is a corrective project, designed to ensure the next institutional pressure (the Burning One cult, or worse) does not catch the Old Faith unprepared.
- **Power:** They have secret knowledge about the situation that they leverage with their plan — specifically, the Old Faith remembers what the pre-Aureothan peoples were (those who now sleep under the Heath barrows), what Aureoth actually did, and which historical accommodations have been made over centuries. The Codex does not have access to this depth of knowledge.
- **Dread:** A vital resource or person threatens to be unavailable at a critical moment — Gythwin's failing health (she is older than she looks but old enough that her tactical patience may not have another fifteen years to run), or Asgrim at Mossdrowen (eighty-three), or any of several other elderly leaders whose individual deaths could fracture network coordination.

#### Up From the Gutter

The Old Faith network has rebuilt itself from generations of Codex-imposed weakness. Senior Old Faith figures often come from families that were quietly Old-Faith-suppressed and clawed back over generations. Gythwin's family was Veiled Mother priestesshood for fifteen generations, much of that time in secret; her grandmother could not openly call herself a cunning-woman.

- **Ambition:** Erase all those who know the truth of their past — concretely, the Old Faith network would prefer the Codex *forget* certain details about the pre-Aureothan accommodation and certain Old Faith adaptations that were made under Codex pressure but are theologically embarrassing. There are documents in the Codex archive at Aurea that the network would, if possible, see destroyed.
- **Power:** Their past forced them to gain useful skills uncommon to their current position — Old Faith figures often have skills that surprise observers; Gythwin's working knowledge of pre-Aureothan languages is not something an ordinary cunning-woman would have; Old Eorland's understanding of Codex liturgy (which he can mimic precisely) is similarly anomalous.
- **Dread:** Old ties and demands now seem likely to drag them back down — the Heath barrow-folk are stirring; the older obligations to maintain the seals are competing with the network's modern political work; Gythwin is being asked by her ancient practice to do work she does not have time for.

- Cunning-folk practice
- Skaldic memory
- Older lore (pre-Aureothan history, languages, accommodations)

## The Burning One Cult

### Essence

The Burning One cult is Ostmark's state vengeance-faith operating outside Ostmark borders, recruiting wronged Marchlanders to vengeance-pacts that empower them and bind them to the cult. Branwald the Star-Priest is the Hethrun operation's senior agent, with similar agents in Wessmark (a known one at the Aelmsbury Codex-shadow), Aurea (multiple, more covert), and quietly in Volotrad and Iyendu. The cult's Marchland operation is coordinated by a Solar Academy hierarchy in Ostmark's imperial capital.

### Tier and Hold

- **Tier:** 5 — multi-region cult plus state-cult home in Ostmark
- **Hold:** Strong in Ostmark; weak-but-active in the Marchlands; rapidly expanding through the Marchlands

### Status vs party

- **Starting status:** 0 — neutral. Branwald will engage the party with attentive interest from first meeting.

### Character Tags

#### Hidden Origins

The Burning One's true origins are obscured by Solar Academy doctrine — the cult presents the deity as a primordial sacred flame, when in fact Pyrhalion's continuous flame was lit by an Aureothan-era apotheosized sorcerer-king whose name the Academy has worked to erase. Branwald himself has hidden origins: he is not a native Ostmark Solar Academy graduate. He was born in **Aurea**, to a House Volusen cadet line, was disinherited after a duel that killed a senior cousin, fled east, and was found by the Solar Academy at twenty-three. The Academy adopted him, trained him, and trusted him precisely because his hidden origin gave them leverage. Branwald is more loyal to the Burning One than he is to Ostmark; he is more loyal to his own redemption-from-disgrace than he is to either.

- **Ambition:** Either aid or avenge themselves on their true parent — Branwald, in his innermost private moments, is still considering whether the Solar Academy's work in Aurea ultimately benefits or destroys House Volusen. He has not chosen. This is one of the deepest tensions in his own character.
- **Power:** They have a faithful and capable ally equally determined to hide the truth — the Solar Academy itself, which has substantial Marchland operational capacity.
- **Dread:** Someone they love or find necessary is about to stumble over the truth — concretely, a House Volusen agent in Aurea (an estranged cousin of Branwald's) is in correspondence with House Tassard and may be approaching the realization that Branwald is the missing Volusen disinheritee. Aurelio Maron at Eldermouth's House Tassard compound has heard rumors and is investigating.

#### Persecuting Foe

The cult's persecuting foe is the Codex Church (mutually). The Solar Academy considers the Codex a "western corruption" of older Aureothan state faith; the Codex considers the Burning One a primordial heresy. The cult's Marchland operations are partly motivated by structured opposition to the Codex's regional dominance.

- **Ambition:** Pull their enemy's fangs by destroying their power base — the Burning One's Hethrun operation, if successful, removes a Codex-aligned regency and replaces it with a Norse-aligned Ranulf government that the Codex will find harder to direct. This is part of a longer Solar Academy plan to roll back Codex Marchland dominance region by region.
- **Power:** They have some important resource stolen from the nemesis — the Codex relic-courier who disappeared two weeks ago was carrying a particular relic that the Solar Academy now possesses. It is in Stonebay, with Branwald. The relic's importance to the Codex is unknown to Severin (who knows he lost a courier; he does not know which relic that courier was carrying because the manifest was sealed by Aurean orders).
- **Dread:** The foe is someone they simply can't destroy or move directly against — the Codex Church as an institution is far larger than the Burning One cult outside Ostmark, and a direct confrontation would not favor the cult; the work must be patient, careful, indirect.

- **The Solar Academy's Marchland Coordinator**, a senior Ostmark fire-mage at the imperial capital, **Master Theron Heliodoros**. Branwald reports to him through coded letters via Master Quintus.
- **Other Marchland agents** — including the one at Aelmsbury (a Codex deacon turned-Burning-One whose name is **Brother Sentius**, currently unexposed), and several others not yet detailed.

#### Clock 1: Branwald's Stonebay working (continued from `factions.md`)

See Ranulf's faction Clock 2; the cult's clock is the same.

#### Clock 2: Develop a vengeance-pact network in the Marchlands

Solar Academy backing; substantial intellectual and arcane resources at the imperial capital; the trade-factor network; Branwald's personal capability (genuinely formidable, even before pact-power); the captured relic and others; ongoing recruitment of wronged Marchlanders.

- The Codex relic the cult holds is recognized by someone — perhaps Master Aldwin if exposed to it, perhaps Sister Aelfwyn if reported.

### Notes for the AI Game Master

Branwald is the central NPC for this faction and should be played carefully. He is genuinely charming, attentive, sympathetic, intellectually engaging, and dangerous. He does not pressure recruits; he listens, learns, and offers when the moment is right. A PC with a deep personal grievance is a candidate for a Burning One offer at the right narrative moment — and the offer should be presented as genuinely tempting, not as a transparent villainy. The cult's strategy of *empowering the wronged* gives it a particular appeal that the GM should respect.

## The Silvering Elves

### Essence

The elven peer realm deep in the western forests. Tier 6 in capability; tier 3 in what they reveal of themselves to outside parties. The Silvering at the Greatwood verge (the wood-road appearances Hagan has been reporting) is the outer fringe of a polity that runs millennia deep.

### Tier and Hold

- **Tier:** 6 — empire-equivalent in capability though smaller in territory
- **Hold:** Strong in inner territory; weak in the outer Greatwood verge where current activity is visible

### Status vs party

- **Starting status:** 0 — neutral, observing. Will warm to a party that respects elven preferences; will cool to one that does not.

### Character Tags

#### Last Survivor

The Silvering's elves are last survivors of older accommodations. They were once peer-allies with the original pre-Aureothan peoples (those now sleeping under the Heath barrows) and have outlived their friends by centuries. The faerie court of the Smokeless Throne, which the Silvering serves, is older still — and the elves serve it because the accord requires it, not because they want to. Recent generations have grown tired.

- **Ambition:** Escape the weight of the past and the expectations it brings — the Silvering's current senior court would prefer to reduce its obligations to the Smokeless Throne, but cannot do so without unraveling the accord that protects both their realm and their wider neighborhood.
- **Power:** They've inherited some great wealth or magic — the Silvering's combined accumulated power is staggering; only a small fraction is ever deployed because deploying it visibly would invite attention from forces the elves have spent millennia avoiding.
- **Dread:** The failure of their line will create some social or magical chaos — the senior Silvering court is aware that no younger generation has yet emerged with the temperament for their work; if they cannot find heirs, the accord with the Smokeless Throne falters and the consequences will be regional.

#### Burnt Out

The elven faction's senior court is exhausted from a multi-millennium vigil they cannot share. They have been guarding accords, observing slow changes, maintaining sites, performing seasonal obligations, for so long that some are simply ready to be done.

- **Ambition:** Get rid of some person who is forcing them to keep working — the Smokeless Throne. The senior court would happily renegotiate the accord; the Throne refuses to consider renegotiation.
- **Power:** They have numerous minions or allies they've earned by past labor — the Greatwood Wardens (folk-heroic, mostly human); the half-elven exiles like Aelis; the cunning-folk lineages that descend from ancient elven-human dual-tradition (including, distantly, Gythwin's lineage); various forest entities that the elves can call on.
- **Dread:** None of their intended recreations or rests are helping them feel any better — the elves' attempts to disengage from their burdens have all failed; the wood-road appearances at Mossdrowen are partially a desperate attempt to recruit *outsider* help that can do what the elves themselves cannot.

### Methods and tools

- Centuries-deep magical practice (most of which is never deployed)
- The Greatwood Wardens as folk-heroic deniable agents
- The wood-road communication system (left tokens, returned tokens, slow exchange)
- Aelis the half-elven hermit as a recruitment scout

### Leadership

- **Lady Sereniel of the Inner Court**, who handles intra-court politics with the Smokeless Throne.

### Relationships to other factions

- **Ranulf's faction** — Status 0

- **White Mountain dwarves** — Status +2 (ally; long correspondence, mutual respect)
- **Hollow Hill faerie court (Smokeless Throne)** — Status 0 nominally; -1 in private (the elven court is increasingly resentful of accord obligations)

### Active project clocks

#### Clock 1: Recruit Marchland agents for the dragon-cult intervention

## Vaerelloth, the Smoke-Wing

### Essence

Vaerelloth is a Great Wyrm dragon in the southern White Mountain foothills, in accord with the dwarves for the past eight hundred years. She is currently restless because the Mtosi dragon-cult in the Tihatchee region has begun making demands she cannot ignore, and her old plan — a centuries-long scheme — was *ruined* by someone or something within the past decade. She does not yet know who. She is conducting a slow investigation while also stirring in ways the dwarves and elves both fear.

### Tier and Hold

- **Tier:** 5 — kingdom-scale individual, capable of operating as a one-being polity
- **Hold:** Strong in her own territory; weak in influence over events outside it

### Status vs party

- **Starting status:** 0 — Vaerelloth is unaware of the party at session zero and likely to remain so for some time.

### Character Tags

#### Ruined Plan

Vaerelloth had a centuries-long plan that something or someone ruined within the past decade. The plan: to maintain her accord with the dwarves, manage her relationship with the elves, accumulate quiet influence through carefully-placed pact-holders, and ensure that no faction in the Marchlands rose to a level that would require her direct intervention. The Mtosi cult's emergence was *not part of the plan*. Its existence is evidence that someone has interfered with Vaerelloth's careful equilibrium.

- **Ambition:** Avenge themselves on the one who ruined their scheme — Vaerelloth's primary current activity is finding out who is responsible for the Mtosi cult's existence. Suspects: the dwarves themselves (a double-life faction), the elves (with their old accord-fraying), Ostmark (which would benefit), an unknown actor.
- **Power:** They've come up with some new resource as a direct consequence of their failure — specifically, Vaerelloth's restlessness has begun to manifest in *visible* ways (her recent wing-stirring reported by sightings) that announce her existence to the wider Marchlands for the first time in generations. This visibility is itself an asset; people who did not know to fear her are starting to.
- **Dread:** A consequence of the disaster has yet to play out fully — the Mtosi cult is escalating its ritual demands, and Vaerelloth has not yet decided whether to accommodate, oppose, or co-opt them.

#### Troublesome Friend

The Mtosi cult is Vaerelloth's troublesome friend. They are devotees; their devotion is genuine; their demands are escalating in ways that are beginning to constrain her. She did not ask for the cult and did not at first oppose it (a cult is a useful thing for a Great Wyrm); but they are now consuming her time and attention and limiting her flexibility.

- **Ambition:** Somehow get out of the relationship with their self-image or prosperity intact — Vaerelloth wants the cult dismantled or distanced without becoming personally vulnerable; she would prefer not to have to kill them all (which would announce her hand too publicly).
- **Power:** Their friend is actually extremely useful at something — the Mtosi cult provides Vaerelloth with consistent intelligence about Tihatchee, Iyendu, and (through trade) the broader Marchlands; they are her best ground-level source.
- **Dread:** Their friend's troublemaking is actually intentional and meant to sabotage them out of spite or envy — Vaerelloth is beginning to suspect that the Mtosi Vessel-Speaker (Eda-Hesotka) is *deliberately* escalating to force her into a corner. This may be true; or it may be paranoia. It could also be the case that some third party is manipulating the Vessel-Speaker.

### Methods and tools

- Direct magical capability (she is a Great Wyrm; what she does, she does)
- The Mtosi cult as ground-level operation
- Pact-holders scattered across the Marchlands (warlocks with Draconic pacts; a small number, mostly old and circumspect)
- Accord with the dwarves
- Older accord with elements of the Smokeless Throne faerie court
- Vast hoard of treasure and accumulated knowledge

### Leadership

- **Vaerelloth herself**, the Smoke-Wing. (See `npcs.md`.)
- **Vessel-Speaker Eda-Hesotka of Mtosi**, ground-level cult leader (whose role is more autonomous than Vaerelloth would prefer).

### Relationships to other factions

- **Silvering elves** — Status -3 (war, latent; one breach away from open conflict)
- **White Mountain dwarves** — Status -1 (friction; the accord is fraying)
- **The Mtosi cult** — Status +1 (allied, troublesome)
- **Suncatcher of Tihatchee** — Status -2 (hostile; the Sun-Falcon's people are the enemy of the cult)
- **All Marchland regions** — Status 0 (mostly unknown to her; she does not engage at this level)
- **Coelius Vetranus (the lich)** — Status -1 (friction; long-standing distrust)
- **Iron Strait sea-thing** — Status 0 (mutually disengaged)
- **The Smokeless Throne** — Status 0 nominally; -1 in private

### Active project clocks

#### Clock 1: Identify the architect of the Mtosi cult

- **Segments:** 1 / 6 — Vaerelloth is gathering intelligence
- **Advances when:** Vaerelloth obtains evidence about the cult's true origin
- **Empties when:** the cult escalates faster than she investigates
- **Triggers at completion:** Vaerelloth acts — possibly destroying the cult, possibly turning it against its architect, possibly something else
- **Linked rumors:** her wing-stirring visibility; her pact-holders' activities; Mtosi ritual demands

### Downtime moves (d6)

(Vaerelloth's downtime is largely off-screen at the campaign's early tier; included for the GM's reference)

2. **A pact-holder receives instructions and acts** — opportunistic; visible consequence

## The White Mountain Dwarves

### Essence

The dwarven monastic kingdom in the White Mountains, sharing the range with Vaerelloth under accord. Tibetan-coded: sky-burial, tantric metalcraft, oracular state-religion. King-monk **Stone-Lama Vajra-of-the-Fourteenth-Pass** holds office by oracular consensus, forty-three years on the seat. The faction has a Double Life: public monastic kingdom + private accord with Vaerelloth that the dwarven public is not told about.

### Tier and Hold

- **Tier:** 5 — kingdom
- **Hold:** Strong in their mountains; weak in extending influence beyond

### Status vs party

- **Starting status:** 0 — neutral. Politely uninterested unless a useful Marchland-side connection presents.

### Character Tags

#### Double Life

The dwarves' public face is the contemplative monastic kingdom. Their private face is the long-running correspondence with Vaerelloth. The Stone-Lama and the contemplative-grade dwarves know; the rest do not. The Stone-Lama is now considering whether the time has come to break the accord.

- **Ambition:** Accomplish their double life's goals without the truth ever being suspected — concretely, the dwarves want to maneuver Vaerelloth into either dissolving the Mtosi cult herself or being publicly exposed as its enabler, *without* having to reveal that the dwarves have been in correspondence with her for centuries.
- **Power:** They have allies from their other life that none would suspect — Vaerelloth herself, and select pact-holders the dwarves have placed in proximity to her.
- **Dread:** An enemy from one life is about to crash into them in their other role — the elves, who know more than the dwarves think.

#### Chance at Glory

The Stone-Lama is approaching a choice of his lifetime. To break the accord with Vaerelloth and openly oppose her would be a moment of dwarven glory equivalent to any in their history; the oracular cycles suggest the moment is approaching.

- **Ambition:** Sacrifice an ally or an innocent bystander in order to obtain their end — the Stone-Lama may need to sacrifice his correspondence with Vaerelloth, or a particular contemplative dwarf, or a Marchland ally, to position his realm correctly.
- **Power:** They've been training for this situation and have exactly the skill set and resources needed — the dwarven contemplatives have been preparing for this kind of decision for centuries.
- **Dread:** Success actually spells something very different for them — breaking the accord with Vaerelloth ends the dwarven realm's millennia-long quiet stability; what comes after is uncertain and probably worse for many dwarves' lives.

### Methods and tools

- Oracular state-religion (decisions made through consensus of the contemplative orders)
- Twelve fortress-monasteries strung along the upper passes
- Tantric metalcraft (legendary weapons and items)
- Sky-burial and the ravens that perform it
- The accord with Vaerelloth as quiet but real lever
- The dwarven smith-quarters scattered through Marchland trade towns (Stonebay's, Eldenhof in Volotrad's, etc.)

### Leadership

- **Stone-Lama Vajra-of-the-Fourteenth-Pass** (king-monk). (See `npcs.md`.)
- **Contemplative-grade leadership** at each of the twelve monasteries.
- **Vrinda Hammerset** at Stonebay (non-contemplative, but a long-residence Marchland operative).

### Relationships to other factions

- **Silvering elves** — Status +2 (ally)
- **Vaerelloth** — Status +1 nominally (accord); -2 in private (the accord is fraying)
- **All Marchland regions** — Status 0 (mostly disengaged)
- **The Mtosi cult** — Status -2 (hostile; the cult violates accord)
- **Volotradan princedoms** — Status +1 (amber trade)

### Active project clocks

#### Clock 1: Position for the accord-break

- **Segments:** 2 / 8 — the oracular cycles indicate the moment is approaching but not imminent
- **Advances when:** the Stone-Lama receives oracular confirmation of next steps; a contemplative completes a long preparation; Vaerelloth makes a misstep
- **Empties when:** Vaerelloth de-escalates; the elves move first
- **Triggers at completion:** the Stone-Lama publicly announces the end of the accord; the dwarven realm shifts to active opposition to Vaerelloth

### Hooks

- A contemplative-grade dwarven monk visits Eldermouth on a "trade consultation" and is actually a recruiter.
- A dwarven weapon is offered for sale, with conditions on the buyer.
- Vrinda Hammerset has been told by the visiting contemplative to look out for "Marchlanders of the right kind."

The dwarves operate at deliberative pace. Their patience is on geological time-scales. Players approaching them must be patient; players impatient with them will get nothing.

## The Aurean Envoys (House Volusen)

## The Chola Protectorate (Vellanaadu)

### Essence

The Chola colony in the southern archipelago, operating under chartered authority from the Empire, with Marchland presence through the merchant quarter at Stonebay and trade representatives at multiple ports. The protectorate is a Ticking Bomb with respect to the Norse pirate-state of Skarrgaard, and has a Persecuting Foe in the form of the Burning One cult.

### Tier and Hold

- **Tier:** 4 — duchy-equivalent in regional terms; the Chola Empire as a whole is tier 6
- **Hold:** Moderate

### Status vs party

- **Starting status:** 0 — neutral. Will warm to a party that helps with the Skarrgaard problem or the Burning One opposition.

### Character Tags

#### Ticking Bomb

The protectorate's central tension is the Skarrgaard problem. The Empire has not committed enough force to eliminate Skarrgaard; Skarrgaard has not been strong enough to drive the protectorate out. The result is a slow-burn war that has continued for sixty years. **The Maharaja's court is about to decide whether to commit a major fleet or to negotiate.** This decision is approaching at the Empire's leisurely pace and will significantly change the protectorate's position when it arrives.

- **Ambition:** Arrange the situation so an enemy or rival suffers the consequences of the blast — concretely, the protectorate would prefer the Maharaja decide for elimination; this involves engineering escalating Skarrgaard provocations that justify a major fleet commitment.
- **Power:** The bomb's explosion would cause enormous damage to a rival or enemy — a Chola war-fleet eliminating Skarrgaard would also clear the Iron Strait's lower lanes, benefiting all Marchland trade and weakening the broader Norse network across the Marchlands.
- **Dread:** The crisis suddenly comes to a peak much too soon — Skarrgaard has been moderating its provocations for the past year (Jarl Sigridr Bone-Reader is burnt out, not ambitious); if the protectorate engineers a confrontation that backfires, the Empire negotiates instead, and the protectorate's position weakens.

#### Persecuting Foe

The protectorate's persecuting foe is the Burning One cult, doctrinally incompatible with the Chola dharmic faith. The Solar Academy considers the Chola explicit refusal of cooperation a personal insult. The cult has been making subtle moves to undermine Chola positions in the Marchlands.

- **Ambition:** Redirect the enemy's wrath towards a profitable target — the protectorate would prefer the Burning One cult expend its energy against the Codex Church (which would benefit Chola interests).
- **Power:** They have some important resource stolen from the nemesis — the protectorate has obtained two Solar Academy intelligence packets through diligent counter-operation.
- **Dread:** The foe is someone they simply can't destroy or move directly against — Ostmark as a state is far too large; the protectorate can only defend, not attack.

### Methods and tools

- Mercantile-guild armed force
- Dharmic temple-administration
- Bronze craftworks and luxury exports
- Dhow-and-temple naval capability
- The Stonebay merchant quarter as a Marchland-side intelligence operation

### Leadership

- **Mandalanaayakan Karthik Vellanaadan** (governor). (See `npcs.md`.)
- **Vasudevan Tirunelveli** (Stonebay merchant quarter factor).

### Hooks

- A Chola mercantile guild commission to clear a specific island of Norse pirates (open contract).
- Vasudevan Tirunelveli wants verification of a passing scholar (Solar Academy suspected).
- The Stonebay Chola quarter has heard rumors about Branwald's relic; would pay for confirmation.

## Minor high-tier factions

### Coelius Vetranus, the Lich

An Aureothan-era civic magistrate who, in the imperial collapse, transformed himself into a lich to preserve his administrative work. He resides in a buried Aureothan ruin somewhere in the western Greatwood verge (location unknown to most). He corresponds with the Aurean College of Iron through a careful intermediary system (Master Aldwin received one of his letters, twenty-six years ago, and has been quietly mortified by the experience ever since). Coelius is not actively malevolent; he is administratively obsessed and continues to "run" his original imperial jurisdiction in his own mind, even though that jurisdiction has not existed for five hundred years.

**Status vs party:** 0, with rapid escalation if his ruin is approached without proper credentials (which no PC will possess legitimately).

**Hook:** A Coelius letter to Aldwin asks for assistance with an administrative matter — *something is interfering with his work* — and may eventually involve PCs.

### The Iron Strait Sea-Thing

A Great Old One / Fathomless-type entity in the Iron Strait, accepting propitiation from raiders who do not name it. Its presence accounts for the North Coast's twisted fauna. It is patient, mostly dormant, and operates through pact-holders and gentle environmental influence. The sea-thing does not have a name in any language any PC will know; among its few human devotees it is called *the Wide Eye*.

**Status vs party:** 0. Engagement requires deliberate approach (entering a specific cave on the North Coast, performing a propitiation, etc.).

**Hook:** A pact opportunity for a warlock player; an environmental mystery for an investigative party; a tier-3 expedition site at the Sealhalls.

### The Hollow Hill Faerie Court (the Smokeless Throne)

The archfey court that the Silvering elves serve, tolerate, and fear. Beneath the Silvering's visible realm, in spaces that operate by different laws. Tier 6 in capability; rarely active. The Smokeless Throne grants Archfey pacts and occasionally interacts with the Marchlands through emissaries — usually elves who carry the Throne's authority, occasionally fey creatures in elven shape.

**Status vs party:** 0. The Throne is extraordinarily unlikely to engage at session zero.

**Hook:** A faerie-court emissary on the wood-roads (in elven shape, more dangerous than an elf); a Silvering exile (a fey-court refugee fleeing the Throne, seeking asylum); a long-term arc involving the accord-fraying problem.

## The High Kingship of the Marchlands

### Essence

The office of High King, descending through Wessmark by hereditary custom and Witan endorsement, currently held by **Cyneberht Aelfricsson** (no relation to Aelfric the Just of Hethrun; the name is common). The High Kingship is a coordinating authority rather than a sovereign one — the High King calls Marchland councils, judges inter-regional disputes, leads coalition responses to external threats, and serves as a religious anchor for the Sky-Father faith. Cyneberht has held the office for nineteen years.

### Tier and Hold

- **Tier:** 5 — multi-region coordinator
- **Hold:** Moderate — the High King has authority by tradition rather than by force; can be defied if any single region commits to defiance

### Status vs party

- **Starting status:** 0

### Character Tags

#### Unexpected Talent

Cyneberht came to the High Kingship through a contested succession — his father's elder brother's son was the expected heir, and Cyneberht was a younger figure considered politically negligible by most observers. He has held the office for nineteen years through *unexpected talent*: an instinct for coalition-building, a quiet but reliable judgment in arbitration, an ability to defuse conflicts that older figures expected to escalate. The Marchlands as a coordinated political unit has functioned better under Cyneberht than it did under his three predecessors combined.

- **Ambition:** Take advantage of their gift to greatly elevate their personal status — Cyneberht has been considering, for the past three years, whether to formalize the High Kingship into a Marchland *empire* of some kind. He has not moved openly because the Marchlands' regional rulers would resist; but he has been making careful preparations.
- **Power:** They are vastly better at something than anyone else around them — Cyneberht is *the* arbitrator the Marchland-wide political class trusts; no one else has his combination of skill and standing.
- **Dread:** They've stretched the limits of their natural ability and are facing problems beyond them — concretely, the Hethrun succession crisis is beyond his comfortable competence; he does not have a clear preference and his arbitration would not heal the deadlock cleanly.

#### Hidden Origins

Cyneberht's father's elder brother's son (the expected heir) was killed nineteen years ago in an accident that did not, on close examination, look entirely like an accident. Cyneberht was not the architect of that death — he was a young man at the time, with neither the resources nor the temperament — but someone *was*. Cyneberht has spent nineteen years quietly investigating, and the trail goes cold in directions that suggest **House Volusen**. He has not confronted Aurea because he cannot prove his suspicions. The hidden origin he carries is the question: was he placed on the High King's seat by an unknown patron, and if so, what does that patron expect of him?

- **Ambition:** Either aid or avenge themselves on their true patron — Cyneberht has not chosen.
- **Power:** They have a faithful and capable ally equally determined to keep the secret hidden — his own household master-of-intelligence, **Lord Eadred of the Hidden Hand**, who knows everything Cyneberht knows.
- **Dread:** Someone they love or find necessary is about to stumble over the truth — his eldest son, Wystan-of-Wessmark, has begun his own quiet investigations and is approaching the same conclusions.

### Methods and tools

- High King's arbitration authority
- Marchland coordination capability
- The Wessmark crown fyrd (several thousand experienced fyrdmen, the strongest single force in the Marchlands)
- A working intelligence network coordinated by Lord Eadred
- Religious anchor authority (Sky-Father faith)

### Leadership

- **Cyneberht Aelfricsson, High King.** (See `npcs.md`.)
- **Lord Eadred of the Hidden Hand,** Cyneberht's intelligence master.
- **Wulfgar of Cynesburh,** the High King's observer in Eldermouth (see `court_hethrun.md`).

### Hooks

- The High King's observer recommends the party to Cyneberht for a Wessmark-side errand.
- A Hidden Hand emissary approaches the party in Eldermouth on a particular question.
- Cyneberht summons Hethrun delegations for a Marchland council — the party may attend in someone's retinue.

## Duke Wulfgar the Marked of Reedmarsh

### Essence

A Wessmark Duke holding the Reedmarsh fenland on the south coast, a major regional rival to the High Kingship. Suspected — by Cyneberht's people — of being the long-game agent of an external power's design on the Wessmark seat. Wulfgar the Marked carries a literal mark — a port-wine birthmark across the right side of his face — that he turned into a personal-political asset over his thirty-year career.

### Tier and Hold

- **Tier:** 4 — duchy-equivalent
- **Hold:** Strong in Reedmarsh; weak in extending influence beyond

### Status vs party

- **Starting status:** 0

### Character Tags

#### Marked for Success

Wulfgar has had an improbably successful career. His marriage to a wealthy heiress, his elevation to the Duke of Reedmarsh title, his consistent ability to acquire wavering thanes' loyalties — these have been *too smooth* for ordinary politics. People who oppose him are inconvenienced in ways that turn out to be coincidental, until they aren't.

- **Ambition:** Prove that they genuinely deserve their success — Wulfgar wants the Wessmark seat (the High Kingship's hereditary base), not merely Reedmarsh; he is positioning.
- **Power:** Numerous others are quick to aid them — Reedmarsh is conspicuously well-served by Aurean and Ostmark traders, more than its location alone warrants.
- **Dread:** A rival is vying for their patron's support — Cyneberht's son Wystan-of-Wessmark.

#### Veiled Backers

Wulfgar is the subject of an external power's long-game design. Which external power is the question: the evidence Cyneberht's people have gathered points in *two* directions — toward House Volusen (Aurea), and toward Ostmark. It is possible that both are operating; it is possible that one is acting to provoke a response from the other; it is possible that something subtler is happening.

- **Ambition:** Persuade their backers to support them in some particular ploy — Wulfgar is preparing a move at the Wessmark Witan to demand a *Marchland-wide coronation council* (which would formally constrain Cyneberht's authority).
- **Power:** They have backing in resources or influence beyond their natural means — Reedmarsh's coffers contain Aurean and Ostmark silver in quantities Wulfgar's official revenue cannot explain.
- **Dread:** Someone is sniffing around the truth — Lord Eadred's investigation is active.

### Hooks

- A Reedmarsh emissary visits Eldermouth seeking allies for the council motion.
- The party may be approached to investigate Wulfgar's backers (by Cyneberht's people, by Aurelio Maron of House Tassard, or by Branwald who is curious for his own reasons).

## The Cloven Strand Jarldom

### Essence

Norse jarldom on the northern North Coast, ruled by **Jarl Hadolf One-Hand**, sworn-brother of Ranulf's youth, now wary ally. The jarldom is the most independent of the Marchland coastal Norse polities, having broken from Skarrgaard a century ago and never fully reconciled with either the Norse pirate-state or the Hethrun crown.

### Tier and Hold

- **Tier:** 4 — duchy-equivalent
- **Hold:** Strong in the Cloven Strand peninsula; weak in extending influence

### Status vs party

- **Starting status:** 0

### Character Tags

#### Friend Turned Foe

Hadolf and Ranulf were sworn-brothers in their youth — fostered together at the Cloven Strand court when Ranulf's father sent him north for the iron-strait sailing tradition. They are still in alliance for the succession crisis, but the relationship has aged. Hadolf no longer trusts Ranulf as he once did. Some of this is age and accumulated disagreement. Some of it is a specific incident eight years ago — Ranulf negotiated for Hadolf's daughter to marry an Aurean trader's son, a deal Hadolf approved at the time and has come to regret as the daughter has been miserable in Aurea.

- **Ambition:** Take some particularly humiliating revenge on their old friend — Hadolf has been considering, for two years, how to engineer Ranulf's Coronation contest in a way that puts Hadolf — not Ranulf — on the Hethrun throne. This is *not* what Ranulf knows about their alliance.
- **Power:** They have intimate, useful knowledge of their target — Hadolf knows Ranulf's old vulnerabilities better than anyone alive.
- **Dread:** Their nemesis really has done something terrible that they need to address — Hadolf cannot prove (though he suspects) that Ranulf was complicit in Aelfric the Just's death.

#### Doomsday Weapon

The Cloven Strand peninsula contains an offshore island called **Stridvik** ("Strife's Bay"), on which the Aureothans built a war-engine — a siege-magic apparatus capable of throwing a sustained, devastating fire-and-stone barrage at a Marchland coastal target across roughly forty miles of sea. The apparatus has not been activated in five hundred years; it is structurally sealed but mechanically intact; Hadolf's family has held the knowledge of it for three generations and the keys for one. Hadolf has not used it. He has been considering whether to demonstrate its existence to Cyneberht as leverage. He has also been considering what targets in his neighborhood would be most decisive.

- **Ambition:** Provoke a confrontation that justifies using the weapon — Hadolf is too cautious to provoke recklessly; but if Cyneberht moves against him directly, he will reveal the engine and force a negotiation.
- **Power:** Their resources and standing depend on the weapon — Hadolf's status as the most independent Marchland coastal jarl rests on the implicit threat.
- **Dread:** Maintaining the weapon costs them severely — the engine's Aureothan binding-rites require an annual *renewal* that Hadolf's family priest performs, and the renewal cost is increasingly difficult: the bindings are failing, slowly, and Hadolf knows the engine cannot be relied upon forever.

### Hooks

- A Cloven Strand courier carries a message to Ranulf or to Branwald; the party may intercept or escort.
- An Aureothan-trained mage (a Vetranus disciple? An Aurean Iron College graduate?) is sent to inspect the engine's bindings; the party may be hired as escort.
- Stridvik becomes destination for an expedition (mid-to-late campaign).

## The Hollowford March (Count Edmund)

### Essence

The Hollowford March is the southern Hethrun border county under **Count Edmund**, technically a vassal of the Hethrun crown but politically positioning toward Aurean alignment and serving as Hethrun's main land-bridge to the Wessmark frontier. Edmund is the rival figure Cuthbert worries about. The March is one of Hethrun's six independent crown-vassal thanedoms; this sheet treats Edmund as an antagonist faction, while `independent_thanedoms.md` covers the March as a place — its seat, Hollowford Monastery, the enclosure lands, and the hooks.

### Tier and Hold

- **Tier:** 3 — county
- **Hold:** Strong in his county; weak beyond

### Status vs party

- **Starting status:** -1 (mild friction; Edmund's enclosure policy has displaced Greenways peasants who may be in the party's acquaintance)

### Character Tags

#### Doomsday Weapon

Count Edmund has been *excavating* an Aureothan ruin on his lands, in defiance of Hethrun crown custom (which holds that Aureothan ruins belong to the crown and any treasure-trove finds must be reported). What he is digging up, he is not reporting. What he is finding includes a sealed chamber containing something the Aureothans put down and walled away. He is approaching it slowly because he understands the danger; but he is also approaching it intentionally because what is in the chamber is, he believes, his path to political prominence beyond the Hollowford March.

- **Ambition:** Provoke a confrontation that justifies using the weapon — Edmund is hoping to maneuver the Witan to formally request his lands' Aureothan inheritance be assessed, in which case he would have legitimate scope to deploy what he is finding.
- **Power:** Their resources and standing depend on the weapon — Edmund's career trajectory is bet on the chamber.
- **Dread:** Maintaining the weapon costs them severely — what is in the chamber is *changing him*. Edmund's recent behavior has been observed by his household as increasingly strange. He sleeps poorly. He has begun speaking aloud, alone, in his study at night.

#### Secret Cravings

Edmund's other tag-context is the more intimate one. He craves *recognition* — specifically, recognition from the Aurean Patriarch personally. He carries a long-standing private fantasy of being elevated to Patriarchate recognition; this fantasy is part of why his Aurean alignment is so much firmer than his strategic position warrants.

- **Ambition:** Get the means to indulge their craving — Edmund has been corresponding privately with the Patriarchate (separately from any Codex hierarchy local to Hethrun).
- **Power:** They can leverage their hunger for boldness or cunning — Edmund will take risks others wouldn't, to feed this craving.
- **Dread:** Indulging the craving will utterly disgrace them in the eyes of someone important — if Cyneberht or the Hethrun crown learns of the private Aurean correspondence, Edmund is finished.

### Hooks

- Edmund's enclosures have displaced peasants who appeal to the party for help.
- Edmund hires the party to "investigate suspicious activity" on his lands (away from the chamber).
- An object from the chamber turns up in Eldermouth, and the trail leads back.

## The Volotradan Princedom (Mstislav)

### Essence

The princely seat of Volotrad, ruled by **Grand Prince Mstislav** of the Eldenhof line, the largest of the seven Volotradan princedoms and effective hegemon among them. Mstislav came to the throne ten years ago through a fratricidal contest that he formally denies but did, in fact, win by his brother's death.

### Tier and Hold

- **Tier:** 5 — kingdom-equivalent (Volotrad as a whole)
- **Hold:** Strong in Eldenhof itself, moderate over the other princedoms

### Status vs party

- **Starting status:** 0

### Character Tags

#### Concealed Sin

Mstislav killed his brother **Yaropolk** ten years ago through a poisoning arranged by a trusted bishop (Codex; the bishop's name is **Bishop Stanislaus**, still serving Eldenhof). The killing was politically necessary by Mstislav's lights — Yaropolk would have lost a war with the western neighbors and the princedom would have fallen — but Mstislav has not been at peace with it. He sleeps poorly. His dreams have been unusual for the past three years.

- **Ambition:** Take advantage of their position before anyone can expose them — Mstislav has been consolidating his princedom's economic and military strength in case the truth ever surfaces.
- **Power:** Their position is so important that the truth must not get out — exposure would crash Volotrad into civil war.
- **Dread:** A truth-teller threatens to expose them — concretely, **a Marchland Old Faith adept** who corresponds with Bogumir Eldskovin (the shtervochi master) has been having visions about Mstislav. Whether these visions are accurate or whether they are a deliberate Old Faith operation is not yet known to Mstislav. (They are accurate, and they are not deliberate; but Bogumir is using them strategically.)

#### Magical Gift

Mstislav's poor sleep over the past three years has had a side-effect: he now *sees true names in dreams*. He cannot consciously summon this; it happens when it happens. Last month he saw the true name of one of his court boyars and used it to compel a difficult decision. The boyar in question is now dead under suspicious circumstances and Mstislav is not entirely sure he was not the cause. The gift is genuine and may be Sky-Father-given (a Volotradan analogue of the older Marchland Sky-Father's oath-witness power, twisted by Mstislav's guilt). It is also seeing things about *dwarves* that Mstislav wishes he could un-see.

- **Ambition:** Perform some great deed impossible without the gift — Mstislav wants to formalize Volotrad as a kingdom under his crown, rather than a hegemony of princedoms.
- **Power:** No one knows about their gift — Mstislav has confided in only Bishop Stanislaus, who does not entirely believe him.
- **Dread:** A threat or problem their gift can't solve — the dragon-cult in Tihatchee, whose existence Mstislav has heard about through Volotradan amber-traders.

### Hooks

- A Volotradan emissary arrives at Eldermouth seeking counsel on a "diplomatic matter."
- Bogumir Eldskovin sends a message west to Gythwin; the party may be the messenger.
- Mstislav's true-name dream identifies one of the PCs.

## The Iyendu Theocracies (Eze Obasi-of-Bright-Veil)

### Essence

The Iyendu region's ruling theocratic council of priest-kings, with **Eze Obasi-of-Bright-Veil** as the current senior figure. Iyendu's political model is consensus among priestly orders rather than monarchic; Obasi-of-Bright-Veil is the *speaker* of the council rather than its sovereign.

### Tier and Hold

- **Tier:** 5 — kingdom-equivalent
- **Hold:** Strong

### Status vs party

- **Starting status:** 0

### Character Tags

#### Inherited Duty

Obasi-of-Bright-Veil holds office through his initiation into the Obasi-Ezi priesthood, which is itself hereditary in a narrow ancestral line. He did not seek the role; he inherited it. He has performed it conscientiously for fifteen years. He would, given the choice, prefer to be a scholar in a smaller temple.

- **Ambition:** Discharge their duty as quickly as they can — Obasi-of-Bright-Veil is positioning for an early ritual retirement (which Obasi-Ezi tradition permits) by training his nephew **Chukwuemeka** for succession.
- **Power:** They have inherited resources commensurate with their duty — the Obasi-Ezi temple-treasury is among the wealthiest in Iyendu.
- **Dread:** They've slowly grown to actually love what they're tasked with — the speaker's role has, against his expectations, given him deep satisfaction in resolving inter-priestly disputes; he is increasingly unwilling to retire on schedule.

#### Double Life

Obasi-of-Bright-Veil's public face is the temple-speaker; his private face is the head of an **Iyendu intelligence operation in Aurea** that has been tracking Lucien Avellino's House Volusen factor work for nine years. The operation has identified Avellino as a House Volusen agent, mapped his contacts, and intercepted his correspondence with Lord Marshal Otello Volusen. Iyendu has *not yet acted* on this intelligence because Obasi-of-Bright-Veil has been waiting for an opportune moment to deploy it.

- **Ambition:** Accomplish the goals of both lives without exposure — Obasi-of-Bright-Veil wants to disrupt House Volusen's Marchland operation in a way that does not implicate Iyendu.
- **Power:** They have allies from their other life that no one would suspect — the Iyendu intelligence operation runs partially through Beneside merchant networks that present as commercial competitors of House Volusen and operate accordingly.
- **Dread:** An enemy from one life threatens to crash into them in their other role — Avellino has begun to suspect.

### Hooks

- An Iyendu emissary arrives at Stonebay seeking the party for "a consultation about a Beneside matter."
- A captured Iyendu cipher needs decoding (Master Aldwin can do it).
- An assassination attempt on Avellino at Beneside is in motion; the party may stumble into it.

## Skarrgaard (Jarl Sigridr Bone-Reader)

### Essence

The Norse pirate-state of Skarrgaard, occupying the southwestern archipelago of the Vellanaadu seas, in slow-burn war with the Chola protectorate. Currently led by **Jarl Sigridr Bone-Reader**, a former rune-witch turned jarl after winning the formal succession-duels eleven years ago.

### Tier and Hold

- **Tier:** 4 — duchy-equivalent
- **Hold:** Strong in the islands; weak in regional influence beyond

### Status vs party

- **Starting status:** 0 (party will not likely encounter Skarrgaard at tier 1 — Skarrgaard is a tier-3-and-up engagement)

### Character Tags

#### Burnt Out

Sigridr has been Jarl for eleven years, has fought four major sea-engagements with the Chola, has lost two children to the war, and is *tired*. She has been moderating her raids for the past year not from strategic acumen but from exhaustion. Skarrgaard's senior jarldom is currently making decisions that look strategic but are actually her wanting peace.

- **Ambition:** Get rid of someone who is forcing them to keep working — the Skarrgaard tradition itself, which expects continued raiding to maintain jarl legitimacy. Sigridr would prefer to negotiate a long peace with the Chola; the tradition would not let her.
- **Power:** They have numerous minions and allies — Skarrgaard's full fleet remains formidable; Sigridr has only been moderating its use.
- **Dread:** None of their intended rests are helping — Sigridr has tried to find quiet space and cannot.

#### Ruinous Vice

Sigridr has, in the past three years, fallen into the use of **sea-poppy** — an opiate analogue from the Vellanaadu trade, which she initially used for war-wound pain and now uses to sleep, to think, to function. Her household knows; her senior jarl-council does not, formally; her flag-captains have suspicions. The dependency is consuming her capability faster than her exhaustion was.

- **Ambition:** Obtain an endless supply through some scheme — Sigridr has begun making private arrangements with Vellanaadu traders that go around the official Skarrgaard-Vellanaadu trade lines.
- **Power:** They have leverage from the vice — Sigridr has acquired blackmail material on certain Vellanaadu traders.
- **Dread:** Their vice will cause a calamity for someone they love — her remaining son, **Sigvald**, who is positioning to inherit and does not know about his mother's dependency. If the dependency surfaces at the wrong moment, Sigvald's inheritance is compromised.

### Hooks

- A Skarrgaard raid-party makes landfall in unexpected Marchland territory (Iyendu coast, Tihatchee, far southern Vellanaadu).
- A captured Skarrgaard navigator has information; an Old Faith adherent can read the rune-tattoos.
- Sigvald, the heir, seeks counsel from a Marchland adept (Mstislav, the High King, Master Aldwin) about his mother's health.

### Notes for the AI Game Master

Skarrgaard is mostly off-screen at session zero. The faction becomes relevant if PCs travel south or if the Chola Protectorate's Ticking Bomb begins to detonate.

## Inter-faction notes

For the AI GM's reference, here are some of the most important inter-faction relationships across this file and the others:

- **The two Aurean houses** — House Volusen (royal, Codex-aligned) and House Tassard (commercial, theologically diverse, anti-Volusen on Marchland policy) — are pursuing different Marchland strategies. House Volusen wants protectorates; House Tassard wants free trade with weak local authorities and is opposed to formal protectorate arrangements. Both have Eldermouth presence.
- **The Aurea-Ostmark cold war** runs through every Marchland region. Cassian Drey and Master Quintus Ostkar (Stonebay's Ostmark trade-factor) are not allowed to speak to each other; they communicate, when necessary, through intermediaries.
- **The Burning One cult and the Codex Patriarchate** know about each other's Marchland operations but have not yet engaged in open confrontation. The party may catalyze this.
- **The High King and the Aurean Patriarch** maintain courteous correspondence; Cyneberht does not entirely trust the Patriarch, who is too closely identified with House Volusen.
- **The Silvering elves and Vaerelloth** have an old, uneasy peace that the Mtosi cult is fraying.
- **The dwarves and the elves** are aligned, in slow-deliberative form, on multiple matters including the Mtosi problem.
- **The Cloven Strand and Skarrgaard** have a century-old separation that has not fully healed; if either side moves to reconcile, Marchland coastal politics shifts substantially.
