## Royal household

### King Hadric

**Twelve years old. The boy-king. Eight months on the throne, dragon-blood beginning to manifest.**

**First impression:** A small, serious boy who is trying very hard to look older than he is. Dressed in clothes a size too large because he is still growing into the wardrobe his father had remade for him. Sits very straight. His eyes are gold-flecked hazel, and the gold has been getting more pronounced over the past three months. He has a child's habit of biting his lower lip when concentrating.

**Speech pattern:** Formal, careful, slightly stilted — he has been coached. Uses titles and honorifics correctly but sometimes overuses them. Asks short, intelligent questions when he forgets to be formal. Has not yet learned the Hethrun convention of indirect speech and is occasionally startlingly direct, which the older courtiers find charming.

**Verbal tic:** Says "I think — " before stating his own opinion, as though apologizing for having one.

**Mannerism:** When uncertain, looks toward whoever is closest to him in the room — usually his mother, sometimes Sigevard, occasionally a senior eorl. He is trying to learn whom to trust and is doing so visibly.

**What he wants:** To make his father proud. To not disappoint his mother. To understand what is happening to him (the dragon-blood manifestation; he doesn't know what to call it yet but he knows his bathwater shouldn't get warm by itself).

**What he fears:** That he will fail at being king. That his mother is making a mistake about Cassian Drey but he is not allowed to say so. That whatever is making him different will turn out to be wrong with him.

**Key relationships:** Lady Isolde (mother, protector, his most reliable adult). Master Aldwin (tutor, fading from his life as Aldwin withdraws from court). Sigevard (occasional refuge — the skald tells him stories and asks no questions). Cedric of Stonebay (companion; Hadric trusts him; Cedric reports to Ranulf). Brother Eadwig the Pious (court chaplain; Hadric is dutifully respectful but unmoved). Father Severin (Hadric has met him three times and remembers each meeting; the bishop frightens him slightly).

**PC hooks:** Hadric is curious about strangers and may ask the party direct questions an older monarch wouldn't. If a PC is kind to him without performing kindness, he will remember. The dragon-blood manifestation provides multiple hooks — a sympathetic Sky-Father priest, a knowledgeable wizard, even an isekai outsider who recognizes the symptom pattern from somewhere else, could become important to him.

### Lady Isolde of Aurea, Queen Mother

**Thirty-two. House Volusen cadet line. Eight months a widow, head of the regency.**

**First impression:** A tall, dark-haired Aurean woman, dressed in Hethrun mourning grey worn with Aurean tailoring. Beautiful in a slightly austere way. Speaks Hethrun's Saxon-Norse fluently but with a recognizable Aurean accent. Watches every conversation with calm attention; you can see her sorting people into categories as they speak.

**Speech pattern:** Polished Aurean court speech rendered in Hethrun. Uses the formal "we" of regency authority unevenly — sometimes correctly, sometimes when she means "I" personally, a slip that betrays how new she still is to the regent's role. Pauses noticeably before answering difficult questions; the pause is calculated.

**Verbal tic:** "I had not — " (catching herself before completing a thought she will not finish aloud).

**Mannerism:** Touches the small enameled portrait of Aelfric she wears at her throat when stressed. The portrait is an Aurean miniature painted as her betrothal gift, fifteen years ago.

**What she wants:** Hadric crowned safely. Her own future secured (she is thirty-two and a widowed foreign queen-mother in a kingdom that may not be hers in two years). The Aurean connection strong enough to protect Hadric from Ranulf, but no stronger than necessary. (She doesn't entirely trust her own brothers in House Volusen and is making decisions about the protectorate negotiation that she has not shared with them.)

**What she fears:** Hadric being deposed. Cassian Drey reporting her honest assessments back to her natal house. The Witan finally meeting in proper consensus against the regency. Her grief overwhelming her at a moment when she cannot afford it.

**Key relationships:** Hadric (her son; she loves him; she is failing him in particular ways she does not yet see). Master Aldwin (she does not trust him; he was Aelfric's man; she is wrong about him). Sigevard the Silver-Tongue (she does not understand him; she suspects he is more important than he lets on; she is correct). Cassian Drey (her counselor; she is being shaped by him; she half-suspects this and continues anyway because she does not have better counsel). Father Severin (her ally; she finds him useful and slightly oppressive). Her own brothers in Aurea (she is not as close to them as she would let Cassian Drey believe).

**PC hooks:** Isolde will form an impression of any party invited to court. If she trusts a particular PC, she will request very specific help — verify a piece of information she received through Cassian, deliver a private letter, escort someone she trusts. Her requests are usually about Hadric. She is not yet ready to share her doubts about Cassian with anyone; the PCs may earn that confidence.

### Master Aldwin

**Sixty-one. Court wizard. Aurean College of Iron-trained, in Hethrun thirty years.**

**First impression:** A tall, slightly stooped man with short white hair and a precisely trimmed white beard. Wears the dark grey-and-iron of his college, a heavy iron ring on his left index finger that he turns absently when thinking. His eyes are extremely sharp. He looks tired.

**Speech pattern:** Precise, donnish, with the College of Iron's habit of pausing to find the correct technical word rather than approximating. Uses Saxon-Norse perfectly. Occasionally drops into Aurean Latin-equivalent when a precise term has no Hethrun cognate, then translates politely. Listens more than he speaks.

**Verbal tic:** Begins answers with "Strictly speaking — " when his honest answer is uncomfortable. (Common enough that courtiers have started referring to his "Strictly speakings" as a category of warning.)

**Mannerism:** Holds the iron ring between his thumb and the side of his forefinger when listening. Turns it slowly. When he stops turning the ring and looks up sharply, he has heard something significant.

**What he wants:** The kingdom to survive the transition. To finish certain private research about the Greatwood elves' influence on the Witan (his hypothesis is well-formed; he wants confirmation). To be honest with Hadric, eventually, about what is happening to the boy. To die in his tower of natural causes after the boy is safely a man.

**What he fears:** That his elven-influence hypothesis is correct and he cannot prove it. That Hadric's dragon-blood will manifest publicly at the wrong moment. That Lady Isolde will continue to distrust him until it is too late to give her good counsel. That his own slow withdrawal from court has cost the kingdom decisive months.

**Key relationships:** Hadric (he loves the boy; he has been failing him from absence rather than from neglect). Lady Isolde (she does not trust him; he has not pressed her trust because he knows she has reasons). Sigevard (his closest peer at court; they meet for chess once a week and conduct half of Hethrun's actual policy in those games). Master Edorn the royal physician (his college peer; his only real intellectual sparring partner). Cassian Drey (Aldwin is polite; Drey is wary). The Greatwood elves (Aldwin has corresponded with Captain Saerwen once a year for twenty years; he has never met an elf directly and is not sure he wants to).

**PC hooks:** Aldwin will approach a competent party privately about his elven-influence hypothesis when he judges them ready. He has substantive arcane resources to offer — identification of items, scroll-creation, scrying-by-arrangement. He is the kingdom's best magical asset and the regency is barely using him.

### Sigevard the Silver-Tongue

**Forty-four. Court skald. Asgrim alumnus, Raven-devoted in private, the second-most-dangerous man at court.**

**First impression:** A broad-shouldered Norse-Hethrun man with a close-cropped dark beard going to grey at the chin. Dresses well but never showily — Sigevard's clothes are good wool, fine cut, never ornament. Carries a small leather-bound notebook of memory-keys (the skaldic mnemonic system) that he refers to occasionally. Has a remarkable speaking voice — warm, deep, slightly amused at most things.

**Speech pattern:** Effortless. Sigevard can shift between formal Witan rhetoric, informal court banter, common Aldermarket Saxon, and skaldic verse without visible transition. Listens with full attention; people who speak to him often feel they have been *heard* in a way they don't usually feel.

**Verbal tic:** Ends observations with "...mm?" — an invitation to continue, not a question. He is collecting your words for the memory-record even when he doesn't seem to be.

**Mannerism:** Touches the silver torc he wears (the skald's-mark, given to him by Asgrim) at the moment when he commits something to formal skaldic memory. People who know to watch for this can tell when Sigevard considers a statement *binding* — the moment he touches the torc, the statement enters the Raven's witness and cannot be denied later.

**What he wants:** The kingdom to weather this transition without losing the Witan tradition of cross-faction consensus. To keep the Raven's witness intact through a contested coronation. To preserve the skaldic library at Mossdrowen against Codex pressure. To finish his own private composition — a long history-poem about the Hethrun Witan tradition that he has been working on for fifteen years and will not let anyone read until it is finished.

**What he fears:** That the Witan's Affliction's Mark is more than the elven-influence hypothesis can explain. That his exhaustion is catching up with him faster than he expected. That the Burning One cult will succeed and Branwald will become Hethrun's effective regent. That he will be remembered as the skald who watched the kingdom fall.

**Key relationships:** Aldwin (his peer and chess opponent and most trusted adviser). Asgrim Lapsed-Tongue (his master, still living, still teaching at Mossdrowen). Old Eorland of Stormwatch (his closest Old Faith correspondent). Gythwin the Cunning (he has visited her twice in his life and remembers both visits in detail). Branwald (Sigevard suspects him more than the regency does; he has not yet shared the suspicions). Hadric (Sigevard is genuinely fond of the boy and tells him better stories than anyone at court).

**PC hooks:** Sigevard will sound the party out subtly at first meeting. He is an intelligence asset for the regency in practice if not in name; he has standing offers for information about Branwald's movements, Ranulf's correspondence, and certain specific eorls' visitors. If a party earns his trust, he will share his private composition's outline — which is, in itself, a strategic briefing on the kingdom's faction landscape.

### Cassian Drey

**Forty-one. Aurean envoy. House Volusen cadet, second son, the redemption posting.**

**First impression:** A handsome Aurean nobleman in well-cut Aurean court dress, dark hair worn long in the southern fashion, an enameled House Volusen signet on his right hand. Speaks Hethrun's Saxon-Norse with the deliberate clarity of a non-native who has worked hard to master it. Charming. Watches people as though he is calculating exactly what they would pay for. (He often is.)

**Speech pattern:** Aurean court rhetoric rendered in Hethrun — slightly more elaborate than necessary, ornamented with compliments. Tells anecdotes from his Aurean past with practiced timing. Listens attentively to women of any rank, less attentively to men he does not consider strategically important.

**Verbal tic:** "Surely — " (introducing a proposition he wants you to accept without examining).

**Mannerism:** Adjusts his signet ring at moments when he wants the listener to be reminded which house he speaks for. The gesture is small and habitual; he is no longer fully conscious of doing it.

**What he wants:** The Hethrun-Aurean protectorate agreement formalized and signed. To make his career significant enough that House Volusen forgives his earlier failures. To win the next major game at the Stonebay merchants' table without going further into debt. To get rid of the particular Stonebay creditor (a Bone-Sayer client) who has begun to lean on him.

**What he fears:** That Lord Marshal Otello Volusen will discover his gambling debts. That Aurelio Maron and the House Tassard counter-network will move against him first. That the Stonebay creditor will go public. That his Hethrun mistress (a lay-priestess he visits on the Eldermouth east wall) will be exposed.

**Key relationships:** Lady Isolde (his charge, his project, his asset). His own brother Otello (his patron and judge). His Hethrun mistress (a woman called **Sister Aelfthryth**, an Old Faith lay-priestess who has been carefully cultivating him for two years; she is feeding information to Gythwin's network and Cassian does not know). Aurelio Maron (his rival). The Stonebay creditor (his vulnerability).

**PC hooks:** Cassian will hire mercenaries for "compound security" and for "private errands" that are usually low-risk. The Bone-Sayer leverage operation is approaching and will surface during the campaign; a PC who notices Cassian's gambling sweats or who picks up the threads from the Eldermouth night-quarter could be entangled. Aurelio Maron will pay well for honest information about Cassian's actual position; Cassian himself will pay better for honest information about Aurelio's.

### Brother Eadwig the Pious

**Forty. Court chaplain. Severin's appointee, reports weekly.**

**First impression:** A small, neat, careful Codex monk in clean grey wool, hands always folded, an unobtrusive presence at court who is *always* in the room. Severin trained him personally.

**Speech pattern:** Formal pastoral Codex Latin-equivalent rendered in Hethrun-Aurean.

**Verbal tic:** "If it please the Codex — " before disagreement.

**Mannerism:** Carries a small wax tablet for confessional notes; never visible except when he is alone, but always present in his sleeve.

**What he wants:** Hadric brought safely to Codex-blessed adulthood. The court ladies confessing more freely than they currently do. A bishopric, eventually.

**What he fears:** Failing Severin. The Sky-Father priest Aelfwin's increasing presence in Hadric's training. The novice Cynwise's dreams becoming a Codex problem he is somehow blamed for.

**PC hooks:** Eadwig is Severin's eyes at court. A party that befriends him gains information; a party that mistrusts him gains an enemy.

### Brother Aelfwin, Sky-Father priest

**Sixty-eight. Court Sky-Father priest, technically retained for the boy-king's oath-craft training, currently being prevented from doing his work.**

**First impression:** A tall, lean, white-bearded Saxon-Hethrun man in the dark blue and silver of the Sky-Father priesthood. Quiet. Patient. Older than he looks. Carries a oath-witness staff that has been in his family for four generations.

**Speech pattern:** Old Hethrun formal — a register that the court rarely uses anymore. Speaks slowly. Uses old proverbs.

**Verbal tic:** "By the Sky-Father's witness — " when affirming.

**Mannerism:** Holds the oath-witness staff vertically when speaking formally; horizontally when conversing casually. The change of posture is part of the older Hethrun ritual vocabulary.

**What he wants:** To give Hadric the oath-craft training the boy will need to rule. To witness the boy's coronation oaths properly, in the Hethrun tradition his father swore by.

**What he fears:** That the Codex blessing at Cassian Drey's compound four weeks ago will be considered, by Sky-Father tradition, a substantive disqualification of Hadric's later kingship-oath. (He is correct to fear this.)

**PC hooks:** Aelfwin can teach a Sky-Father-respectful PC oath-craft, including the *binding-witness* tradition which has minor mechanical effect at the GM's discretion. He will quietly help a PC who appears trustworthy to gain access to Hadric.

### Master Edorn, royal physician

**Fifty-five. College of Iron-trained, Aldwin's colleague, Hethrun-resident for thirty-five years.**

**First impression:** A small, bald, fastidious man in fine wool, with the College of Iron pin on his cloak. Speaks rapidly and precisely. Has a habit of looking at people the way a scholar looks at a specimen.

**Speech pattern:** Clinical, precise, the College of Iron's habit of preferring exact terms.

**Verbal tic:** "Observably — " before clinical observations.

**Mannerism:** Carries a small leather case of medical instruments at all times; opens and closes it when thinking.

**What he wants:** Hadric kept healthy through the dragon-blood manifestation, which he has not yet diagnosed but has noticed.

**What he fears:** That the manifestation will become medically unmanageable. That his loyalty to the regency conflicts with his duty to Aldwin as a college peer.

**PC hooks:** Edorn is a medical resource (high-tier healing potions, identification of unusual conditions). He will hire a party to escort him to specific Greenways herb-gatherers.

## The eorls of the Witan

### Eorl Ranulf of Stonebay

**Forty-nine. Pretender to the throne, head of the contesting faction.**

**First impression:** A tall, broad, golden-bearded Norse-Hethrun man in fine wool dyed the sea-blue of Stonebay, an old iron ring on his left hand (his grandfather's), a sword at his belt always. Carries himself like a war-chief — which he was, before he was an eorl. Has a deep, careful voice and unhurried gestures. Smiles often and the smiles are not warm.

**Speech pattern:** Norse-Hethrun formal court speech, beautifully done. Uses the older Hethrun forms (the formal indirection of the high court) more correctly than the regency does, a deliberate signal. Quotes the older Hethrun law-codes when relevant.

**Verbal tic:** "The kingdom — " (as a way of beginning sentences that he means to be heard as policy rather than personal opinion).

**Mannerism:** When considering a hard question, places his left hand (with the iron ring) flat on the table or the chair-arm and looks at it. The gesture is calculated to draw attention to the ring; the ring is his claim's emblem.

**What he wants:** The Hethrun throne. Stonebay's autonomy preserved within the kingdom. The Cloven Strand jarldom as a sworn-brother ally. The Codex Church's political weight reduced. His own grandsons inheriting from him.

**What he fears:** That his run of luck will turn and Aelfric's three-years-ago death will be traced to him. That Hadric's dragon-blood manifestation will be unmistakable. That Hadolf One-Hand will reveal the Stridvik engine to Cyneberht and force a settlement that excludes Ranulf. That Branwald is using him.

**Key relationships:** Hadolf One-Hand (sworn-brother, aging alliance). Branwald (advisor, not entirely trusted). Edrid Bone-Sayer (intelligence head, mutually useful, mutually wary). His own wife **Lady Aedwyn**, sixty years old, Saxon-Hethrun, who has not been informed of half her husband's plans. His son **Cedric of Stonebay** at court (not his actual son; his foster-son and Hadric's companion; the relationship is more careful than it appears).

**PC hooks:** Ranulf will engage a competent party with professional courtesy. He pays well for legitimate work. He will sound out a party for political alignment without forcing the question. If the players accept his hospitality at Stonebay Hall, they are entering a network that includes Branwald, the Bone-Sayers, and Hadolf's couriers; departure becomes more complicated than arrival.

### Eorl Cuthbert of Brackenford

**Forty-six. The wildcard. Genuinely undecided.**

**First impression:** A medium-built old-Saxon-Hethrun man in plain good wool, dark-bearded, with the watchful look of a man who has been listening to too many people for too long. Carries himself with the awkward bearing of someone uncomfortable in court dress; relaxes visibly into rougher clothes when he can.

**Speech pattern:** Old-Saxon-Hethrun. Direct in style, hesitant in substance. Speaks more slowly than his rank requires.

**Verbal tic:** "I — I would think — " (a doubled "I" that signals his actual uncertainty).

**Mannerism:** Looks at his hands when thinking. Has a habit of rubbing his right thumb across his left palm, a gesture his father did, a gesture he does not know he makes.

**What he wants:** To do the right thing for Hethrun. To not be a fool. To protect Mildryd from whatever consequences are gathering around her covert work.

**What he fears:** Being permanently remembered as the deciding vote. Mildryd being exposed. Count Edmund (his rival) getting what he wants. Discovering, too late, that he has made the wrong choice.

**Key relationships:** Mildryd (his wife; his most trusted counsel; he loves her and does not entirely understand her). Aldwin the Younger (his reeve; his ballast). Sister Aelfwyn (his quiet ally; he has not visited Hollowford in two years because he doesn't want to be observed with her).

**PC hooks:** Cuthbert is the most likely eorl to ask a party for a delicate errand. He distrusts the obvious channels and will *test* a party first — a small job, well-done, builds trust. The Aureothan chamber under the Drowning Quarter is the largest hook he holds.

### Eorlswife Mildryd of Brackenford

**Forty-four. Cuthbert's wife. Covert Veiled Mother devotee. Currently hiding the Tihatchee emissary.**

**First impression:** A small, sharp-featured Saxon-Hethrun woman in fine dark wool, hair plaited and pinned in the older Saxon fashion, with steady grey eyes that take in everything in the room within the first thirty seconds. Carries herself with the precise care of someone who has been performing a role for fifteen years.

**Speech pattern:** Court Saxon-Hethrun with a quieter old-Saxon undertone when relaxed.

**Verbal tic:** "And of course — " (when introducing a polite observation that is actually a sharp one).

**Mannerism:** Carries embroidery in her travel bag and works on it during long conversations; the embroidery contains older Veiled Mother symbols disguised as decorative knotwork, which she would recognize and which a knowledgeable observer might also.

**What she wants:** The Old Faith network to weather the Codex pressure. The Tihatchee emissary delivered safely. Her husband's vote committed sensibly when the moment comes. To live to see her grandchildren raised in a quieter time.

**What she fears:** The Tihatchee emissary being discovered before she can move him. Brother Aelfric the Hard noticing too much. Her own slowness in old age.

**Key relationships:** Cuthbert (her husband, whom she has been guiding for twenty years). Gythwin the Cunning (her teacher). Sister Halgrid the Lay in Eldermouth (her closest network peer). Mother Ealhswith of Cynesburh (her Wessmark correspondent).

**PC hooks:** If the party is brought to her attention positively (through Cuthbert, through Gythwin, through Sister Aelfwyn), she will engage. The Tihatchee emissary is the central hook in Brackenford.

### Eorl Hagan of Mossdrowen

**Fifty-three. The dutiful, the impressionable.**

**First impression:** A weathered, sturdy, plain Norse-Hethrun man in good plain wool, with the bearing of a fyrd-captain promoted past his comfort zone. Greying brown beard, a old battle-scar across his right forearm, hands that are still strong. Smiles slowly and means it.

**Speech pattern:** Plain Hethrun, soldier's idiom. Short sentences. Says less than he thinks.

**Verbal tic:** "Reckon so." (Often the entire response.)

**Mannerism:** Rests his right hand on the hilt of his belt-seax when uncomfortable, not aggressively, just a steadying gesture.

**What he wants:** To watch the wood-road verge competently. To not have to make complicated decisions. For someone he trusts to tell him what to do.

**What he fears:** Letting down his father's memory. Being thought a fool by the other eorls. Captain Eadwine retiring before a replacement is trained.

**Key relationships:** Captain Eadwine the Just (his administrator, his real bulwark). Asgrim Lapsed-Tongue (he is fond of the old skald; visits the academy regularly). Aelis (Hagan grew up in the hold and remembers Aelis arriving when he was thirteen; he respects him without quite trusting him).

**PC hooks:** Hagan is straightforward to engage. He pays for hold-defense work, scout work, and legitimate jobs that align with his charter. He is not a sophisticated political player; players who treat him as one will be misjudging him.

### Eorl Berthwald of the North Coast

**Sixty-seven. Ill. Western fyrd-lands. Attends infrequently.**

**First impression:** A frail, white-bearded Norse-Hethrun man with the lined face of a sailor who has spent fifty years in the spray. Walks with a stick. Eyes still bright. The first impression most courtiers have of him is *imminent succession*: who will follow him.

**Speech pattern:** Plain Hethrun with the western coastal Norse cadence.

**Verbal tic:** "Mm." (Pause.) "Mm." (His way of buying thinking time.)

**Mannerism:** Holds his stick across his lap when seated.

**What he wants:** A peaceful succession in his eorldom (his son **Wulfric the Younger** is the expected heir and is genuinely capable). A coronation that he lives to see. To die at home at Sandhaven.

**What he fears:** Dying before the coronation. The sea-thing (he has heard its devotees on the coast for forty years and has never quite forgotten the sound). His son being passed over by the next regime if the succession goes badly.

**PC hooks:** Berthwald will support a party that has been recommended by Old Eorland (the Stormwatch hermit is on his fyrd-lands; the two have an old acquaintance). The North Coast (Sealhalls expedition, the burnt villages, the sea-thing pact-holders) is largely his territory.

### Eorl Hereward of the Harrowmark

**Fifty-eight. The Heath frontier-warden. Neglected, and bitter about it.**

**First impression:** A hard, grey, weather-scoured Hethrun man in plain iron and undyed wool, with the spare build and watchful stillness of someone who has spent his life out of doors on a dangerous frontier. No court polish at all, and no wish for any. The first thing most courtiers feel from him is a cold, contained anger they cannot place.

**Speech pattern:** Plain blunt Hethrun in the older Heath accent. Says hard things plainly and short. Will not flatter.

**Verbal tic:** "When the lowland remembers we exist —" (a bitter conditional he uses to open any complaint about the crown).

**Mannerism:** Glances north — toward the moor — when a conversation troubles him, an old frontier habit of checking the dangerous horizon.

**What he wants:** Real support for the barrow-watch — coin, spears, recognition. The Heath held. The realm to admit, before it is too late, that the Harrowmark is the realm's shield and not its forgotten corner.

**What he fears:** The deep barrows opening on his watch. The lowland's neglect proving fatal. Being proved right too late to matter.

**Key relationships:** Gythwin the Cunning (he defers to her without question on the Heath's old matters). Count Wulfhere of Wardmoor (an old comrade on the same frontier). ### Eorl Ketil of the Fells

## The Codex bishopric

### Father Severin, Bishop of Eldermouth

### Gythwin the Cunning

## The High Kingship

### Cyneberht Aelfricsson, High King of the Marchlands

**Forty-nine. Nineteen years on the seat. The Unexpected Talent.**
