# The Iron Strait Isles — Region Profile

## Essence

The Iron Strait Isles are the scatter of islands strung across the water between Hethrun's eastern coast and the Ostmark shore — the offshore ground that `eorldom_stonebay.md` names only in passing as "the offshore islands" that breed Norse ne'er-do-wells and raider boats. This region gives them substance. The Isles are a maritime borderland: claimed loosely by Hethrun, worked hard by the Cloven Strand jarldom, watched from Ostmark, and lived on by a sea-blooded island people, the Strandfolk, who answer to none of the three. The Wide Eye lies in the deep channel beneath them. For a Hethrun party the Isles are an accessible tier-1-to-2 region — a few days' sail from Stonebay — offering raider havens, ruined Aureothan works, an insular community with its own law, and the sea-thing's reach made local.

## Geography and terrain

The Isles are perhaps forty islands and a great many more rocks, scattered over two days' sailing of the Iron Strait. Most are small — a single fishing-hamlet, a sheep-cropped headland, a cove. A handful are large enough to hold real communities. The water between them is the danger: the Strait runs strong and cold, the channels shift, fog comes without warning, and the deep central channel — the **Eye-Deep** — is avoided by every pilot who knows the water, for reasons the maps do not give.

The Isles' position makes them matter out of all proportion to their size. They sit astride the Stonebay–Ostmark trade-water; whoever holds them taxes or raids the Strait. Hethrun claims them and cannot patrol them; the Cloven Strand jarldom under Hadolf One-Hand uses them as raider-havens and all but governs the outer scatter; Ostmark watches from across the water and occasionally lands. The Strandfolk, who have lived here longest, treat all three as weather.

## Wilderness tag: Broken Infrastructure

The largest island, **Anchorholm**, holds the bones of an Aureothan naval station — the empire's forward base for the Iron Strait, three centuries abandoned. A great stone mole, a silted basin that was a war-harbor, a collapsed arsenal, and a signal-tower still half-standing. The Aureothans built to last and built more than the Isles have ever needed since; the Strandfolk live in the station's lee, quarry its stone, and shelter in its lower vaults, and have never had the numbers or the cause to clear its deeper works. Parts of the station are flooded, parts are sound, and at least one sealed vault below the arsenal has never been opened.

**Manifests as:**
- A medium dungeon (tier 1-2) — the Aureothan naval works: flooded galleries, the arsenal's collapse, the unopened lower vault, and whatever three centuries have moved into the dark.
- A strategic prize — a power that repaired Anchorholm's harbor and mole would command the Iron Strait's trade-water; Cassian Drey, Hadolf's jarldom, and Stonebay's Council all know it.
- A salvage economy — Aureothan naval fittings, worked stone, and the occasional intact device draw wreck-divers and scavengers, and the trade in them is half the Strandfolk's livelihood.

## Wilderness tag: Demihuman Community

The Isles' oldest people are the **Strandfolk** — an island folk descended, the verge of legend says, from a pact made between shipwrecked Norse and something of the sea, generations before Hethrun's founding. They are visibly not quite mainland-human: pale, broad, with a grey cast to the eye and a webbing to the hand, long-lunged and cold-blooded in the water. They live across a dozen of the larger islands in small steadings, govern themselves by an island-moot, keep an old faith that is neither Codex nor the Marchland trio, and want, above all, to be left alone by Hethrun, Cloven Strand, and Ostmark alike. They are not the Wide Eye's cult — the distinction matters to them more than to anyone else — but they live in its water, and their old pact and the sea-thing's presence are tangled in ways the Strandfolk themselves no longer fully untangle.

**Manifests as:**
- A genuine community with its own law — the island-moot, its customs, and its deep wariness of all three claimant powers; a party that respects the moot gains pilots, shelter, and the only reliable charts of the Strait, and a party that does not gets fog, silence, and worse.
- A people caught between — the Strandfolk are pressured by Cloven Strand raiders, courted by Ostmark, and nominally Hethrun's subjects, and would ally with whoever credibly offered to leave them alone.
- A standing question about the sea-thing — the Strandfolk's old pact is not the Wide Eye's cult, but the line between them is thin, contested among the Strandfolk, and a slow source of horror (see `bestiary.md`, Group 3A; `factions.md`).

## Notable sites

- **Anchorholm.** The Aureothan naval station and the largest Strandfolk settlement. *Tier 1-2 dungeon; the region's strategic heart.*
- **The Eye-Deep.** The deep central channel every pilot avoids; the Wide Eye lies beneath it. *Tier 3+ hazard water; not a destination, a thing to route around.*
- **The Moot-Holm.** A small, treeless island where the Strandfolk island-moot gathers; neutral ground, where even feuding steadings keep the peace. *Parley site.*
- **Raider-coves of the outer scatter.** A dozen sheltered inlets among the small islands where Cloven Strand crews beach, winter, and divide loot. *Tier 1-2; the Reavers' home water — see `bestiary.md`, Group 3B.*
- **Cliftholm.** A small island abandoned within living memory — its hamlet emptied, no one will say by what. *Investigation/horror site; a Sea-Touched matter.*

## Active encounter themes

- Cloven Strand raider boats (the Reavers' home waters; the Isles are where raids are crewed and divided)
- Strandfolk pilots and steaders (wary, capable, the key to safe passage — a roleplay encounter before any island is reached)
- Wreck-divers and salvagers (working Anchorholm and the rocks; rivals, informants, or desperate men)
- Sea-Touched manifestations (`bestiary.md`, Group 3A — the Wide Eye's reach is closer here than anywhere on the mainland)
- Ostmark scouting craft (across the Strait; the Burning One's interest in the Isles is not idle)
- Fog and shifting channels (a recurring non-combat hazard; the Strait drowns the unguided)

## Place in the campaign

The Iron Strait Isles are an accessible region — a short sail from Stonebay, tier-1-to-2, and reachable in the campaign's first act. They open a maritime mode of play the mainland does not: ships, raiders, salvage, and an island people with their own law. Three established threads run here. The **Reavers** burning the North Coast crew, winter, and divide their loot in the outer scatter, so the raider mystery of `eorldom_northcoast.md` resolves on the Isles. **Anchorholm** is a strategic prize that draws Cassian Drey, Hadolf's jarldom, and the Council of Twelve into competition the players can exploit. And the **Strandfolk** are a community to win or to wrong — the region's moral center, and the gatekeepers of the only safe water through the Strait.
