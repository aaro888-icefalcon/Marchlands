# Eorldom: Mossdrowen

The eorldom of Mossdrowen — the Greatwood verge, held by Eorl Hagan, the weak hand. This file covers Mossdrowen as a place: the verge-hold seat, Hagan's court, the four thane houses, the wood-verge lands, and the local hooks. The realm frame and the marriage/feud web are in `realm_titles.md`; Hagan as a Coronation actor is in `court_hethrun.md`; the high-tier elven hook is in `active_threats.md` and the faction files.

## The Mossdrowen eorldom — essence

Mossdrowen is the eorldom of the Greatwood verge — Hethrun's westernmost permanent settlement, a wood-warding hold and a famous skald-academy in one small town, held by an honest, limited eorl out of his depth at everything but watching the wood. Four thane houses hold its thanelands, and the real power is not in the eorl's hand at all — it is split between the thane who quietly runs the eorldom, the prodigy every faction wants to claim, the thane bound to something in the Greatwood, and the thane already sold to Ranulf. Mossdrowen is also where the campaign's highest hook waits: the elves come down the wood-roads here.

## Mossdrowen — the seat

Mossdrowen is a fortified hold of roughly 8,000 on the Greatwood verge — the smallest of the eorl-seats, but stone-walled and well kept, with a mounted force out of all proportion to its size and a working military discipline the larger Hethrun towns conspicuously lack. It exists to watch the verge, and has done so conscientiously for fifteen years. Two pressures define it.

**Incompetent Leaders.** Eorl Hagan is competent at exactly one thing — wood-warding the verge — and by his own admission not much good at anything else. He cannot read; he cannot do basic arithmetic and is cheated by his own reeves; he has refused four marriage offers because he cannot negotiate the contracts and is too proud to delegate them. His Witan votes are reliable only in that he does what the last person he respected told him to — which means the order in which his visitors arrive can flip the eorldom's alignment. The hold's mounted force, by contrast, is genuinely well-drilled: that part Hagan can do, and his retainers love him for training and fighting alongside them.

**Cultural Center.** Against all expectation, Mossdrowen has become a Marchland-wide centre for skaldic verse and the old martial-poetry tradition. Forty years ago the renowned Wessmark skald **Asgrim Lapsed-Tongue** retired here, founded a small academy in the hold's outbuildings, and built a tradition; his apprentices have since spread across the Marchlands. The academy graduates some three skalds a year, and two-thirds of the practising senior skalds of Hethrun and Wessmark — Sigevard the Silver-Tongue among them — trained under Asgrim.

## Mossdrowen — quarters and notable places

The Hold is stone-walled with an inner keep and outer bailey, two centuries old, built for the verge's threat profile — small mounted raids, not siege. Asgrim's Academy is three timber buildings inside the outer wall, twelve students in residence, holding a small but genuinely valuable library of skaldic and martial-poetry manuscripts, several existing nowhere else; every autumn its senior alumni return for a three-day gathering and contest. The chapel is a modest Codex chapel, currently lodging Severin's deacon-emissary. The Old Faith shrine stands outside the walls at a stream-crossing in the verge, kept by local women — one of the practices the deacon is quietly cataloguing. The western wood-road gates open onto the wood-roads where the elves have lately been seen.

## Mossdrowen — significant locals

Full NPC detail is in `npcs.md`. Resident in the eorldom: Eorl Hagan, dutiful and limited, who holds the verge well and little else. Captain Eadwine the Just, captain of the retainers and de facto administrator of the eorldom — genuinely competent, old, tired, considering retirement with no replacement trained. Asgrim Lapsed-Tongue, eighty-three, the senior skald of the Marchlands, who will not say why he left Wessmark forty years ago. Aelis the Half-Elven Hermit, granted asylum forty years past by Hagan's grandfather, living in a cot in the verge proper, who knows far more than he tells and visits Asgrim quietly once a year. Wulfric, Hagan's chief reeve and an embezzler of two years' standing, watched these eight months by Captain Eadwine. Brother Tirelius, Severin's deacon-emissary, three months into a "pastoral visit" spent studying — and listing — the local Old Faith.

## Eorl Hagan's court

Mossdrowen Hall runs on two faults.

**Inadequate Tools.** The court cannot do its job with what it has. Captain Eadwine the Just, who has administered the eorldom thirty years, is sixty and tired with no trained successor; the fyrd-rolls are out of date; the reeves are too few, and one of them embezzles. Hagan knows the court is failing and does not know how to mend it — a fyrd-captain promoted past his competence, and feeling it daily.

**Outside Debts.** Mossdrowen also carries debt. Asgrim's Academy is costly, the eorldom is not wealthy, and Hagan — unwilling to raise food-rents on people he genuinely cares for — has borrowed. The debt is held by House Castermere of Stonebay. Hagan does not grasp what this means: Ranulf's eorldom now holds an economic lever over his, callable whenever the Coronation crisis makes it useful. The household around all this: Captain Eadwine, aging and indispensable; Hagan's wife and grown children, capable and ordinary; and Asgrim, a court ornament Hagan is proud of and does not understand.

## House Oxenfell — Mossdrowen thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Oxenfell, a strong hall near Mossdrowen; a brown ox-head.
**Head:** Thane Frithowald of Oxenfell — a capable, modest-seeming, genuinely industrious man of fifty, by some distance the most competent person in the eorldom.
**Character — Grand Plan.** Because Hagan is weak, Oxenfell has filled the vacuum. Across fifteen years Frithowald's kin and clients have become Mossdrowen's reeves, stewards, fyrd-captains, hall-officers; the eorldom functions because Oxenfell runs it. The plan is not a coup but patience — to make Oxenfell so wholly indispensable that when Hagan's line falters, the hall-moot turns to Oxenfell as the obvious, already-ruling choice.
**Wants:** the Mossdrowen eorldom — eventually, inevitably, without ever drawing a sword for it.
**Secret:** the plan depends on Mossdrowen staying quiet and stable; war, the calling of the Castermere debt, or a sharp crisis would expose how much Oxenfell has taken and frighten the other thanes into opposing it.
**Coronation lean:** regency, for the stability the plan needs; Frithowald speaks for caution in the hall-moot, and Hagan agrees with the last competent voice to reach him.
**Ties:** Oxenfell's people staff Hagan's administration; a quiet rivalry with House Greldon, which has seen the plan and resents it.
**Hook:** Frithowald is no villain — he keeps Mossdrowen alive — but he is an ambition. A party that helps the court without going through Oxenfell finds him subtly obstructive; a party that works through him advances his plan.

## House Briarmere — Mossdrowen thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Briarmere, a modest hall in the Mossdrowen hinterland; a sprig of briar-rose.
**Head:** Thane Cuthwine of Briarmere — an ordinary, kindly, slightly bewildered man of fifty, proud beyond words of a child he does not entirely understand.
**Character — Unexpected Talent.** Small, unremarkable Briarmere has produced, against every expectation, a prodigy: Cuthwine's daughter **Aethelflaed of Briarmere**, seventeen, a skald-scholar of a brilliance Asgrim's Academy sees once a generation — a voice, a memory, and a political mind far beyond her house's standing. Asgrim himself is cultivating her, and everyone who meets her understands she will matter.
**Wants:** Cuthwine wants his daughter safe and well-married; Aethelflaed wants to be allowed to become what she could be.
**Secret:** Aethelflaed is a marriage prize, a future Witan voice, and a piece every faction wants — and her house has no power to protect her from being used.
**Coronation lean:** uncommitted, courted from all sides; whoever secures Aethelflaed's loyalty or her marriage gains a generational asset.
**Ties:** Aethelflaed studies under Asgrim Lapsed-Tongue; Sigevard the Silver-Tongue has heard of her and is interested.
**Hook:** Aethelflaed is an NPC worth developing fully — ally, ward to protect, rival, or, if the wrong faction reaches her first, opponent. The race to claim her is itself an intrigue arc.

## House Hollantun — Mossdrowen thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Hollantun, a hall at the wood-road verge against the Greatwood; a green oak-leaf.
**Head:** Thane Leofric of Hollantun — a frightened, evasive man of forty-five who has not slept properly in a year.
**Character — In Over Their Head.** Three years ago, with the wood-road threatened and Hagan's fyrd too thin to hold it, Leofric made a bargain with something in the Greatwood to keep his lands and the road safe. It worked — and it bound Hollantun to terms Leofric did not fully understand and cannot meet, and the thing in the wood is beginning to ask for what it is owed. He is far past what he can manage, and too ashamed and frightened to tell anyone.
**Wants:** out of the bargain, before it takes what it came for; for no one to learn what he did.
**Secret:** the bargain — its terms, its counterparty (a Smokeless Throne matter; see `factions.md`), and the price now coming due.
**Coronation lean:** barely engaged — Leofric has no attention to spare for the throne, and Hollantun's hall-moot vote drifts wherever it is pushed.
**Ties:** bound by bargain to a Greatwood power; Captain Saerwen of the Wardens has begun to suspect something is wrong at Hollantun.
**Hook:** pressed or reassured, Leofric will beg a competent party to get him out of the bargain — a tier-1-to-2 entry into the Greatwood and Silvering threads, and a study in how one small honest man's desperate choice metastasises.

## House Greldon — Mossdrowen thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Greldon, a hall on the Stonebay-facing edge of Mossdrowen; a black raven.
**Head:** Thane Eadweard of Greldon — a discontented, clever, watchful man of forty-two who has concluded that Hagan is a fool and Mossdrowen a sinking ship.
**Character — Impending Treachery.** Eadweard means to land on the winning side before Mossdrowen goes under. He has been in quiet correspondence with Ranulf's people through the Bone-Sayers, and has all but agreed to deliver Greldon's hall-moot voice, its fyrd, and what influence he can muster to Ranulf — in exchange for Ranulf's favour when the crown changes hands. The betrayal is arranged; it waits only for the Witan vote to be worth springing.
**Wants:** to abandon Hagan profitably and stand high in Ranulf's new order.
**Secret:** the correspondence with Ranulf's people. Letters exist; if they surfaced, Eadweard would be a traitor in open hall-moot, and Hagan — pushed for once past his patience — might be forced to act.
**Coronation lean:** secretly Ranulf; publicly a loyal, if grumbling, Mossdrowen thane.
**Ties:** secret correspondent of Ranulf via the Bone-Sayers; quiet rival of House Oxenfell, whose grand plan Eadweard has seen through.
**Hook:** the Greldon letters are a findable, provable thing — in a Bone-Sayer's keeping or a courier's satchel. Recovering them exposes a traitor and arms the regency; intercepting them lets the holder turn Eadweard into a double agent (see `realm_titles.md`).

## Minor settlements of the eorldom

Seeded for texture; each is a name, community tags, and a hook.

**Wyrmgale** — hamlet, *Tradition-Bound · Sealed Lips.* The last wood-road hamlet before the verge proper; its folk leave the old offerings at the wood's edge and do not discuss why, and they know the safe paths a stranger does not.

**Asgrimsholt** — village, *Friendly Locals · Hidden Lore.* The village grown up below the Hold to serve the academy — unusually literate, fond of visitors who can carry a tune or a tale, and quietly proud of the manuscripts up at Asgrim's.

**Bryntoft** — hamlet, *Outpost · Oppressed.* A stockaded farmstead-cluster on the verge's southern edge, half-forgotten by Hagan's thin fyrd, paying its food-rent to a Greldon under-reeve and wondering why no one answers when raids come down the wood.

## Minor courts and lesser halls

**Asgrim's Academy** — court tag *Cultivated Talent.* The skald-academy functions as a court of its own — Asgrim makes and unmakes reputations, his alumni network reaches every Marchland court, and his blessing on a young skald (or a young thane) carries weight no hall-moot can match.

**Captain Eadwine's reeve-table** — court tag *Caretaker Regime.* The aging captain and the few honest reeves are, in practice, the court that governs Mossdrowen day to day — competent, overstretched, and quietly desperate for the trained successor that does not exist.

## The eorldom's lands — the Greatwood verge

Mossdrowen's lands are the western interior climbing from the Greenways toward the Greatwood proper — old-growth forest at the edge of elven territory, stockaded farmsteads, and the few wood-roads threading east toward Hethrun-proper. Mossdrowen is the only town; all else is hamlet-scale. Notable sites:

**The Wardens' Camp** — a seasonal camp deep in the wood used by the Greatwood Wardens, a seven-strong folk-heroic company under Captain Saerwen the Long-Sighted that works the Silvering verge with quiet elven approval. They camp here in two-week stretches between commissions, take on associate-companies for hard contracts, and know what the wood-road elves are doing. *Ally and employer site.*

**The Elvenmeet** — an ancient grove half a day past the last Mossdrowen hamlet, where the wood-road elves were sometimes seen even before the recent appearances; it holds a single carved stone older than Aureoth, its markings readable only in part, even by Saerwen's half-elven Warden. Leaving the right offering can draw an elven response within days. The Codex declared the site forbidden in the third century; everyone ignores the ban. *Diplomatic-contact and clue site.*

**Aelis's Cot** — half a day's ride west, in the verge proper, the dwelling of the half-elven hermit Aelis. He is not always there; he knows more than he tells. *Single-NPC visit-site, significant if engaged.*

**The Wolfen Crossing** — a natural ford-and-ridge the elves are known to use, currently watched by Hagan's mounted scouts. (Bestiary: the Verge-Beasts, `bestiary.md` Group 6A.)

## Adventure hooks

- **The elven contact** (tier 2–3, the campaign's central high hook) — Captain Saerwen of the Greatwood Wardens may approach a competent party with the elves' need for an agent in the Tihatchee dragon-cult matter. This is the highest hook in the campaign and it starts at Mossdrowen.
- **Aelis's quiet request** (tier 1–2) — the half-elven hermit will know a party has come within days; he will approach them indirectly about an old debt of his own he wishes to settle before he goes home.
- **The reeve** (tier 1) — Captain Eadwine would welcome a discreet investigation of Wulfric's two years of embezzlement, handled cleanly and without scandal.
- **Brother Tirelius** (tier 1) — Severin's emissary is collecting the names of Old Faith shrine-goers; whatever a party's Codex alignment, his report going south will reshape Mossdrowen's politics, and a party that cares can intervene.
- **Hollantun's bargain** (tier 1–2) — Thane Leofric, if reached, will beg help out of his Greatwood bargain before the price comes fully due.

## At a glance

Mossdrowen is the weak-hand eorldom — Hagan's Witan vote belongs to whoever last reached him, which makes the eorldom a contest of access, not persuasion. Real power sits below the eorl: Oxenfell quietly runs it, Greldon is quietly sold, Hollantun is quietly bound to the wood, and Briarmere holds the prize everyone wants. Crisis stance: nominally regency (Oxenfell's caution, Cuthbert's recent visit), but a single well-timed visit could flip it. Mossdrowen is also the gateway to the campaign's largest threads — the elves, the Greatwood, the Tihatchee dragon-cult. Bestiary groups operating here: the Verge-Beasts (6A) in the wood, the Burning One's Cells (A2), and the Arrival-Hunters (A1). For the realm frame and the web see `realm_titles.md`; for the elven and dragon-cult arcs see `active_threats.md` and the faction files.
