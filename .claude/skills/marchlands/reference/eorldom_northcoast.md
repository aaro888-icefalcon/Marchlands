# Eorldom: the North Coast

The eorldom of the North Coast — the western fjord-coast, held by the dying Eorl Berthwald. This file covers the North Coast as a place: the fjord-head seat of Sandhaven, Berthwald's failing court, the four thane houses, the storm-coast lands, and the local hooks. The realm frame and the marriage/feud web are in `realm_titles.md`; the sea-thing and barrow threads are in `active_threats.md` and the faction files.

## The North Coast eorldom — essence

The North Coast is Hethrun's western eorldom — broken fjords, stone beaches, raider-haunted water, and the ruins of a drowned imperial harbour. It is the realm's poorest and grimmest eorldom, and its whole politics turn on one question: Eorl Berthwald is dying, and no one yet knows who follows him. Four thane houses hold its thanelands, and the contest for the succession runs straight through them — most sharply through House Fenwick, split into two branches that back the two rival heirs. Beneath the politics runs something older and worse: the Iron Strait sea-thing, the Wide Eye, has pact-holders on this coast, and the wards that hold it back are fraying.

## Sandhaven — the seat

Sandhaven is a fjord-head burh of roughly 2,500 at the mouth of the North Coast's deepest inlet — the eorl's seat, a fishing-and-fyrd town of timber halls and stone jetties under a palisade, with none of Eldermouth's size or Stonebay's wealth. It is a working town: net-lofts, fish-curing yards, a fyrd-armory, a Sky-Father temple older and plainer than any Codex church on the coast. Two pressures define it.

**Border Town.** Sandhaven lives on a raid-frontier. The North Coast has no near enemy it can name, yet boats come — two villages burned in the last month alone, the raiders unidentified, Stonebay loudly denying involvement. Sandhaven keeps its fyrd mustered more often than a town its size should need to, its beacon-watch is permanent, and its people have the wary, weather-hard temper of folk who have learned the sea brings strangers and strangers bring fire.

**Sealed Lips.** The coast does not talk to outsiders. Sandhaven's fishermen know which bays to avoid and will not say why; they know the names of things in the water and will not speak them to a stranger; they know more about the burned villages than they have told the eorl's reeves. The silence is partly the ordinary closeness of a poor coast and partly something worse — a coast-wide refusal to look directly at the thing the Wide Eye's pact-holders feed.

## Sandhaven — quarters and notable places

Berthwald's Hall stands at the fjord-head — an old timber long-hall, well-built but plainly kept, the eorl's quarters and the hearth-hall and, lately, a sick-room at its heart. The Sky-Father Temple is the oldest building in the burh, a steep-roofed hall of black timber where the coast's conservative Old Faith keeps the storm-rites the Norse-fused liturgy elsewhere has half-forgotten. The Net-Lofts and curing-yards crowd the waterfront. The Beacon-Tower on the headland above the burh is the inland end of the North Coast beacon-chain that House Beaconfell keeps. The Strand-Market is small, irregular, and the place a traveller learns — if the coast will let them learn anything — what has been coming ashore.

## Sandhaven — significant locals

Full NPC detail is in `npcs.md`. Resident in the eorldom: Eorl Berthwald, old and dying, his strength coming and going. Wulfric the Younger, his son and expected heir — genuinely capable, anxious, already half-ruling and not yet trusted to rule. The eorlswife, Berthwald's wife and, in his illness, his secretary and the keeper of which orders carry his true will. Seeded local figures: Reeve Hunlaf, the Sandhaven harbour-reeve, an honest tired man trying to keep the burh fed while two half-rulers contradict him; Mother Saewynn, the senior Sky-Father priestess of the coast, who knows the storm-rites and the headland wards and distrusts every faith but her own; and Skipper Ulf, a Sandhaven fishing-captain who has seen what gathers in the wrong bays and has decided, after the burned villages, that he might finally tell the right outsider.

## Eorl Berthwald's court

Berthwald's Hall runs on two faults.

**Capricious Orders.** Sandhaven issues commands that contradict each other from week to week. Berthwald is dying and his strength comes and goes; on his strong days he rules, on his weak days Wulfric the Younger rules in his name, and the two do not always agree — so the eorldom's reeves and captains never know which order stands. The capriciousness is not malice but a court with two half-rulers and no clear one.

**Diplomatic Demands.** The court is besieged from outside. The High King wants a stable, uncontested North Coast succession and presses for it through Wulfgar of Cynesburh. The Cloven Strand jarldom watches the headlands with coastal hunger. And the Wide Eye's pact-holders make their own quiet, unspeakable demands of the houses that propitiate it. Sandhaven cannot satisfy all of them and is too weak to refuse any of them cleanly. The household: Berthwald, dying; the eorlswife-secretary; Wulfric the Younger, the anxious heir; and a court visibly divided between those who serve the father and those who already serve the son.

## House Saltmere — North Coast thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Saltmere, a sea-cliff hall on the outer headland; a black wave.
**Head:** Thane Sigeweard of Saltmere — a grim, taciturn, weather-beaten man of fifty-five who looks at the sea more than he looks at people.
**Character — Concealed Sin.** Saltmere's coast catches well and its waters stay calm in storms that wreck its neighbours, and its luck has held five generations. The luck is a pact: an ancestor bound Saltmere to the Iron Strait sea-thing, the Wide Eye, and the house propitiates it still. The concealed sin is what propitiation costs — and the burned villages along the North Coast are tied to it in ways Sigeweard will not say and can barely live with.
**Wants:** to keep the pact, keep the secret, and be left alone by the wider world.
**Secret:** the pact, its price, and the burned villages; exposure would mean the house's destruction and a Codex or Crown reckoning.
**Coronation lean:** insular and uncommitted — Saltmere wants only to be ignored, and votes in hall-moot for whatever outcome promises the least scrutiny.
**Ties:** bound to the Wide Eye (see `factions.md`); the burned-village hook runs through Saltmere.
**Hook:** the burned villages draw investigators, and the trail runs to Saltmere — coastal horror at thane-house scale, a small grim guilty house and the thing it feeds.

## House Fenwick — North Coast thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Fenwick — two halls, the elder at Fenwick proper, the younger at Fenwick-Nether; crossed grey oars, flown by both branches, which fools no one.
**Heads:** the elder branch under Thane Osferth of Fenwick — solid, traditional, loyal to the eorl's heir. The younger branch under Thane Edith of Fenwick — sharp, aggrieved, certain she was cheated of the senior seat a generation ago.
**Character — Family Alienation.** House Fenwick is split down the middle and the two branches do not speak. The split is old — a contested inheritance two generations back — and the Berthwald succession has reopened it: the elder branch backs Wulfric the Younger; the younger branch backs an alternative claimant, a great-nephew of Berthwald's whom Edith means to make eorl with herself the power behind him. House Fenwick is the North Coast succession crisis in miniature.
**Wants:** elder branch — Wulfric crowned eorl, the house reunited under its rightful senior line. Younger branch — the great-nephew made eorl, the old inheritance-wrong avenged at last.
**Secret:** each branch has gathered, over the years, material that could ruin the other; neither has used it, and the Berthwald succession may force the issue.
**Coronation lean:** both branches are absorbed by their own succession war and lean toward whichever realm-faction will back their candidate — elder regency-ish, younger Ranulf-ish.
**Ties:** the Fenwick split mirrors and feeds the Berthwald succession; the younger branch's candidate is a cadet of Berthwald's own line.
**Hook:** each branch will recruit outside agents to discredit, outmanoeuvre, or dig dirt on the other; a party can be hired by either, or become the swing weight that decides which Fenwick — and which North Coast eorl — prevails.

## House Beaconfell — North Coast thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Beaconfell, a watch-hall on the high northern headland; a gold flame.
**Head:** Thane Cyneweard of Beaconfell — a dutiful, dour, plainspoken man of fifty who has never in his life shirked a charge or sought a reward.
**Character — Inherited Duty.** House Beaconfell holds a hereditary charge older than the eorldom: it keeps the North Coast beacon-chain and maintains the headland wards that, the old rites hold, keep the sea-thing's reach from the inland. The duty is heavy, thankless, and constant, and it has kept Beaconfell honest, poor, and proud for three centuries; Cyneweard carries it without complaint or question.
**Wants:** to keep the duty well; to see the North Coast succession settled lawfully so the duty is not disrupted.
**Secret:** the wards are weakening — the same neglect loosening the Heath barrow-seals is fraying the headland wards. Cyneweard has noticed and does not know whom to tell.
**Coronation lean:** legitimacy and lawful order — effectively the regency and the High King's preference — because the duty needs a stable, lawful realm.
**Ties:** the headland wards connect to the Sealhalls and the sea-thing threads; Cyneweard corresponds with Old Eorland of Stormwatch on the Iron Strait coast; a Beaconfell child is married into House Merrowdene of Stonebay (see `realm_titles.md`).
**Hook:** Beaconfell is the North Coast's most trustworthy house and a natural ally; the weakening wards are an early, quiet warning of the deeper coastal and barrow threats, offered by an honest man who has no one else to tell.

## House Caldfen — North Coast thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Caldfen, an inland North Coast hall; a withered branch.
**Head:** Thane Aelfwaru of Caldfen — an anxious, watchful woman of forty who studies every guest's family tree before she studies their face.
**Character — Chronic Illness.** House Caldfen carries a hereditary affliction in the blood — a slow wasting weakness, the Blight, that has thinned the line five generations and dwindled a once-strong house. The Blight does not always show, but the eorldoms' houses know, and they are wary of marrying into Caldfen's blood. Aelfwaru's whole policy is the search for a clean-blooded marriage to refresh the line before it fails.
**Wants:** a marriage into a strong, healthy, un-Blighted house — at almost any price.
**Secret:** Caldfen's desperation itself. A house that will pay almost anything for a good marriage can be bought with the offer of one; Aelfwaru's hall-moot vote, her small fyrd, and her loyalty are all available to whoever produces a credible match.
**Coronation lean:** for sale — Caldfen will trade its support for a marriage, and both the regency's and Ranulf's brokers could buy it if either thought to.
**Ties:** the Blight is a faint, grounded echo of the realm's hidden-bloodline theme — texture, not a connection to overstate.
**Hook:** Caldfen is a vote and a fyrd for the price of a marriage-broker's success; a party that arranges a match for Aelfwaru, honestly or not, gains a grateful house, and a party that exposes a Blight a match-maker concealed earns a bitter enemy.

## Minor settlements of the eorldom

Seeded for texture; each is a name, community tags, and a hook.

**Skerryholm** — fishing village, *Battle Site · Doomed.* One of the two villages burned in the last month — half its boats gone, its survivors sheltering with kin up-coast, its blackened net-lofts an investigation site for anyone tracing the raiders (and the trail toward Saltmere).

**Cliftaven** — fishing village, *Battle Site · Sealed Lips.* The second burned village — but Cliftaven's survivors are stranger than Skerryholm's: too few bodies were found, and the ones who lived will not say what they saw come out of the water.

**Garrowness** — village, *Tradition-Bound · Honest Folk.* A headland fishing village under one of the coast's three Old Faith shrines, conservative and decent, where Mother Saewynn's rites are kept properly and a traveller who respects them is fed and sheltered without a fee.

## Minor courts and lesser halls

**Wulfric the Younger's following** — court tag *Heir's Shadow Court.* The half of Sandhaven's court that already serves the son — younger reeves, the captains Wulfric has won, the thanes who have read the dying eorl correctly. It governs alongside, and against, Berthwald's own men.

**Mother Saewynn's temple** — court tag *Old Authority.* The Sky-Father temple of the coast is a power in its own right — Saewynn keeps the storm-rites, knows the headland wards, and her word carries weight with the fishing villages that the eorl's reeves cannot command.

## The eorldom's lands — the storm-coast

The North Coast's lands are broken fjords and stone beaches opening to the open sea, raid-prone and poorly patrolled, dotted with fishing villages and headland shrines. Notable sites:

**The Stormhold** — the ruin of an Aureothan weather-control station on a cliff above a silted imperial harbour. The station is partly functional: storms still cluster around it unnaturally, ships still wreck where it once protected them. It has not been entered in three centuries — the last attempt lost six people — and an Aureothan weather-engine presumably waits inside, to be repaired or only contained. *Tier-3 dungeon. If its malfunction worsens, the whole coast becomes unsafe for shipping — a clock.*

**The Sealhalls** — a coastal cave system where seals gather, and where lately the gatherings have become *organised* in disquieting numbers and configurations. *Tier 2–3 mystery/horror site, tied to the Wide Eye.*

**The headland shrines** — three small Old Faith shrines on coastal headlands, each dedicated to a different aspect: storm, catch, drowning. Kept by local fisherfolk; the drowning-shrine in particular draws those with grief or guilt to the sea.

**The twisted fauna** — the offshore waters hold creatures that should not exist: birds with too many eyes, fish that swim against the current, and at night, sometimes, something very large surfacing. The fauna are partial manifestations of the Wide Eye's presence, and their patterns can be read by someone with the right knowledge. (Bestiary: the Tainted Sea-Beasts, `bestiary.md` Group 7A.)

## Adventure hooks

- **The burned villages** (tier 1–2) — Skerryholm and Cliftaven draw investigators; the trail runs, by way of unmarked raider boats, to House Saltmere and the pact it keeps.
- **The Berthwald succession** (tier 1–3) — the dying eorl's heir is contested; the two Fenwick branches will hire agents to dig dirt, and the realm-factions are beginning to take sides through their candidates.
- **The weakening wards** (tier 1–2) — Thane Cyneweard of Beaconfell, finding the headland wards fraying and no one to tell, will quietly confide in a party that seems trustworthy and competent.
- **Skipper Ulf's confession** (tier 1) — a Sandhaven fishing-captain who has seen what gathers in the wrong bays may, after the burnings, finally decide to tell the right outsider what the coast will not say.
- **Caldfen's marriage** (tier 1) — Thane Aelfwaru seeks a clean-blooded match and will reward the broker who finds one; the work invites both honesty and fraud.

## At a glance

The North Coast is the succession eorldom — Berthwald is dying, the heir is contested, and Berthwald's own Witan vote is hostage to that until it resolves. Its four houses run the gamut: a guilty house feeding a sea-thing (Saltmere), a house split into the succession's two sides (Fenwick), the coast's one honest bulwark (Beaconfell), and a desperate house for sale (Caldfen). Crisis stance: frozen until the succession breaks one way or the other. Beneath the politics, the North Coast is the campaign's coastal-horror front — the Wide Eye, the Stormhold, the fraying wards. Bestiary groups operating here: the Tainted Sea-Beasts (7A) offshore, the Strait-Raiders (5A), and the Arrival-Hunters (A1).
