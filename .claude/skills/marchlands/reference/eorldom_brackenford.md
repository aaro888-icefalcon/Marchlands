# Eorldom: Brackenford

The eorldom of Brackenford — the inland heart of Hethrun, held by Eorl Cuthbert, the wildcard of the Coronation. This file covers Brackenford as a place: the inland trade-town seat, Cuthbert's court, the four thane houses, the inland lands, and the local hooks. The realm frame and the marriage/feud web are in `realm_titles.md`; Cuthbert as a Coronation actor is in `court_hethrun.md` and `active_threats.md`.

## The Brackenford eorldom — essence

Brackenford is the eorldom of the inland heart — the river-and-marsh country where Hethrun's southern road meets the western wood-road, the kingdom's most insular ground, held by the realm's least predictable eorl. Four thane houses hold its thanelands, and three of them are not what they appear: an Old Faith house in Codex costume, a bought house, and a house quietly murdering the eorl's heir. Cuthbert himself is the wildcard whose Witan vote no faction can count — and a party that wants the Coronation settled will spend real effort here learning why.

## Brackenford — the seat

Brackenford is an inland trade-town of roughly 4,000 at the river-crossing where the Eldermouth–Hollowford southern road meets the wood-road west to Mossdrowen — no true walls, only palisade and ditch, the river and the bracken-marsh its real defence. It is the kingdom's inland distribution point and Eorl Cuthbert's seat. Two pressures define it.

**Xenophobic Locals.** Brackenford is Hethrun's most insular town. The native population is old Saxon stock, barely touched by Norse settlement, and considers itself the *real* Hethrun — Eldermouth's Norse aristocracy and Stonebay's mercantile cosmopolitanism both treated as recent intrusions. Travellers pay double at inns; non-local merchants must register at Cuthbert's hall and pay a "stranger's fee"; strangers in the wrong quarter after dark are beaten rather than watched. Two inns split the town — the Stranger's Inn, the only one permitted to host outsiders, and the locals-only Hearth-House. Cuthbert will not crack down on the xenophobia: his position depends on local loyalty, and that reluctance is part of why he is a wildcard.

**Sinking City.** Brackenford was built on bog-substrate two centuries ago, and the bog is failing — water tables risen, foundations shifted, buildings collapsed. The west quarter is now the Drowning Quarter, ten blocks settled three to seven feet below their original level, some buildings sunk entirely into the bog. The Lifting — raising the salvageable central buildings on new foundations — is the town's great project and is crippling Cuthbert's treasury.

## Brackenford — quarters and notable places

Cuthbert's Hall is modest by an eorl's standard — a well-kept Saxon long-hall of timber and thatch where Cuthbert and Mildryd live. The Eastern Manor, Cuthbert's late father's old hall, is his secondary residence and Mildryd's preferred ground — and at session zero it is hiding a fugitive. The Drowning Quarter is the abandoned sinking west district, dangerous, still squatted by the desperate — and two months ago a building's collapse there exposed **a sealed Aureothan chamber**, intact and undisturbed, that no one in Brackenford remembers being told of; Cuthbert has posted a single fyrdman to guard it and has sent no word to Eldermouth. The Veiled Mother shrine at the eastern town-edge is older than the town, beautifully kept by Brackenford's women, technically illegal under strict Codex doctrine and quietly tolerated. The river crossing — wooden bridge and stone ford — is the town's founding site, slowly undermined by the same subsidence. The Wagon Yard is where north- and south-bound caravans bivouac, the eorldom's chief commercial node.

## Brackenford — significant locals

Full NPC detail is in `npcs.md`. Resident in the eorldom: Eorl Cuthbert, the wildcard, old Saxon stock and deeply local. Eorlswife Mildryd, far more politically engaged than her husband, a covert Veiled Mother devotee running an Old Faith network — and currently sheltering a fugitive Cuthbert does not know about. Brother Aelfric the Hard, the town's Codex priest, Severin-aligned, disliked, and Severin's most reliable Brackenford informant. Mistress Hild of the Hearth-House, the locals-only innkeeper, hub of all local news and Mildryd's confidante. Aldwin the Younger — no relation to the court wizard — Cuthbert's competent chief reeve, carrying the eorldom while his lord is distracted. The fyrdman Sigewulf, who runs the Stranger's Watch and has a brother in the Hollow-Hill Crew. **Atohi-Hesotka**, the surviving Tihatchee emissary Mildryd is hiding in the Eastern Manor — fevered when he arrived, now recovering, carrying word of the dragon-cult that the Suncatcher of Tihatchee tried to send to the High King.

## Eorl Cuthbert's court

Brackenford Hall runs on two faults.

**Affliction's Mark.** Cuthbert's heir, his son **Wulfnoth**, fourteen, has a wasting illness the physicians cannot name — tiring, weakening, losing flesh across two seasons. Cuthbert's indecision over the Coronation is partly simple grief; he cannot think past the boy. The illness also makes the Brackenford succession an open question every ambitious thane has noticed. What no one at the hall yet knows is that the illness is murder (see House Maerstow).

**Dark Secret.** Brackenford Hall keeps more than one. Mildryd runs a covert Veiled Mother network reaching into the hall itself; she is hiding the Tihatchee emissary in the Eastern Manor; and beneath the Drowning Quarter sits the sealed Aureothan chamber Cuthbert has told almost no one of. The household around all this: Mildryd, his wife and truest counsel; Wulfnoth, the ill heir; Mildgyth, his younger daughter, eleven, fostered with House Wivelham; and Aldwin the Younger, the reeve who is his ballast.

## House Wivelham — Brackenford thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Wivelham, an inland hall near the Wivelfen at the Heath's edge; a white hind.
**Head:** Thane Aelfflaed of Wivelham — a calm, respectable, Codex-attending matron of fifty-five whom all Brackenford takes for the model of proper piety.
**Character — Double Life.** Wivelham's respectability is a costume. Aelfflaed is a senior Veiled Mother devotee and the spine of Mildryd's Old Faith network among the thane houses; the hall hosts the network's quiet meetings, and its place at the Heath's edge keeps it close to Gythwin's people. The whole house lives the double life — children raised in both faiths, the old rites kept in the cellar.
**Wants:** the Old Faith network to survive the Codex pressure; Brackenford kept out of the hard-liners' reach.
**Secret:** the entire double life. Brother Aelfric the Hard suspects Wivelham and is building a case; exposure ends the house and cripples Mildryd's network.
**Coronation lean:** publicly Codex-proper; really regency — the Old Faith wants stability, not Ranulf's war. But the house's true priority is the network, not the throne.
**Ties:** fosters Cuthbert's daughter Mildgyth — the thread by which Mildryd's network reaches the eorl's own family; allied with Gythwin's Heath network.
**Hook:** earn Mildryd's or Gythwin's trust and Wivelham becomes a safe house and information node; come at it through Brother Aelfric's case instead and a party may be the ones to expose — or shield — the house.

## House Thornwic — Brackenford thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Thornwic, a border hall on the disputed frontier between Brackenford and Stonebay; a red thorn-branch.
**Head:** Thane Beorhtric of Thornwic — a grim, spare, unsmiling man of fifty who keeps the memory of every wrong done his house and recites them like a liturgy.
**Character — Bitter Grudge.** Forty years ago a Holmgarth raiding-party out of Stonebay killed Beorhtric's grandfather and three kin in a quarrel over the border meadows; no weregild was ever paid. Beorhtric has organised his whole life around the grievance, and Thornwic's politics are simple — whatever side opposes Holmgarth, Thornwic takes.
**Wants:** vengeance on House Holmgarth; the border meadows restored; the weregild paid, in gold or blood.
**Secret:** the grudge has made Beorhtric predictable — anyone who knows the feud can steer Thornwic by pointing out where Holmgarth stands.
**Coronation lean:** because Holmgarth is hard for Ranulf, Thornwic is hard against — leaning regency for the grudge's sake more than the regency's.
**Ties:** the Holmgarth–Thornwic blood feud, the oldest live feud in the eorldoms (see `realm_titles.md`).
**Hook:** Beorhtric pays well for anything that wounds House Holmgarth — evidence, sabotage, a border incident framed the right way. The Coronation is, to him, mainly the chance to settle a forty-year account.

## House Sedgemoor — Brackenford thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Sedgemoor, a hall within Brackenford town; three green reeds.
**Head:** Thane Cenwulf of Sedgemoor — a smooth, helpful, attentive man of forty, forever at Cuthbert's elbow with a sensible suggestion.
**Character — Double Life.** Sedgemoor wears the costume of Cuthbert's most useful, loyal thane. Beneath it, Cenwulf is bought: Cassian Drey, on House Volusen's account, has paid Sedgemoor handsomely for two years to do one thing — nudge the undecided eorl, gently and constantly, toward the regency. Cenwulf's sensible suggestions are scripted in Eldermouth.
**Wants:** Volusen's coin to keep coming; Cuthbert delivered to the regency at the Witan vote; never to be discovered.
**Secret:** the whole arrangement. Cuthbert values nothing above honest counsel; if Sedgemoor's purchase were exposed, he might turn on the regency itself and throw the wildcard to Ranulf out of sheer betrayal.
**Coronation lean:** secretly regency; publicly "whatever the eorl decides."
**Ties:** Cassian Drey and House Volusen are Sedgemoor's paymasters; Aurelio Maron of House Tassard suspects something and is digging.
**Hook:** uncovering Sedgemoor's purchase is a real coup — for Ranulf's people, for Aurelio Maron, for anyone wanting leverage over the regency — and a dangerous one, because the discovery could cost the regency the very eorl it bought.

## House Maerstow — Brackenford thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Maerstow, a hall near Brackenford; a pale lily.
**Head:** Thane Wynflaed of Maerstow — a soft-spoken, pious-seeming woman of forty-eight, known for her charity to the sick and her frequent solicitous visits to Brackenford Hall.
**Character — Grand Plan.** Maerstow is a middling house with an enormous ambition: to marry into the Brackenford eorl's line. Wynflaed's son is unwed; Cuthbert's daughter Mildgyth is eleven. If the ill heir Wulfnoth dies, Mildgyth becomes heir to Brackenford, and a Maerstow marriage would, in a generation, make the eorldom Maerstow's.
**Wants:** the Brackenford eorldom, by marriage, within two generations.
**Secret:** Wynflaed's gentle "remedies," brought to Wulfnoth on her frequent visits, are not helping him — they are slowly and undetectably killing him. The Affliction's Mark on Cuthbert's court is Maerstow's work. This is the darkest single thread in the eorldoms.
**Coronation lean:** backs whatever advances the plan; currently flatters the regency, because a stable Brackenford suits a long game.
**Ties:** angling for a marriage into Cuthbert's line; the poisoning clock in `active_threats.md`.
**Hook:** a party investigating Wulfnoth's illness — at Mildryd's request, a physician's, or by chance — can uncover the poisoning. Proving it against a pious, charitable, well-liked thane is a harder problem than discovering it.

## Minor settlements of the eorldom

Seeded for texture; each is a name, community tags, and a hook.

**Marshgate** — village, *Sealed Lips · Tradition-Bound.* A bracken-marsh village on the Mossdrowen wood-road where the bog-bark dye is harvested; the dyers guard their craft and their marsh-paths from outsiders as jealously as Brackenford itself does.

**Cuthbertsham** — village, *Honest Folk · Oppressed.* An old freemen village on the southern road, hard-pressed by the Lifting's costs — Cuthbert's reeves take more in food-rent each year, and the village reeve is the last buffer between the farmers and the eorl's strained treasury.

**Drownmere** — hamlet, *Doomed · Desperate Outlaws.* A marsh-edge hamlet slowly losing its ground to the same subsidence eating Brackenford; the families that have not left have little left to lose, and a few have taken to the marsh-roads as robbers.

## Minor courts and lesser halls

**The Stranger's Watch** — court tag *Petty Tyrant.* Not a noble court but a power-node: the paid watch that monitors non-local movement in Brackenford, run by the fyrdman Sigewulf — friendlier in seeming than in fact, and the eorldom's quiet filter on who sees what.

**The Hearth-House circle** — court tag *Sealed Lips.* The locals-only inn is the true court of native Brackenford — Mistress Hild and the old families settle the town's real business here, and an outsider is admitted to it only by Mildryd's word.

## The eorldom's lands — the inland heart

Brackenford's lands are the inland heart of Hethrun: the bracken-marsh basin around the town, the river country, and the settled lowland of thane manors and freemen farms that the southern road threads. It is connective ground more than wilderness — the eorldom a traveller crosses to reach somewhere else — but it has its features. The **bracken-marshes** themselves are genuine hazard off the causeways, and the source of the bog-bark dye trade. The eorldom's northern marches climb to the Heath's southern edge near House Wivelham and the Wivelfen (the Heath proper and the Wivelfen are in the Harrowmark — see `eorldom_harrowmark.md`). Its western reach runs toward the Greatwood verge and Mossdrowen along the wood-road. The country between is thane-manor land — ordinary villages, royal-forest fragments, the occasional old cairn on a rise — and rarely needs a wilderness roll of its own.

## Adventure hooks

- **The Aureothan chamber** (tier 1–2) — Cuthbert has not decided whom to trust with the sealed chamber under the Drowning Quarter: he distrusts Severin, distrusts the regency-aligned Aldwin, distrusts Ranulf. A discreet, unaligned party is exactly what he wants — or the party finds the chamber first, themselves.
- **The Tihatchee emissary** (tier 1–2) — Mildryd grows anxious about hiding Atohi-Hesotka; the right party could be entrusted to get him safely to Eldermouth or to the High King at Wessmark, or to carry his dragon-cult warning forward without exposing him.
- **The Tassard caravan** (tier 1) — an Aurelio Maron caravan is making unusual contact with Mistress Hild; Severin wants its trade-records, Cuthbert wants assurance it carries no weapons, the Bone-Sayers want any Tassard movement reported.
- **The Hollow-Hill connection** (tier 1) — a party investigating bandit movement may approach the fyrdman Sigewulf, who can trade his outlaw brother's intelligence for the right favour.
- **Wulfnoth's illness** (tier 1) — anyone who earns Mildryd's worry can be set to find out what is wrong with the heir; the trail leads to House Maerstow.

## At a glance

Brackenford is the wildcard eorldom — Cuthbert's Witan vote is the realm's most contested, and the reasons sit in this file: an heir being poisoned, a court of secrets, and a bought thane whispering in the eorl's ear. Of its four houses, three are masks (Wivelham hides a faith, Sedgemoor hides a purchase, Maerstow hides a murder) and one is a predictable grudge (Thornwic). Crisis stance: officially undecided; in practice a regency-leaning hall held there by a bribe that could blow up. Bestiary groups operating here: the Road-Outlaws (4A) on the marsh-roads, the Marsh-Things (3A) in the bracken-fens, and the Arrival-Hunters (A1) anywhere drift-folk are sought. For the realm frame and the web see `realm_titles.md`; for Cuthbert as a crisis actor see `court_hethrun.md` and `active_threats.md`.
