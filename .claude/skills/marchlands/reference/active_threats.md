# Active Threats

This file enumerates the active threats and pressures of the Hethrun region at session zero. Most are tracked as clocks with explicit segments, trigger conditions, and downstream effects. Slower pressures are noted as ambient conditions.

The intent: the GM can read this file and know exactly what is moving in the background, on what timescale, and what player action would accelerate or retard each pressure.

A campaign-spanning note: the Hethrun region is in *crisis at session zero* — specifically, the live spine of the campaign is **the Succession War.** Hethrun's late king (boy-king Hadric's father, Aelfric the Just) had risen to **Marshal of the High King** and was rallying a Marchlands-wide invasion of Ostmark when he **died suddenly** (cause ambiguous and suspicious; an Ostmark or Ranulf hand is a live possibility to discover in play). The armies and ambitions he mobilized turned *inward*, and the realm is sliding into civil war. The **Coronation** (Hadric must be crowned by midwinter, ~6 weeks out) is the flashpoint, contested by four power-players: the legitimate-but-weak **Regency**; **Eorl Ranulf of Stonebay** (Ostmark-backed, the pretender, **already in open war**); **Count Edmund of the Hollowford March** (Aurea-backed, west, **poised**); and **Count Halfdan** (deep-north-backed, seizing the North Coast succession, **poised**). The Coronation Clock, the Succession-War fighting, and Branwald's Working Clock dominate the first six-to-ten weeks of campaign time. The deeper pressures (the barrow-folk stirring, the dragon-cult escalating, Vaerelloth's investigation, the dwarven accord-break) operate on slower timescales but become live as the early clocks resolve.

**On grit and danger:** the Marchlands' lethality lives in its *threats*, not in any scarcity of magic. Low magic is common (see the canon digest); what keeps death real is that monsters, Named Threats, the deep wilds, the war's marching hosts, winter, and disease all run **high-powered and deadly**. Frame the threats in this file as genuinely dangerous — they are tuned to be tough, not soft, and permadeath is the default.

## The rolled nation problems

The WWN roll for Hethrun's active nation-level problems produced four entries:

### Problem (8) — Dark cults attracting the ambitious

The Burning One cult is the dominant manifestation. The cult's vengeance-pact offer specifically appeals to the ambitious-but-wronged, and the Marchlands' political turmoil is producing a steady supply of such people. Branwald has been the visible Hethrun-side operator for three months; he is the cult's recruitment scout as much as Ranulf's advisor.

**The Mtosi dragon-cult in Tihatchee** is the second manifestation, operating at the Marchlands' far southern edge but with reach into Vellanaadu trade lines and (recently, with the failed emissary group) into Hethrun. The cult is not currently active in Hethrun but its existence is part of the load on the Old Faith network's attention.

**A nascent Aureothan-revivalist cult**, smaller and less consolidated, exists in fragments across the Marchlands. The Aureothan chamber under Brackenford's Drowning Quarter is a possible nucleation point. Count Edmund's excavation is another.

### Problem (3) — A rebel front (now: open civil war)

Ranulf's faction is the rebel front — and it has passed from procedural obstruction into **open war.** Ranulf still presses the legal argument in the Witan (he refuses consent to Hadric's coronation), but his Stonebay levies and Cloven Strand-routed raiders are now fighting in the marches; he is the first of the realm's four contenders to take the field. The Stonebay Council of Twelve is functionally an alternative governance authority for the kingdom's wealthiest port. The Cloven Strand jarldom's alliance — raiders already committed, the main fleet held back — gives the rebellion real military force.

The rebel front is no longer the only one. The Succession War has **four** power-players, and the front is widening: **Count Edmund of the Hollowford March** (Aurea-backed, western hardliner and encloser, holding a strong keep and excavating an Aureothan ruin) is **poised to rise**; and **Count Halfdan** (a cold northern war-captain, deep-north-backed by Skarrgaard and the Cloven Strand) is **moving to seize the dying Eorl Berthwald's North Coast succession**, his troops already patrolling the northern marches and skirmishing Ranulf's raiders. Edmund and Halfdan hold a wary marriage-of-convenience against Ranulf. See Clock 11 below, and the faction entries in `factions.md`.

### Problem (11) — Malcontents with an artifact

The Burning One cult holds the captured Codex relic (taken from the disappeared courier two weeks ago). The relic is in Stonebay, in Branwald's possession. What the relic *is* matters: it is a Codex resurrection-component reliquary, the kind required for certain high-tier Codex resurrection rituals, the loss of which materially constrains Severin's Codex political reach for as long as it is gone. Branwald has not yet decided whether to destroy, hold, or strategically reveal it.

Count Edmund's Aureothan chamber excavation is a parallel artifact threat. Something he has found is changing him; he has not yet deployed whatever it is.

### Problem (1, slow pressure) — Farmland exhaustion

The Greenways' agricultural base has been degrading slowly for two decades — soil exhaustion from the post-fall expansion of grain-fields without proper rotation; deer-population shifts from the Old Forest's edge inward, damaging crops; the Heath's bog-iron extraction encroaching on traditional pasture. Count Edmund's enclosures have made it worse by displacing peasants from the practices that had maintained marginal balance. The kingdom is one bad harvest from food-supply pressure that would escalate every other crisis.

## The rolled good things

The campaign is not entirely darkness; the roll produced three positive entries:

### Good (3) — A magical bloodline manifests

Hadric's dragon-blood is the manifestation, hidden until now and beginning to surface. The bloodline has been latent in the royal Hethrun line for generations (it descends through Aelfric the Just's grandmother, who was a half-elven exile of a particular kind, the lineage suppressed by Codex pressure and politely forgotten). The manifestation is *not* random — it has been triggered by the rite that crowned Hadric and by the boy's approaching coming-of-age.

The manifestation is a decisive positive in the campaign: if it stabilizes in time and the boy is recognized for what he is, the regency's position consolidates and Ranulf's contest collapses. The risks are real (a Codex Patriarchate that does not approve of draconic ancestry; a Burning One cult that sees Hadric as a threat; an inexperienced boy-king who does not yet understand what is happening to him) but the underlying current is positive.

### Good (17) — Old unrest is calmed

The wood-road verge has been quieter than at any time in the past three decades. Captain Saerwen's Wardens have not engaged in a hostile contact in five years; the western Old Forest's incursions into Greenways farmsteads have effectively ceased. Some of this is the elves' active patrol; some of this is the elven outer court's *intentional* calming, in preparation for the Mtosi cult intervention they are arranging.

### Good (8) — Good harvests last year

The previous harvest (AF 206) was unusually good throughout the Greenways. The kingdom is not yet hungry. This window is closing — AF 207's harvest has been adequate but not generous, and the Greenways agricultural pressure is real — but for the immediate session-zero moment, the food supply is stable.

## The clocks

The clocks listed below are tracked simultaneously. Player actions advance or retard them; some clocks compete (advancing one may empty another); the GM should reference each clock when major scenes resolve, marking the appropriate segments forward or backward.

---

### Clock 1: The Coronation Clock

**Segments: 6/12.**

**Description:** The procedural deadline for Hadric's coronation. By Hethrun tradition, the boy must be crowned by the forty-second day after his thirteenth year begins; he is currently approaching the back end of that window. Six weeks from session zero is the hard deadline.

**Six weeks** corresponds to **midwinter** in the Hethrun calendar (specifically, the third day before the longest night), the traditional coronation moment.

**Six segments are already past:** the Witan's three deadlocked sessions; Cassian Drey's tribute-pressure pause; Ranulf's procedural objection; the rumor-counter-rumor cycle.

**Six segments remain.** The remaining segments correspond to the six weeks of campaign time before midwinter, *if no decisive action is taken*. The clock fills automatically at one segment per week unless players act.

**Advances faster if:**
- The regency capitulates on a major Aurean demand
- A Witan session deadlocks a fourth time without resolution
- Branwald's Stonebay working completes a stage
- The Tihatchee emissary's information is suppressed rather than delivered

**Retards (segments removed) if:**
- A wavering eorl publicly commits to Hadric (Hagan most likely; Cuthbert most decisive)
- The High King's arbitration is formally invoked
- Hadric's dragon-blood manifests in a way the Witan must address openly
- Branwald is exposed or his working is disrupted

**At 12/12:** Hadric is not crowned by midwinter. His claim is procedurally compromised. The Witan is forced into either a forced coronation (which damages legitimacy and probably triggers Ranulf's open claim), or accepting Ranulf as crowned king, or formally requesting High King Cyneberht to arbitrate (which is a face-saving option but extends the crisis).

**At 0/12:** The boy is crowned at the Kingsmound in proper form. Ranulf's faction reorganizes. The kingdom continues under regency in functional terms with a now-crowned boy-king.

### Clock 2: Branwald's Stonebay working

**Segments: 2/4.**

Mirror of Ranulf's faction Clock 2 in `factions.md` and the Burning One cult's Clock 1 in `factions.md`.

**Description:** Branwald's multi-rite fire-working at the Stonebay headland. Two segments are already complete (an initial rite three months ago at his arrival, and the new-moon rite four weeks ago observed by a Stonebay fyrdman). Two more segments remain: a rite at the midwinter conjunction (six weeks from session zero — coincident with the Coronation deadline), and a stellar conjunction rite four weeks after that.

**Advances at:**
- The midwinter conjunction (week six, coincident with Coronation Clock final segment)
- The stellar conjunction (week ten)

**Retards (segments removed) if:**
- Branwald is killed
- Branwald is captured and detained through a conjunction
- The Stonebay headland rite site is interrupted or desecrated
- The captured relic is recovered (a partial retard — would not fully empty but would slow)

**At 4/4:** The working completes. Ostmark moves openly to claim Marchland-side gain — a formal trade-protectorate offer to Stonebay, a Burning One state-cult Marchland mission, the Solar Academy's regional operations consolidating. Ranulf's position becomes substantially more secure. The Codex Church's Marchland-wide position weakens.

**At 0/4:** Branwald's mission fails. He is recalled or killed. The Solar Academy reorganizes. Master Theron Heliodoros may send a higher-ranking replacement.

### Clock 3: Hadric's dragon-blood manifestation

**Segments: 2/8.**

**Description:** The boy's draconic ancestry is awakening. The current visible signs are subtle (gold flecks in the iris, slightly elevated body temperature; he no longer requires a winter cloak when his courtiers do). Forward progress brings increasingly visible signs, eventually impossible to ignore.

**Segments:**
- 1/8: gold flecks in irises (already manifest, four weeks ago).
- 2/8: elevated body temperature (already manifest, two weeks ago; Master Edorn has noticed).
- 3/8: bathwater heating involuntarily; a small thing, easily explained away (two weeks from session zero, on current trajectory).
- 4/8: a moment of clear visible heat-shimmer when angered (six weeks from session zero — coincident with the Coronation deadline).
- 5/8: speaking single draconic syllables in his sleep (two months from session zero).
- 6/8: a moment of involuntary draconic vocalization while awake (three months from session zero).
- 7/8: visible scale-marks at the wrists and at the spine (four months).
- 8/8: full visible manifestation — golden scales at the temples, visibly draconic eyes, a presence that is no longer ambiguously human.

**Advances faster if:**
- Hadric is under significant stress (a Witan vote against him; a confrontation he cannot avoid)
- A specific Sky-Father or Old Faith ritual triggers — there is one (the *Awakening of the Long Line*) that Aelfwin the court Sky-Father priest would recognize and could perform, accelerating manifestation to roughly 5/8 immediately
- Hadric is taught about his condition (his understanding of what he is accelerates the manifestation)

**Retards (segments removed) if:**
- A Codex anti-draconic working is performed (only the Patriarch could authorize; an inferior version could be attempted by Severin but the magical strength is doubtful)
- Hadric is quietly removed from court to a hidden location (the manifestation slows in low-stress environments)
- A particular component (a *Tear of the First Sky*, kept in Codex archive at Aurea) is administered; it would reset the manifestation to 0/8 but would also probably kill the boy

**At 4/8 (the threshold of public manifestation):** Severin will press for a Codex assessment; Aldwin will press for a Sky-Father consultation; the Witan can no longer ignore the matter. The political ramifications are substantial — either positive (consolidating Hadric's claim by visible "heroic-greatness manifestation") or negative (a Codex-Patriarchate decision against draconic ancestry that effectively disinherits him).

**At 8/8:** The boy is unmistakably draconic-blooded. His political fate depends on whether the Marchlands accept this as legitimate.

### Clock 4: The barrow-folk stir

**Segments: 1/6.**

**Description:** The pre-Saxon peoples sleeping under the Heath barrows are stirring. The seals are weakening due to neglect; Gythwin's annual rites have been on hold for three years (the political pressures have consumed her time). The novice Cynwise's dreams are an early symptom — she is being touched by what the barrow-folk dream of in their sleep.

The Heath is now the **Harrowmark** eorldom, and this clock is that eorldom's central threat — see `eorldom_harrowmark.md`. Three Harrowmark houses bear directly on it: House Blackfen, whose bog-iron delvings are breaking into sealed barrow-ground for profit and accelerating the clock; House Greymoor, whose under-manned watch-forts are the realm's failing physical bulwark; and House Eldgrave, which secretly feeds the barrow-folk and steers their hunger onto its neighbours. Count Wulfhere of Wardmoor, on the Harrowmark's southern border, is an early honest witness to the stirring (see `independent_thanedoms.md`).

**Segments:**
- 1/6: dreams in sensitive individuals (Cynwise; an apprentice cunning-woman at the Wivelfen; one of the Heath bog-iron extraction-workers who is unusually sensitive). Already at this stage.
- 2/6: visible night-cries from the Wivelfen at certain Heath barrows (one month from session zero on current trajectory).
- 3/6: the older Heath barrow openings begin to show partial seal-failure (two months).
- 4/6: a small group of barrow-folk briefly enters the world (four months); seen by isolated cunning-folk; identifiable as pre-Saxon by language and dress.
- 5/6: full active emergence from one or more major barrows (six months).
- 6/6: the Heath becomes the barrow-folk's territory; major political crisis; tier-3-and-up engagement required.

**Advances faster if:**
- Gythwin is incapacitated or killed
- A specific barrow is disturbed — House Blackfen's delvings, a tomb-robber, or House Eldgrave's escalating offerings
- The novice Cynwise's dreams are not properly addressed
- House Blackfen's bog-iron delvings push further into sealed barrow-ground (already under way)

**Retards (segments removed) if:**
- Gythwin completes a seasonal rite-sequence (the slow work; one set of rites at the autumn equinox, one at midwinter, one at the spring equinox)
- A party assists Gythwin with the rites (a more efficient retard; one rite per assistance opportunity, plus a substantive segment removal if the party performs additional consecration)
- The novice Cynwise is brought to Gythwin and trained as an additional apprentice — this would substantially retard the clock (Cynwise is a powerful sensitive whom Gythwin could integrate into the rite-work)

**At 6/6:** The Heath is unstable; the barrow-folk have territorial presence; the kingdom must respond either with war (which would lose; the pre-Saxon peoples are powerful and the seals were placed for good reason) or with negotiation (which is what Gythwin would advocate, and what no Hethrun monarch has done for three centuries).

### Clock 5: Vaerelloth's investigation

**Segments: 1/6.**

**Description:** Vaerelloth is investigating who is responsible for the Mtosi cult's existence. This is a slow clock that operates largely off-screen; the players will affect it only if they engage with the dragon-cult intervention or with Vaerelloth's pact-holders.

**Advances if:** Vaerelloth gains direct evidence about the cult's true architect, through her pact-holders, through Marchland intelligence she captures, or through the dwarven correspondence.

**Retards if:** Vaerelloth is distracted by a Marchland crisis (e.g., the Coronation crisis spilling into the Iron Strait); the architect successfully covers their tracks; Vaerelloth's wing-stirring reveals her existence prematurely and she retreats to discretion.

**At 6/6:** Vaerelloth acts. The action depends on what she has learned:
- If the architect proves to be **the dwarves** (Stone-Lama Vajra), she breaks the accord first — significant Marchland-wide upheaval.
- If the architect proves to be **the elves**, the Silvering-Vaerelloth war goes hot.
- If the architect proves to be **Ostmark** (which is one of the GM-table possibilities), she allies with the Codex Patriarchate.
- If the architect proves to be **the Smokeless Throne** itself, all bets are off.

The architect's actual identity is a GM choice; I recommend leaving it open for emergence based on player exploration. The most thematically rich answer is **the Smokeless Throne**, which would clarify several other ambiguities (the elves' Burnt Out tag, the accord-fraying, why the dwarves have been so cautious).

### Clock 6: Dwarven accord-break preparation

**Segments: 2/8.**

**Description:** Stone-Lama Vajra is positioning his realm for the eventual break with Vaerelloth. This is a generations-scale project that has been in slow execution for forty years; the current segments reflect specific recent moves (Sera-of-the-Eleventh-Sky's Stonebay visit, an oracle-confirmation at the Sixth Pass monastery six months ago).

**Advances if:** Vajra receives oracular confirmation of next steps; a contemplative completes a long preparation; Vaerelloth makes a misstep visible to Vajra's intelligence.

**Retards if:** Vaerelloth de-escalates her Mtosi cult tolerance; the elves move first; an internal dwarven contemplative succession crisis distracts the realm.

**At 8/8:** The Stone-Lama publicly announces the accord's end. The dwarven realm reorients to active opposition to Vaerelloth. Marchland-wide implications are substantial (the White Mountain trade reorganizes, the southern smith-quarters are affected, the dragon-cult dynamic shifts).

**Description:** The Stonebay creditor who has been leaning on Cassian Drey is approaching the threshold at which the Bone-Sayers will formally engage Drey for *information exchange* rather than for payment. The leverage operation has been moving for two months; three more weeks brings it to completion.

### Clock 8: Count Edmund's chamber

**Segments: 2/6.**

**Description:** Count Edmund of Hollowford March is excavating an Aureothan ruin on his lands; he has reached a sealed inner chamber and is approaching it cautiously. What is inside is *changing him* through proximity; full opening would precipitate a regional crisis.

**Advances if:** Edmund pushes the excavation faster (he is currently being cautious; his Aurean Patriarchate correspondence is encouraging him to be bolder). A specific tool or expertise becomes available (an Aureothan-trained mage; an artifact-key Edmund acquires through Patriarchate channels). 

**Retards if:** Edmund's household intervenes (his chief steward suspects something is wrong with his lord). A Hethrun crown intervention (Cuthbert or the regency formally challenging the excavation, which neither has the political weight to do alone). Severin intercedes (which he might if he learns of the Patriarchate's direct correspondence with Edmund, which he does not approve).

**At 6/6:** The chamber is opened. What emerges is at GM discretion; I recommend a *long-suppressed Aureothan-imperial revenant* (a high-tier undead with imperial authority-claims of a kind the Marchlands have not seen in five centuries), which would create a tier-3-and-up regional threat that crosses Hethrun-Wessmark borders.

### Clock 9: The Aldermarket rumor escalation

**Description:** The Aldermarket rumor about Ranulf and Wystan's killing surfaced last week. It is currently mid-burn — most Eldermouth merchants have heard it; most have not yet acted on it. If the rumor consolidates (more witnesses, more corroborating detail, more visible regency endorsement), it becomes a politically usable accusation.

### Clock 10: The Maerstow poisoning

**Segments: 3/6.**

**Description:** Wulfnoth of Brackenford, Eorl Cuthbert's fourteen-year-old heir, has a wasting illness no physician can name. The illness is murder: Thane Wynflaed of Maerstow, on her frequent charitable visits to Brackenford Hall, brings the boy gentle "remedies" of her own preparation that are slowly and undetectably killing him — to clear the Brackenford succession for a Maerstow marriage to Cuthbert's daughter Mildgyth (see `eorldom_brackenford.md`). Three segments are already past — three seasons of decline.

**Advances** one segment per Wynflaed visit, roughly one per two weeks.

**Retards (segments removed) if:**
- Wulfnoth is removed from Maerstow's reach (sent away, his visitors screened)
- The remedies are identified and stopped (a physician, a cunning-woman, an investigating party)
- Wynflaed is exposed and barred from the hall

**At 6/6:** Wulfnoth dies. The Brackenford succession opens; the fostered Mildgyth becomes heir; House Maerstow springs its marriage-suit. Eorl Cuthbert, broken by grief, becomes wholly unpredictable in the Coronation crisis — the Coronation Clock advances.

**At 0/6:** the poisoning is stopped and Wulfnoth recovers; whether Wynflaed is exposed or merely thwarted is a separate question, and an unexposed Maerstow will try again by another road.

### Clock 11: Count Halfdan seizes the North Coast

**Segments: 3/6.**

Mirror of the Northern Marchers faction Clock 1 in `factions.md`.

**Description:** Count Halfdan — the cold northern war-captain, deep-north-backed by Skarrgaard and the Cloven Strand — is moving to take the dying Eorl Berthwald's North Coast succession before Berthwald's heir Wulfric the Younger can secure it. Three segments are already past: Halfdan's host is in position, his patrons' silver and ships are flowing, and the North Coast moot is being worked. His troops patrol the northern marches and skirmish Ranulf's raiders, which doubles as a claim to be the coast's protector.

**Advances faster if:**
- Eorl Berthwald's health fails (his is Clock-adjacent; his death throws the succession open)
- Halfdan wins or coerces North Coast moot-support, or establishes a marriage-tie or a battlefield fact
- Ranulf's raiders are visibly driven off (Halfdan as the coast's defender)
- The Edmund marriage-of-convenience holds, pinning Ranulf from the south

**Retards (segments removed) if:**
- Wulfric the Younger secures a clean succession (the regency or High King formally backing him is decisive)
- Halfdan's deep-north backing is exposed and discredited as a foreign land-grab
- The Edmund alliance collapses, leaving Halfdan to face Ranulf alone
- Halfdan learns, and resents, that his patrons mean to trade him to Ranulf when convenient (his own Dread; could turn him)

**At 6/6:** Halfdan holds the North Coast in fact if not yet in law — a fourth crowned-realm power and a direct northern counterweight to Ranulf, propped by the deep-north.

**At 0/6:** Wulfric the Younger holds the succession; Halfdan's bid collapses or is bought off; the deep-north loses its planned client on the Iron-Strait flank.

### Clock 12: The Succession War widens

**Segments: 2/8.**

**Description:** The realm-wide slide from a deadlocked Coronation into open civil war. Ranulf is already fighting (Clock segments above and the rebel-front problem); Edmund and Halfdan are poised. This clock tracks how far the *general* war spreads — from Ranulf's marcher skirmishes toward a multi-front conflict that drags in the wavering eorls, the crown levies, and the foreign backers (Ostmark behind Ranulf, Aurea behind Edmund, the deep-north behind Halfdan). Two segments are past: Ranulf's open mobilization, and Halfdan's northern skirmishing.

**Advances faster if:**
- The Coronation Clock reaches 12/12 (no crowning by midwinter — the procedural legitimacy that restrains the contenders fails)
- Edmund openly rises (Clock 8, the chamber, or an Aurean prompt tips him from poised to committed)
- A power-player's foreign backer commits openly (an Ostmark fleet, an Aurean garrison, a deep-north host)
- A wavering eorl is forced to take up arms for a side

**Retards (segments removed) if:**
- Hadric is crowned in proper form (the Coronation Clock empties), restoring a center the contenders must reckon with
- The High King's arbitration is formally invoked and accepted
- A power-player is removed, bought off, or forced to stand down (e.g., Ranulf's luck turns, Halfdan's bid collapses, Edmund's correspondence is exposed)

**At 8/8:** Open multi-front civil war across Hethrun, foreign powers committed, the realm a battlefield — a tier-3-and-up strategic crisis that reshapes the campaign.

**At 0/8:** The war is contained to its present scale or settled; the realm pulls back from the brink.

## Ambient pressures (not clocked)

These are slow-running conditions that affect the campaign without being on segment-tracked clocks:

- **The Witan's Affliction's Mark.** The fatigue and uncertainty across the Witan members is ambient; it slows decision-making but has no specific segment-trigger. Aldwin's elven-influence hypothesis may resolve it.

- **The plague-quarter.** The bog-iron extraction-workers' chronic illness is an ambient social pressure. A specific crisis (the child Sister Halgrid the Lay is asking help with) could elevate to a clock if neglected.

- **The Greenways' agricultural exhaustion.** A two-decade slow pressure. Affects food prices, peasant mood, Count Edmund's enclosure justification.

- **The Iron Strait sea-thing's slow weather work.** The North Coast's atmospheric oddness, the burnt villages, the propitiation-pact-holders. Ambient unless approached.

- **Coelius Vetranus's letters.** The lich writes to Aldwin once a year on average. The next letter is overdue. If it arrives during the campaign, it is a hook rather than a clock.

- **The Smokeless Throne's faerie-court politics.** Ambient at session zero. May surface if players take Archfey pacts or interact with the Silvering inner court.

## How to read this file at the table

The GM should track the active clocks on a single sheet (the campaign session-log). At the close of each session, review:

1. Which clocks advanced? (Set the new segment.)
2. Which clocks retarded? (Set the new segment.)
3. Did any clock trigger? (Resolve consequences; remove the clock from active tracking.)

The Coronation Clock is the campaign's clock-of-clocks at session zero. As that clock resolves (one way or the other), the campaign's pressure-set shifts: the dragon-blood manifestation, the barrow-folk stirring, and the more distant clocks (Vaerelloth's investigation, the dwarven accord-break) come into focus.
