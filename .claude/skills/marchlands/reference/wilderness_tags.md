# Wilderness Tags

Wilderness-stocking tags for any unnamed wilderness region the party crosses. Used by `ruin_site_builder.md` (for the wilderness around a ruin), by `threat_generator.md`'s Cursed Land / Tainted Region sub-type, and by direct call when generating a region the corpus hasn't yet specified. Each tag carries five sub-tables — Enemies, Friends, Complications, Things, Places — that the GM rolls on to populate the specific wilderness.

Tag names, discussion text, and sub-table entries reproduced from *Worlds Without Number* by Kevin Crawford / Sine Nomine Publishing, for personal worldbuilding use. Slight Hethrun parentheticals added where the corpus has a natural region or feature to point at; the WWN content itself is unchanged.

## How to use this file

The procedure for a freshly-introduced wilderness:

1. **Roll twice on the d100 master table** below for the region's two tags. Read the two tags as parallel pressures or synthesize them — synthesis is usually richer.
2. **For each rolled tag, roll d3 on each of its five sub-tables** — one Enemy, one Friend, one Complication, one Thing, one Place. Embed those specific entries in the region's file rather than the bare tag name.
3. **Reconcile against existing canon.** If the rolled wilderness sits inside a named eorldom or zone, the bestiary's regional groups take precedence over the tag's Enemies sub-table where they conflict; the wilderness tag adds texture rather than overriding the zone.
4. **Hand off to the bestiary** for encounter pressure within the wilderness — see `bestiary.md` for region dispatcher and groups.

For named Hethrun wildernesses (the deep Heath, the Greatwood Deeps, the Wivelfen, the Iron Strait approaches, the Fells, the Old Forest), pick tags deliberately to reinforce the existing canon rather than rolling fresh.

## Abandoned Village

Wilderness villages tend to be unstable “gold rush” settlements in the best of times, attracting those who lack any sound place elsewhere. This village failed as rapidly as it was formed. It may have been overrun by marauders, depopulated by disease, hollowed out by a failed harvest, torn apart by civil strife, or just abandoned for better prospects elsewhere.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Bandit chief or sorcerer responsible for the ruin
2. Crazed village head turned hermit
3. Monstrous entity nesting in the remains

**Friends (d3):**

1. Last stubborn holdout
2. Lord’s bailiff looking for answers
3. Hapless pioneer who’s arrived to an empty ruin

**Complications (d3):**

1. It’s now populated by something inhuman
2. It was built on top of a place abandoned for good reason
3. The wilderness is reclaiming it in a dangerous way

**Things (d3):**

1. Heirloom left behind in the flight
2. Buried treasure of the villagers
3. The relic that caused the ruin

**Places (d3):**

1. Overgrown village meeting hall
2. Home with everything left just as it was at that moment
3. Burnt-out shrine to the local god

## Ancient Guardians

There are guardians in this site derived from some ancient polity, whether constructs built to protect some long-forgotten place here or the restless shades of the dead who once dwelled in the location. They may have a simple hatred towards “invaders” or they may be confused, thinking interlopers are part of some ancient enemy group. What they once guarded may not even exist any more in any perceptible way.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Unrelenting golem leader
2. Maddened sorcerer- lord shade
3. Sinister heir seeking to suborn the guardians to their service

**Friends (d3):**

1. Diplomatic guardian entity
2. Greedy treasure- hunter
3. Local who got trapped in the place

**Complications (d3):**

1. The guardians are to keep something in
2. A local group uses the guardians as a trap for outsiders
3. The guardians were never meant to be harmful but what they do to intruders is very damaging

**Things (d3):**

1. Key to shut down or ward off the guardians
2. The precious relic they were guarding
3. Ritual garb that makes the wearer invisible to the guardians

**Places (d3):**

1. Place of ominously martial statuary
2. Ghostly street-image of a vanished place
3. Broken remnant of an ancient landmark

## Ancient Monument

A grand monument to some ancient polity is found here. While its actual meaning and significance may be inscrutable, it’s probably big enough and dramatic enough to dominate the hex, even if it’s collapsed or been defaced by the ages. The remains of the civilization that raised it might be found at its base, along with whatever heirs to it remain. Some monuments still have active enchantments or guardians to prevent defacement.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Zealous modern cultist of the vanished polity
2. Last bitter heir of them
3. Vile monster that nests in the monument

**Friends (d3):**

1. Eager archaeologist
2. Weary old hereditary keeper of the place
3. Friendly creature that’s made the monument a home

**Complications (d3):**

1. The monument is a powerful Working
2. The monument served a secular or residential purpose
3. The bulk of the monument is underground or in an extradimensional pocket Iterum

**Things (d3):**

1. Costly decorations or ornaments on the monument
2. The precious core of the work
3. Ancient offerings made to it by vanished supplicants

**Places (d3):**

1. Before some towering megastructure-monument
2. Amid the cyclopean wreckage of its fall
3. Secret passages within the monument

## Arratu Land

There is a large pocket of arratu at the site, even though it may be too small to show up on the hex map. Such a pocket might be the last remnant of a formerly-great swath, or it could just be a localized tumor created by an Outsider device, a malevolent sorcerer, or a magical disaster. Something about the arratu’s placement likely forces the party to go well out of their way to bypass it safely; it might command a narrow pass or be flanked by dangerous terrain on either side.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Outsider beast within the waste
2. Half-human marauder chief lairing inside
3. Crazed Outsider wizard who made the wasteland

**Friends (d3):**

1. Foolish explorer who tried to cross it
2. Wretched slave taken by raiders
3. Not-too-homicidal native

**Complications (d3):**

1. The wasteland is being contained by a potentially- breakable object here
2. Locals know how to get through it safely
3. It’s a very new wasteland and the locals haven’t adjusted to it yet

**Things (d3):**

1. Equipment to survive the hostile atmosphere within
2. Precious Outsider relic
3. Wealth seized by raiders from the waste

**Places (d3):**

1. Blasted village overrun by alien life
2. Desolate plain with an unearthly sky above
3. Lakeshore of a pool filled with a noxious chemical

## Bandit Lair

Bandits lair in this hex, which is presumably close enough to civilized lands to serve as a base for their operations. Not every bandit lair is a hidden cave; some operate out of subverted or cowed villages, others hide among remote monasteries, and some just camp in convenient glades long enough to rest, refit, and trade with black marketeers. It might be a seasonal lair as well, with local peasants turning bandit at certain profitable times of the year.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Ruthlessly pragmatic bandit chief
2. Wealthy grandee wielding the bandits against rivals
3. Heartless slaver gathering stock

**Friends (d3):**

1. Hapless missionary of a kinder way
2. Local taken as a slave
3. Runaway bandit who repents

**Complications (d3):**

1. They have a traditional prey and don’t usually rob others
2. They’re the degenerate remnants of a once-noble cause
3. They’re a confederacy of thieving groups that often quarrel

**Things (d3):**

1. Loot taken from a recent success
2. Priceless heirloom owned by a noble-turned-bandit
3. Precious relic the bandits don’t realize is so valuable

**Places (d3):**

1. Raucous glade full of drink and debauchery
2. Re- purposed estate
3. Makeshift palisaded camp

## Beast Swarm

There is a swarm of some dangerous beast in the area, whether it consists of a tide of minuscule vermin or a pack of magically-warped predators. Some of these swarms may serve more as environmental hazards than monsters to be fought; sweeping carpets of mutated radioactive fire ants might be something for the PCs to dodge rather than engage. If the beasts have some mundane value, they might have attracted suitably reckless interest from the locals.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Supernaturally intelligent god-beast
2. Mad wizard who spawned them
3. Brutal hunt master driving his wretched minions on

**Friends (d3):**

1. Victim of a beast attack
2. Animal collector on a mission
3. Hunter in search of vengeance

**Complications (d3):**

1. The beasts are usually harmless but have recently changed
2. The beasts are the only thing holding a worse peril back
3. The beasts gain magical powers in sufficiently large groups

**Things (d3):**

1. Poison that is very lethal to the beasts
2. Relic that can control the swarms
3. Priceless treasure held by a luckless hunter

**Places (d3):**

1. Site of a bestial massacre
2. Fetid den of a swarm
3. Overrun hunter’s camp

## Blighted Tribe

A tribe of Blighted live in the hex. They may be violent war-creatures naturally hostile to normal humanity or beings cursed with some trait or nature that makes it impossible for them to live in civilization. Relations are unlikely to be warm in either case, but the latter might be persuaded to parley if the party can work around their limitations and win their trust.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Brutal Blighted war-chief
2. Outsider using them as living tools
3. Human hunter bent on slaughtering inoffensive Blighted

**Friends (d3):**

1. Native not so Blighted as their brethren
2. Human trader who deals with them
3. Local human who knows their ways

**Complications (d3):**

1. Their blight was once very useful to the ancient polity that existed here
2. They’re only Blighted because they lack something to temper it that this site once provided
3. They’re dying out and desperate to win allies against their many predators

**Things (d3):**

1. Valuable Blighted-made product
2. Relics of those who twisted them
3. Trophies taken from their prey

**Places (d3):**

1. Human structure reworked to fit their nature
2. Site of a gruesome massacre
3. Shrine or monument honoring their creators

## Broken Infrastructure

There is a ruin here that once enabled some grand ancient infrastructure. A lost canal lock, irrigation control center, magical gate nexus, weather control station, underground transit station, occult communications center, or some other great function was once performed here. The ruin has been unusable for ages, but remnants of its former function might still be found in the surroundings.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Sorcerer looting the wreckage
2. Monstrous remnant of the former inhabitants or guardians
3. Modern parasite-monster come to feed on the scraps

**Friends (d3):**

1. Curious arcane student
2. Tribal who lives in the ruin
3. Wayfarer forced to seek refuge there

**Complications (d3):**

1. It still works but in a dangerous and unhelpful way
2. It would cause a tremendous local disaster if it were re-activated
3. The locals are exploiting it in an unintended fashion

**Things (d3):**

1. Control key for the original function
2. Relic related to the original function that does something magical
3. Precious component from it

**Places (d3):**

1. Long-ruined control nexus
2. Collapsed service or operational area
3. Surrounding area scarred by the decayed remnants of its function

## Chokepoint

There’s a natural choke point in this area: a mountain pass, a land bridge, a dry ridge in a swamp, a narrow strand of untainted arratu land, a gap in a megastructure, or some other slender passage. Such a chokepoint is likely part of a feature that extends outside the hex, and it may be prohibitively difficult to cross this feature anywhere else. Such valuable, defensible areas are rarely left unoccupied.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Bandit lord who “taxes” passersby
2. Monstrous creature who hunts there
3. Ancient guardian of a long-dead polity

**Friends (d3):**

1. Trader in dire need
2. Explorer seeking a better way
3. Local currently inhabiting the chokepoint

**Complications (d3):**

1. It can be opened or blocked by a controller
2. It’s only recently become abnormally dangerous
3. It’s known of only by those who want it kept secret

**Things (d3):**

1. Map of the passage
2. Token allowing safe passage
3. Key to controlling the passage

**Places (d3):**

1. Perfect ambush site
2. Natural fortress
3. High observation point commanding the pass

## Collapsed Deep

An ancient Deep once was located here, but time, warfare, or disaster caused it to collapse. A significant portion of the surface has likely subsided to form an uneven crater. Small sections of the Deep might still be accessible from the surface, and dangerous uncollapsed passageways may still connect these smaller zones. The heirs of the survivors may still persist on the surface or they may have long since died out to natural hazards or dispersion.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Buried undying lord
2. Surface looter with dreams of rule
3. The thing that caused the collapse

**Friends (d3):**

1. Friendly survivor descendant
2. Curious explorer
3. Native guide to the ancient pit

**Complications (d3):**

1. Things below are digging their way up
2. The Deep is leaking something dangerous
3. The collapse was recent and monstrous survivors still flee

**Things (d3):**

1. Priceless Deep treasure cast up
2. Exposed arcane components
3. Potent relic carried by survivors

**Places (d3):**

1. Ancient vault torn in two
2. Crevasse cutting through a hundred ruined levels
3. Buckled structure fragment jutting through the surface

## Criminal Meet

This site is used as a meeting place for smugglers, bandits, illegal slavers, fences, or other criminals. Something about the place gives them a good view of any incoming strangers and discreet privacy for their dealings. Some few criminals may remain here at all times to maintain the place, or it might be used only as the situation requires. These criminals may be particular, dealing only with known associates, or they may be far enough from the law to feel comfortable about trading with adventurers.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Corrupt merchant grandee
2. Sinister black market boss
3. Monstrous entity that enforces the meet’s peace

**Friends (d3):**

1. Theft victim searching for their goods

**Complications (d3):**

1. The local authorities turn a knowing blind eye to it
2. The meet is trading in something much worse than everyone thinks it is

**Things (d3):**

**Places (d3):**

## Cryptic Art

A vast ancient work of art has been raised here, back when the wilderness was not so wild. It may be a statue or other sculpted monument, perhaps of entities in no way human, or it may be fashioned of solid light, sound, emotions, or stranger media. The intended meaning of this art is usually incomprehensible to modern humanity and the structure is too large or too difficult to harm for ordinary vandals or time to have erode it entirely. Some ancient forms of art can be dangerous or even lethal to modern humans.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Ancient site guardian
2. Priest of a cult that’s made a religion of the art
3. Sorcerer bent on suborning a magical artwork

**Friends (d3):**

1. Reckless art connoisseur
2. Native living in the artwork
3. Historian trying to decipher it

**Complications (d3):**

1. It has a positive or negative effect on a viewer’s body or mind
2. It’s been warped into something monstrous by time or decay
3. It encodes a terrible or precious secret to those who understand it

**Things (d3):**

1. Ancient texts that can decode the art’s meaning
2. Precious object used in its construction
3. Fragment of damaged art that’s still valuable

**Places (d3):**

1. Intended viewing site for the art
2. Churning inner mechanism of a complex work
3. Hidden chamber revealing a different perspective of the work

## Cursed Land

This site has been cursed. Rather than being a simple xenoformed arratu, some malevolent magic has laid a persistent, dangerous affliction on the area. Creatures may be warped, inhabitants may be plagued, terrible luck might strike all passers-by, or natural physical processes might be perverted. There should be something valuable or attractive here, or else few people would have any reason to engage with the location.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Vile sorcerer responsible for the curse
2. Hideously- warped native creature
3. Curse-beast that sweats the malison with its very existence

**Friends (d3):**

1. Local curse victim
2. Native sorcerer trying to lift it
3. Hapless pioneer who didn’t realize it was there

**Complications (d3):**

1. It has a seemingly positive effect as well
2. The seemingly obvious source of the curse isn’t at all responsible
3. Someone profits by the curse

**Things (d3):**

1. Ward to protect against the curse
2. Precious byproduct of the magic
3. Key to undoing the curse

**Places (d3):**

1. Warped and twisted natural feature
2. Nest of misshapen beasts
3. Field of grossly-mutated plant life

## Decayed Working

There’s an ancient Working in the area that is broken or decayed, and the original effect it was intended to produce has been twisted into something dangerous. Infrastructure Workings meant to provide good fortune, health, or swift travel might now create ill- luck, plagues, or vomit forth extradimensional horrors. Small Workings of private buildings might now create localized hazards. Completely destroying the Working might end the problem or create an even greater disaster from the magical fallout.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Working-born abomination
2. Ancient caretaker gone berserk
3. Sorcerer tearing apart the Working recklessly for lore and parts

**Friends (d3):**

1. Native who knows how to live with the blight
2. Ambitious would-be repairman
3. Curious scholar looking for details

**Complications (d3):**

1. The decay can be contained with vile or costly rituals by the locals
2. The decay is endured because it fends off outsiders
3. A local enemy wants to worsen the effect

**Things (d3):**

1. Parts to repair the Working
2. Valuable magical byproducts
3. Precious Working fragments

**Places (d3):**

1. Decaying Working chamber
2. Site twisted by the magical effect
3. Collapsed arcane structure

## Demihuman Community

There’s a community of demihumans living here, likely intentionally avoiding more well-controlled lands in order to maintain their autonomy. Autonomy alone is unlikely to be all the reason they’ve avoided civilized lands, however, as few human rulers find it worth the trouble to demand more than a fixed tribute from demihumans, leaving them to control their own affairs. There may be something about this group that makes it impossible for them to live comfortably near humans.

**Enemies (d3):**

**Friends (d3):**

**Complications (d3):**

2. They’re remnants of a former demihuman kingdom

## Hermitage

A small hermitage was established here sometime in the past to provide solitude for some ascetic. Occasionally small clusters of hermits form around such a central point, meeting at times to check on each other or join in shared discipline. Such anchorites usually shun visitors who aren’t also devotees of their path, but occasionally a hermit wins such fame for sorcery or wisdom that pilgrims seek them out. Some hermits have been known to respond with great anger toward these repeated worldly interruptions.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Hermit sorcerer gone mad
2. Sinister villain hiding as a hermit
3. Alien hermit with inscrutably evil philosophical principles

**Friends (d3):**

1. Worried relative of a new hermit
2. Petitioner seeking holy help
3. Hermit rethinking solitude

**Complications (d3):**

1. The hermits have control over a local magical energy or Working
2. They keep themselves separate to keep others safe from them and their irrepressible powers
3. The hermit is hated by the local secular authorities for their aid to its foes

**Things (d3):**

1. Precious religious relic carried by the hermit
2. Cast-off worldly wealth
3. Map to finding them

**Places (d3):**

1. Austere wilderness cave
2. Hermit hole in a grave- mound in the swamps
3. Tall and narrow stone column for sitting on

## Historical Survival

**Enemies (d3):**

3. Transhuman leader of incomprehensible cognition

**Friends (d3):**

3. Determined preserver of ancient identity

**Complications (d3):**

1. The empire they belonged to was infamous
2. They’re only notionally human
3. They make deals with rebels and exiles of neighboring lands

**Things (d3):**

1. Long-preserved ancient regalia
2. Now-lost relic in common use in their empire
3. Secret lore to unlock hidden storehouses of their people

**Places (d3):**

1. Crumbling and dilapidated public building
2. Crude shrine with too-advanced salvaged ornaments
3. Passages kept secret from the world

**Things (d3):**

**Friends (d3):**

1. Native who knows a cure for the toxin

**Complications (d3):**

3. The toxin is exuded by the substance or objects that are most valuable in the ruin

**Things (d3):**

1. Cure or protection from the poison

3. Damaged ancient relic that’s causing the toxic effect

**Places (d3):**

1. Eerily barren land around the ruin
2. Camp full of poisoned explorers
3. A grove of unnatural life amid the toxic stew

## Treacherous Terrain

A stretch of this area is naturally treacherous and dangerous in its terrain. It may be prone to sinkholes, mudslides, avalanches, quicksand, explosive flora, magical eruptions, boiling geysers, plunging crevasses, or falling stones. This terrain should block the path toward some interesting site or desired destination, or else the party likely has no reason not to simply turn around or go around it.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Malevolent nature-spirit of the place
2. Vile outcast who lairs amid the terrain
3. Monstrous beast native to the dangerous area

**Friends (d3):**

1. Native guide who knows a way through
2. Explorer fascinated by the terrain
3. Traveler forced to find a way through

**Complications (d3):**

1. The perils manifest on a particular little-known schedule
2. The perils sometimes uncover lodes of valuable materials
3. The perils are an accidental legacy of an ancient malfunction

**Things (d3):**

1. Map of the safe way through
2. Device to protect against the peril
3. Ancient treasure hidden within the peril

**Places (d3):**

1. Small safe zone inside the terrain
2. Ruined road or path amid the peril
3. Site of a camp destroyed by the peril

## Twisted Fauna

The fauna in the area has been warped by some power, and is now dangerous to other forms of life. A lingering curse may have twisted the beasts, as might have ancient techno-sorcerous waste, Outsider manipulation, half-faded arratus, mad wizardry, religious cult manipulation, or ancient artistic goals. The beasts probably have some unifying characteristic or trait imbued by the process that warped them.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Savage beast-tamer
2. Hideously intelligent god- beast
3. The mad power that warped the beasts in the first place

**Friends (d3):**

1. Native trying to cope with the fauna
2. Hunter determined to bag one
3. Scholar trying to find the cause of the change

**Complications (d3):**

1. The beasts are very useful or valuable in some way
2. The beasts were once humans
3. The beasts masquerade as normal animals

**Things (d3):**

1. Loot of a victim of the beasts
2. The thing that changed them is a treasure itself
3. Trove coincidentally located in their territory

**Places (d3):**

1. Fetid lair of the beasts
2. Grove scarred by their activities
3. Tainted site that birthed them

## Uncanny Weather

The area is affected by unnatural or magical weather conditions. It may be a pocket of snowy wasteland in a jungle, an area racked by cataclysmic storms, a shifted zone of space that overlaps with some alien world’s atmosphere, an area of perpetual balmy summer, or some other obvious anomaly. Natives may have figured out some way to exploit this condition, or it might be too dangerous for regular occupation.

**Enemies (d3):**

**Friends (d3):**

3. Entrepreneur trying to profit by it

**Complications (d3):**

**Things (d3):**

3. Abandoned loot from the original inhabitants of the area

**Places (d3):**

## Working Wreckage

A Working once was in the area, but has long since collapsed, decayed, or been destroyed by others. It’s at least building-sized, with some Workings being full- scale megastructures raised by ancient empires. While the Working’s original effect has long dissipated, pockets or fragments of it may still persist in the area, along with the automatons or servitor-people once charged with maintaining the site.

**Enemies (d3):**

3. Brutal chief of the natives mutated by its power

**Friends (d3):**

3. Local from the surviving caretakers

**Complications (d3):**

1. It would cause a regional catastrophe if re- activated
2. Even its remnant effects have large consequences
3. It was ruined when it was re- tuned to create a different effect

**Things (d3):**

**Places (d3):**

## Zealot Colony

There is a settlement of radical religious or ideological zealots here, ones too extreme to be tolerated in civilized lands. They may not necessarily be hostile, and they might have need of outside assistance, but their principles are likely to be bizarre or abhorrent to the PCs. Most such colonies are bent on creating new societies that may not actually be physically or psychologically possible for unaltered humanity.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Charismatic demagogue
2. Iron-fisted hereditary dictator
3. Dreamer willing to break any number of eggs for their theoretical omelet

**Friends (d3):**

1. Local who quietly wants out
2. Reformer trying to temper things
3. Relative of a now-trapped member

**Complications (d3):**

1. They’ve recently acquired an object or an alliance that gives them great power
2. The ostensible leader is just a puppet of the real and more pragmatic boss
3. They’re beginning to purity spiral into violent factions

**Things (d3):**

1. Desperately-necessary supply cache
2. Wealth given up for the group
3. Product manufactured by near-slave members

**Places (d3):**

1. Grand temple or ideological hall amid shanties
2. Mass public meeting
3. Prison for deviants
