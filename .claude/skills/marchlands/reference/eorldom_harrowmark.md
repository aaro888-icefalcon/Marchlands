# Eorldom: the Harrowmark

The eorldom of the Harrowmark — the Heath, held by Eorl Hereward, the realm's barrow-frontier. This file covers the Harrowmark as a place: the frontier seat of Harrowfast, Hereward's neglected court, the three thane houses, the moor-and-barrow lands, and the local hooks. The realm frame and the marriage/feud web are in `realm_titles.md`; the barrow-folk clock is in `active_threats.md`.

## The Harrowmark eorldom — essence

The Harrowmark is the Heath — the moor-and-bog plateau north of Eldermouth, riddled with the barrows of old kings, surfaced with the bog-iron the whole realm depends on, and held by the eorldom the rest of Hethrun would rather not think about. It is a hard frontier: thin-settled, poor, conservative, Old-Faith to the bone, and watched over by an eorl who believes — correctly — that the comfortable lowland court has forgotten the Heath exists. Three thane houses hold its thanelands. Beneath the Harrowmark's whole life runs one fact: the barrows are no longer entirely quiet, and the Harrowmark is the realm's only watch against what stirs in them.

## Harrowfast — the seat

Harrowfast is a frontier burh of roughly 10,000 at the Heath's southern edge, where the lowland ends and the moor begins — Eorl Hereward's seat, a fortified muster-town of grey stone and turf-roofed halls behind a stone wall that is the only real wall in the eorldom. It is poor and plain and built to hold, and small by an eorl-seat's measure. Two pressures define it.

**Border Town.** Harrowfast is the frontier against the Heath itself. It is the muster-point for the barrow-watch, the place the deep-Heath watch-forts are supplied from, and the last safe roof before the moor. Its fyrd drills oftener than its size warrants, its gates are barred at dusk by old custom, and its people have the wary practical hardness of folk who live where the wilderness has teeth.

**Tradition-Bound.** The Heath keeps the old ways harder than anywhere in Hethrun. The Veiled Mother priesthood is strong and respected here, the seasonal barrow-observances are kept without apology, and the Codex has barely a footing — the one small Codex chapel in Harrowfast is half-empty and its priest knows it. The Harrowmark does not keep the old rites out of mere conservatism; it keeps them because the people who stopped keeping them, in the old stories, did not last.

## Harrowfast — quarters and notable places

Hereward's Hall is a long grey hall of stone and turf at the burh's heart — austere, well-kept, hung with the watch-banners of the deep-Heath forts and little else. The Muster-Yard before it is where the barrow-watch is called and the fort-companies rotated. The Veiled Mother's House on the burh's edge is the senior shrine of the Heath, larger and busier than the Codex chapel, where the season-rites are sung and the cunning-folk gather. The Iron-Market is where the bog-iron of the Heath is weighed and sold south — the eorldom's one real source of coin, and a sore one. The Wayhouse at the north gate is the last inn before the moor, where guides are hired and travellers learn, if they listen, which ground not to walk.

## Harrowfast — significant locals

Full NPC detail is in `npcs.md`. Resident in the eorldom: Eorl Hereward, the Heath frontier-warden, grim and plain and bitterly certain the lowland has abandoned him. **Gythwin the Cunning**, the Heath's great cunning-woman, whose hut stands "in a hollow no one finds the same way twice," who keeps the deepest barrow-lore and the truce with the barrow-folk, and whom even Hereward defers to on the Heath's old matters. Seeded local figures: Mother Eadlin, the senior Veiled Mother priestess of the Heath, who keeps the season-rites and is increasingly afraid they are no longer enough; Captain Beornoth, Hereward's barrow-watch captain, an exhausted veteran rotating too few men through too many forts; and Hroth the Guide, the most trusted moor-guide at the Wayhouse, who has lost two apprentices to the Heath and will not say to what.

## Eorl Hereward's court

Hereward's Hall runs on two faults.

**Outside Threat.** The barrows are waking. The seals on the deep pre-Aureothan barrows — kept by the Veiled Mother's observances and by the watch-forts — are loosening, and the Harrowmark's whole court is organised around that slow emergency: the fort-rotations, the season-rites, the moor-guides, the watch. Hereward takes the threat with deadly seriousness, because he has seen what comes out of an unsealed barrow, and the rest of the realm does not believe him.

**Dwindling Resources.** The Harrowmark is starved. It is poor to begin with — bog-iron is its only wealth, and the iron's profit flows south to lowland merchants — and the Eldermouth regency, absorbed in the Coronation, sends the Heath nothing: no coin, no spears, no recognition that the barrow-watch is the realm's shield as much as its own. Hereward's court is undermanned, his watch-forts under-supplied, and his patience with the lowland nearly gone. The household: Hereward; his eorlswife, Lady Burgthryth, who manages the hall's thin accounts; and his heir, Aethelm — a grim capable young man already commanding a fort-company on the deep moor.

## House Blackfen — Harrowmark thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Blackfen, a delving-hall on the richest bog-iron grounds of the central Heath; a black iron-bar.
**Head:** Thane Osric of Blackfen — a hard, grasping, impatient man of fifty who measures everything, including the Heath itself, by what can be drawn out of it.
**Character — Heedless Greed.** Blackfen holds the bog-iron lands, and bog-iron is the one wealth of the Harrowmark. Osric has driven the delvings harder and deeper every year — more workers, more pits, the digging pushed into ground the old observances marked off — because the lowland's hunger for iron is endless and Blackfen grows rich on feeding it.
**Wants:** more iron, more pits, more profit; the digging never slowed.
**Secret:** Osric knows the omens are real. He knows the deep delvings have broken into barrow-ground, knows the workers' sicknesses and the bad ground and the night-noises trace to his own pits, and he conceals all of it — because to stop digging is to ruin the house.
**Coronation lean:** regency-ish, for the trade-stability that keeps the iron selling; really only self.
**Ties:** Blackfen's delvings are, in plain fact, a cause of the barrow-folk's anger (see `active_threats.md`); its iron feeds the Toxic Economy of Eldermouth.
**Hook:** a party tracing the barrow-folk's stirrings finds the trail starts in Blackfen's pits. Stopping Osric — by persuasion, by exposure, or by force — is a tier-1-to-2 problem, and Osric will fight to keep digging.

## House Greymoor — Harrowmark thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Greymoor, an old watch-hall on the deep northern Heath; a grey watch-fire.
**Head:** Thane Aldhelm of Greymoor — an old, upright, weather-worn man of sixty-two who has given his whole life to a charge the realm no longer values.
**Character — Exhausted Vigil.** House Greymoor holds a hereditary charge older than the eorldom: it keeps the chain of watch-forts on the deep Heath that, with the Veiled Mother's observances, holds the barrow-folk to their barrows. The charge has kept Greymoor honest, poor, and proud for three centuries — and it has very nearly used the house up.
**Wants:** the watch-forts held and manned; the realm to admit, before it is too late, that the barrow-watch is real and necessary.
**Secret:** Greymoor can no longer man its forts. Too few men, too little coin — and Aldhelm has been hiding the gaps in the watch, leaving forts dark and rotations falsified, because to admit the watch has holes is to invite the barrow-folk through them and to shame three centuries of duty.
**Coronation lean:** regency, because lawful order serves the watch — and, like Hereward, genuinely winnable by any faction that sends real support to the Heath.
**Ties:** Greymoor's watch is the honest counterpart to House Eldgrave's secret pact; Aldhelm and Hereward are old comrades in a losing labour.
**Hook:** Greymoor is the Harrowmark's honest bulwark and a natural ally — an old man holding a frontier with too little, who will trust a competent party with the truth he cannot tell his eorl. The dark, unmanned forts are a tier-1 entry into the barrow threat.

## House Eldgrave — Harrowmark thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Eldgrave, a hall set among the barrow-fields of the eastern Heath; a green barrow-mound.
**Head:** Thane Wynstan of Eldgrave — a calm, certain, soft-spoken man of fifty-five who carries himself like a priest of something, and is.
**Character — Inherited Pact.** Eldgrave's lands are the most barrow-thick in the Harrowmark, and they are also the safest — its harvests hold, its cattle thrive, its nights are quiet. The reason is an inheritance Wynstan regards as a sacred duty: for many generations House Eldgrave has kept its own observances with the barrow-folk, its own offerings left at its own howes, and Wynstan believes with serene conviction that this is simply the proper old way, and that the rest of the Heath suffers because it has let the true rites lapse.
**Wants:** the old offerings kept; Eldgrave's lands safe; the "duty" honoured and, ideally, restored across the whole Heath.
**Secret:** the offerings have escalated. What an honest ancestor began as ale and grain has, generation by generation, become worse — and Eldgrave's lands are safe not because the rites are holy but because the barrow-folk, fed and courted there, take their hunger to the neighbours instead. Eldgrave is not holding the barrow-folk back; it is steering them, and it does not entirely admit this even to itself.
**Coronation lean:** disengaged — Wynstan cares nothing for the throne; the Heath's old ways are his only politics.
**Ties:** the dark mirror of House Greymoor's honest watch; what Eldgrave does feeds the barrow-folk clock in `active_threats.md`, and Gythwin the Cunning suspects the house and has not yet moved.
**Hook:** the discovery that the Heath's danger is partly *cultivated* — that a respected, pious-seeming thane house has been managing the barrow-folk by feeding them, and aiming their hunger at others — is a tier-2 turn in the barrow arc, and a hard one to prove against a man who believes he is doing right.

## Minor settlements of the eorldom

Seeded for texture; each is a name, community tags, and a hook.

**Iron-Delve** — extraction camp, *Oppressed · Doomed.* The largest of Blackfen's bog-iron camps — a sprawl of turf huts where the extraction-workers live and sicken; the Codex will not treat them free and the Veiled Mother's cunning-folk cannot reach them all, and the camp has the grim patience of people who expect no rescue.

**Howegate** — village, *Tradition-Bound · Holy Site.* A moor village built around a cluster of well-kept Hethrun-era cairns, where the season-rites are sung most faithfully in the realm and Old Faith pilgrims come to pour the fyrd-libations; the folk here know exactly which barrows must never be approached.

**Stane** — hamlet, *Outpost · Sealed Lips.* A shepherds' hamlet on the deep moor, supplied through one of Greymoor's watch-forts, half-forgotten and wholly self-reliant; lately its people have taken to barring their doors at dusk and will not say what they have heard on the wind.

## Minor courts and lesser halls

**Mother Eadlin's priesthood** — court tag *Old Authority.* The Veiled Mother's House at Harrowfast is a power the eorl does not command — Eadlin's priestesses keep the season-rites that hold the barrows, and on the Heath's old matters their word outweighs Hereward's.

**The barrow-watch fort-captains** — court tag *Caretaker Regime.* Captain Beornoth and the company-captains of the deep-Heath forts form a court of their own — soldiers running a frontier on too little, whose practical loyalty Hereward needs and whose honest reports he does not always get (see House Greymoor).

## The eorldom's lands — the Heath

The Harrowmark's lands are the Heath itself: a moor-and-bog plateau riddled with the barrows of old kings — Hethrun-era cairns, Saxon-era long-mounds, and, deepest and most dangerous, the pre-Aureothan barrows that hold the remains of the peoples Aureoth drove into exile. Bog-iron lies at the surface; the Veiled Mother priesthood keeps ancient observances at certain sites because not keeping them would wake the barrow-folk. Notable sites:

**The Heath barrows** — the three layers, across the whole moor. The Hethrun cairns are easily known and safely visited; the Saxon long-mounds are well-mapped; the deep pre-Aureothan barrows, marked by carved entrance-stones, are shunned, and their seals are the ones now loosening. The mass-grave cairn of the fyrd sacrificed in the barbarian invasion is here, and fyrdmen pour ale-libations at it. *Multiple dungeon sites; the deep barrows are tier-3 expeditions.*

**The Black Pit Mines** — three abandoned Aureothan tin-and-lead mines in a cluster on the western Heath, partly collapsed, partly connected by old workings. The Old Faith avoids them; Codex folk think them merely dangerous; Gythwin will not say what is in them but discourages all approach. *Tier 1–2 dungeon network — something has been down there since Aureoth fell, and it is hungry.*

**The Wivelfen** — a stretch of bog some four miles across in the deep northern Heath, where an Aureothan arcane experiment went wrong and was sealed rather than cleaned up. The fauna are altered — amphibians grown vast, herbs that heal above their tier and harm with it, birds that will not enter — and Aldwin, examining it once, judged the original working *purposive* and still maintained by something. *Tier 2–3 expedition; it has killed three Hethrun expeditions in fifty years.*

**Gythwin's Hut** — the cunning-woman's dwelling, "in a hollow no one finds the same way twice." The moving path is genuine magic. Gythwin is the Heath's deepest authority on the barrow-folk and the single most useful person in the realm for a party walking into the barrow arc. *Visit-site of major importance.* (Bestiary: the Barrow-Dead, `bestiary.md` Group 1A.)

## Adventure hooks

- **The barrows wake** (tier 1–3, the Heath's central arc) — the deep barrow-seals are loosening, and the Harrowmark is the only watch against it; the arc runs from a single dark watch-fort up to the deep barrows themselves, and connects to the barrow-folk clock in `active_threats.md`.
- **Gythwin's warning** (tier 1) — the Heath's cunning-woman will warn — and, to the right party, guide — those walking toward the barrow danger; reaching her hut is the first step of the arc.
- **The Blackfen pits** (tier 1–2) — a party tracing the barrow-folk's anger finds it starts in Osric of Blackfen's bog-iron delvings, dug into barrow-ground for profit.
- **Greymoor's dark forts** (tier 1) — Thane Aldhelm's watch-forts stand half-manned and falsified; an honest old man will trust a competent party with the gaps he cannot admit to his eorl.
- **The Ashcombe curse** (tier 1, cross-eorldom) — crown-thane House Ashcombe (see `eorldom_eldermouth.md`) is cursed by a barrow its ancestor robbed; the restitution that could lift it lies on the Heath, through Gythwin and the barrow-folk.

## At a glance

The Harrowmark is the frontier eorldom — the realm's barrow-watch, poor and neglected, held by an eorl whose Witan vote is *winnable* by whoever finally takes the Heath's danger seriously. Its three houses are the threat in miniature: Blackfen wakes the barrows for profit, Greymoor exhausts itself honestly fighting them, Eldgrave secretly feeds them. Crisis stance: soft regency, bitter, and for sale to recognition rather than coin. The Harrowmark is the campaign's barrow-horror front and the ground of the Ashcombe curse. Bestiary groups operating here: the Barrow-Dead (1A) across the moor, the altered fauna of the Wivelfen, and the Arrival-Hunters (A1). For the realm frame and the web see `realm_titles.md`; for the barrow-folk arc see `active_threats.md`.
