# Noble Court Generator

Rollable tables for producing new thane houses, court and household rosters, and intra-court conflicts during play — and for mixing the results into the intrigue board distributed across the `eorldom_<name>.md` files and `realm_titles.md`.

Two depths are supported. **Fast** uses the curated d-tables in this file and takes seconds. **Deep** uses the full WWN character tags (see `faction_generator.md` Step 4) and the full WWN court tags (Section 2 below) — 50 entries each, with Ambition/Power/Dread and Enemies/Friends/Complications/Things/Places sub-tables — for richer results. Roll fast when a house is a minor piece; roll deep when a house or court will carry an arc.

All results are raw material. Synthesize, don't transcribe — a roll is a prompt, not a sentence.

---

## 1. Generate a new thane house

Run steps in order. Reroll any result that collides with an existing house or strains plausibility.

### Step 1 — Seat name

Roll a prefix (d20) and a suffix (d12); combine.

**Prefix (d20):** 1 Ash · 2 Ox · 3 Stone · 4 Grey · 5 Salt · 6 Thorn · 7 Briar · 8 Holt · 9 Raven · 10 Wolf · 11 Hart · 12 Bone · 13 Cold · 14 Storm · 15 Eld · 16 Beck · 17 Fen · 18 Hawk · 19 Marsh · 20 Rook

**Suffix (d12):** 1 -stead · 2 -garth · 3 -holt · 4 -wic · 5 -ham · 6 -tun · 7 -fell · 8 -mere · 9 -dene · 10 -cliff · 11 -stow · 12 -burh

The house is "the thanes of [Seat]." Its banner-mark is usually drawn from the prefix (Ravensgarth flies a raven; Hartdene a hart) — or roll Step 2 for contrast.

### Step 2 — Banner-mark (d20)

Pre-heraldic; a single beast or object. 1 black boar · 2 white hart · 3 raven · 4 grey wolf's-head · 5 three gulls · 6 leaping salmon · 7 crossed oars · 8 golden sheaf · 9 silver key · 10 gold flame · 11 red hand · 12 green oak-leaf · 13 thorn-branch · 14 black wave · 15 ox-head · 16 iron anchor · 17 stooping hawk · 18 bee-skep · 19 war-horn · 20 single star

### Step 3 — Standing (d6)

How the house sits in its eorldom. 1–2 **rising** (gaining land, marriages, fyrd) · 3–4 **stable** (middling, secure) · 5 **declining** (slipping, indebted, or thin of heirs) · 6 **nearly spent** (a name and a cold hall; one crisis from ruin).

Standing sets rough fyrd-strength and hall-moot weight: rising/stable houses muster a real war-band and a vote that matters; declining/spent houses muster little and are vulnerable to debt-leverage or a buyout.

### Step 4 — House character (d20)

The defining trait. Drawn from the WWN character tags; for the full d50 master list with sub-tables, see `faction_generator.md` Step 4.

1 **Bitter Grudge** — an unsettled wrong drives the house's politics
2 **Burnt Out** — the head is spent; the house drifts
3 **Concealed Sin** — a hidden crime in the house's past or present
4 **Double Life** — the house is not what it publicly appears
5 **Family Alienation** — branches or kin estranged, working against each other
6 **Grand Plan** — a patient, multi-generation scheme underway
7 **Great Disappointment** — a failed heir, lost cause, or squandered chance
8 **Hidden Origins** — the house's blood or claim is not what it says
9 **Impending Treachery** — the house is arranging to betray someone
10 **Inherited Duty** — a hereditary charge defines and constrains the house
11 **In Over Their Head** — the house took on something beyond it
12 **Marked for Success** — fortune and favor are flowing to the house
13 **Misplaced Trust** — the house relies on someone it should not
14 **Ruinous Vice** — the head or heir is captured by a vice
15 **Secret Cravings** — the head hungers for something unspoken
16 **Stolen Reputation** — the house's standing rests on a lie
17 **Ticking Bomb** — a hidden problem will detonate if untouched
18 **Unexpected Talent** — a member has a gift far above the house's station
19 **Up From the Gutter** — a new house, low-born, climbing fast
20 **Veiled Backers** — the house is funded or steered by a hidden patron

For a house meant to carry an arc, roll twice and synthesize the pair (a *Grand Plan* + *Veiled Backers* house is a scheme that is itself someone else's instrument).

### Step 5 — Ambition (d8)

What the house reaches for. 1 a vacant or weakly-held seat above its station · 2 a marriage into the eorl's line · 3 mastery of a trade, road, or resource · 4 vengeance on a rival house · 5 a hereditary wrong corrected · 6 simple survival through a crisis it sees coming · 7 the eorldom's real administration, office by office · 8 a buried thing — a relic, a chamber, a secret — and what it promises.

### Step 6 — Secret or vulnerability (d8)

What could break the house. 1 crushing debt held by a rival or a creditor-faction · 2 a faith or loyalty the house must hide · 3 an heir who is ill, unfit, illegitimate, or false · 4 a crime — old or ongoing — that could be proven · 5 a bought or coerced member feeding an outside power · 6 a feud the house cannot afford to have reopened · 7 a bargain with something inhuman, its price now due · 8 the house's whole standing rests on a lie.

### Step 7 — Coronation lean (d6)

1–2 **regency** · 3–4 **Ranulf** · 5 **genuinely uncommitted** (courted by all) · 6 **for sale / coerced** (the real lean belongs to whoever holds the house's debt, secret, or marriage-need). Cross-check against the house's character and secret; let the *real* lean differ from the *public* one where Step 4 or 6 invites it.

### Step 8 — The head

**Name.** Dithematic Saxon-Norse: prefix + suffix.
*Prefix (d20):* 1 Aelf · 2 Ead · 3 Wulf · 4 Beorht · 5 Sige · 6 Os · 7 Cuth · 8 Cyne · 9 Leof · 10 Frith · 11 Here · 12 Hroth · 13 Thor · 14 Gar · 15 Wig · 16 Aethel · 17 Ord · 18 Brun · 19 Sae · 20 Eormen
*Suffix, men (d12):* 1 -ric · 2 -wulf · 3 -here · 4 -stan · 5 -gar · 6 -red · 7 -weard · 8 -helm · 9 -noth · 10 -beorht · 11 -mund · 12 -wine
*Suffix, women (d12):* 1 -gyth · 2 -flaed · 3 -wyn · 4 -hild · 5 -burh · 6 -waru · 7 -thryth · 8 -run · 9 -gifu · 10 -swith · 11 -leofu · 12 -fled

**Manner (d8) — a quick voice trait** (one is enough for a thane; major figures get the full `npc_` treatment): 1 blunt, says exactly what they mean · 2 smooth, never loses a negotiation · 3 grim, keeps every grievance · 4 anxious, studies every guest for danger · 5 tired, holding on past their strength · 6 pious-seeming, soft-voiced, watchful · 7 proud, carries borrowed dignity · 8 bewildered, decent, out of their depth.

### Step 9 — Hook (d6)

How the players touch the house. 1 the house hires the party for honest, well-paid work that brushes its secret · 2 the house's secret is the thing the players were already chasing · 3 the house begs the party's help with the crisis Step 6 named · 4 a rival pays the party to act against the house · 5 the house's hall-moot vote is the prize a patron sends the party to win · 6 the house's heir, marriage, or feud is the lever in a larger scheme.

## 2. Generate a court / household roster

For any noble hall — an eorl's court or a thane's household. The hall's *character* comes from court tags; its *people* come from the office table.

### Court tags — fast (d20 quick-pick)

When a hall is a minor piece, pick two from this d20 quick-list. Read the two together as the hall's two live tensions:

1 Affliction's Mark · 2 Ancestral Obligation · 3 Awkward Birth · 4 Cadet Branches · 5 Dark Secret · 6 Decadent Court · 7 Disputed Inheritance · 8 Excess Heirs · 9 Foreign Ties · 10 Hidden Blight · 11 Inept Ruler · 12 Ministerial Capture · 13 New Generation · 14 Outside Debts · 15 Poisonous Cliques · 16 Priestly Influence · 17 Restive Lessers · 18 Rival Power · 19 Threatened Violence · 20 Waning Wealth

Read the two tags together — they are the hall's two live tensions.

### Court tags — deep (full WWN d100 master + sub-tables)

For richer results — when a house will carry an arc, when the court itself is the scene's center, or when the GM wants layered sub-tables to draw on — use the full d100 set below. Tag names, discussion text, and sub-table entries reproduced from *Worlds Without Number* by Kevin Crawford / Sine Nomine Publishing for personal worldbuilding use. Slight Hethrun parentheticals added where the corpus has a natural court to anchor the tag at; the WWN content itself is unchanged.

**Procedure:** Roll twice on the d100 master table. Read the two tags as parallel tensions or synthesize them into one hybrid pressure. For each rolled tag, also roll d3 on each of its five sub-tables — Enemies, Friends, Complications, Things, Places. Embed those specific entries in the court's file rather than the bare tag name.

## Affliction’s Mark

Some sort of persistent, incurable illness is gnawing at the court. The principle leader might have been felled by it, or a beloved member, or it may be a spreading curse within its circles. Acute diseases will likely have the sufferers desperate to find some cure, while chronic illnesses might need regular infusions of some costly or morally-dubious remedy.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Manipulative quack
2. Amorally desperate parent
3. Secret spreader of plague

**Friends (d3):**

1. Appealing victim
2. Struggling physician
3. Worried spouse-to-be of a victim

**Complications (d3):**

1. The sickness is a direct result of their choices
2. A faction would profit enormously if the current victims died
3. The only known cure comes with a dire moral or material cost

**Things (d3):**

1. Palliative that can slow or ease the disease
2. Proof of its source or cause
3. The price gathered to pay for a perhaps-real cure

**Places (d3):**

1. Hushed and shadowed sickroom
2. Gathering place with an air of forced gaiety
3. Untouched quarters of the recently dead

## Ancestral Obligation

The court was impressed with some great task, duty, or role by its founder, and much of its influence or moral authority hinges on continuing to carry out that task. Its traditions and structure revolve around being able to carry out the work. Failure means disgrace and perhaps tangible penalties as old pacts are broken. Aside from this, neglecting the duty may result in other negative consequences, perhaps to parties other than the court itself.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Schemer who’d profit by the duty’s disruption
2. Rebel who hates the duty and care nothing for the consequences
3. Tyrannical leader who forces others to bear the duty’s cost

**Friends (d3):**

1. Grimly-determined keeper
2. Sympathetic member who suffers from the work
3. Outsider desperately reliant on the work being done well

**Complications (d3):**

1. No one fully understands the duty’s meaning any more
2. The duty comes at a terrible cost
3. Recent calamity has left them too weak for the work

**Things (d3):**

1. Vital tool for carrying out the job
2. Ancient payment for the work
3. Key to lifting the burden

**Places (d3):**

1. Ancient work-chamber
2. Hall emblazoned with symbols of the work
3. Training hall for carrying on the work

## Awesome Legitimacy

The things that are certain in life are death, taxes, and this court. They are important, they have always been important, and they always will be important. They may wax and wane, but no one in their society can imagine them ever ceasing to exist, and at most the locals can only think of seizing control of the court or compelling its cooperation. Its destruction is unthinkable. It may have absolute control of some critical social function, have members of legendary awe, or be viewed as the personal property of some divinity or godlike entity.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Implacable tyrant consecrated by custom
2. Manipulator who cloaks his will in the court’s legitimacy
3. Outsider who’ll ruin the court despite the chaos such a thing would create

**Friends (d3):**

1. Court member keenly aware of their responsibility
2. Local haplessly oppressed by the court
3. Outsider who deals cautiously with the court

**Complications (d3):**

1. The court really is as indispensable as it seems
2. The source of its untouchability is weakening
3. If it goes down it’s taking much of local society with it

**Things (d3):**

1. Regalia of ancient authority
2. Dusty deed to a rich property
3. Wealth of a failed rebel against it

**Places (d3):**

1. Long-forgotten spare throne room
2. Ancient archive
3. Gathering place in use for ages

## Awkward Birth

A pregnancy is roiling the court and causing severe problems. It may be that a member is pregnant by the wrong man, or a pairing took place that was shocking to society, or a newly-born heir is showing signs of inherited Blight, or a new heir suddenly excludes a displeased prior incumbent. The court can’t hush it forever, and abortion is either impractical, unacceptable, or too dangerous to the mother. This may not stop certain members from trying.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Elder who would see the problem “solved”
2. Murderously suspicious husband
3. Demoted heir

**Friends (d3):**

1. Naive young lover
2. Desperate hidden paramour
3. Worried sire of the unwanted child

**Complications (d3):**

1. Secret accusations of illegitimacy are being passed through the court
2. The disgraced husband’s family is enraged
3. The pregnancy is just a tool the mother is using to get her way

**Things (d3):**

1. Proof of the real father
2. Blackmail material on the court’s leadership
3. Wealth stolen away by the former presumptive heir

**Places (d3):**

1. Nursery much worse-appointed than expected
2. Prison-like room for the expectant mother
3. Stronghold estate of the disappointed former heir

The leadership has been led astray by one or more pretty girls or boys. They dote on their paramours, granting them extravagant favors and imperiling the court with the lengths they go to in order to please their beloved. Those not given to romantic dizziness may be enraptured by more physical talents, so occupied in the business of night that nothing is done in the day.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Rival who sent them the playthings as gifts
2. Ruthlessly manipulative lover
3. Murderously angry neglected spouse

**Friends (d3):**

1. Sadly displaced former favorite
2. Frustrated underling
3. Disapproving moralist

**Complications (d3):**

1. The paramours have a supernatural edge in their charms
2. Their spouse likes them distracted so as to carry out their own plans
3. The court is fighting over influence with the paramours rather than the leadership

**Things (d3):**

1. Priceless luxury demanded by a lover
2. Trinket of great worth abandoned by them
3. Wealth gathered to build some pleasure for them

**Places (d3):**

1. Seraglio of constant liveliness
2. Court hall abandoned by the leadership
3. Pleasure-garden redolent of luxury

## Cadet Branches

The authority of the court is somewhat splintered, with multiple cadet branches of the family, enterprise, or department having their own share of its power. These branches are likely to struggle over control of the main court, with some of them perhaps preferring the court’s dissolution rather than allowing a hated rival to gain control of it.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Disgraced scion with a plan for revenge
2. Ruler who hates a particular branch
3. Scheming leader of a minor branch

**Friends (d3):**

1. Bastard offspring seeking recognition
2. Visionary from a cadet branch
3. Rightful leader denied their place

**Complications (d3):**

1. One “cadet” branch actually has a right to rule but is too weak to claim it
2. The main court plays the branches against each other
3. Outside rivals want the court to remain splintered

**Things (d3):**

1. Proof of a branch’s illegitimacy
2. Bribe paid to get a branch to cooperate
3. Blackmail material on the main court’s ruler

**Places (d3):**

1. Ostentatious branch estate
2. More modest but far older main estate
3. Court hall with places for all the branches

## Capricious Orders

The court is issuing unreasonable orders to those under its authority, demanding excessive tribute, unreasonable obediences, or similar sacrifices. The court leader may be new and over-confident of their power, or the court may be in dire trouble and needs more resources, or rival forces may be goading or manipulating the court into overplaying its hand out of recklessness or ignorance of the true state of affairs.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Arrogant new lord
2. Mentally unstable or bewitched ruler
3. Scheming advisor in a rival power’s pay

**Friends (d3):**

1. Mistreated subject of the court
2. Frustrated moderate member
3. Reasonable rival of the current ruler

**Complications (d3):**

1. The demands are all to fulfill a great secret plan
2. The demands are punishment for a failed rebellion
3. The court doesn’t want to make the demands but is somehow forced to do so

**Things (d3):**

1. Heavy load of tribute
2. Stolen treasure that the court demanded be produced
3. Privately-diverted wealth from the demands

**Places (d3):**

1. Angry court reception hall
2. Public plaza with darkly-grumbling locals
3. Enterprise closed down due to a failure to deliver on the demands

## Cultural Insignia

The court produces some famously revered cultural product, such as literature, poetry, painting, sculpture, music, dance, or other art form. Their members may have a direct tradition of production, or they may be the critical patrons of a tradition of artists or a school that produces the product. The product wins them respect among the cultured, and many would be glad to assist them in exchange for the luster of association.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Ruler who would crush rival schools
2. Chief artist gone mad with jealousy or megalomania
3. Rival artist determined to destroy the tradition

**Friends (d3):**

1. Earnest young artistic genius
2. Poor court member determined to patronize as best they can
3. Unworldly aesthete concerned only with art

**Complications (d3):**

1. The art has actual magical powers when well- made
2. The art encourages or glorifies some reprehensible cause
3. The artists are savagely factional in their different schools

**Things (d3):**

1. Lost legendary work of art
2. Proof that a famed piece of art is a forgery of the original
3. Hidden masterwork of a dead genius

**Places (d3):**

1. Salon dedicated to appreciating the art
2. Workshop where the art is made
3. Grand public structure adorned with the art

## Daring Ambition

The court has a grand ambition which is driving its actions, and it’s making a major bet on its ability to achieve its goal. Failure will mean catastrophe for the court, while success promises great rewards. Not all the members of the court are necessarily sharing the same risks and potential payoff, however, and some may be paying more than they’d ever profit.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Megalomaniacal leader
2. Well-meaning but hopelessly optimistic ruler
3. Secret manipulator driving the ambition

**Friends (d3):**

1. Sympathetic necessary sacrifice for the cause
2. Court member struggling to do their share
3. Ally who’d profit if the court succeeded

**Complications (d3):**

1. The court thinks the ambition is one thing but the leadership knows it’s another
2. The ambition’s success would be a poisoned gift
3. A faction plans to monopolize the benefit or shift all the loss

**Things (d3):**

1. Critical material for the ambition
2. Costly remnants of a failed effort
3. A valuable result of progress in the ambition

**Places (d3):**

1. Enterprise or establishment dedicated to the cause
2. Newly-constructed edifice
3. Hall optimistically prepared to celebrate victory

## Dark Secret

The court has a dark secret that would cost it dearly if it were to be revealed. Loss of influence, station, money, or power might all be consequent. It may involve old treacheries, hidden crimes, secret illegitimacies, dark pacts, or harsh choices that were unavoidable at the time. The uppermost ranks likely know the truth, but the lesser members may have nothing but unpleasant suspicions.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. The villain they’re allied with
2. Ruthless keeper of secrets

**Friends (d3):**

1. Secretly worried lesser court member

3. Bitter survivor of the court’s crime

**Complications (d3):**

1. The act was acceptable or normal at the time it was committed
2. Revealing it would implicate other important powers
3. The court’s leadership has tried to quietly make amends for it

**Things (d3):**

1. Proof of the heinous crime
2. Precious treasure acquired through the sin
3. Valuable relic taken from a victim of the crime

**Places (d3):**

1. Secret archive full of dark evidence
2. Noble monument built on a dark substrate
3. Private sanctum for only the inner circle

## Decadent Court

The court is too absorbed in their pleasures and indulgences. Their cooperation is purchased with coin or flesh, their disports grow ever more costly and excessive, and even the minor members are getting accustomed to shameful enjoyments. Their power is being turned toward facilitating their membership’s appetites rather than fulfilling whatever role got them their status in the first place.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Jaded and sensation-starved ruler
2. Cruel panderer for the court’s hungers
3. Monstrous entity that sponsors or encourages vice

**Friends (d3):**

1. Escaped would-be subject
2. Inquisitive outside investigator
3. Frustrated native in need of the court’s execution of its duty

**Complications (d3):**

1. The court’s leadership is uninvolved and using the vices to blackmail and control their underlings
2. The court’s trying to spread its vices in its own social stratum and profit accordingly
3. A faction is taking things much too far even for their brethren and involving occult powers

**Things (d3):**

1. Debt-slave contracts
2. Hideous but precious idol or art object
3. Valuable drug made from slaves

**Places (d3):**

1. Debauched salon
2. Court public hall made sinister
3. Pit where the human chattel are kept

## Devil’s Bargain

The court made a bargain that’s now coming back to haunt them. Some pact with an outside power gave them a crucial edge or a desperately-needed boost, but now it needs to provide services, favors, or help that could end up destroying it or costing its leadership dearly. The pact was probably secret at the time, and even revealing it exists could have dire repercussions.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Vile entity it pacted with
2. Ruler planning on foisting the price off on a victim
3. Heartless creditor who cares nothing for the damage it does

**Friends (d3):**

1. Undeserving victim of the price
2. Rival who suspects something is up
3. Enemy of the power they pacted with

**Complications (d3):**

1. Only a small faction of the court knows about the bargain
2. The current leadership didn’t know about the deal until it was threatened into compliance
3. The power they pacted with is a mortal enemy of their society

**Things (d3):**

1. Blackmail material the pact granter is using to control them
2. Potent relic granted in the deal
3. Heavy tribute due to the pacting power

**Places (d3):**

1. Monument to the victory the pact secretly gave them
2. Secret chambers for the pact’s granter
3. Shrine to the dark entity they propitiated

## Diplomatic Demands

Some rival or outside power is making demands on the court that they cannot easily ignore. It might involve some prior offense in need of reparations, a past deal that’s come due, or a price required for a favor that the court desperately needs. These demands are more than the court can easily pay, and determining where the burden will fall most heavily is a matter of fierce internal politics.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Scheming shifter of burdens
2. Outside rival bent on beggaring the court
3. Incompetent diplomat who’s only making things worse

**Friends (d3):**

1. Victim of unfair exactions
2. Harried diplomat with no cards to play
3. Native who desperately needs the deal to go through

**Complications (d3):**

1. The exactions demanded are purely punitive
2. There’s a specific temporary reason the court has to acknowledge the demands
3. Another party could give a better deal if they were persuaded

**Things (d3):**

1. Huge down payment on the demands
2. The precious object the court is trying to acquire
3. Proof that the demands are unjustified

**Places (d3):**

1. Diplomatic retreat full of tense people
2. Site of the problem causing the demands
3. Hushed court hall full of whispers

## Disputed Inheritance

The court’s rule or property is due to pass on to the next generation, but there are multiple claimants to it. Each has some colorable legitimacy, and factions and secret supporters are doubtless rife in the court. They’re well-balanced enough that brute force seems a risky route to take, though matters are rapidly coming to a head as the need to establish a new leader is intense.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Cruel and vicious heir
2. Incompetent heir who has the best claim
3. Outside enemy backing the strife

**Friends (d3):**

1. Most talented heir but with the worst position
2. Hapless heir in dire need of protection
3. Loyal retainer trying to avert disaster

**Complications (d3):**

1. Legitimacy rests on possession of a now-lost relic
2. If the struggle lasts much longer the patrimony will be ruined or lost
3. A selfish regent is exploiting the court during the interregnum

**Things (d3):**

1. Bribe intended for the faction leaders
2. Precious relic of legitimacy
3. The real and verified will

**Places (d3):**

1. Tense and angry court meeting hall
2. Heir’s country estate
3. Court plaza where the factions don’t mingle

## Excess Heirs

While the ruler remains vigorous, they have too many legitimate heirs for the court to comfortably absorb. There’s not enough wealth or power to give the losers a dignified station, or else the losing heirs may expect to be executed, or the heirs are locked in a court-disrupting battle to force their desired outcome. Various heirs might be backed by rival outside powers searching for a convenient catspaw.

**Enemies (d3):**

1. Cruel but talented crown prince
2. Scion in league with dark powers
3. Secretly murderous spare heir

**Friends (d3):**

1. Capable but hard-pressed candidate
2. Hapless ruler unable to enforce a choice
3. Vengeful retainer of a murdered heir

**Complications (d3):**

1. The ruler desires a Darwinian culling of heirs
2. The court desperately needs the heirs to cooperate to overcome a threat
3. Several heirs were formerly unknown

**Things (d3):**

1. Proof of a candidate’s illegitimacy
2. Bribe from an enemy power
3. Relic meant to eliminate a rival

**Places (d3):**

1. Well-fortified home of an heir
2. Court hall where the factions are well-separated
3. Ritual occasion of enforced amity

## False Prize

The court is trying to obtain a goal or ambition that will actually be disastrous for it. Either through political miscalculation, ignorance of the truth, or willful blindness to the consequences, they’re plunging headlong towards calamity. Rivals might be secretly aiding them in their purpose, while factions within the court may be uselessly protesting the danger of the path they’ve chosen.

## 3. Generate a conflict

For a fresh quarrel between houses, within a court, or across an eorldom.

### Conflict type (d12)

1 **Disputed inheritance** — two claimants, an ambiguous will or hall-moot precedent
2 **Marriage feud** — a match promised and broken, or made and resented
3 **Border quarrel** — disputed land, meadow, water, or road-toll between two houses
4 **Blood debt** — a killing unanswered; weregild owed and refused
5 **Religious split** — Codex against Old Faith inside a house or court
6 **Bought vote** — a house's hall-moot voice secretly purchased
7 **Hidden defector** — a house arranging to betray its eorl or its bloc
8 **Succession ambiguity** — an ailing or dying lord, an unsettled heir
9 **Debt leverage** — one house holds another's debt and means to use it
10 **Fostering dispute** — a fostered child withheld, mistreated, or used as a hostage
11 **Accusation of a crime** — a house charged, rightly or not, with theft, murder, or treason
12 **Rival claim** — a cadet branch, a forgotten kinsman, or a returned exile asserting a right

### Stakes (d8)

What is actually at risk. 1 a seat or title · 2 land, a road, or a revenue · 3 a marriage and the alliance it carries · 4 a house's standing and good name · 5 a hall-moot vote and the eorldom's balance · 6 a life — an heir's, a rival's, a hostage's · 7 a secret that ruins a house if it surfaces · 8 the loyalty of a third house watching to see who wins.

### The lever (d8)

What would resolve — or escalate — it. 1 a document: a will, a letter, a charter, a debt-bond · 2 a marriage that satisfies or inflames both sides · 3 a death, timely or untimely · 4 a hall-moot or Witan ruling · 5 an outside patron's coin or threat · 6 a witness who can confirm or deny · 7 a weregild or restitution paid · 8 a buried thing dug up — a relic, a chamber, a corpse.

### Escalation if ignored (d6)

1 it festers quietly; positions harden · 2 a third house is drawn in and takes a side · 3 it reaches the eorl's hall-moot and demands a ruling · 4 it turns violent — a killing, a raid, a hall burned · 5 a faction in the Coronation crisis adopts it as a proxy fight · 6 it forces an eorl to commit, shifting the Coronation Clock.

## 4. Mixing into the board

Generated content is only useful once it is *attached*. Apply these rules.

**Place the house.** Assign a new thane house to an eorldom (or the crown demesne). Its Coronation lean goes straight into the relevant `eorldom_<name>.md` file, and the realm-wide board in `realm_titles.md` — and a freshly generated house is most interesting when it does *not* match its bloc's expected lean.

**Hang it off a web thread.** Roll d8 to tie a new house to the existing board: 1 it feuds with a named house · 2 it is married into a named house · 3 it fosters, or is fostering with, a named house · 4 it holds, or owes, a named house's debt · 5 it shares a named house's secret or faith · 6 it is a rival to a named house's ambition · 7 it is a cadet branch of a named house or an eorl's line · 8 it is the hidden counterparty to a thread already on the board (the buyer behind a bought vote, the patron behind a veiled-backed house).

**Splice two character tags.** A house with one tag is a piece; a house with two synthesized tags is an arc. *Grand Plan + Stolen Reputation* — a house climbing on a lie. *Inherited Duty + Burnt Out* — an ancient charge its keepers can no longer bear. *Concealed Sin + Marked for Success* — a house whose fortune is the wage of its crime.

**Chain conflicts.** A conflict's escalation result (3.Escalation) is the seed of the next conflict — a hall-moot ruling creates a loser with a fresh Bitter Grudge; a proxy fight pulls in a third house with its own stakes. Two conflicts sharing a house, a lever, or a stake are one larger intrigue; run them as a single arc with two faces.

**Convert a conflict to a clock.** Any conflict that will resolve with or without the players becomes a clock (4–8 segments). Advance it on the escalation triggers in 3.Escalation; let player action retard it. Add live clocks to the campaign clock-set alongside those in `active_threats.md`.

**Keep the board honest.** New houses obey the same rule as the built ones: the *public* lean and the *real* lean may differ, and the gap between them is where the players earn their intrigue. Never generate a house whose loyalties are simply what they appear to be.
