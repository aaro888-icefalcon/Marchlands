# Faction Generator

Random rolls for generating any faction the campaign needs — a smuggler ring in Stonebay, a Burning One cell newly arrived in Brackenford, a mercenary company camping at Wealdham, a hedge-cult on the Heath, a foreign cabal under a Tassard caravan-cover. Built on the WWN character-tag system (faction-applied) layered with Blades-in-the-Dark tier/hold/status and project-clock mechanics. For new noble houses use `noble_court_generator.md`; for the individual NPC who heads the faction use `npc_generator.md`.

The WWN character-tag content reproduced below is derived from *Worlds Without Number* by Kevin Crawford / Sine Nomine Publishing. Faction-state mechanics (Tier, Hold, Status, project clocks) are derived from *Blades in the Dark* by John Harper / One Seven Design.

## How to use this generator

The eleven-step procedure produces a complete faction sheet. Roll each step in order; the rolls compound into a coherent picture. If a roll contradicts an earlier one, treat it as friction inside the faction rather than an error — that friction is often the most interesting part of the result.

1. **Faction type** (d12) — what kind of organisation.
2. **Tier** (1–6) — its scale and reach.
3. **Hold** — Strong (secure) or Weak (aspiring, sliding, contested).
4. **Character tags** — roll d50 twice (or pick from the d20 quick-list in `noble_court_generator.md` Step 4). For each tag, roll Ambition (d3), Power (d3), Dread (d3).
5. **Methods** (d8) — how the faction operates.
6. **Power base** (d12) — where in the realm it sits.
7. **Leadership** — one named figure (use `npc_generator.md` for full voice).
8. **Status vs party** (−3 to +3, default 0) — its session-zero stance.
9. **Relationships to existing factions** — a status track per named faction.
10. **Project clocks** (d10 seed table; Blades-style segments) — 1–2 clocks.
11. **Downtime moves** (d6) — the actions the faction takes off-screen between scenes.

Note all rolls in the build log. When two rolls suggest the same content (e.g., Veiled Backers + a foreign power base + a Tassard methods tag), let them reinforce — that's a Volusen-funded cell.

## Step 1 — Faction type (d12)

| Roll | Type |
|------|------|
| 1 | **Thane house** — stop here and use `noble_court_generator.md` instead; its nine-step procedure handles the seat, banner-mark, court tags, secret, and Coronation lean |
| 2 | **Merchant guild** — a chartered trade body (Stonebay Council-style, or a smaller market guild) |
| 3 | **Criminal ring** — smugglers, fences, gangs, a Hollow-Hill-style crew |
| 4 | **Cult or secret sect** — Burning One cells, hedge-faiths, Mtosi infiltrations, Aureothan revivalists |
| 5 | **Mercenary company** — a sworn-band serving for pay; can be Hethrun-resident or foreign |
| 6 | **Foreign cell** — an Aurean, Ostmark, Volotradan, or Cloven Strand presence inside Hethrun |
| 7 | **Faith network** — the Old Faith strand (Sky-Father / Raven / Veiled Mother), a Codex chapter, or a hidden sectarian network |
| 8 | **Scholar circle or monastic order** — Asgrim's Academy alumni, a Codex monastery's external work, an Aurelian-revivalist study group |
| 9 | **Adventuring company or sworn band** — the Greatwood Wardens model, a Marchland-spanning company of repute |
| 10 | **Insular community** — Strandfolk, a fishing-village confederacy, the displaced peasants of Count Edmund's enclosures organised as a body |
| 11 | **Conspiracy or covert lodge** — an intelligence service (Bone-Sayer-style), a blackmail ring, a hidden sodality |
| 12 | **Hybrid — roll twice and synthesise** — the result is one faction wearing two faces (a guild that is also a cult; a mercenary company that is also a foreign cell) |

## Step 2 — Tier

Tier is the CK3 noble ladder, with non-noble equivalents at every rung. Pick to fit the scale you need; or roll d6 for randomness.

| Tier | Noble equivalent | Non-noble equivalents |
|------|------------------|------------------------|
| **1** | **Banneret** — a landed knight with a retinue, a captain commanding a small unit | Village militia under a strong captain; a small bandit crew; a neighbourhood crime ring; a hedge-mage with apprentices; a single monastery's outreach chapter |
| **2** | **Baron** — holds a single castle or town with surrounding land | A town merchant guild; a town-scale criminal syndicate; a parish priest with a substantial flock; an established mercenary company; a small academy |
| **3** | **Count** — rules a province, or a handful of holdings (a thanedom equivalent in Hethrun) | A regional guild; a multi-town criminal network; an established temple with regional authority; an adventuring company of repute |
| **4** | **Duke** — holds a major province or multiple provinces with counts beneath them (an eorldom equivalent) | A great guild federation; a kingdom-spanning criminal organisation; a major religious order; a scholar-mage circle |
| **5** | **Kingdom** — a crowned monarch ruling a realm (Hethrun itself, Wessmark, Aurea) | A continental trade league; a cross-kingdom secret society; a dominant kingdom-wide faith; an established arcane academy |
| **6** | **Empire** — an emperor ruling multiple kingdoms or a continent-spanning realm (Ostmark, the High Kingship at its strongest, Chola at home) | A continental conspiracy; a world-religion; an ancient cosmic order; a transnational sorcerer cabal |

Most Hethrun-internal factions land at Tier 1–3. The eorldoms themselves are Tier 4. The crown is Tier 5. External powers (Aurea, Ostmark, Chola, the Silvering) operate at Tier 5–6 and reach into Hethrun through Tier 2–3 cells.

## Step 3 — Hold

Each faction's Hold expresses how secure it is at its current Tier.

- **Strong Hold** — the faction is secure at its Tier, well-resourced, and its position is not contested. Its clocks tend to be *expansion* projects (push to a higher Tier; absorb a rival; seize new ground).
- **Weak Hold** — the faction is aspiring, sliding, or contested at its Tier. Its clocks tend to be *survival* or *consolidation* (don't slip a Tier; defeat a rival; finish what is owed before the bill comes due).

A faction's Hold can shift in play as its clocks resolve. Note its starting Hold and the trigger that would flip it.

## Step 4 — Character tags

Roll d50 twice on the master list below for the two character tags that define the faction (or its leader). For each tag, roll Ambition (d3), Power (d3), and Dread (d3) on the sub-tables. Embed the rolled entries directly into the faction sheet — don't leave them as references.

For a small or quick-generated faction, the d20 quick-list in `noble_court_generator.md` Step 4 covers the most common tags and is faster.

### Expanded tags — full sub-tables

The twelve tags below are fully expanded with their Ambition, Power, and Dread sub-tables — the ones most useful for faction-level generation in the Hethrun corpus. For other tags, the WWN book carries the full sub-tables; the d50 gloss above is enough for quick play.

#### Bitter Grudge

They hate some person or institution with aching intensity. Their hatred is an animating force, and even when concealed it is never far from their mind. They will suffer terribly to inflict equal misery on their enemy.

**Ambitions (d3):** 1 worm into a position of trust to betray the enemy · 2 inflict vengeance on the things and people the enemy loves · 3 personally destroy the enemy or institution after inflicting terrible pain.
**Powers (d3):** 1 incredible focus and self-discipline driven by hate · 2 many friends who also hate their enemy · 3 a talent or association acquired from the wrong itself.
**Dreads (d3):** 1 the enemy is destroyed by some unrelated event · 2 people they rely on are drawn into the enemy's circle · 3 destroying the enemy would somehow ruin something they love.

#### Burnt Out

Nothing is worth it any longer. Spent — physically, mentally, emotionally — and unable to step aside.

**Ambitions (d3):** 1 escape some burdensome duty without getting in deeper trouble · 2 complete one last task that will let them finally rest · 3 get rid of whoever is forcing them to keep working.
**Powers (d3):** 1 they were extremely good at the task they're spent on · 2 minions or allies earned by past labour · 3 considerable resources their talents acquired.
**Dreads (d3):** 1 something threatens to hang a new duty on them · 2 the event or person who caused the burnout returns · 3 none of their intended rests are helping.

#### Concealed Sin

A grave crime by the standards of the society, kept hidden at great cost. They may still be committing it, or may be the concealed sin of someone else.

**Ambitions (d3):** 1 justify the sin to themselves or others through a scheme or investigation · 2 plot to gain more opportunities to sin · 3 make amends through some particular deed.
**Powers (d3):** 1 the sin was profitable or built resources · 2 others compromised by the sin help them · 3 the sin led them to acquire useful skills.
**Dreads (d3):** 1 unexpected consequences threaten to surface · 2 driven to rash acts by the need to atone · 3 hiding or feeding the sin is costing vital resources.

#### Friend Turned Foe

A dear ally is now an enemy. Affection has become hatred, or circumstances have set them on opposite sides against their will.

**Ambitions (d3):** 1 prove their justification to the former friend · 2 destroy the former friend before being destroyed · 3 subdue or neutralise the former friend without killing them.
**Powers (d3):** 1 the friend gave them a powerful item or resources · 2 the occasion of betrayal rewarded them richly · 3 the old friend's enemies now help them.
**Dreads (d3):** 1 a sudden need requires help only the old friend could give · 2 the betrayal proves futile · 3 a close friend betrays them as they once betrayed another.

#### Grand Plan

A magnificent, plausible, far-reaching scheme — actually achievable if they bring it together. Or they are part of someone else's plan, a knowing cog they may not fully understand.

**Ambitions (d3):** 1 destroy a rival person or cause with the plan's magnificent success · 2 redeem a past failing by succeeding in this · 3 escape some awful threat by succeeding in the plan.
**Powers (d3):** 1 secret knowledge about the situation they leverage with the plan · 2 an incredible stroke of luck that makes the plan possible · 3 inherited knowledge and resources of someone else who set the plan up.
**Dreads (d3):** 1 a vital resource or person threatens to be unavailable at a critical moment · 2 an underling plots to usurp the fruits of success · 3 someone is letting the plan progress in order to undermine a rival before quashing it.

#### Hidden Origins

A past intentionally concealed. The circumstances of their birth or upbringing would be disastrous if revealed.

**Ambitions (d3):** 1 either aid or avenge themselves on their true parent or origin · 2 permanently silence someone who knows the truth · 3 force the original parent or origin group to secretly aid them now.
**Powers (d3):** 1 inherited resources or gifts from the true origin · 2 a faithful and capable ally equally determined to hide the truth · 3 a figure from the origins is secretly working to aid them.
**Dreads (d3):** 1 forced into a situation choosing between present life and real nature · 2 someone they love is about to stumble over the truth · 3 evidence privately casts doubt on what they understand of their past.

#### Impending Treachery

A spectacular betrayal is in motion — by them, or against them. Hints are afoot.

**Ambitions (d3):** 1 exploit a desperate opportunity opened by the betrayal's circumstances · 2 take revenge on not only the traitor but the traitor's friends and loved ones · 3 recover a vital item the traitor took.
**Powers (d3):** 1 the traitor is enormously effective while concealing their malice · 2 the traitor has channelled extensive resources to them to earn trust · 3 the traitor's enemies are eager to lend aid.
**Dreads (d3):** 1 the treachery reveals a cherished relationship was all a lie · 2 they cannot bring themselves to believe it was really an intended betrayal · 3 the traitor destroyed something else loved as collateral damage.

#### Marked for Success

Designated for glory — by a patron, a gift, or a bloodline. Whatever their personal capacity, everyone expects them to rise.

**Ambitions (d3):** 1 prove they genuinely deserve the success that is coming · 2 carry out a critical task for their patron · 3 quash a rival who threatens to outdo them.
**Powers (d3):** 1 remarkably good at some skill central to their role · 2 the patron is very generous in aid · 3 numerous others rush to befriend them for future advantage.
**Dreads (d3):** 1 the patron is reconsidering · 2 they have no personal skill to justify their rise · 3 a rival is vying for the patron's support and may get it.

#### Misplaced Trust

They rely on someone who will fail them at the worst moment — incompetence, self-interest, or secret malice. Or they are that trusted someone, whose interests differ from what their ally supposes.

**Ambitions (d3):** 1 share glory or reward with the trusted ally · 2 promote the trusted ally's interests · 3 put the ally in a position to give them a great reward or help.
**Powers (d3):** 1 the ally is useful up until the inevitable failure · 2 the association impresses others · 3 the ally has given valuable resources as a token of good faith.
**Dreads (d3):** 1 a crucial plan-step relies on the ally and will go awry · 2 the ally is privately in league with their enemies · 3 the ally expects them to nobly sacrifice their interests.

#### Persecuting Foe

A powerful nemesis who will not be reasoned with — driven by past acts, ethnic or religious animosity, or sheer enmity.

**Ambitions (d3):** 1 destroy the nemesis before the nemesis destroys them · 2 pull the enemy's fangs by destroying their power base · 3 redirect the enemy's wrath toward a profitable target.
**Powers (d3):** 1 an important resource stolen from the nemesis · 2 skills or allies developed to blunt the foe's usual means · 3 alliance with powerful enemies of the nemesis.
**Dreads (d3):** 1 the nemesis is striking at their base of power · 2 the persecutor has made an opening for other enemies · 3 the foe cannot be destroyed or moved against directly.

#### Up From the Gutter

A wretched origin behind their present rise. The past may be a secret carefully kept; or they may still be in the gutter and climbing.

**Ambitions (d3):** 1 get revenge on those who once scorned them · 2 erase all those who know the truth of their past · 3 raise the local status of their despised origins.
**Powers (d3):** 1 useful skills uncommon to their current position · 2 allies from the past who still serve well · 3 considerable blackmail material gathered along the way.
**Dreads (d3):** 1 old ties and demands threaten to drag them back · 2 a rival works tirelessly to discredit them for their origins · 3 the next level of success is barred by their past.

#### Veiled Backers

One or more powerful patrons fund and steer them — and the aid must be kept secret. The patrons have their own goals.

**Ambitions (d3):** 1 carry out a secret command that conflicts with apparent duties · 2 take out an enemy of the backers in a deniable way · 3 deal with another client of the patrons who has gone rogue.
**Powers (d3):** 1 the backers grant considerable resources · 2 secret allies embedded in many local institutions · 3 the backers give access to secrets and hidden intel.
**Dreads (d3):** 1 the backers have decided to burn them · 2 enemies of the backers are sniffing too close · 3 the backers are demanding a service that is mortally perilous or intolerable.

## Step 5 — Methods (d8)

How the faction gets things done. Roll once; a complex faction can carry two.

| Roll | Method |
|------|--------|
| 1 | **Bribery, favour, and patronage** — moves through gifts, debts, and gratitude |
| 2 | **Force of arms** — open or threatened violence; a fyrd, a sworn-band, hired blades |
| 3 | **Social manipulation** — rumour, reputation, the right whisper to the right ear, the Aldermarket-style consensus shift |
| 4 | **Trade and commercial leverage** — debt, contracts, supply lines, the Castermere method |
| 5 | **Faith and rites** — blessings withheld or bestowed, doctrine deployed, the season-observances kept or denied |
| 6 | **Intelligence and blackmail** — the Bone-Sayer method; information as the realm's hardest currency |
| 7 | **Patient infiltration** — placing kin and clients into other institutions, the Oxenfell method |
| 8 | **Magical or supernatural means** — pact-power, ritual, the witch-roads, Branwald's working |

## Step 7 — Leadership

Generate one named figure who heads the faction. Use the `npc_generator.md` procedure for the figure's class, characterisation, and Hethrun layer; use the prefix-suffix name table from `noble_court_generator.md` (Step 8) for a Hethrun-native name, or the Foreign-origin sub-table in `npc_generator.md` for a non-Hethrun name. A recurring faction leader earns full NPC voice — treat an entry in `npcs.md` as a template.

For a faction's secondary leadership — 1–2 lieutenants, a council, a moot — sketch each in one sentence with a single distinguishing trait. Full detail comes only when a lieutenant becomes a recurring PC contact.

## Step 8 — Status vs party

Where the faction sits relative to the PCs at session zero. Default is **0 (neutral)** unless something in the rolls suggests otherwise (a Persecuting Foe tag aimed at the PCs starts at −2; a Veiled Backers tag suggesting the PCs *are* the backers starts at +1).

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **−3** | **War** — the faction will move against the party on sight |
| **−2** | **Hostile** — actively working against the party; will refuse, sabotage, threaten |
| **−1** | **Friction** — distrustful, uncooperative; the next clash is a matter of time |
| **0** | **Neutral** — the faction has no fixed stance; treats the party transactionally |
| **+1** | **Cooperative** — willing to deal; some shared interests |
| **+2** | **Allied** — formal cooperation; meaningful aid in a contest |
| **+3** | **Sworn** — bound to the party; will take real losses to keep faith |

Status shifts in play, advanced or retarded by player action, the faction's clocks, and the faction's relationships with other factions the party is allied to.

## Step 9 — Relationships to existing factions

For each named faction the new one is likely to interact with, set a Status value on the same −3 to +3 track and write a one-line reason. The Hethrun internal factions in particular benefit from being placed: the Regency, Ranulf's Faction, Cuthbert's Wildcards, the Old Faith network, the Codex Bishopric, the Burning One cult, the Hollowford March, the Cloven Strand jarldom, the Silvering Elves, and the Aurean Envoys.

A faction can have a different Status with each. The cross-eorldom marriage and feud web in `realm_titles.md` is a useful sanity check — if the new faction's clocks press on House Holmgarth, expect a friction-or-worse with House Thornwic, and so on.

## Step 10 — Active project clocks

Each faction carries 1–2 project clocks at session zero. A clock has:

- **Name** — what the faction is trying to do
- **Segments** — 4 (a session's labour), 6 (an arc), or 8 (a long campaign labour)
- **Starting state** — usually 0/N, but can begin partial if the faction has been working on this already
- **Advance conditions** — specific in-world triggers (a Witan vote held, a courier received, a season-rite kept, a particular asset acquired)
- **Trigger consequence** — the concrete world-change at full segments

Roll d10 on the seed table below for the clock's flavour, then write the specifics.

### d10 Hethrun-keyed project-clock seeds

| Roll | Clock seed |
|------|------------|
| 1 | **Sway a wavering Witan vote** — typically 4–6 segments, advance when a named eorl is courted, retard when the rival faction gets to them first |
| 2 | **Embed an agent at a target court / hall / guild** — 6 segments, advance when the agent is placed and accepted, retard if discovered or recalled |
| 3 | **Build a lever against a named rival** — debt, blackmail, a stolen object, a fostered child, an exposed sin (6 segments) |
| 4 | **Acquire a specific resource** — an Aureothan artifact, an Aurean letter, a relic, a name-list, a piece of the Stridvik engine (4 segments) |
| 5 | **Discredit a rival publicly** — the Aldermarket-rumour method; advance via witnesses, written records, or skaldic record (4 segments) |
| 6 | **Win the loyalty of a community** — a quarter, a village, a guild, a clan-holt; the patient method (8 segments) |
| 7 | **Complete a long-prepared rite or working** — a Burning One conjunction-rite, a Veiled Mother season-rite, an Aureothan reactivation (8 segments) |
| 8 | **Survive a clock running against the faction** — cap a hostile clock; the survival-mode case (segments inverse — every retard of the hostile clock is a segment toward survival) |
| 9 | **Spring a long-planned betrayal** — Impending-Treachery-style; advance via trust-earned, retard via suspicion-aroused (6 segments) |
| 10 | **The faction's own grand plan** — define from the rolled character tags; an 8-segment campaign-spanning clock |

## Step 11 — Downtime moves (d6 example list)

The actions the faction takes off-screen between scenes. Build a d6 table per faction; this is the generic template, customise to fit. Each move maps to a specific clock or relationship-track shift.

| Roll | Downtime move |
|------|---------------|
| 1 | **Court a wavering ally** — shift Status +1 with a named faction (or party member) |
| 2 | **Apply pressure to a rival** — shift Status −1 with a named faction (or party member) |
| 3 | **Advance the primary clock** — by one segment, narrating the specific in-world action |
| 4 | **Recruit a new asset** — an NPC, hireling, supplier, informant; added to the faction's roster |
| 5 | **Convert resources** — gold to influence, influence to information, information to leverage (one rung per cycle) |
| 6 | **Lay low** — retract from a contest; an active clock running against the faction stops advancing this cycle |
