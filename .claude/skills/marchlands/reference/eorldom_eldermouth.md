# Eorldom: the Crown Demesne

The crown demesne — Eldermouth and the central Greenways — held by the King directly, not by an eorl. This file covers the demesne as a place: the capital, its crown-thanes, its lands, and its hooks. The royal court and the Witan that govern from Eldermouth are in `court_hethrun.md`; the realm frame is in `realm_titles.md`.

## The crown demesne — essence

The crown demesne is the heart of Hethrun: the walled capital of Eldermouth, the central Greenways farmland around it, and the royal forests west of the River Elder. The King holds it directly — at session zero that means the regency holds it, governing for the boy-king Hadric. Three crown-thanes hold thanelands of the demesne, owing service to the crown rather than to any eorl. The demesne is the realm's administrative and trade centre and the ground on which the Coronation crisis is most densely fought: all three crisis factions keep residences within a quarter-mile of the Royal Hall.

## Eldermouth — the capital

Eldermouth is a walled town of roughly 12,000 at the mouth of the River Elder, built on Aureothan foundations — Hethrun's capital, chief southern port, and seat of the Codex bishopric. Two pressures define it.

**Toxic Economy.** Eldermouth's prosperity rests on three loads its merchants do not advertise. Bog-iron from the Heath is drawn under the Old Faith's ancient bog-water exemption, but the extraction-workers sicken, and the Codex Church does not treat them free. Much "southern trade" through Eldermouth's customs is Aurean coastal-raid plunder, fenced through the Hollow-Hill Crew with customs officials taking a cut. The cathedral's silver altarware was paid two generations ago by a single Heath bog-iron contract that left fifteen years of visible illness behind it. The wealth is real; the cost is real; most townsfolk choose not to think about it.

**Population Boom.** The town has roughly doubled in twenty years — Aurean trade, bog-iron expansion, Codex administrative growth, and Greenways refugees displaced by Count Edmund's enclosures. Its institutions have not kept up: walls built for 6,000 hold 12,000, the New Quarter outside the southern wall is unwalled and ramshackle, the watch is forty fyrdmen and undermanned, and a sharp class tension runs between established Hethrunfolk and the newcomers they treat as freeloaders.

## Eldermouth — quarters and notable places

The Royal Hall stands at the town's heart — stone and timber, the throne hall and hearth-hall, the queen-mother's quarters, and Sigevard's skald-chamber in the eastern wing; Hadric and Isolde live here. Beside it the Codex Cathedral, the diocesan seat, its treasury vault below. The Aldwin Tower, an Aureothan tower on the western inner wall, is Master Aldwin's residence and workshop. The Old Mead Hall is the chief civic gathering place — raucous neutral ground where eorls can meet without political weight attaching, held nine generations by a family that takes no side. The Harbor Quarter holds the docks, warehouses, the notoriously crooked customs office, and the foreign trade compounds. The Plague-Quarter east of the walls is the bog-iron workers' district — disease, two tolerated Veiled Mother shrines. The New Quarter outside the southern wall is the immigrant peasant district, dangerous after dark. The Aldermarket is the central square; the kingdom's political mood can be read in how the Aldermarket talks. The Aureothan Stonework Bridge crosses the Elder to the southern road.

## Eldermouth — significant locals

Full NPC detail is in `npcs.md`. Resident in the capital: Father Severin, Bishop of Eldermouth, ambitious, wants the see raised to a metropolitan. Master Aldwin, court wizard. Sigevard the Silver-Tongue, court skald. Cassian Drey, Aurean envoy, holding court at the Aurean compound. Aurelio Maron, an Aurean of House Tassard who runs a quieter counter-network and hates Cassian Drey politely. ## The royal court

The royal court and the Witan sit in Eldermouth's Royal Hall but are governed in their own file: see `court_hethrun.md` for the boy-king's household, the regency around Lady Isolde, the seven-eorl Witan, and the Coronation deadlock. For the demesne as a place, what matters here is that the court draws every faction's agents into the capital — the regency in the Royal Hall, Cuthbert's coronation party in a townhouse near the cathedral, Ranulf's lieutenants in a harbor compound, Branwald visiting weekly, Cassian Drey at the Aurean compound.

## House Aldermere — crown-thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Aldermere, a hall in the Greenways near Eldermouth; an alder-leaf.
**Head:** Thane Berhtwulf of Aldermere — charming, attentive, widely liked; a familiar welcome face at the Royal Hall.
**Character — Impending Treachery.** Berhtwulf has done the arithmetic and concluded the boy-king will fall.
**Wants:** to be on Ranulf's side of the table when the crown changes hands, and to have got there early enough to be rewarded.
**Secret:** he has opened a quiet line to Ranulf's people and feeds them the regency's moods, Lady Isolde's worries, and the Witan's leanings. He is a spy inside the crown's own house, not yet suspected.
**Coronation lean:** secretly Ranulf; publicly a pillar of the regency.
**Ties:** secret correspondent of Ranulf's faction; cultivating House Hartwell as a fellow future defector.
**Hook:** Aldermere is the traitor the regency does not know it has. A party doing intelligence work for the regency could be the ones to find him; a party working the other way could use him.

## House Hartwell — crown-thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Hartwell, a prestigious old hall in the central Greenways; a white hart.
**Head:** Thane Oslaf of Hartwell — proud, dignified, anxious; carries himself like the heir of a legend.
**Character — Stolen Reputation.** House Hartwell's standing rests on the Defense of the Hart-Ford, a famous stand against the barbarian invasion three generations ago, credited to a Hartwell ancestor in skald-verse ever since. The defense happened; the Hartwell did not lead it. The true commander was a thane of a since-extinct house, written out of the verse by a Hartwell who paid the right skald.
**Wants:** the lie to hold one more generation.
**Secret:** the truth survives — in an old skald's unsung draft, in an honest Codex chronicle (possibly at Hollowford), in the scattered kin of the written-out house. Whoever holds the proof holds Oslaf entirely.
**Coronation lean:** loudly regency in public; privately manipulable by anyone who knows the truth — a vote, in practice, for sale to a blackmailer.
**Ties:** being cultivated by House Aldermere as a fellow defector.
**Hook:** the Hart-Ford truth is a recoverable secret and a perfect lever; finding it can make Hartwell do almost anything, or exposing it can collapse a respected house.

## House Ashcombe — crown-thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Ashcombe, a Greenways hall on the inland edge of the Heath; a grey ash-tree.
**Head:** Thane Eanred of Ashcombe — hollow-eyed, haunted, exhausted; he flinches at bad news because he has had so much of it.
**Character — Cursed Misfortune.** For a decade everything Ashcombe touches fails — rotted harvests, sickened cattle, a hall lost to fire, two heirs dead young. The realm whispers the house is cursed, and it is right: an Ashcombe ancestor broke open a Heath barrow for its grave-goods, and the barrow-folk have not forgotten. The misfortune compounds as the barrow-seals weaken.
**Wants:** the curse lifted; the house's survival; an end to the dying.
**Secret:** Eanred has run out of ordinary remedies and is contemplating dangerous ones — a cunning-folk pact, a desperate excavation to "give back" what was taken.
**Coronation lean:** no attention for the throne; leans toward whichever faction's agents will help with the curse.
**Ties:** the curse is a barrow-folk matter, tied to Gythwin's Heath work and the barrow-folk clock in `active_threats.md`.
**Hook:** a tier-1 entry into the barrow-folk plot — help Eanred reach Gythwin and make the right restitution, or watch him do something far worse out of desperation.

## Minor settlements of the demesne

Seeded for texture; each is a name, community tags, and a hook.

**Penbroke Crossing** — village, *Tradition-Bound · Honest Folk.* A Greenways road-village around the Penbroke Crossroads Shrine, where a Sky-Father priest and a cunning-woman — an elderly married couple, Brother Eadric and Wennild — keep one of the few shrines in Hethrun the two faiths share. A party that needs the faiths to cooperate starts here.

**Elderford** — village, *Trade Hub · Quarrelsome.* The last Greenways village before the southern road reaches the Wealdvale; a market-stop grown prosperous and fractious on through-traffic, its reeve and its miller locked in a property feud the demesne reeves have given up settling.

**Ashleydown** — hamlet, *Oppressed · Sealed Lips.* A demesne hamlet on the Heath's inland edge near House Ashcombe's land; the curse on Ashcombe is felt here too, and the folk have learned not to speak of it to outsiders.

## Minor courts and lesser halls

**The customs-house of Eldermouth** — court tag *Rampant Corruption.* Not a noble court but a power centre: the harbor customs office, nominally a royal asset, run as a private revenue source by whoever manages it; Severin and the regency both skim it. A hook for any party investigating the fence network.

**The Old Mead Hall company** — court tag *Awesome Legitimacy.* The family that has held the Old Mead Hall for nine generations governs a small institution everyone needs — the realm's recognised neutral ground. They take no political side and guard that neutrality fiercely; their goodwill is worth more than their wealth.

## The demesne's lands — the Eldermouth environs

The demesne's countryside is the River Elder valley, coastal cliffs to the south, the lower Heath slopes to the north, and royal hunting forest to the west. Notable sites:

**Brendannian's Hermitage** — a small Codex monastery in a wooded fold three hours north-west of the capital, eight monks under Brother Caedmon, a former fyrdman who took orders after killing his brother in a feud. Caedmon takes no orders from Severin and will shelter travellers running from the bishop's people. *Small dungeon if explored — an Aureothan tomb beneath the chapel the monks will not enter.*

**The Hollow-Hill Crew's range** — the rougher hills west of Eldermouth, where a seasonal bandit company of eleven under Eadgyth the Soot-Faced preys on Aurean traders but leaves Hethrun caravans alone. They are a black-market node and an information source for those who can approach without dying — and a clock: if the Coronation fails and Hethrun destabilises, the Crew triples. (Bestiary: the Road-Outlaws, `bestiary.md` Group 4A.)

**The Wivelpool** — a spring-fed pool in a hidden valley a half-day out, sacred to the Veiled Mother; Aldwin has twice noted the water is genuinely unusual. *Investigation site.*

**The Old Watch Tower** — a ruined Aureothan signal tower on the coast south of Eldermouth, inhabited by something the Hollow-Hill Crew fears and avoids. *Small dungeon, three levels.*

## Adventure hooks

- **The Codex courier disappearance** (tier 1) — Severin will quietly hire a discreet party to find a relic-courier from Aurea who never reached the cathedral; the courier carried a relic case Severin will not describe. Last seen leaving Stonebay.
- **The Aldermarket rumor** (tier 1) — a rumor that Ranulf engineered Crown Prince Wystan's "hunting accident" is running fast. The regency, Ranulf's people, and Cuthbert's people all pay for knowing who started it.
- **The plague-quarter child** (tier 1) — Sister Halgrid the Lay appeals for help with a bog-iron worker's child whose symptoms exceed her craft; the case may be the first manifest sign of something Aldwin has been quietly tracking.
- **Olaf's caravan** (tier 1–2) — Olaf Trader-King needs guards for a major caravan south to Aurea; several contracts were declined because the cargo is not what the manifest says. He pays extra for guards who do not ask.
- **Cassian Drey's retinue** (tier 1) — Drey hires mercenaries to "augment his retinue"; the work is suspicious but legal.

## At a glance

The crown demesne is the regency's seat and the campaign's political centre. Its three crown-thanes are all compromised — Aldermere a hidden traitor, Hartwell blackmailable, Ashcombe distracted by a real curse — which makes the demesne's own noble layer a set of levers rather than a loyal bloc. Crisis stance: held by the regency, but porous. Bestiary groups operating here: the Road-Outlaws (4A) in the western hills, the Burning One's Cells (A2) and the Arrival-Hunters (A1) anywhere, and the Barrow-Dead (1A) where the demesne meets the Heath. For the realm frame and the marriage/feud web see `realm_titles.md`; for the royal court and Witan see `court_hethrun.md`.
