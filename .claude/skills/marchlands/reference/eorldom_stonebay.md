# Eorldom: Stonebay

The eorldom of Stonebay — the Iron Strait coast, held by Eorl Ranulf, the realm's rival claimant. This file covers Stonebay as a place: the port-city seat, Ranulf's court, the four thane houses, the coastal lands, and the local hooks. The realm frame and the marriage/feud web are in `realm_titles.md`; Ranulf as a Coronation actor is in `court_hethrun.md` and `active_threats.md`.

## The Stonebay eorldom — essence

Stonebay is Hethrun's wealthiest eorldom and its most dangerous — the kingdom's window on the Iron Strait, the closest harbour to Ostmark, and the power base from which Eorl Ranulf contests the throne. Four thane houses hold its thanelands; the city itself answers as much to a guild oligarchy as to the eorl. Branwald the Star-Priest is resident here as a "diplomatic guest," the Bone-Sayers run the realm's best intelligence service from here, and the Cloven Strand jarldom is a short and friendly sail away. A party sent to canvass the Coronation will find Stonebay the one eorldom already decided — and the one where being noticed is most costly.

## Stonebay — the seat

Stonebay is a port-city of roughly 6,000 built into a stone-cliff deep-water cove on the eastern coast — the wealthiest single settlement in Hethrun per head, two generations rich on Iron Strait trade. Two pressures define it.

**Widespread Prosperity.** Ostmark silk and worked metal come in, Hethrun bog-iron goes out, with fat margins both ways, and the wealth has reached the commoners — a Stonebay fyrdman owns his house, eats meat twice a week, dresses better than a Greenways thane's son. The town mints its own coin, the Stonebay penny, slightly heavier than the royal penny and accepted at face value across the Marchlands. Paved and lit streets, a public well system, a fyrd-armory better stocked than Eldermouth's, guild-backed banks, and a small Codex temple decorated more lavishly than Severin's cathedral. The armorers produce surplus weaponry, sold to Cloven Strand and the Hollow-Hill Crew in quiet breach of crown law.

**Guild Oligarchy.** Real power rests with the Council of Twelve, the senior masters of the twelve principal guilds; Ranulf sits the Council as eorl but does not vote, and its decisions carry the practical weight of law. Non-guild craftsmen are absorbed or edged out. The town's political trajectory is therefore not wholly Ranulf's to direct — the guilds prefer his success but are not committed to it.

## Stonebay — quarters and notable places

The Inner Harbor is the natural cove, docks tiered up the cliffs, stone-arched warehouses cut into the rock, a chain-boom across the mouth. The Stonebay Hall, Ranulf's seat, is larger and better-built than Eldermouth's Royal Hall, though newer — the most impressive eorl's hall in Hethrun. The Council Chamber is a separate purpose-built guild assembly, twelve seats around one great table. The Codex Chapel is small, lavish, guild-patronised. The Bone-Sayers' Hall looks like a guild hall and functions as a small fortress; the Cordwainers' Hall looks like a wealthy guild hall and functions as a money-laundering centre. The Chola Temple, stone and tile with a ritual water-tank, stands in the Chola merchant quarter. The Stonebay Lighthouse, an Aureothan tower on the headland north of the harbour mouth, is kept by the Shipwrights' Guild — and the headland beyond it is where Branwald has performed at least two observed fire-rites. The Outside-the-Walls Quarter is the poor district, smaller and better-managed than Eldermouth's New Quarter because the guilds employ its people.

## Stonebay — the Council of Twelve and the Bone-Sayers

The Council of Twelve seats the Stoneworkers, Smiths, Cordwainers, Shipwrights, Net-Weavers, Salters, Sea-Merchants, Land-Merchants, Brewers, Bakers, Money-Changers, and the Bone-Sayers. Two seats matter most to a visiting party. The **Cordwainers' Guild** is a hollow shell — the leatherwork tradition is preserved, but the guild's real function is laundering the Stonebay–Cloven Strand trade, and its hall is among the richest buildings in town. The **Bone-Sayers**, once a guild of haruspices, now run guild-political intelligence with no trade function at all: they know what the Aldermarket is saying, what Branwald did on the headland, and possibly what Crown Prince Wystan was hunting three years ago. The Bone-Sayers are the single best information source in the realm and the single most dangerous body to lie to.

## Stonebay — significant locals

Full NPC detail is in `npcs.md` and `npcs.md`. Resident in the eorldom: Eorl Ranulf, the rival claimant, charismatic and ruthless. Branwald the Star-Priest, a Burning One vengeance-priest of Ostmark's Solar Academy, a "diplomatic guest" at Ranulf's hall. Guildmaster Edrid Bone-Sayer, elderly, one-eyed, vicious of mind, who pretends to more frailty than he has. Guildmistress Wystild of the Sea-Merchants, the wealthiest individual in Stonebay, a careful neutral preparing for either outcome. Guildmaster Eorad of the Cordwainers, who runs the laundering and has Cloven Strand kin. Captain Sweyn Stormhammer, captain of the Stonebay watch and Ranulf's most reliable officer, the town's only man of Skarrgaard descent. Master Quintus Ostkar, an Ostmark trade-factor and probable Solar Academy reporter. Vasudevan Tirunelveli, factor of the Chola Vellanaadu company, whose forty households keep the Chola Temple and refuse all Burning One cooperation. Vrinda Hammerset, senior dwarven smith of the small dwarven quarter, lately visited by a contemplative-grade dwarven monk she will not discuss.

## Eorl Ranulf's court

Ranulf's court inside the Stonebay Hall runs on two faults.

**Poisonous Cliques.** The court is split into a **war-party** and a **prosperity-party**. The war-party, led by Thane Sigeric of Holmgarth, wants Ranulf to press his claim by force and treats the guilds' caution as cowardice. The prosperity-party, led by Lady Gunnhild of Merrowdene, wants the claim pursued only so far as trade allows and fears a war that would close the Iron Strait. The two factions poison each other's proposals as a matter of course; Ranulf plays them against each other to keep either from dominating him.

**Ministerial Capture.** Ranulf's steward, **Aldhune the Reeve-Master**, controls the eorldom's correspondence, its audiences, and its account-books — and Aldhune has been quietly turned. Branwald reaches Ranulf's decisions through Aldhune: which letters surface, which petitioners are delayed, which figures are shaded. Ranulf does not know his own steward is a channel for Ostmark. The household around all this: Lady Aedwyn, Ranulf's wife, sharper than she shows; Thorgar, his heir, eighteen and eager for the war-party's war; and Cedric, a young Ranulf kinsman fostered into the boy-king's household at Eldermouth, serving as a low-level information channel back to Stonebay.

## House Holmgarth — Stonebay thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Holmgarth, a fortified coastal hall north of the city; a black boar.
**Head:** Thane Sigeric of Holmgarth, called the Iron-Hand — blunt, loud, contemptuous of caution; speaks of every problem as a thing to be hit.
**Character — Secret Cravings.** Sigeric leads Ranulf's war-party and serves as his fiercest captain, and beneath the loyalty he covets the Stonebay seat itself. He believes a bolder man — himself — should hold the eorldom, and that a war would prove it.
**Wants:** the war; and, unspoken, Ranulf's chair after it.
**Secret:** he has begun sounding out which thanes and guild-captains would follow Holmgarth over Ranulf if the eorl ever faltered.
**Coronation lean:** hard for Ranulf's claim, by force — but for his own reasons.
**Ties:** carries the forty-year Holmgarth–Thornwic blood feud against House Thornwic of Brackenford (see `realm_titles.md`).
**Hook:** Sigeric is the loyal lieutenant who is also Ranulf's most dangerous rival; a party can expose the craving to Ranulf, feed it, or be hired into the war he is pushing for.

## House Merrowdene — Stonebay thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Merrowdene, a prosperous estate-hall in the fertile coastal vale south of the city; a golden sheaf.
**Head:** Lady Gunnhild Sweynsdottir of Merrowdene — measured, formidable, courteous; the kind of opponent who never raises her voice.
**Character — Friend Turned Foe.** House Merrowdene and Ranulf's line were once sworn-brothers; a broken marriage-promise a generation ago turned the friendship into estrangement. Gunnhild now leads the prosperity-party against Ranulf's war from inside his own court.
**Wants:** Stonebay's trade kept open and its wealth intact; the claim pursued, if at all, without a ruinous war.
**Secret:** she is in quiet contact with the regency — not as a partisan, but as insurance, so that Merrowdene survives whichever way the Coronation falls.
**Coronation lean:** against Ranulf's war; soft regency by self-interest.
**Ties:** a Merrowdene child is wed to a Beaconfell of the North Coast (see `realm_titles.md`); the old estrangement with Eorl Ranulf himself.
**Hook:** Merrowdene is the crack inside Ranulf's eorldom — a regency party can widen it; a Ranulf party can be sent to close it.

## House Castermere — Stonebay thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Castermere, a hall close to the city, more counting-house than castle; a silver key.
**Head:** Thane Osbeorn of Castermere — affable, quick, always mid-calculation; remembers every debt owed in any direction.
**Character — Grand Plan.** Osbeorn intends House Castermere to outgrow its thane's rank and become a power in its own right by marriage, credit, and leverage rather than by the sword.
**Wants:** Castermere woven so deeply into the realm's money that no eorl can act against it.
**Secret:** he holds the Mossdrowen eorldom's debt — Stonebay money quietly owning Eorl Hagan's accounts — and has married Castermere into a dozen Stonebay guild families, so that the Grand Plan is already further along than anyone outside the house realises.
**Coronation lean:** whichever outcome is best for credit; backs Ranulf for now because Stonebay's success serves the Plan.
**Ties:** holder of Mossdrowen's debt; married into the guild Council's families.
**Hook:** Castermere's debt-paper is a lever on a whole eorldom; a party that wants Mossdrowen's vote may have to deal with Osbeorn first.

## House Greycliff — Stonebay thane

**Seat & banner-mark:** Greycliff, an old cliff-hall on the northern coast, proud and visibly fraying; a grey gull.
**Head:** old Thane Wulfstan of Greycliff — tired, dignified, courteous to a fault; a man managing a long decline with as much grace as he can.
**Character — Burnt Out.** House Greycliff was once a leading Stonebay house; bad seasons, bad ventures, and the cost of keeping up appearances have hollowed it. Wulfstan has neither the heir nor the means to recover.
**Wants:** to die before the house does; to see his line settled somewhere safe.
**Secret:** Greycliff is mortgaged to the Bone-Sayers — the house owes Edrid Bone-Sayer more than it can ever repay, and Edrid holds the paper.
**Coronation lean:** whatever the Bone-Sayers wish; effectively Ranulf, through the debt.
**Ties:** mortgaged to Guildmaster Edrid Bone-Sayer.
**Hook:** whoever buys or seizes the Greycliff debt owns its hall-moot vote and its fyrd — a quiet way to gain or lose a piece of the eorldom.

## Minor settlements of the eorldom

Seeded for texture; each is a name, community tags, and a hook.

**Netherwick** — fishing village, *Honest Folk · Sealed Lips.* A net-weavers' village down the coast from Stonebay; its boats run quiet errands to Cloven Strand and back, and its people have a fisherman's gift for not having seen anything.

**Saltmerrow** — village, *Trade Hub · Quarrelsome.* A salters' and curers' village on the southern coast road, prosperous and fractious, where the Salters' Guild and an independent curing family fight a long commercial war.

**Hoarstone** — hamlet, *Holy Site · Tradition-Bound.* A clifftop hamlet near a standing-stone older than the Aureothans, where the Old Faith of the coast keeps a sea-shrine the Stonebay Codex priest pretends does not exist.

## Minor courts and lesser halls

**The Council of Twelve** — court tag *Secret Masters.* The guild council that truly rules Stonebay; treated as a court for play because canvassing the Coronation in Stonebay means canvassing the Council, not only the eorl.

**Edrid Bone-Sayer's guild** — court tag *Hidden Hand.* The Bone-Sayers function as a court unto themselves — an intelligence service with its own agenda, its own debts owed to it, and its own quiet say in who holds what.

## The eorldom's lands — the Iron Strait coast

Stonebay's lands are the cliff-and-cove eastern shore, the fertile southern coastal vale, and the headlands north of the city. Notable sites:

**Sigerith's Tears** — a spring in a fold of the southern coast whose water is genuinely, measurably strange; the Old Faith calls it the tears of a drowned woman, and cunning-folk travel to draw from it. *Investigation site, tied to coastal Old Faith.*

**Stormwatch Hermitage (Old Eorland)** — the ruin of the first eorl's hall, abandoned when Stonebay's seat moved to the cove, now a single hermit's dwelling. The hermit, a former Bone-Sayer, knows old Stonebay secrets and will trade them for the right kindness. *Small dungeon below — the old hall's flooded undercroft.*

**The Drowned Hall** — a hall lost to the sea a century ago when the cliff gave way, its roof-beams still visible at low tide; the coastal folk say its hearth still burns under the water. *Tier-2 dungeon, partly submerged.*

**Cloven Strand waters** — the strait between Stonebay and the Cloven Strand jarldom, technically Hethrun water, in practice patrolled by Hadolf One-Hand's longships as much as by anyone's. Raider boats move here; a sea-crossing is never simple. (Bestiary: the Strait-Raiders, `bestiary.md` Group 5A.)

## Adventure hooks

- **Stonebay hire** (tier 1–2) — the Council of Twelve and individual guildmasters routinely hire for caravan guard, harbour security, and "specialty work"; pay is excellent, discretion expected.
- **The Bone-Sayers want to know** (tier 1–3) — Edrid has standing offers for word of Aurean and Ostmark intelligence movements, Cloven Strand politics, what Master Aldwin is working on, and Branwald's real schedule; such knowledge trades very well here.
- **Vrinda Hammerset's visitor** (tier 1) — the dwarven quarter buzzes over the contemplative monk who came; Vrinda will not say what passed, the Bone-Sayers will pay for it, and a party earns Vrinda's trust by refusing to sell it.
- **The Chola Temple** (tier 1) — Vasudevan Tirunelveli wants travellers to verify whether a Chola scholar bound for Eldermouth is in truth a Solar Academy agent before he lodges him.
- **Branwald's second rite** (tier 2) — at the next astronomical conjunction — six weeks from session zero, the Coronation deadline — Branwald will perform another headland fire-rite; anyone rightly placed could witness what the first observers did not.

## At a glance

Stonebay is the one eorldom not in play in the Coronation canvass — it is Ranulf's, decided, and the dangerous ground a party works behind enemy lines. Its four houses run from hard-Ranulf (Holmgarth, by ambition) through bought-Ranulf (Greycliff, by debt; Castermere, by interest) to a real internal crack (Merrowdene, estranged and quietly hedging). The true power to court here is the Council of Twelve and the Bone-Sayers, not the hall alone. Bestiary groups operating here: the Strait-Raiders (5A) offshore, the Burning One's Cells (A2) around Branwald, and the Arrival-Hunters (A1) anywhere drift-folk are sought.
