# Adventure Seeds

Eighteen adventure hooks for the Hethrun campaign, tier-tagged and isekai-friendly marked. Each seed includes: the hook, the patron (or trigger), the tier, the location, and a one-line note on what success and failure look like. Many seeds cross-reference settlement, region, faction, and threats files for full context.

A note on tiers: WWN tier 1 corresponds to 5.5e levels 1-4, tier 2 to levels 5-10, tier 3 to levels 11-16, tier 4 to levels 17-20. Hethrun is built primarily as a tier-1-to-2 campaign with tier-3 expansion hooks. Isekai-friendly seeds are marked with **[ISEKAI]** — these are hooks where outsider perspective is an asset rather than a liability.

---

## Tier 1 seeds (levels 1-4)

### 1. The missing Codex courier [ISEKAI-friendly]

**Hook:** Father Severin of Eldermouth hires a discreet party to investigate a Codex courier who disappeared two weeks ago on the Hollowford road. The party is asked to retrace the route, identify the disappearance point, and recover the relic case the courier was carrying (Severin will not specify the contents).

**Patron:** Father Severin (Codex bishopric).

**Tier:** 1.

**Location:** The southern road, Brackenford, Hollowford, possibly Stonebay.

**Success:** The party recovers (or locates) the relic case, which leads them to the Burning One cult and Branwald.

**Failure:** The Codex assumes the relic is permanently lost; Severin's political position weakens; Branwald moves more boldly.

**Isekai note:** An isekai PC has no preconceptions about the relic's importance and can pursue the trail without being intimidated by Codex authority. Severin will likely *prefer* the isekai outsider because the party seems unaffiliated with Hethrun factional politics.

---

### 2. The Heath cunning-woman's invitation

**Hook:** Gythwin the Cunning sends word to the players — through Sister Halgrid the Lay at Eldermouth, or through Wennild at Penbroke Crossroads, or through a particular cunning-folk apprentice the players encounter on the road — inviting them to her hut on the Heath. She has work for "people who could see clearly without needing to be told why."

**Patron:** Gythwin the Cunning (Old Faith network).

**Tier:** 1 (escalating to 2 if the work extends).

**Location:** Gythwin's hut at the Heath edge.

**Success:** The party assists with a seasonal barrow-rite, retards the barrow-folk Clock by one segment, gains Gythwin's confidence.

**Failure:** The Heath remains unstable; the novice Cynwise's dreams escalate uncontrolled; Gythwin's network loses a critical opportunity.

---

### 3. The Brackenford chamber

**Hook:** Eorl Cuthbert of Brackenford has discovered a sealed Aureothan chamber under the Drowning Quarter of his town. He cannot trust Severin (Codex-aligned), Aldwin (regency-aligned), or Ranulf (rival eorl). He needs a party with no factional alignment to investigate the chamber, document what is there, and report only to him.

**Patron:** Eorl Cuthbert (the Wildcards faction).

**Tier:** 1.

**Location:** The Drowning Quarter of Brackenford.

**Success:** The chamber is documented and contained. Cuthbert gains a substantial political asset and a clearer sense of his Witan vote.

**Failure:** What is in the chamber is disturbed and either escapes or activates in ways that compromise Brackenford and Cuthbert.

---

### 4. The Tihatchee emissary's passage [ISEKAI-friendly]

**Hook:** Lady Mildryd of Brackenford has been hiding a Tihatchee emissary, Atohi-Hesotka, who carries information about the Mtosi dragon-cult intended for the High King Cyneberht. She needs a party to escort him out of Hethrun, through Wessmark, to Cynesburh.

**Patron:** Lady Mildryd (Old Faith, working through covert channels).

**Tier:** 1 (escort through Wessmark; the route is relatively safe but politically sensitive).

**Location:** The southern road through Hethrun, then the Wessmark Aelmsbury road to Cynesburh.

**Success:** Atohi-Hesotka reaches Cynesburh; the High King is informed; Marchland-wide attention is brought to the Mtosi problem.

**Failure:** The emissary is captured or killed; his information dies with him; the dragon-cult escalates uninterrupted.

**Isekai note:** Outsiders carrying foreign passengers face fewer political assumptions than Hethrun-native parties. The journey gives an isekai PC a guided introduction to the broader Marchland geography.

---

### 5. The Aldermarket rumor's source

**Hook:** Multiple parties want to know who started the Aldermarket rumor about Ranulf and Wystan. The regency would pay; Ranulf's people would pay; Cuthbert would pay; Aurelio Maron of House Tassard would pay; Severin would pay. The party is offered the commission by one of these patrons; pursuing it will likely require touching the others.

**Patron:** Any of the named parties (the party may choose).

**Tier:** 1.

**Location:** Eldermouth (the Aldermarket itself, the New Quarter, the Old Quarter); potentially Stonebay if the trail runs east.

**Success:** The source is identified; the party's chosen patron gains a substantial advantage; the rumor either consolidates or unravels based on the answer.

**Failure:** Multiple wrong answers are propagated, the Aldermarket becomes more confused, and several parties make decisions based on bad information.

---

### 6. Master Aldwin's quiet research [ISEKAI-friendly]

**Hook:** Master Aldwin approaches the party privately about his elven-influence hypothesis. He needs help testing it — observing Witan members for the headache pattern, gathering accounts of when fatigue intensified, possibly visiting the wood-road verge where the elves have been appearing. He cannot conduct this work through the obvious channels.

**Patron:** Master Aldwin (court wizard, regency-aligned but operating privately).

**Tier:** 1.

**Location:** Eldermouth and Mossdrowen.

**Success:** Aldwin confirms or disproves his hypothesis; if confirmed, the elven contact becomes a productive avenue.

**Failure:** The hypothesis remains untested; the Witan deadlock continues; Aldwin's private withdrawal deepens.

**Isekai note:** An isekai PC may be useful precisely because they are *outside* the elven influence pattern — they can be the control sample.

---

### 7. The novice's dreams

**Hook:** Sister Aelfwyn of Hollowford asks the party to help with the novice Cynwise — a sixteen-year-old who has begun having vivid dreams that Aelfwyn does not have the expertise to interpret. The party may consult with Master Aldwin, with Gythwin (who would be Aelfwyn's preferred expert if politics permitted), or with Old Eorland.

**Patron:** Sister Aelfwyn (Hollowford monastery; Old Faith ally).

**Tier:** 1.

**Location:** Hollowford monastery; possibly the Heath; possibly Stormwatch.

**Success:** Cynwise's gift is identified and properly oriented (Gythwin's network, or a Sky-Father-trained adept, can guide her); the barrow-folk clock retards meaningfully.

**Failure:** Cynwise's dreams escalate; she has a public episode at the monastery that draws Codex attention; Brother Tirelius's catalogue gains a major entry.

## Tier 1-to-2 transitional seeds

### 8. The Stonebay headland rite

**Hook:** A Stonebay fyrdman (Captain Sweyn Stormhammer's nephew, who is genuinely loyal but troubled) confides in someone at a tavern about an unusual fire-rite he witnessed at the headland four weeks ago. The information eventually reaches the players; with the midwinter conjunction approaching, Branwald will perform the next rite at the same site. The party can observe, disrupt, or investigate.

**Patron:** Initially anonymous (the troubled fyrdman); ultimately patrons could include the regency, Severin, Sigevard, Aurelio Maron, the Bone-Sayers (who would pay to know but would not condone disruption), or Aldwin.

**Tier:** 1-2 (observation is tier-1; disruption requires tier-2 capability to handle Branwald and his Solar Academy reinforcements).

**Location:** The Stonebay headland.

**Success:** Branwald is disrupted, exposed, or killed; the Burning One Working Clock retards substantially or empties.

**Failure:** The party is captured; Branwald advances his clock; the Ostmark-Stonebay alliance consolidates rapidly.

---

### 9. The Stormhold expedition

**Hook:** Eorl Berthwald of the North Coast asks the party to investigate the burned villages on his fyrd-lands. Three villages, each burned in a particular pattern that does not match the usual Cloven Strand raid signature. Old Eorland will assist (his hermitage is on the way).

**Patron:** Eorl Berthwald (regency-aligned eorl, ill, attendance infrequent).

**Tier:** 1-2 (the burned villages are tier-1; the pact-holders are tier-2).

**Location:** The North Coast.

**Success:** The pattern is identified as sea-thing pact-holders propitiating the Wide Eye; the party either eliminates the local pact-holders or negotiates with them; the North Coast stabilizes.

**Failure:** The pact-holders escalate; the sea-thing's slow weather-work intensifies; further North Coast villages are at risk.

---

### 10. Brother Tirelius's catalogue

**Hook:** Multiple parties have reasons to interfere with Brother Tirelius's pastoral-visit cataloguing in Mossdrowen: Sigevard (Old Faith ally, working through the Witan), Mildryd (covert Old Faith devotee, working through the network), Sister Aelfwyn (working through quiet correspondence with senior Codex moderates). The party may be approached by any of them.

**Patron:** Sigevard, Mildryd, or Sister Aelfwyn.

**Tier:** 1-2.

**Location:** Mossdrowen, Hagan's hold, the Old Faith shrine outside the walls.

**Success:** Tirelius is reassigned, discredited, or his catalogue is destroyed; the Old Faith network gains months of breathing room; the Codex-Hollowford Censure Clock retards.

**Failure:** Tirelius completes his catalogue; the Codex moves against Hollowford; Sister Aelfwyn is censured.

## Tier 2 seeds (levels 5-10)

### 11. The Cloven Strand visit

**Hook:** A Cloven Strand jarldom delegation visits Eldermouth, ostensibly on trade business; their actual purpose is to communicate with Ranulf and to assess the regency. The party may be hired by the regency to monitor the delegation; or by Ranulf to facilitate it; or by Aurelio Maron to disrupt it.

**Patron:** Regency, Ranulf, or House Tassard.

**Tier:** 2.

**Location:** Eldermouth, possibly Stonebay (if the delegation continues east).

**Success:** The delegation's purpose is identified; the party's chosen patron gains a substantial advantage.

**Failure:** Misidentification of intent leads to a serious political error.

---

### 12. Aelis's old debt [ISEKAI-friendly]

**Hook:** Aelis the half-elven hermit approaches the party with a quiet personal request. He has an old debt he wishes to settle before he "goes home" (returns to the Silvering). The debt's nature is something he will only disclose to a party he trusts, but it involves a specific Wessmark man who is now a senior thane in Aelmsbury and who, forty years ago, was the reason Aelis fled north.

**Patron:** Aelis personally (operating outside formal elven authority).

**Tier:** 2.

**Location:** Aelis's cot near Mossdrowen; the road through southern Hethrun to Wessmark; Aelmsbury.

**Success:** The debt is settled (the nature of the settlement is to be determined by Aelis's choice; he may want vengeance, restitution, reconciliation, or simply confrontation). Aelis returns to the Silvering, possibly accompanied by a Marchland party as honored guests.

**Failure:** The debt is unsettled; Aelis cannot return home with a clear conscience; the Silvering loses an experienced outer-fringe operative.

**Isekai note:** An isekai PC may be precisely what Aelis is looking for — someone outside the historical Wessmark-Silvering tangle who can carry his message without being read as either side.

---

### 13. The elves' dragon-cult intervention [ISEKAI-friendly]

**Hook:** Captain Saerwen of the Greatwood Wardens, possibly accompanied by Aelis, approaches the party. The Silvering needs deniable agents to deal with the Mtosi dragon-cult in Tihatchee — to investigate, to identify the architect, possibly to eliminate the Vessel-Speaker Eda-Hesotka. The elves cannot move directly without breaking the accord.

**Patron:** The Silvering through Captain Saerwen and Aelis.

**Tier:** 2-3 (Tihatchee is far; the cult is tier-2 in capability; engaging Vaerelloth-adjacent activity is tier-3).

**Location:** Begins at Mossdrowen and the Elvenmeet; extends to Tihatchee (a long journey).

**Success:** The Mtosi cult is disrupted; the architect is identified; the elven-Vaerelloth accord is preserved.

**Failure:** Vaerelloth's investigation completes on the elven side; the Silvering-Vaerelloth war goes hot; Marchland-wide consequences.

**Isekai note:** This is the central isekai-friendly hook for the broader Marchland geography. An isekai PC has no historical baggage with the dragon-cult, with Vaerelloth, or with the elves; they can be precisely the deniable agent the Silvering needs.

---

### 14. Cassian Drey's leverage

**Hook:** The Bone-Sayers' leverage operation on Cassian Drey completes; he is being squeezed for House Volusen information. The regency may suspect (Sigevard may notice Drey's anxiety). The party may be hired by the regency to investigate Drey's vulnerability — or by Aurelio Maron to verify it — or by Cassian himself, if he becomes desperate enough, to *neutralize* the Bone-Sayer creditor.

**Patron:** Sigevard, Aurelio Maron, or (the most dangerous offer) Cassian Drey himself.

**Tier:** 2.

**Location:** Eldermouth and Stonebay.

**Success (any patron):** The leverage operation is documented; the party's patron gains substantial intelligence.

**Failure:** Cassian is leveraged successfully; Ranulf's Clock 1 advances substantially; the regency loses its diplomatic advantage with Aurea.

---

### 15. Count Edmund's chamber

**Hook:** Word reaches the party that Count Edmund of Hollowford March has been excavating an Aureothan ruin on his lands and that his behavior has been strange. Several parties want to intervene: Cuthbert (Edmund is his rival), Severin (who does not approve of the Aurean Patriarchate's direct correspondence with Edmund), Sister Aelfwyn (whose monastery is in Edmund's territory), the regency (which has crown rights over Aureothan finds).

**Patron:** Cuthbert, Severin, Sister Aelfwyn, or the regency.

**Tier:** 2 (extending to 3 if the chamber is opened).

**Location:** Hollowford March, Edmund's manor lands.

**Success:** The excavation is halted; the chamber remains sealed; Edmund is brought to account through one of the available channels.

**Failure:** The chamber is opened; what emerges becomes a tier-3 regional threat.

## Tier 3 seeds (levels 11-16)

### 16. The Stridvik engine

**Hook:** A leak in the Cloven Strand jarldom (or a deliberate intelligence operation by the High King's Hidden Hand) reveals the existence of the Aureothan war-engine on Stridvik island. The High King wants the engine assessed and (if possible) neutralized; Hadolf One-Hand wants the secret kept; Ranulf wants the engine preserved as a leverage asset.

**Patron:** The High King's Hidden Hand.

**Tier:** 3.

**Location:** Cloven Strand peninsula; Stridvik island.

**Success:** The engine is neutralized; the regional balance shifts; the High King gains substantial leverage over Hadolf.

**Failure:** The engine remains operable; Hadolf retains his threat-credit; the Marchland balance is unchanged.

---

### 17. The Sealhalls expedition

**Hook:** Old Eorland of Stormwatch tells the party of a deep-coast ruin called the Sealhalls — a place where the older pre-Aureothan peoples gathered, and where the Iron Strait sea-thing's longest-standing pact-holders maintain a permanent shrine. He believes the Sealhalls are connected to the Heath barrow seals and that something is happening there. (He is correct.)

**Patron:** Old Eorland; possibly Gythwin if the barrow-folk clock has advanced; possibly Aldwin if his elven-influence hypothesis has led him to a wider perspective.

**Tier:** 3.

**Location:** The North Coast Sealhalls (deep coastal ruin).

**Success:** The Sealhalls are surveyed; the sea-thing's slow weather-work is documented and either negotiated with or partially contained; a major piece of the Marchland mythic geography is mapped.

**Failure:** Whatever is happening at the Sealhalls continues; the North Coast's wider stability erodes.

---

### 18. Coelius Vetranus's administrative request

**Hook:** Master Aldwin receives Coelius Vetranus's overdue annual letter; it contains a procedural request that Aldwin cannot fulfill himself. The lich's "jurisdiction" requires assistance with a matter Coelius considers routine — actually, an interference in his archive-maintenance that suggests something else is operating in or near his ruin. He needs an outside party (with proper diplomatic credentials he will provide) to investigate.

**Patron:** Master Aldwin, transmitting Coelius Vetranus's request.

**Tier:** 3.

**Location:** Western Greatwood verge; Coelius's buried Aureothan ruin.

**Success:** The party serves as Coelius's investigators, learns what is interfering with his work (one possibility: a fragment of the original Aureothan working — the same working whose collapse caused Aureoth's fall — has been awakened by Count Edmund's chamber excavation, and is now leaking toward Coelius's archive), and resolves it on the lich's terms.

**Failure:** Coelius's archive is compromised; an additional Aureothan-revivalist resource is loose in the Marchlands.

## Tier 4 / long arc

A note: the campaign's long arc — what happens at session 80-plus, if the campaign runs that long — would converge several threads. The most likely tier-4 endpoint is the Smokeless Throne's architect-of-the-Mtosi-cult role being uncovered, followed by either a Silvering-faerie-court war or a renegotiation of the accord. A party that has survived to tier 4 has played the previous arcs through and would now be operating at the level of Vajra, Vaerelloth, and Lord Avariel. The GM should not pre-script this; let the players' choices through tiers 1-3 determine which threads are still live.

---

## Hook seeding for session zero

For the first session, the GM should seed (not necessarily resolve, just *introduce*) two or three of these hooks. Recommended starting set:

- **Seed 1** (the missing Codex courier) — gives the party an immediate, well-defined task with a known patron and a clear destination
- **Seed 2** (the Heath cunning-woman's invitation) — gives the party a Old Faith network introduction
- **Seed 6** (Master Aldwin's quiet research) — gives the party access to the court wizard, who is a node many other hooks pass through

These three combined cover the major Hethrun political and religious factions (Codex, Old Faith, court), give the party three potential patrons, and naturally pull them toward Eldermouth, the Heath, and (eventually) the wood-road verge and Brackenford. From there, the campaign's wider geography opens.

The other fifteen hooks should be in the GM's pocket, ready to be deployed when player choices and clock advancement create the right narrative moment.
