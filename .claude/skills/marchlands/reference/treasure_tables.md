# Treasure Tables

Treasure tables calibrated for solo level-3-up Hethrun play (gritty permadeath D&D 2024). Tier-banded, RAG-friendly. DMG 2024 rarity buckets (Common / Uncommon / Rare / Very Rare / Legendary) anchor the magic-item slots; for specific item statblocks, the GM looks up the chosen DMG entry. This file provides the **slot calibration** (how often, what rarity, what value-band) and the **Hethrun flavor layer** (renamed item categories, faction-specific relics, named curiosities) — never the DMG items themselves.

Coin and gear are original to the Marchlands; magic items refer to DMG by rarity only.

## How to use this file

The treasure procedure:

1. **Identify the source.** Encounter aftermath, ruin cache, faction relic, named-threat reward, downtime acquisition — each has its own pacing in the **Treasure budget** section below.
2. **Roll on the appropriate table.** Coin and curiosities roll on the Hethrun-flavored tables in this file. Magic items roll on the **DMG slot table** below for rarity, then the GM consults the DMG 2024 entry for the specific item.
3. **Apply Hethrun flavor.** For any rolled item not already Hethrun-named, apply a flavor overlay from the **Hethrun item category** tables — bog-iron weapons, Veiled Mother charms, Sky-Father skystones, Aureothan residue-curiosities, Stonebay guildwork, Cloven Strand reaver-gear, Ostmark Solar-marked relics, Aurean court-pieces, faith-relics. The mechanical statblock stays the DMG entry; only the name and description shift.
4. **Track value in Hethrun coin.** Conversion in the Coin section.

For named-threat-tier loot and faction relics, pick from the **Named relics** section directly rather than rolling.

## Treasure budget

How much treasure, and what rarity, the GM should give out as a function of scene type and PC level. Solo gritty play means slower magic-item accretion than a default 5.5e curve; bias toward useful Uncommons over rare or stronger items, and bias toward consumables over permanent items.

### By scene type

| Scene type | Coin / gear band | Magic-item odds |
|------------|------------------|------------------|
| Random encounter (Mode A) — loot rolled | Minor (1–25 gp equivalent per defeated foe; gear of the tier the foe carried) | None unless lieutenant or higher |
| Encounter against a lieutenant (CR 4–6) | Modest (50–200 gp equivalent in goods, gear, and saleable curiosities) | 1 Common or Uncommon, 30% |
| Encounter against a boss (CR 7+) | Substantial (300–1000 gp) | 1 Uncommon guaranteed; 25% chance of a second Common or a consumable |
| Ruin cache (minor) | Modest (50–250 gp) | 1 Common, often a consumable |
| Ruin cache (main vault) | Hoard (500–2,500 gp at PC level 3–5; 2,000–8,000 gp at level 6–10) | 1 Uncommon and 1 Common, or 1 Rare at PC level 7+ |
| Ruin cache (named ruin's primary vault) | Hoard plus narrative relic | 1 Rare guaranteed at PC level 5+; named relic instead at PC level 7+ |
| Named-threat aftermath | Hoard plus the threat's signature item | 1 Rare or Very Rare keyed to the threat |
| Downtime acquisition | Variable per cost | Common / Uncommon by negotiation; Rare requires faction patronage |

### By PC level — magic item rarity slot

At each level milestone, the corpus expects (does not guarantee) the following accumulated magic-item slots. Solo play is harsher than party play; falling behind this is fine and survivable.

| PC level | Common | Uncommon | Rare | Very Rare | Legendary |
|----------|--------|----------|------|-----------|-----------|
| 3 | 1–2 | 0–1 | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| 5 | 2–3 | 1–2 | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| 7 | 2–4 | 2–3 | 1 | 0 | 0 |
| 9 | 2–4 | 2–4 | 1–2 | 0–1 | 0 |
| 11+ | as previous | 3+ | 2+ | 1 | 0–1 |

Consumables count toward the rarity slot they fall under but get used up; they're the safe pressure-release valve when the GM wants to reward the player without escalating the long-arc curve.

## Coin and currency

Hethrun trades in three coin systems that converge at Eldermouth's exchange. For solo play, use the Hethrun penny (Hp) as the base unit and convert other coin on the spot when foreign exchange enters the scene.

### Hethrun coin

| Coin | Material | Value (in Hp) | Notes |
|------|----------|---------------|-------|
| Hethrun penny (Hp) | Silver, low purity | 1 | Base unit. A day's labour, a meal at a country inn, a tallow candle. |
| Hethrun shilling (Hs) | Silver, full purity | 12 Hp | A night at a town inn with food, a fyrd-week's pay, a decent dagger. |
| Hethrun mark (Hm) | Silver, struck heavy | 160 Hp | A horse, a quarter-year's huscarl service, a small farmstead's annual rent. |
| Hethrun crown (Hcr) | Gold | 1,920 Hp | Rare in circulation; a noble settlement, a manor's purchase, a guildmaster's wager. |

### Foreign coin and exchange

| Foreign | Origin | Conversion | Notes |
|---------|--------|------------|-------|
| Wessmark mark (Wm) | High Kingdom | 1 Wm = 1.1 Hm | Higher purity; preferred for large transactions at Eldermouth. |
| Aurean sol (As) | Aurea | 1 As = 200 Hp | Romance-coded gold solidus; Volusen-stamped sols are sometimes refused by Old Faith priesthoods. |
| Ostmark stavros (Os) | Ostmark | 1 Os = 180 Hp | Silver, fire-embossed; Solar Academy issue. |
| Stonebay guild penny | Stonebay Council | 1 Sg = 1 Hp face, 1.05 Hp by weight | Higher silver content than the crown penny; preferred for guild trade. |
| Cloven Strand ring-silver | Cloven Strand | Weight-priced | Not coined; the Norse pay in cut-silver rings and arm-rings. |
| Skarrgaard hack-silver | Skarrgaard | Weight-priced | Loot-only; the deeper Norse don't coin at all. |
| Chola fanam | Chola Vellanaadu | 1 Cf = 25 Hp | Rare in Hethrun; Tihatchee envoy's coin. |

For Cloven Strand and Skarrgaard silver, weigh at the rate of 1 Hp per silver-penny-equivalent. A handful of raid-silver is typically 20–80 Hp by weight.

### Pre-Aureothan coin

Found in ruins. Worth significant collector value to Eldermouth scholars, the Stonebay guildmasters, and certain Codex archivists.

| Coin | Description | Value to a buyer | Notes |
|------|-------------|------------------|-------|
| Aureothan aurum | Heavy gold, sunburst-stamped | 50–200 Hp per coin | Master Aldwin at the Royal Academy buys at 200 Hp; a guild appraiser pays 80–120; a Brackenford trader pays 50–80 if at all. |
| Aureothan silver | Imperial silver, eagle-stamped | 10–25 Hp per coin | Same buyers; less heat than aurum. |
| Imperial relic-coin | Mint-state, signature emperor named | 500–5,000 Hp by named emperor | Aldwin pays top; Codex archivists pay middle; a Wessmark collector might outbid for a specific emperor. |

Pre-Aureothan coin is technically the property of the crown by ancient writ; selling it openly to the wrong buyer is a minor charge. Most buyers don't ask.

## Magic items — DMG 2024 rarity slots

For magic items, roll the rarity slot below, then the GM consults the DMG 2024 magic item entries at that rarity for the specific item. Apply a Hethrun flavor overlay from the next section to rename and re-describe.

### Rarity slot table (d100 by scene context)

For an **encounter aftermath** with magic-item odds (per the Treasure budget table):

| d100 | Rarity slot |
|------|-------------|
| 01–60 | Common (or a Common consumable) |
| 61–88 | Uncommon |
| 89–97 | Rare |
| 98–99 | Very Rare |
| 100 | Legendary (re-roll unless the encounter was a named-threat boss; legendaries are story-event drops) |

For a **ruin cache main vault**, shift the d100 up by 20 (an 01 result becomes 21, etc.). For a **named-threat aftermath**, the GM picks the rarity from the threat's level, not the dice.

### Hethrun item-category overlay

Apply one category to any rolled magic item to re-flavor the rename and description. The category does not change the DMG mechanical statblock.

**Bog-iron weaponry** — weapons forged from Harrowmark bog-iron, marked with the foundry's stamp. Distinctive heft, slightly darker than standard steel, edge holds longer than ordinary iron. Common: any +0 weapon. Uncommon: any +1 weapon. The bog-iron overlay implies the weapon was made in the Harrowmark and carries a faint earth-and-bog smell that hounds notice.

**Veiled Mother charm** — Old Faith devotional pieces, often paired with a small wax-and-herb component. Common: a one-use blessing, a minor ward, a healing draught. Uncommon: a wondrous item with a charm flavor — necklace, bracelet, sash, talisman. The charm overlay implies the item was made by a Veiled Mother cunning-woman and carries her blessing; bearing it openly draws Codex attention.

**Sky-Father skystone** — Old Faith devotional, the descendant of meteorite-iron. Cold to the touch, faintly luminous in moonlight. Common: a one-use protection or warding effect. Uncommon: any +1 weapon or armor with a sky-iron description. The skystone overlay implies the item was made or blessed by a Sky-Father priest and is recognised as Old-Faith-sacred.

**Aureothan residue-curiosity** — pre-Aureothan magical residue solidified into a usable form. Crystalline or glass-like, faintly humming at certain hours. Common: a one-use spell-effect (the residue discharges and is consumed). Uncommon: a wondrous item, often with an unusual activation condition. The residue overlay implies the item was found in an Aureothan ruin and may carry a small Aureothan-Constructs-attention risk if displayed openly.

**Stonebay guildwork** — Council-of-Twelve-made, the most reliable mundane craft in the Marchlands carried just over the line into the magical. Common: any +0 weapon or armor item described as "guild-marked." Uncommon: any +1 weapon, armor, or wondrous item with the guild's maker stamp. The guildwork overlay implies the item is registered in Stonebay's records — a knowledgeable witness can sometimes trace its provenance.

**Cloven Strand reaver-gear** — Norse-made, often bearing rune-marks for sea-faring, raid-glory, or weather. Distinctive in cut and finish. Common: any +0 weapon or armor. Uncommon: any +1 weapon, often an axe or sword, with one rune-active property. The reaver-gear overlay implies the item carries a name and a saga — and may interest a Cloven Strand investigator if displayed.

**Ostmark Solar-marked relic** — fire-bound items from the Ostmark Solar Academy. Often embossed with a sunburst or a flame-script. Common: a one-use fire-related effect. Uncommon: a wondrous item with a heat- or light-related property. The Solar overlay implies the item is potentially Burning-One-cell relevant; Branwald's network would pay or kill for it.

**Aurean court-piece** — Romance-coded fine work from Aurea. Elegant, often jewelled or enamelled. Common: a wondrous item of social-grace effect. Uncommon: any +1 weapon or armor of fine make. The Aurean overlay implies House Volusen and the Patriarchate have interests; displaying it openly in Brackenford draws hostile attention.

**Faith-relic (Codex)** — Codex-Church-blessed items, often the regalia of a saint or a martyred priest. Common: a one-use protection from undead or fiends. Uncommon: any +1 weapon or holy symbol with a radiant-damage property. The relic overlay implies the item is tracked by Codex archives; carrying it without sanction is a small ecclesiastical problem.

**Tihatchee dragon-cult piece** — Mtosi-tied items, often carved with dragon-imagery or wyrm-script. Common: a one-use fire- or acid-effect. Uncommon: any wondrous item with a draconic property. The Mtosi overlay implies A4 Mtosi Cult Cells and Vaerelloth's investigation both notice the item.

**Drift-come anomaly** — items from beyond Hethrun's normal magical inventory, brought (or matched) by a drift-come arrival. Strange materials, unfamiliar maker-marks, sometimes inscriptions in a language no Hethrun scholar reads. Common to Legendary, any rarity. The drift-come overlay implies A1 Arrival-Hunters are interested; the item may be a campaign-significant artifact.

## Named relics

For named-threat aftermath, faction-relic recovery, and story-significant treasure events. Each is a specific item tied to a corpus arc; the GM places it deliberately rather than rolling for it.

### Crown-realm relics

- **The Aelfric Crown** — Hethrun's traditional crown. Currently in the regency's possession. Possession is the central object of the Coronation crisis. Legendary; political, not mechanical. The GM never places this; it is the campaign's gravity.
- **The Witan Seal** — the seven-eorl-mark stamp that validates Witan votes. A faction holding the Seal can stall or accelerate the Coronation. Very Rare. Currently with Sigevard the Silver-Tongue's confidant in the Royal Hall.
- **The Aldermarket Charter** — the original Eldermouth merchant-charter, dating to the realm's founding. Rare. Currently in Stonebay's Council archives, on permanent loan from the crown.

### Stonebay and the Bone-Sayers

- **The Bone-Sayer's Ledger** — Edrid's master leverage record, kept in cypher. Whoever holds it controls the leverage network. Very Rare. Currently with Edrid himself.
- **Ranulf's Charter of Trade-Privilege** — the regency-era charter that lets Stonebay charge harbor fees independent of the crown. Rare. Currently in the Council of Twelve's archives.
- **The Stridvik Compact** — the secret arms-trade agreement between Ranulf and Hadolf. Rare. Currently in three copies — Ranulf's strongbox, Hadolf's hall on Cloven Strand, the Bone-Sayer's Ledger.

### The Old Faith

- **The Veiled Mother's Spindle** — Mother Eadlin's hereditary office-piece, kept at the Harrowmark shrine. Rare. Active in seasonal rites; passive otherwise. Tied to Clock 4 (barrow-folk stir) — the Spindle is part of the rite that maintains the deep-barrow seals.
- **Sigevard's Raven-Pendant** — the skald's office-piece, gifted to him by Aelfric the Just. Uncommon. Mechanically a *bardic* style consumable focus.
- **The Lawspeaker's Stone** — Old Sigrún's stone at the Fellmoot. Rare. Channels Old Faith fate-readings when used in the Fellmoot Stones ritual circle.

### The Codex Church

- **Severin's Reliquary** — Father Severin's office reliquary, holding a finger-bone of Saint Aelfric the Confessor. Rare. Tied to Codex doctrine debates with the Patriarchate's Reformed faction.
- **The Patriarch's Mandate** — a sealed letter from the Aurean Patriarchate authorizing crusade against named heresies. Rare to Very Rare depending on what's named. Currently being assembled by Brother Tirelius in Mossdrowen.

### Aureothan and pre-Aureothan

- **The Stormhold Engine-Key** — the activation key for the Stormhold's weather-engine. Very Rare. Currently lost; rumored to be in the Stonebay underwater chamber, the Hollowford Monastery vault, or with Count Edmund.
- **Edmund's Cipher-Plates** — the working notebooks of Edmund's Aureothan excavation. Rare. Currently in his manor at the Hollowford March.
- **The Drowning Quarter Seal** — a pre-Aureothan binding seal in the chamber below Brackenford. Very Rare. Currently active and stable; if disturbed, the chamber's contents stir.
- **The Wivelfen Compendium** — a partial codex of pre-Aureothan magical residue-binding, found in fragments in the Wivelfen experiment site. Rare to Very Rare depending on completeness.

### External-power relics

- **Hadolf's Saga-Axe** — Jarl Hadolf's named axe, runed for sea-glory. Very Rare. With Hadolf on Cloven Strand.
- **The Solar Academy's Star-Reckoner** — a portable orrery used to time Branwald's working. Very Rare. With Branwald in his Stonebay safe-house.
- **A Smokeless Throne Contract** — a sealed contract, vellum bound in iron. Very Rare. Several in circulation; Hollantun has signed at least one.
- **The Silvering Greeting-Token** — a small carved leaf that, when shown at an Elvenmeet, grants safe-passage on the wood-roads. Rare. Saerwen carries one; the Silvering has more.
- **The Tihatchee Wyrm-Tooth** — a Mtosi-cult relic in the shape of a dragon's tooth, dimly warm to the touch. Rare. Atohi-Hesotka carries one.
