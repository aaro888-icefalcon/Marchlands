# D&D 5.5e (2024) Reference

Companion to `GM_Further_Guidelines.md`. All rule specifics, tables, and 2024-vs-5e change notes live here. Consult by section as needed during play.

---

## 1. Character Creation

### Background

- Grants ability score increases: **+2/+1** to two scores, OR **+1/+1/+1** across three.
- Grants an **Origin Feat**.
- Two skill proficiencies, one tool proficiency.
- Starting equipment package OR 50 gp.

### Origin Feats (selected list)

Alert, Crafter, Healer, Lucky, Magic Initiate (Arcane / Divine / Primal), Musician, Savage Attacker, Skilled, Tavern Brawler, Tough.

### Species (no longer "Race")

Provides traits only — **no ability score increases**. Examples:

- **Human** — choose one: Resourceful (extra Heroic Inspiration after long rest), Skillful (extra skill proficiency), Versatile (Origin Feat).
- **Elf** — Darkvision 60 ft, Trance, Keen Senses, Fey Ancestry; choose lineage (Drow / High / Wood) at level 1, gains scaling spells.
- **Dwarf** — Darkvision 120 ft, Dwarven Resilience (advantage vs. poison, resistance to poison damage), Dwarven Toughness (+1 HP/level), Stonecunning.
- **Halfling** — Brave, Halfling Nimbleness, Lucky, Naturally Stealthy.
- **Dragonborn** — Draconic Ancestry, Breath Weapon (uses now scale with prof bonus), Damage Resistance, flight at level 5.
- **Gnome / Orc / Tiefling / Goliath / Aasimar** — see PHB.

### Class

All 12 classes revised. **Subclass at level 3** for nearly all classes (Cleric, Sorcerer, Warlock, Wizard included — they no longer pick at level 1).

### Class group features

- **Martial classes** get Weapon Mastery slots scaling by level (Fighter starts with 3, gains more).
- **Spellcaster Mastery** prepared spell counts simplified across casters.
- **Half-casters** (Paladin, Ranger) start spellcasting at level 2.

### Feats

Four categories: **Origin Feats** (level 1 from Background), **General Feats** (every ASI level), **Fighting Style Feats** (where granted by class), **Epic Boon Feats** (level 19+).

### Common skill DC anchors

- Climb a knotted rope: DC 5 (or no roll if uncontested)
- Pick a simple lock: DC 10–15
- Climb a sheer cliff with handholds: DC 15
- Pick a complex magical lock: DC 20–25
- Identify a creature by lore: DC 10 (common) → 20 (rare/extraplanar)
- Notice an obvious threat: passive Perception 10
- Spot a hidden enemy in dim light: passive Perception 14–16
- Persuade Indifferent NPC to a small favor: DC 10
- Persuade Hostile NPC against their interests: DC 20+

## 3. Heroic Inspiration (2024)

- Granted on a **natural 20** on any d20 test.
- Plus from class/feat/species sources (e.g., Human Resourceful, Bard's Bardic Inspiration interactions).
- Stored. Spent to **reroll any d20** (yours, after seeing the result).
- Maximum one Heroic Inspiration held at a time unless a feature says otherwise.

## 4. Exhaustion (2024)

Each level imposes:

- **−2 to all d20 tests** (attack rolls, saves, ability checks)
- **−5 ft. speed**

Six levels = death. Long rest removes one level. Greater Restoration removes one level.

## 5. Death Saves & Critical Hits

### Death Saves (2024)

- Triggered when reduced to 0 HP and not killed outright.
- d20 vs DC 10 at start of each turn while at 0 HP.
- **Three failures** = dead. **Three successes** = stable at 0 HP.
- **Nat 20** → regain 1 HP and become conscious immediately.
- **Nat 1** → counts as **two failures**.
- **Massive damage:** any damage taken at 0 HP equal to or greater than HP max = dead outright, no saves.
- Damage at 0 HP otherwise = one failure (two if it was a critical).
- Stabilized creatures regain 1 HP after 1d4 hours.

### Critical Hits (2024)

- Natural 20 on attack roll = critical hit.
- Roll the weapon's (or spell's) damage dice **twice**, then add modifiers normally. Do not double the flat modifier.
- Additional dice from features (Sneak Attack, Divine Smite, Hunter's Mark) also roll twice on a crit.
- **2024 change:** monsters generally do not crit; only PCs and specific named monsters do. The GM may grant crit capacity to particular boss or villain NPCs case by case.

### Healer's Kit

- Costs 5 gp. 10 charges per kit.
- Each charge: expended to stabilize a dying creature (with the Stabilize action / Medicine check) **or** grant 1d6+4 HP via the Healer feat.
- Refilling is essentially repurchase — a depleted kit is discarded.
- Carrying multiple kits is normal practice in a gritty register.

## 6. Action Types

### Standard actions (2024 list)

- **Attack** — make weapon attacks per Extra Attack rules.
- **Cast a Spell** — casting time of an action.
- **Dash** — extra movement equal to speed.
- **Disengage** — no opportunity attacks this turn.
- **Dodge** — attacks vs. you have disadvantage; advantage on Dex saves until next turn.
- **Help** — give an ally advantage on next ability check or attack roll, OR provide first-aid.
- **Hide** — Stealth check vs. passive Perception of observers.
- **Influence (NEW)** — Cha check (Persuasion / Deception / Intimidation / Performance) vs. NPC's stated DC, modified by attitude.
- **Magic** — use a magic item or feature with a Magic action cost.
- **Ready** — prepare action with trigger; uses your reaction.
- **Search** — Wisdom (Perception / Insight / Survival) or Int (Investigation) check.
- **Study (NEW)** — Intelligence check to recall lore or analyze something.
- **Utilize (NEW)** — use a non-weapon object that requires an action (lever, pulley, lantern).
- **Improvise** — anything else not on the list; DM sets the resolution.

### Other turn elements

- **Bonus Action** — only when a feature/spell grants one.
- **Reaction** — once per round, off-turn, on a defined trigger. Common triggers: Opportunity Attack (enemy leaves your reach without Disengage), Counterspell (enemy casts within 60 ft), Shield spell (you are about to be hit), Hellish Rebuke (you take damage), readied action (your trigger fires).
- **Movement** — splittable across the turn.
- **Free interaction** — one object per turn outside of an action (drawing a weapon, opening an unlocked door, etc.).

## 7. Weapon Mastery (Eight Properties)

Apply automatically on hit. When multiple apply, the player picks one.

|Property  |Effect                                                                                                 |Example weapons                                  |
|----------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
|**Cleave**|On hit, a second creature within 5 ft of the first takes weapon damage (no ability mod). Once per turn.|Greataxe, Halberd                                |
|**Graze** |On a *miss* with the Attack action, deal ability mod damage of the weapon's type.                      |Glaive, Greatsword                               |
|**Nick**  |Light + Nick off-hand attack folds into the Attack action; no bonus action used.                       |Dagger, Light Hammer, Scimitar, Sickle           |
|**Push**  |On hit, push Large or smaller 10 ft directly away.                                                     |Greatclub, Pike, Warhammer, Heavy Crossbow       |
|**Sap**   |On hit, target has disadvantage on its next attack roll before your next turn.                         |Mace, Spear, Morningstar, Flail                  |
|**Slow**  |On hit, target's speed −10 ft until start of your next turn.                                           |Longbow, Club, Whip, Javelin, Sling              |
|**Topple**|On hit, target makes Con save (DC 8 + prof + Str/Dex mod) or falls prone.                              |Battleaxe, Lance, Quarterstaff, Maul, Trident    |
|**Vex**   |On hit, advantage on your next attack vs. same target before end of your next turn.                    |Shortsword, Hand Crossbow, Rapier, Dart, Shortbow|

### Mastery slot progression by class (selected)

- **Barbarian:** 2 → 3 (lvl 4) → 4 (lvl 10)
- **Fighter:** 3 → 4 (lvl 4) → 5 (lvl 10) → 6 (lvl 16)
- **Paladin / Ranger:** 2 → 3 (lvl 10)
- **Rogue:** 2 → 3 (lvl 5)

Players can change which weapons they have masteries with after a long rest.

## 8. Two-Weapon Fighting (2024)

- Both weapons must be **Light**.
- Bonus action off-hand attack; ability mod **NOT added to damage** unless a feature grants it (e.g., Two-Weapon Fighting Style).
- With a **Nick** weapon in main hand, the off-hand attack folds into the Attack action — bonus action stays free.
- Drawing two weapons in one turn is part of the free object interaction (one each, but both with the Attack action's "draw a weapon" allowance).

### Conditions

|-----------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Blinded**      |Can't see; auto-fail sight checks; attack rolls vs. you have advantage; your attacks have disadvantage.                                  |
|**Charmed**      |Can't attack the source; source has advantage on social interactions with you.                                                           |
|**Deafened**     |Can't hear; auto-fail hearing checks.                                                                                                    |
|**Frightened**   |Disadvantage on ability checks and attacks while source in line of sight; can't move closer to source.                                   |

|**Incapacitated**|No actions, bonus actions, reactions; speed 0; can't concentrate.                                                                        |
|**Invisible**    |Can't be seen without magic; advantage on attacks; attacks vs. you have disadvantage.                                                    |
|**Paralyzed**    |Incapacitated, can't move/speak; auto-fail Str/Dex saves; attacks vs. you have advantage; melee hits within 5 ft are crits.              |
|**Petrified**    |Turned to stone; incapacitated; resistance to all damage; immune to poison/disease.                                                      |
|**Poisoned**     |Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks.                                                                                         |
|**Prone**        |Disadvantage on attacks; melee vs. you has advantage; ranged vs. you has disadvantage.                                                   |

|**Surprised**    |**Disadvantage on initiative roll only** (NEW — no longer skipped turn).                                                                 |
|**Unconscious**  |Incapacitated, prone, drops items; auto-fail Str/Dex saves; attacks vs. you have advantage; melee hits within 5 ft are crits.            |

### Cover

|Cover           |Effect                                                       |
|----------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Half cover**  |+2 AC and Dex saves                                          |
|**Three-quarters cover**|+5 AC and Dex saves                                  |
|**Total cover** |Cannot be targeted directly; spells with area effects may still affect|

A creature behind another creature gets at most half cover. Cover does not apply if the attacker is at the same point of origin as the cover source.

### Vision & Light

|Light level     |Effect                                                       |
|----------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Bright light**|Normal vision. Most outdoors-during-day or well-lit interiors.|
|**Dim light**   |Lightly obscured. Wis (Perception) checks relying on sight at disadvantage.|
|**Darkness**    |Heavily obscured. Effectively Blinded when looking into it; attacks against creatures inside have disadvantage; their attacks have advantage. Darkvision negates this to dim light at the listed range.|

A torch sheds bright light in a 20-ft radius and dim light for an additional 20 ft. A candle: bright 5 ft, dim 5 ft additional. A lantern (hooded): bright 30, dim 30. Sunrod-equivalent items vary by setting.

## 10. Spellcasting

### Mechanics

- **Concentration save:** Con save, DC 10 or half damage taken (whichever higher), on each instance of damage while concentrating.
- **Components:** V (silenced characters can't), S (need a free hand or held focus), M (consumed only if cost is listed in the spell).
- **Casting time:** action, bonus action, reaction, or longer ritual time. **Bonus action spells:** if you cast a non-cantrip with a bonus action, your *only* other spell that turn must be a cantrip with a casting time of an action (this restriction returned in 2024).

### Key 2024 spell revisions to apply by default

- **Hunter's Mark** — damage and duration scale with slot level; concentration retained.
- **Conjure Minor Elementals** — now a damage-aura buff (added damage on hits within range), no creature summoned.
- **Conjure X** spells generally — most reworked to grant effects rather than summon discrete creatures.
- **True Strike** — promoted to weapon-attack-rider cantrip; can deal radiant damage as alternative.
- **Counterspell** — target makes a save against the casting caster's spell save DC (no longer a flat ability check).
- **Find Familiar** — familiars can take the Help action.
- **Magic Missile** — does not multiply on upcast for separate concentration purposes; force damage as before.
- **Power Word Kill** — HP threshold raised to 100.
- **Bless** — granted die scales with slot.
- **Healing Word / Cure Wounds** — minor scaling adjustments.
- **Spike Growth, Spirit Guardians, Aid, Heroes' Feast** — scaling and duration tweaks.

When in doubt, use the 2024 version of any spell.

### Ritual casting

Add 10 minutes to casting time; consumes no slot. Class must have ritual access (Bard, Cleric, Druid, Wizard, etc.).

## 12. Rest

- **Short Rest** — 1 hour. Spend Hit Dice (1d[class HD] + Con mod per die) to recover HP. Short-rest features reset.
- **Long Rest** — 8 hours, max once per 24 hours. Effects: full HP, half max Hit Dice restored (rounded down, min 1), all spell slots back, exhaustion −1, most class features reset.
- **Interrupted rest** — disrupted if it includes >1 hour of strenuous activity (fighting, casting non-trivial spells, walking >1 mile, etc.).
- **Survival check for camp** — DC 10–15 to find a defensible site in wilderness; failure means random encounter check at disadvantage.

## 13. Influence Action — Attitude DCs

|Attitude       |Behavior                                                              |
|---------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Friendly**   |Low DC (≈10). Will help with minor concessions; harder for major ones.|
|**Indifferent**|Medium DC (≈15). Helps if cost is small; won't take risk for free.    |
|**Hostile**    |High DC (≈20+). Won't agree to anything against their interests.      |

A successful Influence check shifts attitude **one step** maximum (Hostile → Indifferent, Indifferent → Friendly). It does not rewrite core motivation. A nat 20 doesn't make a sworn enemy a lifelong ally.

DCs are GM-determined in the moment based on the specific NPC, the size of the request, alignment with NPC interests, and any leverage or context. The table above is anchoring guidance, not a fixed schedule.

## 15. Carrying & Encumbrance (variant)

- Carry capacity = Str × 15 lb (lifted/dragged Str × 30).
- Encumbered (optional rule) at Str × 5 lb: speed −10 ft.
- Heavily encumbered at Str × 10 lb: speed −20 ft, disadvantage on Str/Dex/Con checks/attacks/saves.

## 16. Hazards & Environment Quick Reference

- **Suffocation:** hold breath = 1 + Con mod minutes (min 30 sec); then 0 HP and dying next round.
- **Falling:** 1d6 bludgeoning per 10 ft fallen, max 20d6.
- **Fire (open flame):** 1d10 fire damage per round of contact.
- **Lava:** 10d10 fire damage per round of immersion.
- **Cold (frigid water):** Con save DC 10 every minute or gain exhaustion.
- **Cold (winter exposure, inadequate clothing):** Con save DC 10 each hour at temps below freezing, DC scales by 5 per −10°C; failure = 1 level of exhaustion.
- **Starvation:** after Con-mod days without food, gain exhaustion daily.
- **Thirst:** half normal water → DC 15 Con save daily or exhaustion; no water → exhaustion in 1 day.

### Common Diseases (selection)

|Disease         |Contracted by                              |Effect                                                                                                                                |
|----------------|-------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Filth Fever**  |Bite from diseased rat / contaminated water |Con save DC 11 after 1d4 days incubation. Failure: disadvantage on Str checks/saves; cannot regain HP except by magic. Lasts 1d4 days unless cured.|
|**Sewer Plague**|Bite from contaminated wound or sewer exposure|Con save DC 11 after 1d4 days. Failure: long rest only recovers HALF normal HP and slots. Lasts 1d4 weeks unless cured.                |
|**Cackle Fever**|Inhaled / contact, common in crowded settlements|Con save DC 13 after 1d4 days. Failure: madness (random behavior 1d10 min during stress). Long rest required between episodes.       |
|**Sight Rot**   |Contaminated water in stagnant springs     |Con save DC 15 after 1 day. Failure: vision blurs, blindness within 1d4 days. Cured by *Lesser Restoration* or specific herbs.        |

Disease can be removed by *Lesser Restoration*, *Greater Restoration*, *Heal*, or extended bed rest with successful Medicine checks (DC = save DC, one per day; three consecutive successes cures).

## 17. Heroic Inspiration vs. Class Inspiration

- Bardic Inspiration, Bard's Inspiration die, etc. are class-specific resources distinct from Heroic Inspiration.
- Heroic Inspiration is binary (have/don't have); class inspirations vary in form (dice, bonuses, etc.).
- Some classes (Fighter, Bard) interact with Heroic Inspiration via specific subclass features.

## 18. Travel Pace & Procedure Snippets

### Travel pace (overland)

|Pace      |Per hour |Per day  |Effect                                                                              |
|----------|---------|---------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Fast**  |4 mph    |30 miles |−5 to passive Perception. No Stealth.                                              |
|**Normal**|3 mph    |24 miles |No Stealth.                                                                         |
|**Slow**  |2 mph    |18 miles |Stealth possible. Forced march beyond 8 hours: Con save DC 10+1/hour or exhaustion.|

Terrain modifiers apply:
- Difficult terrain (forest, swamp, snow): halve pace.
- Mountains / dense forest: typically reduce to 12 miles/day.
- Mounted travel: as listed, but mounts must rest after 1 hour at fast pace or accumulate exhaustion.

### Random encounter check (wilderness)

1d20 per watch / per day of travel. On 18+, encounter. Roll on regional table.

### Hidden creature reveal

Stealth ≥ Passive Perception → hidden. Recheck if conditions change (light, noise, line of sight). Active Perception (Search action) uses opposed Wis (Perception) vs. Stealth roll.

### Chase scenes

Each round: Dash for free; Con save DC 10 to keep Dashing past round 3 (gain exhaustion on fail, escalating DC). Add complications drawn from terrain (DC 10–15 Athletics/Acrobatics to navigate).

### Downtime activities (per workweek = 7 days)

Crafting, training, carousing, gathering rumors, recovering from lingering injuries. Gold cost varies by lifestyle (Squalid 7sp/wk → Aristocratic 70gp+/wk).

## 19. Output Format References

The project instructions point here for formatting conventions during play.

### Combat — enemy turn block

Use a structured block per enemy turn. Compact, one line per element:

```
Hobgoblin Captain (Init 18) — 28/45 HP
  Action: Greatsword vs Lyra. Atk 17 vs AC 16 — hit. 11 slashing. → Lyra: 18/29.
  Bonus: Tactical command — ally gets +1d4 next attack.
  Move: 5 ft to flank.
```

Damage results land with an arrow showing the target's new HP.

### Combat — PC turn prompt

Open each PC turn with a status line and immediate situation:

> *Lyra, you're up. AC 16, HP 18/29, Slots 2nd 1/2. Two enemies in melee, archer at 40 ft behind cover. What do you do?*

Always include: name, AC, HP, available slots if relevant, immediate threats with positions. End with the open question.

### Spellcasting — slot ledger

After any cast, post a one-line ledger:

```
Slots — 1st 2/4 · 2nd 3/3 · 3rd 1/2
```

Update incrementally. Don't re-list unchanged levels unless reconciling state.

### Clock ledger — end of scene or significant exchange

Format each entry as: name (current state → next threshold).

```
Clocks
- Coronation (5 weeks remaining → faction maneuvering at 4 weeks)
- Tribute (10 weeks remaining → Aurean envoy escalation at 8 weeks)
- Marek's network (probing → next move ~2 weeks)
- Edda's interests (background → next reach-out within 1 month)
```

List only active clocks. Add new ones when introduced. Retire clocks that have resolved.

### Campaign Log — end of session

```
Campaign Log — [in-fiction date / location]

Key events:
- [event]
- [event]
- [event]

NPCs introduced: [names with one-line roles]
Hooks left dangling: [active threads not yet resolved]
Time elapsed: [in-world]
Party state: [HP, slots, conditions, exhaustion, items of note]
New rulings: [any house rulings made this session]
```

## 20. Lingering Injuries (DMG 2024 Variant)

A variant rule for gritty campaigns. Triggers per `GM_Further_Guidelines.md`: critical hit on a PC, dropping to 0 HP, or failing a death save by 5 or more.

Roll 1d20 on the table below. Effects persist until specifically healed.

|d20 |Injury                |Effect                                                                                                                                              |Healing                                                                                  |
|----|----------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|1   |**Lost eye**          |Disadvantage on Wis (Perception) checks relying on sight; disadvantage on ranged attacks.                                                          |*Regenerate* spell. Otherwise permanent.                                                 |
|2   |**Lost limb (arm)**   |Cannot hold two items, dual-wield, or perform two-handed tasks. Specific actions impossible (climb with one hand, etc.).                            |*Regenerate*. Otherwise permanent.                                                       |
|3   |**Lost limb (leg)**   |Speed halved; disadvantage on Dex saves. Crutch or peg-leg restores some mobility; speed −10 ft from base.                                          |*Regenerate*. Otherwise permanent.                                                       |
|4-5 |**Internal injury**   |Disadvantage on Con checks and Con saves. Each strenuous action: Con save DC 10 or 1d6 damage.                                                      |1d4 weeks bed rest with daily Medicine DC 15 (success/day reduces remaining time by 1 day). *Greater Restoration*.|
|6-7 |**Broken ribs**       |As internal injury, but disadvantage on Str checks/saves instead.                                                                                   |1d3 weeks rest with Medicine DC 12 daily. *Greater Restoration*.                         |
|8-9 |**Horrible scar**     |Disadvantage on Cha checks with strangers (excluding Intimidation, which gains advantage). Permanent without magic.                                 |*Regenerate*, *Heal*, or cosmetic magic.                                                 |
|10-12|**Festering wound**  |HP max reduced by 1 per day (cumulative). Daily Medicine DC 15: success halts reduction; three consecutive successes cure.                          |As described, or *Lesser Restoration* (clears it immediately).                          |
|13-14|**Sprained joint**   |Random limb: speed −5 ft; Dex checks/saves disadvantage involving that limb.                                                                        |1d3 weeks of rest. *Lesser Restoration*.                                                |
|15-16|**Concussion**       |Disadvantage on Int checks for 1d6 days. Spell-casting on natural d20 = 1 may have minor mishap (GM discretion).                                    |1d6 days bed rest. *Lesser Restoration*.                                                |
|17-18|**Nerve damage**     |Random limb: −2 to attack rolls or skill checks involving it.                                                                                       |1d4 weeks rest or *Greater Restoration*.                                                |
|19  |**Limp**              |Speed −10 ft permanently. Disadvantage on Stealth.                                                                                                  |*Regenerate* or *Greater Restoration*.                                                  |
|20  |**Minor scarring**    |No mechanical effect. Cosmetic only.                                                                                                                |Time. Cosmetic magic.                                                                    |

Multiple injuries stack. A PC with three lingering injuries is a PC near retirement from active adventuring.

GM may substitute thematically appropriate alternative effects when an injury would be implausible (e.g., a PC who lost an arm last session cannot roll "Lost limb (arm)" again — reroll, or apply a thematic alternative like additional nerve damage).
