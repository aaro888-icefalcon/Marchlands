# Seed Deck — The Crossing at Skellan's Reach
_Refreshed at end of **Scene 1**. **33 cards.** The pen is behind you; the deck now points at the
besieged-town flight, the five roads, Maerwyn's claim and the Heath, the lost gear, and the new Front.
A seed is **consumed** when a scene / Turning Point / Random Event invokes it — replace it next refresh._

## Channel 1 — the live world ledger (clocks · threats · powers' moves) — ~half the deck
1. **The troll hits a second wall:** the screaming behind you peaks, then a whole section of palisade goes down — it's coming *through* the town, not staying in the pen. *(Moor's Hunger 1/8)*
2. **A road is choked:** the gate you're making for is jammed with a crush of fleeing townsfolk and fyrd — a slaughter-funnel, not an exit. *(Escape the Reach)*
3. **The Red Captain, loose:** you glimpse him in the chaos — free, armed, gathering his band and the hard strangers around him, his eye already on the valuable drift-come. *(Front: Lone Apex 0/6)*
4. **The dead in the undercroft answer:** under the road-station, something that was sealed five centuries starts to knock. Grave-cold seeps up through a cellar grate. *(Front: Restless Spirits 0/6)*
5. **The wasting shows:** a fleeing New Yorker stumbles — fevered, black-veined, too fast for any plague you know. Curse or contagion? *(Front: Plague/Curse 0/6)*
6. **Caine doesn't let go:** the inquisitor survives the breach and marks you for later — "the one the hag stole." The ordeal is deferred, not cancelled. *(Codex purge 0/8)*
7. **Godric salvages the disaster:** the reeve rallies the fyrd to hold a line — and starts pressing every able body, stranger or not, into the Crown's service. *(oath)*
8. **Hask cuts losses cruelly:** the factor's coffle breaks for the road with whatever "stock" he can drag — and an eye out for stragglers. *(enslavement / Slavers 0/6)*
9. **Slavers smell opportunity:** road-buyers move *in*, not out — a broken town full of unclaimed strays is a market. *(Slavers 0/6)*
10. **The Witan can't wait:** a Coronation rider reaches the Reach mid-catastrophe and finds the wildcard town in flames. *(Coronation 0/12)*
11. **The drift-come left behind:** the nurse, the paralegal, the lone kid — still penned, with the troll. Their faces, when you ran. *(personal · the penned drift-come)*
12. **A word on Akhil:** a fleeing stranger gasps they saw "a young Indian medic" with another cluster — east, days ago, alive. *(Find Akhil)*
13. **Maerwyn collects:** she makes plain what "you're the Mother's now" costs — she expects you to *come with her*, north, and she'll say why only on the road. *(Who holds you · Maerwyn w3)*
14. **The kit you left:** smoke or scavengers take the cart — your tools and armour are *gone*, not merely dropped. Re-arming means finding new. *(Re-arm)*
15. **Powers clash in the wreck:** Godric's men and Hask's bravos come to blows over the same fleeing strangers. *(Dueling lords)*
16. **Your back betrays you:** the claw-furrows stiffen and bleed; untended, infection is a clock of its own — and *you're* the only surgeon. *(HP 17/24 · wound)*

## Channel 2 — canon near the PC (the five roads · Maerwyn & the Heath · the undercroft)
17. **The drovers' ways:** Maerwyn knows the town's seams — beast-lanes and bolt-holes the fyrd doesn't watch. Follow her or strike out alone.
18. **The north road to the Heath:** Maerwyn's road — toward **Gythwin**, the barrows, and the thing whose name she gave you.
19. **The five roads fork at the road-station:** Eldermouth (south/court) · Stonebay (east/Ranulf) · the Harrowmark (north) · Mossdrowen & the Fells (west) · the Wealdvale (back). Each is a different campaign.
20. **The Veiled Mother's craft:** salt, threshold, the veil, the reading of the Working-trace — what Maerwyn says is "wet" on your skin.
21. **The Codex's long arm:** inquisitors test town by town; a man "stolen from the ordeal" is a marked man on every road.
22. **The Aureothan undercroft:** sealed under the Reach five centuries — and now knocking. The empire's dead keep rank.
23. **Eorl Cuthbert's wildcard vote:** whoever ends up holding the Reach's strangers holds leverage on the Coronation.
24. **The boy-king & the Aurean debt:** a Regency desperate enough to conscript the drift-come — and sitting on a secret it doesn't understand.
25. **Stonebay buys people:** Ranulf's reach to the east; the Tally-man was only a finger of it.
26. **Gythwin distrusts the bog:** even the Heath's own cunning-folk fear what's waking out there. *(Moor's Hunger)*

## Channel 3 — novelty (honest generator rolls, end of Scene 1)
27. **[wild: Seductive Peril]** On the moor-road north, something *easy* — a warm spring, a dry path, a soft voice — exactly where the hard land should offer none. The Heath baits its hooks.
28. **[community: Dueling Lords]** The Reach's authority fractures under the disaster: reeve, count's-factor, and church each claim command, and none yields.
29. **[npc: Venerable family elder]** On the road or in the first steading north, an old village matriarch/patriarch who remembers the *last* time the barrows stirred — and what it cost.
30. **[npc quirk: asks far too many questions]** A traveller who attaches to you and *probes* — where you're from, what you are, what you carry. Curious stray, Codex ear, or something wearing a face?
31. **[threat: Restless Spirits]** Not all the dead are below: a barrow-wight or grave-shade is already walking the Reach's bone-yard, drawn up by the Working. *(Front 0/6)*
32. **[treasure: a purse]** Somewhere in the flight — a dropped purse, **1d6 silver and an unreadable note** in a hand that isn't Hethrun-Saxon. A first coin in this world, and a small mystery. *(Re-arm)*
33. **[wild card]** The sky is still *wrong* — and every sensitive in Hethrun felt the Crossing. You are not the only drift-come who matters, and not the only one being hunted.
