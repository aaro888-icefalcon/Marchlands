# Seed Deck — Marchlands: Shake Rao
> 35 cards. Refreshed each bookkeeping (`bridge/seeds.md`). A card is consumed when a scene / Turning Point / Random Event invokes it; replace at next refresh.
> Channels: **[L]** live world ledger (~half) · **[C]** Harrowmark/Heath canon near the PC · **[N]** random novelty (generator rolls).
> Refreshed: end of scene 1.

## [L] Live world ledger
1. The Low-Barrow Net's cart hauls the herded New Yorkers off toward a Wardmoor-road handoff.
2. Hroth posts word on the Heath roads: a bounty for "the man with the grey dog" — a second awakened that slipped the net.
3. Aescwynn sells Tara's description (and a half-seen second drift-come) to an arrival-hunter buyer.
4. Tara, terrified and untrained, flares again in captivity — and something on the Heath answers.
5. One captive (Rachel? Marcus?) cuts a deal or escapes and surfaces ahead of Shake on the road.
6. Sibba Twice-Bought (the coerced translator) tries to slip a captive free at the next crossroads.
7. Lilla of Wardmoor angles to deliver Hroth's cell to a lawful patrol for amnesty.
8. The marked barrow-stone pulses again; a fresh arrival-pulse seeds another drift-come on the Heath.
9. The crowned stone's cold spreads downslope; frost where there should be rain.
10. A Coronation courier rides the Heath road south, sealed and in a hurry (the clock ticking offscreen).
11. Night-cries rise from a Wivelfen barrow (the Stir nearing 2/6 if neglected).
12. Bog-iron workers from House Blackfen's delvings break new ground into sealed barrow-earth.
13. Garm the hound takes Shake's scent from the crest and the Net follows the road south.
14. A drift-come who awakened as a "caster" is being hunted nearby — visible magic drew the hunters.
15. Word of the disappeared (Akhil among them) — a rumor that the taken can be *found*, for a price.
16. The Heath winter closes a day early: a hard cold front, exposure pressure on anyone caravan-less.
17. The Net's buyer is named — an arrival-hunter ring with reach to Stonebay's markets.

## [C] Harrowmark / Heath canon near the PC
18. **Skellan's Reach** ahead: a poor, fallen-prosperity hamlet wary of strangers; bog-iron and barrow-dread.
19. **Gythwin the Cunning** sends word — through a cunning-folk apprentice on the road — inviting "people who see clearly."
20. **Count Wulfhere of Wardmoor** (southern border) — an honest witness to the stirring; needs hands he can trust.
21. The Heath **wayhouse / road-shrine** at the ford: shelter, a keeper, travelers who trade news for warmth.
22. A barrow-wise local (Mother Eadlin / a Wivelfen apprentice) reads the cold and the click for what they are.
23. The **plague-quarter** of the bog-iron camp — a wasting sickness no local can name (a surgeon would).
24. House Greymoor's under-manned **watch-fort** — the realm's failing bulwark against the barrows.
25. House Eldgrave quietly **feeds the barrow-folk**, steering their hunger onto neighbours.
26. An Old-Faith libation-cairn (the sacrificed fyrd) on the Heath — disturbing it has a cost.
27. A wood-road **elf** or half-elf who recognizes a drift-come on sight and does not flee.

## [N] Random novelty (generator rolls)
28. **[Abandoned Village]** off the road — emptied recently, doors open, no bodies; why?
29. **[Fallen Prosperity]** Skellan's Reach: a town that remembers being more, and resents what took it.
30. **[Black Sheep]** a local outcast (disgraced heir / shunned cunning-child) who'd help an outsider to spite their kin.
31. **[Creeping Affliction]** a blight/sickness spreading from the delvings or the dead — slow, lethal, untreated.
32. **[Lingering Curse]** a barrow or relic whose curse clings to whoever disturbed it (barrow-cold writ small).
33. A scavengeable wreck/cache on the road — supplies, and a sign of who came through before.
34. A lone traveler on the Heath: guide, grifter, or bait — read which.
35. Weather-beat: mist swallows the road; the safe line is not the obvious one.
