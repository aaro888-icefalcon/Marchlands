# Seed Deck — Skellan's Reach (reset)
_Built for the revised frame: Crown intake, Edmund (execute) vs Stonebay (acquire) vs Crown (be rid of
them, but not to Stonebay). **24 cards.** Refresh to 30–40 next bookkeeping._

## Channel 1 — the live world (the three powers · the intake · the strangers)
1. **Hask presses for the noose:** Edmund's factor demands the Crown stop coddling "the abominations" and hang a batch as example. *(Edmund 0/6)*
2. **Stonebay's buyer makes an offer:** quiet coin to a guard, or to Godric — let a wagonload of strangers "escape" east tonight. *(Stonebay 0/6)*
3. **Godric stalls them both:** the reeve won't hang them for Edmund or sell them to Stonebay — but he wants them *gone,* and is short on options.
4. **A snatch in the night:** Stonebay's people don't wait for a yes — they come for strangers over the wall. *(Stonebay 0/6)*
5. **Edmund arrives in person:** the count rides to the Reach to force the issue, and the intake yard goes cold. *(Edmund 0/6)*
6. **The Crown's writ:** word from the capital — process the strangers, bother no one important, and keep the eorls from each other's throats over them.
7. **A stranger is made an example:** someone in the line is dragged out — Edmund's doing, or a "flight risk" — and what happens to them tells you your odds.
8. **The buyer prices YOU:** Stonebay's agent takes an interest in the PC specifically — a skill, a look, a usefulness.
9. **A guard can be turned:** Sefa, or another, is sick of the cruelty and might bend a rule — for a reason, or a price.
10. **The strangers organize:** the New Yorkers start to cohere — a leader, a plan, a list of who's missing.
11. **Rumor of another cluster:** strangers landed elsewhere — a town that welcomed them, or burned them. *(the Crossing)*
12. **The ledger:** your name goes on the Crown's roll — and being *on a list* in this world cuts both ways.

## Channel 2 — canon near the PC (the Reach · the three powers · the roads)
13. **Skellan's Reach:** walled crossroads town, dead-center Hethrun; five roads out to five eorldoms.
14. **Reeve Godric:** holds the Reach for the Crown — competent, tired, no friend and no monster.
15. **Count Edmund's March:** crowds the Reach from the west; a hard lord who reads the strangers as a threat to be ended.
16. **Stonebay & Ranulf:** the eastern power, rich on trade and traffic in people; wants the strangers as a windfall.
17. **The Crown / the Regency:** stretched thin, distracted by its own troubles, wanting only quiet.
18. **The roads:** south to the court, east to Stonebay, west to Edmund's March, north & back-country to thinner law — each a different kind of out.
19. **No coin, no name, no rights:** a stranger in Hethrun starts with nothing the law recognizes.

## Channel 3 — novelty / hooks
20. **A dropped purse:** somewhere — a few silver and a note in a hand no one here can read. A first coin, a small mystery. *(Find your people / the Crossing)*
21. **Someone asks too many questions:** a fellow "stranger" who probes you — where you're from, what you can do. Curious, desperate, or a plant?
22. **A useful skill, recognized:** something you can *do* (heal, fight, build, read people) gets noticed — and noticed means wanted.
23. **A face from home:** a New Yorker you half-recognize, or who knows someone you're looking for.
24. **The wrong kind of attention:** you do something that marks you as more than one more frightened stranger — and now all three powers have a reason to care which way you go.
