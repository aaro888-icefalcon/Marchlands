# Seed Deck — Marchlands: The Relief Ride
_Refreshed at the act-break (after Scene 9 / arriving Aldersweir, Day 3). Shake has broken out of the
besieged Hollowford to forge a relief army; first waypoint **Aldersweir** (a war-scarred horse-market),
his cast-out folk converging there. CF 6. **34 cards.** Three channels: the road/relief · the besieged keep ·
the deep threads. Refresh next bookkeeping._

## Channel 1 — the road & the relief ride (Aldersweir · the thanes · the muster)
1. **A wary, war-scarred welcome:** Aldersweir's lost herds and men to the war already; armed riders from the keep that just cast out refugees get a cold, frightened, half-hostile reception. *(Ill Will)*
2. **Cenred's cup:** the village's leading man — a drink-fond, modestly-prosperous horse-farmer — must be won before the village gives Shake anything (mounts, shelter, men). *(rally the village)*
3. **The herd that's left:** Aldersweir breeds horses — remounts an army needs — but raiders thinned the herd; what's left is the village's lifeblood and Shake's potential cavalry. *(resource / leverage)*
4. **The refugees arrive footsore:** Shake's cast-out Hollowford folk trickle in over the day — hungry, grieving, leaderless, a following to feed, organize, and turn into something. *(the gathering)*
5. **A banner to raise:** a relief army needs a *name* to gather to. Aldwin's? Edmund's? Shake's own? The first man to plant a standard owns the muster. *(the relief)*
6. **Grudging thanes:** the March's lesser thanes — scattered, some resentful of Edmund's enclosures — must be ridden to and *forged* into a host; each will want a price. *(THE RELIEF RIDE)*
7. **The field-host, leaderless:** Edmund's main strength is somewhere east toward Brackenford, Wystan dead at its head; whoever finds and rallies it gains an army — if the Ash-Captain's host doesn't find it first. *(THE RELIEF RIDE)*
8. **Sir Oswin opens doors:** the old household knight's unimpeachable word is Shake's key with suspicious lords — spend his credibility well. *(ally)*
9. **Eadgyth's price:** Lady Hildith made her a marriage-offer to seal Halfdan's horse — but the girl pines for someone *else,* and the road south is where she'd rather it went. *(lever / hook)*
10. **Coin runs short:** a purse and an Aurean sol buy a few thanes' goodwill; an *army* costs more than Shake carries. Where's the war-chest? *(the relief)*

## Channel 2 — the besieged keep behind you (Aldwin · the siege · the spine)
11. **The ring closes:** the Ash-Captain's host (~2,500–3,000) reaches Hollowford and the siege begins; every day Shake's away, the larder ticks down. *(the clock)*
12. **The boy alone:** Aldwin holds the walls on Shake's promise, with Garmund at his ear and guilt in his gut — does the regent harden into a lord, or break? *(the line / the leaderless March)*
13. **Garmund unchecked:** with the leech gone, the hostile captain owns the boy's command — for good (he's a real soldier) and ill (his cruelty, his Codex spite). *(rival)*
14. **The hunt for the emissary:** the broken patrol told the host an armed party slipped out; the Ash-Captain knows a relief is being raised — and sends men to stop it. *(pursuit)*
15. **Word from Wulf:** the doomsman keeps the keep's law and his own buried loyalties; a message from him could reach Shake — warning, plea, or worse. *(ally / mystery)*
16. **The plague in the camp:** the sickness Aldwin scattered may be ravaging the *besiegers* far worse than the walls — Shake's accidental weapon, if he could exploit it. *(the Creeping Sickness)*
17. **The dozen in the gatehouse:** the sick Shake salvaged at the gate — do they live, and does that mercy buy anything? *(consequence)*
18. **Soren's contract frays:** every unpaid week, the bought spine of the defense loosens; the day the mercenaries treat is the day Hollowford opens from within. *(the spine)*

## Channel 3 — the deep threads (the conspiracy · the Ash-Captain · his people · the secret)
19. **The Bone-Sayers reach ahead:** the broker-network behind Oslac and the assassin has agents in the Greenways markets — *including Aldersweir* — and the escaped marauder-captain now moves with them. *(A knife in Hollowford / An Organization)*
20. **Suspicion on Soren:** a thread at Aldersweir hints the keep's mercenary commander is the enemy's lever — his concealed sin a hold the Bone-Sayers have on him. Proof? And how to warn a keep you've left? *(Suspicion)*
21. **The marauder-captain's grudge:** the man who first named Shake a healer is free, hostile, and knows exactly what Shake is worth — and where he's going. *(Ill Will / loose end)*
22. **Who is the Ash-Captain, really?** a low-born foreign warlord starving for legitimacy, his host coordinated with the line-killers — what does he truly want, and can he be turned or bought? *(the host)*
23. **The hidden paymaster:** the same rich hand sent the host *and* the knives. Cut it — expose the coordinator (Ranulf? a thane? Ostmark?) — and the whole campaign loses its purse. *(the spine of the enemy)*
24. **The Mossdrowen trail, cold:** the bog-iron, the verge-fletching, the Bone-Sayer tally — the lead runs west to Mossdrowen and House Greldon. A thread for when Shake can ride it. *(GM-truth: Ranulf→Greldon→Bone-Sayers)*
25. **A word of sky-folk at last?** a realm crossed is a realm of news; somewhere on this ride, the first true whisper of Akhil or Shake's scattered fifty. *(Find your people)*
26. **The drift-marked road:** away from Maerwald's eyes, Shake is freer to use his real powers — and more exposed to new witnesses who'll wonder what a leech is doing with a war-hound and a hawk. *(the secret)*
27. **Maerwald's filed suspicion:** the court-mage clocked Shake handling an arcane scroll too knowingly; that debt waits at the keep. *(the secret)*
28. **The Codex eye:** a realm where drift-folk are "drift-tainted" to hardliners; the further Shake's deeds spread, the more some churchmen mark him. *(Drift-marked)*
29. **The Crown's price:** the Regency would relieve Hollowford to deny it to Ranulf — but the Crown helps no one for free, and envoy Aldhelm already has Shake's name in a ledger. *(Three powers)*
30. **Halfdan's calculation:** the northern count's horse could make the relief an army — but he moves for his own gain (the North Coast succession), and a marriage to Eadgyth is the coin he'd want. *(the relief / Halfdan)*
31. **The Coronation clock:** midwinter nears; whatever Shake builds, the realm's whole succession crisis tightens around the boy-king's crowning. *(Succession War)*
32. **The Crossing's question:** what tore eight million souls into Hethrun — and is there a way home — still unanswered under everything. *(The Crossing)*
33. **The home-front of the soul:** a surgeon turned warlord's-counsel killing men in the mist — what the Crossing is *making* of Shake, and what he'll have left when it's done. *(Personal)*
34. **The thing in the old stone (latent):** the cold under Hollowford's east tower that the animals feared — the Howe-Stirring waits, unrelated to the war, patient. *(the Front)*
