# Seed Deck — The Crossing: Hollowford Keep
_Refreshed end of Scene 1. Shake is off the rope, claimed by Count Edmund as his leech, taken to the
east tower to prove himself on a wounded body; tools (focus) returning under guard. A latent **Front**
(howe-stirring / lone-apex marauders / a noble faction's bid) now hums under the keep. **32 cards.**_

## Channel 1 — the live world (the east tower · the proving · the keep)
1. **The patient on the pallet:** the body Edmund wants alive — a war wound gone septic, fevered, hours from death; your modern hands know things this century doesn't. *(Survive / Drift-marked)*
2. **Your tools, under a guard's eye:** smith's kit (your focus) and medical roll laid out — but Wulf's man watches every move; using *real* magic in front of him is a choice. *(Drift-marked secret)*
3. **Who is the patient?** A favored knight, a kinsman, a captured noble worth ransom — someone whose life is *political*. Failing him costs more than the man. *(Three powers)*
4. **The cantrip tell:** Mending or Prestidigitation in the sickroom reads as ordinary low magic — but *Cure Wounds* closing a mortal wound does not. How much do you show? *(Drift-marked)*
5. **Wulf the sergeant:** Edmund's blunt instrument, not stupid; bored of lords' games, takes a man's measure by his hands. A potential ally — or your jailer. *(escape / leverage)*
6. **The smith's tools = the road back:** hold these long enough, find a long rest, and you can rebuild **Samosa**. The guard won't let them leave your wrist. *(recover companion)*
7. **Edmund returns to judge:** the Count comes to see his leech's work himself. Pass and you have a place; fail and the unfinished threat lands. *(Survive)*
8. **A second patient, worse:** the keep is full of wounded from the fighting; word of a working healer spreads down the wards fast. *(Drift-marked / leverage)*
9. **The strong-room remainder:** your longsword, +1 shield, bag — still logged below, NOT returned. Tools yes; arms no. *(recover gear)*
10. **A keep on a war footing:** Aurean coin, fresh walls, couriers in and out, the garrison drilling. Edmund is *getting ready* for something larger. *(Succession War / Noble Faction)*

## Channel 2 — canon near the PC (the war · the powers · the ledgers)
11. **Aldhelm's ledger:** the Crown envoy left promising to report Edmund's grab of you; the Regency keeps accounts, and your name is in them now. *(Two ledgers)*
12. **Halfdan's silent officer:** the man whose troops took you watched Edmund take his catch's prize and said nothing — his master will hear of it. *(Three powers)*
13. **The marauder-captain, still alive:** roped below, pleased with himself, sure you owe him; a chip who knows your face landed among raiders. *(Conflated / leverage)*
14. **The boy-king's clock:** Hadric must be crowned by midwinter (~6 wks) or the realm has no center; everyone here is racing or exploiting that. *(Succession War)*
15. **Three foreign hands:** Aurea behind Edmund, Ostmark behind Ranulf, the deep north behind Halfdan — your host serves an outside master too. *(Noble Faction's Bid)*
16. **Ranulf at open war:** Stonebay's pretender campaigns in the east; his raiders are why you were on that road at all. *(Lone Apex / Succession War)*
17. **The alliance that won't hold:** Edmund & Halfdan, allies of convenience against Ranulf — and everyone in the keep knows it's a season, not a peace. *(Three powers)*
18. **Magic is common here:** the keep has its own hedge-casters and a chaplain; basic spells won't shock anyone — being *drift-marked* and *Ranulf's* is what marks you.

## Channel 3 — the latent Front, your people, your secret (surfaces in play)
19. **Hollowford's older stones:** the keep sits on something far older; the east tower's lower courses aren't Hethrun work. *(Howe-Stirring — latent)*
20. **A cold that isn't weather:** in the deep keep, a wrongness — a guard who won't take the night watch below, a dog that won't go down a stair. *(Howe-Stirring — latent)*
21. **Edmund's dig:** the Count has men opening an old imperial ruin near/under his March; they've stopped reporting what they find. *(Howe-Stirring / horror — latent)*
22. **Raiders on the wind:** couriers bring word of a war-host under one dread captain burning its way across the march — bigger than Ranulf's usual. *(Lone Apex — latent)*
23. **The named noble's move:** somewhere a lord's faction is making a play big enough to crack the whole succession — and Hollowford may be a piece on that board. *(Noble Faction's Bid — latent)*
24. **Word of another cluster:** a soldier or captive mentions sky-folk landing elsewhere — a thread toward Akhil or your ~50. *(Find your people)*
25. **A face that knows a face:** someone here has met a drift-folk matching one of your people. *(Find your people)*
26. **The drift-tainted slur:** a Codex-leaning hardliner in the keep names you *drift-tainted* and means harm; another sees *drift-blessed* and means use. *(Drift-marked)*
27. **Find Familiar:** a quiet hour + 10 gp of components and **Daegon** flies again — eyes in the sky, if you can get the materials. *(recover companion)*
28. **The invisibility card:** still unspent; no one knows you can vanish once. The question is when spending it buys the most. *(escape)*
29. **A patron's offer:** Edmund — or, quietly, the Crown or Halfdan — floats terms: service, oath, indenture, in exchange for your neck and your hands. *(Three powers)*
30. **The war comes to the gate:** a raid, a courier with black news, an attack — the keep's attention yanked away at a useful or terrible moment. *(Lone Apex / Succession War)*
31. **Too useful to free:** the better you heal, the more valuable you become — and the harder any of these powers will work to *own* you, not free you. *(Drift-marked / Survive)*
32. **The thing that slips out:** you do something only a kept-class drift-marked could, in front of the wrong witness — and now you're a prize three powers will fight over. *(Drift-marked)*
