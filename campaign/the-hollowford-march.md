# The Hollowford March — a gazetteer

The independent thanedom of **Count Edmund**, in the **southern Greenways** of Hethrun — a
prosperous-but-resented marcher county held of the Crown directly. Built from canon
(`reference/realm_titles.md`: southern Greenways, "aggressive land-enclosure; the realm's
standing antagonist-county; self-interested") + the campaign's play.

## The land
Rolling arable farmland and hedged pasture in the valleys of two rivers — the **Hollow**
(west-central) and the **Marl** (east) — which meet in the south and run down to the crown
demesne and Eldermouth. Sheep and horse country, fat fields, good stone where there's stone
at all. The defining scar on it is **Count Edmund's enclosures:** over a generation he swallowed
commons and grazing into his own demesne, growing rich and *hated* — which is why the March is
the realm's "standing antagonist-county," why its own thanes resent it, and why a war that should
unite it instead finds it brittle.

**Borders:** WEST — the Greatwood verge, rising toward **Mossdrowen** (the cold trail to the
conspiracy). EAST — **Brackenford** eorldom (the Brackenford wood, where Wystan was ambushed).
SOUTH — the **crown demesne / Eldermouth** (the Crown) and the **Wealdvale** grain-vale. NORTH —
upland toward the **Heath / Harrowmark.**

## Settlements

### The heart (central, the Hollow)
- **Hollowford Keep & town** — Count Edmund's seat; a strong, fresh-walled castle on the ford of
  the Hollow, an unwalled market town below it. **STATUS: BESIEGED** by the Ash-Captain's host;
  held by boy-regent **Aldwin** on Garmund's command and Soren's bought spine.
- **Stanmere** — **Thane Beorhtric's** hold, central-north; the March's largest thaneland and
  retinue (~40 horse + 300 fyrd), strong-walled. The relief's would-be backbone — *if* Shake can
  master the man's bitter grudge over the enclosures. *(Uncommitted; the next great prize to court.)*
- **Harbury** — *Cultural Center.* The March's market-fair-and-**moot** town: where the thanes'
  hall-moot meets and a renowned autumn horse-and-harvest fair draws the whole county. The civic
  and social heart (as Hollowford is the military one). News, coin, and the March's *opinion* live here.

### The east (the Marl, toward Brackenford)
- **Marlford** — a walled river-**burh** on the Marl-ford on the Brackenford road. Held the
  leaderless field-host; **STATUS: SHAKE'S** — taken from the profiteer **Cray** (fled); now the
  relief's muster-point and Shake's forward base. Saewulf holds its keep for him.
- *(beyond: the Brackenford wood — Wystan's death-place — and the eorldom border.)*

### The south (down the Greenways, toward Eldermouth)
- **Aldersweir** — *Unique Product (horses) · Scars of War.* A war-scarred horse-market, ~half-day
  south of Hollowford; raided early, its herds thinned. **STATUS:** Shake's cast-out refugees
  gather here (wary headman **Cenred**); a thin, hostile-turning-hopeful foothold.
- **Edenmoor Abbey** — an isolated Codex abbey-school, scriptorium & hospice (southwest).
  **STATUS: SACKED** by a paid marauder band; ~2 dozen survivors saved by Shake, now at Godric's.
  (Aldred, Eadgyth's love, died here.)
- **Sir Godric's hall** — a young landed knight's fortified hall & village; sheltering the Edenmoor
  survivors. **STATUS:** sworn to Shake (Godric + 10 lances).
- **Goldmead** — *Widespread Prosperity.* The March's fat breadbasket — rich fields, a well-off
  market village, so far untouched by the war. A prize of **supplies, coin, and recruits** for the
  relief — and exactly the sort of plum the Ash-Captain's foragers will turn to next.

### The west (the verge, toward the Greatwood)
- **Oxmere** — a steading and mill on the western edge. **STATUS: BURNED** — the Ash-Captain's
  first stroke into the March; its folk are the core of Shake's refugees.
- **Brightwell** — *Upstart Faith.* A hamlet round an old holy well where, in the war's terror, a
  charismatic preacher has raised a fervent new following (Old-Faith revival or Codex firebrand —
  unresolved). Zealots: a potential ally, a potential mob, and a sharp edge near a *drift-marked*
  captain.
- **Thornholt** — *Xenophobic Locals.* An insular fen-and-wood-edge hamlet that trusts no
  stranger and likes drift-folk least of all. Closed gates and cold eyes; knows the verge-paths a
  stranger doesn't.
- **Banneret Osmund's hall** — a once-proud seat fallen to ruin (a marginal western holding); its
  lord **won't answer** the call and *might* be bought by the wrong hand.

### Scattered gentry
- **Sir Brunwulf's hall** and a clutch of lesser Greenways knights' seats — small fortified halls,
  a few lances each; the snowball of Shake's muster.

## The military overlay (now)
- **The Ash-Captain (~2,500–3,000)** holds the **center** — besieging Hollowford, having ravaged
  the western March (Oxmere). His foragers range hungry; **Goldmead** and **Harbury** are the next
  fat targets.
- **Shake's relief** (~800–1,000 field-host + ~40 lances + Saewulf's holdouts) sits **east at
  Marlford**, the hammer to the keep's anvil — needing to gather more (Stanmere, Halfdan, the
  Crown) and time the blow for when the siege has gnawed the enemy weak, before midwinter's
  Coronation Reckoning decides the realm.
