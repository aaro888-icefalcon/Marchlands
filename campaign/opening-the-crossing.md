# THE CROSSING AT SKELLAN'S REACH
### Session Zero opening for Marchlands (a mythic-gm companion)

A cold open for a drift-folk PC: New York to a binding-rope in one black second; condemned in a
crossroads town beside the very raiders you've been mistaken for, while **four powers** argue what you're
*for* and **two faiths** argue what you *are* — until something the Crossing dragged in tears the town
open, and you run.

> Run as the engine's **First Scene** (never Scene-Tested). Honest dice from the first roll. The
> catastrophe is **rolled** (§6); the four powers each rope to a different apex (§5); so the same opener
> points at a different campaign every game. Permadeath is live. PC-agnostic.

> **THIS CAMPAIGN'S ROLLS (locked at setup):**
> - **§6 catastrophe — d10 = 5 → "The Moor came down":** a famished **moor-troll** (GM pick over the
>   ankheg) driven south onto the Reach because **the Heath is waking — "Moor's Hunger."** Aims the
>   campaign at the *fifth* road (the Old Faith / Maerwyn / the Heath), not one of the four powers' —
>   so **Maerwyn** is the natural survivor who names the apex, and it ties straight into the
>   waking-barrow crisis.
> - **PC:** Abhishek "Shake" Rao (reused build — Human Battle Smith Artificer 3). Character creation
>   is folded in rather than re-run; his diegetic interrogation answers are his existing sheet.

## 1. The Crossing (canon)
In one instant the entire population of New York City — some eight million souls — was torn out of its
world and scattered across this one. Not in one place: in **clusters, across every realm**. Thousands
woke in the avenues of Aurea, in Ostmark's war-camps, on the Iron Strait, under the Greatwood, in the
deep Heath. Every power on earth is, this week, finding a flood of impossible strangers in its lands —
and the wise are already asking what could move eight million souls at once, and what it cost. The sky
still looks wrong. There is smoke on every horizon.

The Crossing is a **Working of impossible scale**, and the strain of it rang through the realm's old
seals, barrows, ruins, and pacts like a struck bell. Things stirred. (That bell is the engine of §6.)

## 2. Skellan's Reach (where — central by design)
A walled market-town at the **dead center of Hethrun**, in the **Brackenford eorldom** (Eorl Cuthbert —
the Coronation's wildcard vote). The Reach grew around an **Aureothan road-station** where the old
imperial road forks to every eorldom: **Eldermouth** south · **Stonebay** east · the **Harrowmark**
north · **Mossdrowen** and the **Fells** west · the **Wealdvale** at its back. Whatever happens here,
five roads lead out, each somewhere different. Beneath the town is the road-station's **undercroft**,
sealed five centuries. A cluster of the Crossing landed on and around the Reach; the terrified town did
the only thing it could think of — it **penned the strangers**.

## 3. Cold open + who you are (character creation = the interrogation)
Run it tight:
- **The last true thing** was New York — a specific second the player names (the platform, the kitchen,
  the cab, the 3 a.m. desk). *Ask it; that second is the character's old life.*
- **Then black.**
- **Then rope.** Wake in a holding pen, wrists bound, a sack-hood just pulled off, the light wrong, the
  smell of tar and river-mud and too many frightened people.

**Character creation is diegetic.** A Crown clerk works the line with a wax tablet, and the questions
*are* the sheet: your name; what you did in the world you came from (**background**); what your hands
know — "can you fight? read? heal? hunt?" (**class**); what crossed over with you (**any item/edge**).
Build the 5.5e sheet from the answers.

**The edge an AI can actually run:** you *know* things — what a quarantine is, how a mob turns, what
gunpowder smells like, how to talk a scared man down. Against people, that's real leverage. Against what
comes in §6, it is worth nothing — and that gap is the point.

## 4. The pen (the company you're kept in)
You're penned with two kinds of people, and the Reach can't tell them apart:
- **Scared New Yorkers** — dozens in the yards and barns: a paralegal repeating his title like a password
  that still works somewhere, a nurse already triaging, a kid with no one. Unarmed, modern, lost.
- **A real captured threat you're conflated with** — a **marauder band** the fyrd took off the imperial
  road that same chaotic day (default; swap to **sea-raiders** for a coastal lean). Chained heavier in
  the same pen. Their leader, **the Red Captain**, watches everything and has already worked out that the
  strangers are worth more *free* than dead.

*The town's logic: strangers who appeared from nowhere, and raiders who appeared on the road — all of a
piece, all for the rope.* This conflation is why you're **bound and condemned**, not merely detained.

## 5. What you're FOR, and what you ARE — four powers, two faiths
Two questions hang over the pen, and the Reach can't move past either: **what are these strangers FOR**
(four powers, four sets of terms) — and **what ARE they**, drift-folk or demons (two faiths, two tests).
The faiths insist you be cleared of taint before any power may claim you; the powers chafe at the delay.
You may be mid-test when §6 lands.

### The four powers — each binds you on different terms, each a thread to a big bad
*(A spectrum, most legitimate to least.)*
1. **The Regency — Reeve Godric** (holds the Reach for boy-king Hadric; Aurean-funded, desperate).
   **Binds by OATH:** swear fealty and become a sworn Crown ward — fed, shielded, and owned by the throne
   (and its Aurean debt). **Thread → the dragon:** Hadric's blood is manifesting draconic; the regency is
   unknowingly sitting on a lure for **Vaerelloth** and her dragon-cult.
2. **The Pretender — the Tally-man** (a one-eyed Bone-Sayer; Ranulf of Stonebay's buyer).
   **Binds by COERCIVE CONTRACT:** a mercenary indenture pressed on you in the rope's shadow — signed
   because the fire is the other option. **Thread → the fiend:** Ranulf's luck is bought; the Star-Priest
   **Branwald** and the **Burning One** of Ostmark are the hand behind him.
3. **The Mosswood Wardens — Serjeant Brona** (the wood-watch of Mossdrowen, under Captain Saerwen).
   **Binds by ENLISTMENT:** take the oath-of-the-wood and hold a failing frontier — hard service, but out
   of the pen and clear of the court's games. **Thread → the forest:** the **Sere's** spreading blight and
   the **Smokeless Throne**, the archfey court the Wardens and their elf-allies hold the line against.
4. **The Count — Hask, Count Edmund's factor** (the encloser whose March crowds the Reach).
   **Binds by ENSLAVEMENT:** no oath, no contract — a brand and a coffle to the Count's Aureothan dig.
   **Thread → the undead:** what Edmund digs toward is no empty ruin — **Coelius Vetranus** the lich, and
   a jurisdiction of the dead that wants re-asserting.

### The two faiths — both test for demonic taint, by opposite methods
- **The Codex — Inquisitor Caine.** By **ordeal**: hand on the blessed relic, the catechism, consecrated
  iron to the skin — a demon flinches, and a flinch is the fire. Harsh, public, rigged toward guilt.
- **The Old Faith — the cunning-woman Maerwyn** (Veiled Mother; sister-in-craft to Gythwin of the Heath).
  By **reading**: the Working-trace on your skin, your dreams, salt and the threshold and the Mother's
  veil — to prove you Mother-touched, not damned, and wrest you from Caine's ordeal.
The faiths agree only that you must be tested; they despise each other's method, and your life rides on
whose wins. Maerwyn is the one most likely to cut you loose when it all goes wrong (§7).

### But the claim is usually moot
Whatever a power stakes here, the siege (§6) almost always breaks it — you escape, and a claim you fled
doesn't hold. The binding-type matters in only two cases:
- **They keep control of you.** If a power gets you out of the Reach still in hand (you didn't break free
  clean, or you let an agent haul you out), you leave **actually bound on their terms** — sworn,
  contracted, enlisted, or enslaved. The hardest, most hooked start.
- **You rejoin under better terms.** Later, by choice and with leverage, you come back to a power — and
  now the terms are yours to set. The claim becomes a real, *chosen* allegiance.
Otherwise the four claims are the opening's political map: who wanted you, what they'd have made of you,
and the debts and doors you carry out the gate — **not chains.**

Play it hot: argued *over*, then *tested*, then suddenly none of it matters — because §6.

## 7. Freed
The siege frees you — pick what the fiction makes truest, or roll: the threat smashes the pen · the Red
Captain cuts the line loose to make his own break and your bindings go with it · **Maerwyn** cuts you free
mid-test · a power's agent frees you to pull you their way (if they then keep hold of you, §5's "kept
control" applies) · or you seize the half-second yourself. Your hands come free, and nothing is yet decided.

## 8. Grab what your hands know (class-tailored gear)
A besieged crossroads town is full of suddenly-unguarded steel — the fyrd's rack, the smithy, the chapel,
the cunning-woman's bag, a merchant's strong-room, the **raiders' confiscated kit on a cart**. The PC
grabs **one good thing that says who they are.** *(For Shake, the natural grab is his own confiscated
drift-folk kit off that cart — the +1 longsword / +1 shield and his tools, which the fyrd lumped in with
the raiders' seized steel.)*

## 9. Escape the besieged Reach — and what sticks
The town is a trap closing; the roads are the way out. In the chaos each power's agent grabs for you — but
**fleeing toward a power is choosing a direction, not signing on.** Three outcomes:
- **Break free clean (the usual).** You leave owing no one; the claims go moot. Choose a road by where you
  want to go.
- **A power keeps control.** If you don't break clean — or you let an agent haul you out — you leave
  **bound on their terms** (oath / contract / enlistment / chains). The hardest start, the strongest hook.
- **Rejoin later, better.** Whatever you do now, the doors stay open: come back to a power down the road
  with leverage, and set the terms yourself.

Who's reaching for you, and where their road leads:
- **Godric / Sefa → Eldermouth & the court** (the Regency; the **dragon**-blooded king).
- **the Tally-man → Stonebay** (Ranulf; the **Burning One**).
- **Serjeant Brona → the Mossdrowen wood** (the Wardens; the **Smokeless Throne** / the Sere).
- **Hask → the Hollowford March** (Count Edmund; the **Lich**) — mostly an escape *from*.
- **Maerwyn → the Heath** (the Old Faith; she saves you, toward Gythwin and the waking barrows) — the
  sympathetic road, **and the one this game's §6 points down.**
- **Alone → any of the five gates.** You owe no one.

Either way you leave hurt, half-armed, **hunted by §6**, your name on the clerk's tablet, every road in
Hethrun in front of you — and a clear sense of who, in this realm, already wants to own you.
